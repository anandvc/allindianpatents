Title of Invention

"A COLD EXPLOSIVE COMPOSITION FOR UNDERGROUND MINES AND OTHER EXCAVATION WORK AND A PROCESS FOR THE PREPARATION OF THE COMPOSITION THEREOF"
Abstract A cold explosive composition for underground mines and other excavation work and a process for the preparation of the composition thereof by oxidizing agent(s) Ammonium nitrate 43.0.3 to 44.20 wt%, Calcium nitrate (hydrated) 26.45 to 26.85 wt%, Reducing agent(s) Aluminium powder 2.30 to 2.35 wt%, Sugar 6.74 to 7.93 wt%, Thiourea 0.10 to 0.09 wt%, Urea 2.38 to 2.47 wt%, GuargumO.9 to 1.0 wt%, Cooling agent(s) Water 6.50 to 6.60, Ammonium chloride 9.50 to 11.30 wt%, Cross-linking agent(s) 0.04 to 0.05 wt%, Gassing agent(s) 0.04 to 0.05 wt%.
Full Text This invention relates to a cold explosive composition for underground mines and
other'excavation works and a process for the preparation of the composition there of

The nitroglycerin (NG) based explosives are available in cartridge form in
diameter 32 mm and weight 200 gm. These explosives have velocity of detonation (VOD) ranging from 2200 to 2900 m/sec and strength from 40 to 45 percent blasting gelatin explosive. The density varies from 1.10 to 1.25 gm/cm3. The nitroglycerine base compositions contain (i) Ammonium nitrate 55.50%, NG 20.00%, Nitro-cellulose 0.50%, Wood flour 2.50% and Sodium Chloride 21.50%; Ref. Encyclopedia of Explosives and related items PATR 2700 Vol. 3, p C450-455. Its cartridge material is waxed paper. These are highly sensitive to shock, friction, heat and flame. The fume quality is poor. When misfired, explosives are mixed in blasted muck may cause accident during loading and thereby increased danger during the operation.
The slurry explosives are available in cartridge form having a diameter from 32 mm , weight 200 gm. and cartridge material is plastic tube with copper clips or blow molded tube. They are cap sensitive composition. Their velocity of detonation varies from 2800 to 3200 m/sec. and density 1.10 to 1.15 gm/cm3. The available explosives are poor in quality and deteriorates within six months. Their strength varies from 30 to 36% of BG. The explosion temperature varies from 2200-2600 °C with longer flame duration 14-15ms. Due to the higher explosion temperature and longer flame duration the ignition of methane air mixture occurs and low energy also causes poor rock fragmentation. There is a need for providing a new synergistic explosive composition which has lower explosion temperature and overcome the above noted drawbacks. Higher percentage of water in the formulation makes composition poor shelf life and affects its explosive parameters. Low percentage of water creates problem which makes explosives hard and grainy due to the crystallization of inorganic oxidizer salts on the contrary a higher percentage of water

decreases the sensitivity as well as the strength, Ref, T. Urbanski, Chemistry and Technology of Explosives, P. 546-547, Vol.4.
In our copending Indian Patent application No. 3071/DEL/98, Nabiullah, B. M. P. Pingua, Jagdish and T. N. Singh have described an explosive composition useful for safe blasting of coal and claimed the composition in the range of (g/100) Ammonium nitrate 40.15 to 41.10, Calcium nitrate 13.50 to 14.00, Aluminium (flake grade) 2.50 to 3.00, Sugar 7.30 to 7.40, Urea 1.80 to 2.36, Thio-urea 0.90 to 1.00, Sodium chloride 14.01 to 14.50 and water 18.45 to 19.00. The composition was prepared for highly gassy mine in P-5 conditions.
In another copending Patent Application No. 2642/DEL/96, B. M. P. Pingua, Nabiullah, Jagdish and B. B. Dhar have described and claimed a coal base composition for surface blasting for dry holes. The composition claimed was (g/100) (i) Ammonium nitrate =92.00, Coal powder = 6.4, Guar gum=0.8, sodium nitrite = 0.4 and Potassium dichromate = 0.4 . (ii) Ammonium nitrate = 73.6, Sodium nitrate =15.6, Coal powder = 8.8, Guar gum = 1.5, Sodium nitrite = 0.2 and Potassium dichromate =0.3. C. In still another copending Patent application No. NF-112/99, B. M. P. Pingua, Nabiullah, Jagdish and T. N. Singh have described and claimed an explosive composition for cut face blasting in P-3 condition for underground coal mines. The said composition comprises composition (g/100) in the range of Ammonium nitrate 40.60 to 40.80, Calcium nitrate = 31.30 to 31.40, Aluminium powder = 2.75 to 3.00, sugar = 8.95 to 9.13, Flour = 1.47 to 1.50, Guar gum = 0.99 to 1.00, Water = 4.30 to 4.50, Sodium Chloride = 9.00 to 9.10, Potassium pyro-antimonate = 0.04 to 0.05 and Sodium nitrite = 0.04 to 0.05. The main objective of the present invention is to provide a cold explosive composition for underground mines which obviates the above noted drawbacks.

Yet another object is to provide a synergistic composition having velocity of detonation between 3000 to 3200 m/sec; explosion temperature in between 1100 to 1,200°K; explosion energy in the range of 1550 - 1575 kJ/kg to break the rock with good fragmentation; strength of composition in between 35 to 37 % of BG.
Another object of the present invention is to provide a process for preparing the novel explosive composition.
Yet another object of the present invention is to develop a synergistic composition suitable as cold explosive for underground mines and other excavation work.
This permitted group of explosive is cap- sensitive, low velocity of detonation (less than 3300 m/sec.) and non- incendive in methane and coal dust explosive mixture. The detonation temperature of this invented synergistic composition is in between 1100-1200° K with short flame duration in the range of 8-10 ms.
The composition of the present invention utilizes ingredients of oxidizing agents such as ammonium nitrate and calcium nitrate, reducing agents such as sugar, urea, thio-urea and aluminium powder, cooling agents such as ammonium chloride and water, cross-linking agents such as potassium dichromate, potassium pyro-antimonate or mixture thereof and sodium nitrite as a gassing agents was used to produce zero oxygen balance to provide a composition suitable as a slurry explosive.
