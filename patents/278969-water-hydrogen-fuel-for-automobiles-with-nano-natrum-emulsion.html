How usually an internal combustion engine works:
In a single stroke engine, the cylinder and piston are arranged as shown in the diagram. The piston moves up and down in the cylinder freely with airtight mechanism. The base of the piston is attached to the connecting rod. The cylinder is closed with a metal head. It is air tight in nature. It is provided with intake pipe for the automizer and another air intake cum exhaust pipe.




The internal combustion engine makes use of the explosive energy released in the burning of diesel fuel to generate motive power.
As the piston moves down fuel from automizer nozzle and air from intake and exhaust pipe is drawn into cylinder. The mixture is compresed as the piston rises up.
3w "the vertical diesel engine there is no spark plug. The temperature produced by compression creates ignition (200-600°c). Fuel and air mixture explodes and brings in working stroke.
The piston goes down by working stroke. It comes up again pushes out all hot and waste gases and smoke. In this way suction, compression,working and exhaust strokes happen one after another and bring in rotatory motion to the engine.
