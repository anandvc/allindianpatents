Title of Invention

A BOTTOM SUPPORT FOR WASTE HEAT RECOVERY BOILER (WHRB) INLET GAS DUCTING.
Abstract A bottom support means for Waste Heat Recovery Boiler inlet gas ducting comprising a bottom duct supported by means of bottom supporting arrangement, said bottom supporting arrangement comprises beam/beams adapted to be connected to the main boiler columns such that to transfer the load, a slide bearing plate being secured to said beam at the location where said bottom duct being rested , a vertical buck stay with a beam being provided to connected the duct wall therewith by means of bolts and nuts, said duct wall being connected to a T-bar level bracings through a T-bar stiffeners and a plate member secured therewith.
Full Text This invention relates to a bottom support means for Waste Heat Recovery Boiler (WHRB) inlet gas ducting. In particular this invention relates to an improvement in or relating to s supporting means for Waste Heat Recovery Boiler.
The Waste Heat Recovery Boiler inlet duct comprises a bottom duct portion made of stainless steel and a top duct portion made of alloy steel. The bottom duct is connected to the top duct by a plurality of hanger supports having pins and link arrangements at the ends and in the middle of the duct. Diamond shape plates are provided on either sides of the duct for connecting the ducts with each other rigidly.
There? are certain disadvantages associates- with the prior art, such as failure of hanger links, cracking of diamond plate and other supporting elements. Further the maintenance of the conventional supporting means is very high and the provision for on line inspection of critical support elements are not provided with the conventional supporting means.
Therefore the main object of the present invention is to propose an improved bottom support means which obviates the disadvantages associated with the prior art.
Acccsrding to this invention there is provided a bottom support for Waste Heat Recovery Boiler having a bottom duct supported means comprising beam/beams adapted to be connected to the main boiler columns such that to transfer the load, a slide bearing plate being secured to said beam at the location where said bottom duct being rested, a vertical buck stay with a beam being provided to connect the duct wall therewith by means of bolts and nuts, said duct wall being connected to a T-bar level bracings through a T-bar stiffeners and a plate member secured therewith.
In accordance with this invention the bottom duct it connected separately by means of bottom supporting arrangement at T-bar lever. The bottom support arrangement comprises beam/beams connected to the main boiler columns for load transfer. A slide bearing plate is attached on the beam at the palce where the bottom duct rests. A duct wall with external insulation is connected with a vertical buck stay with a beam by means of nut and bolts. Another horizontal beam is secured with the vertical buck and rests on the bearing plate.
The horizontal beam is further connected to the duct at the second level by means of interconnecting structure. Plate stiffeners are provided for strengthening the structural members. The constant load hangers (CLH) support arrangement is also provided at the overhang and is provided with a tie rod having the height adjustment arrangements.
