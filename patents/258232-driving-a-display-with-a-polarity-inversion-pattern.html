Abstract The present invention refers to a procedure for the large-scale amplification of human lymphocytic cell lines for therapeutic use, consisting of a homogeneous culture system. The claimed invention also refers to the production of therapeutic doses of lymphocytic cells cultured homogeneously.

The field of the invention concerns the in vitro cell culture and the large-scale expansion of isolated human cells. Prior art
An approach to the anti-tumour therapy is based on the use of ex vivo isolated cell lines or cells endowed with cytotoxic activity.
In the early '90, a number of T-lymphocyte cell lines derived from children with acute T-ceJJ lymphoblastic leukemia, named TALL, were isolated. They include T-cell lines TALL-104, TALL-107, TALL-103/2, described by O'Connor et al (Blood, J99V77^t534-4545J and by Cesano and Santoli (In Vitro Cell. Dev. Biol., 1992, 28: 648-656). They exhibit such interesting characteristics that they are now successfully used in the treatment of tumours in animal models and in man (Cesano ef a/., BJood J991. 87:393-403; Cesano et a/., Cancer Res., 1996, 56: 3021-3029,4JS.5,272,0S2; US 5,683,690; US 5,702,702).
TALL cell lines are endowed with cytotoxic, specifically anti-tumour, activity and are active against different types of tumours: the main characteristic of these cells is that they are MHC non-restricted (Cesano et a/., J. Immunol., 1993, 151: 2943-2957) and, therefore, can be administered to any patient, independently of the histocompatibility antigens phenotype. Furthermore, unlike some types of cytotoxic lymphocytes, such as for example TIL and LAK, TALL cell lines do not need, after in vivo administration, a concomitant treatment with iymphokines. This is a further advantage of said cells, since the simultaneous administration of Iymphokines has several drawbacks.
Moreover, TALL lymphocytes have been successfully tested in a variety of tumours. These are the reasons why they are considered an interesting therapeutic alternative. However, the cell expansion systems used so far are iimiting because the cells prepared for adoptive immunotherapy derive from cultures grown in single flasks, as described in Cesano et al., Cancer Res., 1996 56: 4444-4452 and Visonneau et ai, Clin. Cancer Res., 1997, 3: 1789-1797, although large-scale cell cultures apparatuses like those used in the preparation of

monoclonal antibodies and recombinant proteins have long been utilised.
Therefore, a large-scale culture system suitable for cells of this type is highly
desirable.

Adenylate-kinase is released from the cells when they lose the membrane integrity and converts the substrate "ADP into ATP in the presence of Mg++, thus allowing its measurement. The determination was performed by plating 105 target cells, K562, onto e many-well microplate; cells prepared according to the method were added, in amounts of 106, 5x105 and 2.5x105, to obtain T/K {or effector/target) ratios equal to 10.5 and 2.5, respectively.
