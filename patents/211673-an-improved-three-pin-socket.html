The invention relates to an improved three pin socket. More particularly the invention relates to an improved three pin socket for electrical connection with overlapping grooves.
The conventional two-in-one three-pin socket that is in a market having six contacts each three for 6 Amp. and 16 Amp. plug members which are connected with a top member with matching holes having four fastening means. This type of three pin socket requires more contact materials and requires more number of groove therefore it is not cost effective. Also apart from this it requires more labour.
Hence there is a need to prevent the material wastage and to provide improved three pin socket with overlapping grooves so that three contacts can serve the purpose of six contacts socket of two-in-one type.
Accordingly there is provided an improved three pin socket comprising a bottom member of box type construction open at one end made from mouldable material having three contacts in triangular fashion in which two are base contacts and third is topmost central contact, wherein both base contacts made from metallic strips which are configured in to shape of numeral 8 so as to provide contacts for both 6 Amp and 16 Amp plug members and topmost central contact is common for both type of plug members; a front member to cover said bottom member, said front member having three grooves for the contacts, wherein the top groove is common but the both base grooves are having overlapping grooves of different diameter in which upper groove having smaller diameter than the bottom groove; said front member is fastened with the base member by screwing from the back of the base member. According to present invention the distance of the contacts for smaller and the bigger plug pin are according to the standard size of the plug pin available in the market. Also front member having fixing means at the top and bottom and front member is with or without flap member.
