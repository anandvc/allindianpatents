Title of Invention

"STAND OF MOTORCYCLE"
Abstract [MEANS FOR SOLVING THE PROBLEMS] A protection plate 57d is integrally provided to a tread bar 57c so that muddy water D does not splash on the body 2, Id and fellow passenger P. [EFFECT] The protection plate will not allow muddy water to splash on the body and fellow passenger to contaminate the body and legs of fellow passenger.
Full Text The present invention relates to a 'stand of a motorcycle.

[Problems to be Solved by the Invention] Fig. 11 is a side elevation showing the operation of the main stand of the related art. If the reinforcing member 104 of the main stand 100 is splashed by muddy water 107 when the motorcycle 110 is running toward the left direction of the drawing, the muddy water 107 also splashes to the side of body 108 to contaminate. Moreover, if a fellow passenger is taking the rear seat, legs of this fellow passenger may be contaminated.
Therefore, it is therefore an object of the present invention to provide a stand of motorcycle which can protect
the body and a driver and fellow passenger from the splashed muddy water.
[Means for Solving the Problems]
According to the present invention there is provided a stand of motorcycle provided with a main stand at the lower part of the body and a tread bar for stepping operation is provided to this main stand in the manner as extending toward outside of the body, characterized in that the tread bar is integrally provided with a protection plate for protecting the body or a driver from splashed muddy water.
Since the protection plate is provided, the body, a driver and fellow passenger are protected from muddy water and thereby the body and legs of driver and fellow passenger are never contaminated.
The protection plate is provided in almost the running direction of motorcycle when the main stand is accommodated. Therefore, it is possible to prevent that the muddy water is splashed upward.

Operation of the stand device explained above will then be explained.
Fig. 7 is a diagram for explaining operation of the stand device of the present invention. Even when muddy water splashes to the main stand 57 from the road surface as
indicted by the arrow mark  the protection plate 57d of the main stand 57 prevents that the muddy water is splashed to the rear upper side of the vehicle and yet splashes muddy water to the downward as indicted by the arrow marks (2), (3). Therefore, the side cover 17 and rear upper cover 19 (refer to Fig. 1) are not contaminated with muddy water.
[0021 ]
Moreover, even when a fellow passenger P sitting on
the rear part of the seat 23 (refer to Fig. 2) places her legs on the step 72, the legs F and clothes of the fellow passenger P are not contaminated by muddy water.
In addition, even if a stone, for example, hits the main stand 57 , the body or fellow passenger P can be protected from the stone by the protection plate 57d. [0022]
Furthermore, since the protection plate 57d is provided in almost the vehicle running direction, it prevents that muddy water D splashes upward. [0023]
Figs. 8(a), 8(b) are sectional views indicating the other embodiments of the stand device of the present invention. Fig. 8(a) shows the condition that the protection plate 57d is welded to the upper part of the tread bar 57c, the flat plate 75 is attached to the lower part of the protection plate 57d and these protection plate 57d and flat plate 75 reinforce the tread bar 57c.
With this structure, the protection plate 57d and flat plate 75 effectively prevent that the side cover 17 and fellow passenger P shown in Fig. 7 are contaminated by muddy water. Moreover, use of the flat plate 75 simplifies the manufacturing and reduces the manufacturing cost of the stand device.
[0024]
Fig. 8(b) shows the condition that a reinforcing member 76 having the V-shape cross-sectional view is welded
in front of the tread bar 57c for the reinforcing purpose.
The reinforcing member 76 includes the flat area 76a and the inclined area 76b and the flat area 76a corresponds to the protect.on plate 57d explained previously.
In such a structure, the reinforcing member 76 prevents that the side cover 17 and fellow passenger P are contaminated by muddy water as indicated in Fig. 7. Moreover, since the reinforcing member 76 is formed as a single member, it can be formed easily by the press machining and can also be fixed easily to the tread bar 57c with the less number of welding points.
