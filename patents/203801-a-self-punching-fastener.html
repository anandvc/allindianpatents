A SELF-PUNCHING FASTENER
The present invention relates to a self-punching fastener, which in a way safe against rotation and pressing out can be pressed into a metal sheet having an annular body, the side of which facing the metal sheet is having radially positioned projections which are pressed into the metal sheet into the surface thereof during the setting with an interior thread being positioned in the centre thereof and which on the side of the annular body facing the metal sheet in the set state is having a surrounding annular depression into which the metal sheet material displaced during the setting is pressed.
The closest prior art in this respect can be learnt from the US-B-6 220 804 which already is showing the above-mentioned features. Fasteners according to this prior art however can be set merely in relatively small ranges of thickness of the metal sheet, which means each an specially designed fastener is needed for metal sheets with very little difference in thickness. Further the safety against rotation and pressing out is still not optimal in connection with fasteners according to the prior art. Further, the fastener according to this reference is having a polygone-shaped body (in the embodiment each octagonal). The projections there in the vicinity of the so-called "punching collar" are prominent and are flatly ta-pering-off to the exterior.
Starting out from the above prior art, it is the task to be solved the present invention to further develop such a fastener in such a way that with the same geometry a maximal or hinge of gauges of the metal sheet can be used with a high safety against rotation and pressing out without a previous punching and a previous special preparation of the holes into which the fastener is to be sent being necessary.

Accordingly, the present invention provides a self-punching fastener, which in a way safe against rotation and pressing out can be pressed into a metal sheet having a body, which, on the side facing the metal sheet, is having radially positioned projections, which during the pressing-in are pressed into the metal sheet member and thereby are securing the fastener against rotation, wherein in the centre there is positioned an interior thread and, wherein on the side facing the metal sheet member in the set state there is provided an annular depression, wherein the projections are positioned in said depression equally distributed, characterized in that the projections are positioned at the interior rim of a collar limiting the depression and, in that said collar limiting the depression at the exterior rim thereof is having an undercut in the direction of the depression to be able to receive metal sheet material for locking against an axial pressing out, the body and the depression being annularly designed, wherein the annular depression on the side facing the metal sheet member is limited by a punching collar in the direction of the interior thread which is designed such that it is punching a hole with his diameter in the metal sheet member during the setting of the fastener, with the punching collar in the direction of the depression is having an undercut receiving metal sheet material occurring during the pressing-in to ensure a locking of the fastener against an axial pressing out. Preferably, the punching collar with respect to its height is adapted to the gauge of the respective metal sheet member to be punched. Preferably, the bottom face of the annular depression is inclined from the interior to the exterior in the direction of the metal sheet member.
According to the prior art, the known solutions were based on the assumption that an optimal safety against rotation and pressing out and a broad range of use of the fastener can be achieved if for the locking against rotation continuous ridges are provided within the annular depression and if the bottom of the annular depression is parallel to the surface of the metal sheets to be worked.^

A further advantage of the solution according to the invention is consisting in the fact that by the above described displacing of the security against rotation and the inclined formation of the bottom of the annular depression at the innermost limit of the annular depression - the punching collar - there is more space for the penetration of the cutting ring of the punching die available, which means it is possible to work with larger thicknesses of the metal sheets or larger gauge ranges with the solution according to the invention.
The coordination between the punching die geometry and the shape of the annular depression and the kind of security against rotation according to the invention is ensuring that after the pressing-in of the metal sheet material a jamming on the punching die is prevented and therefore a high working safety is secured.
Into the undercuts formed during the production of the parts as well at the inner punching collar as on the supporting collar of the annular depression during the pressing-in of the parts the material of the metal sheet is shaped into said undercuts ensuring that even with the working in thin metal sheets a high safety against rotation and pressing out of the fastener is achieved.
The shape of the projections or burls can be arched as well as with sharp edqes (trianqular).
