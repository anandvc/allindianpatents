Title of Invention

AN IMPROVED PIERCE SELECTOR SYSTEM AND A METHOD FOR OPERATING THE SAME
Abstract This invention relates to the pierce selector system used in press tools which consists of power cylinder assembly, cable, wedge, punch, and punch holder, key, rubber stripper, wherein the wedge is slidably placed inside the punch holder and connected to the power cylinder assembly with the help of cables and the said wedge has a taper surface on one side and the punch also has a corresponding taper surface at one end on its base to have a sliding movement with respect to wedge. Longitudinal motion of the punch beyond the required stroke and rotational movement is prevented by providing a key in the punch holder and a slot on the punch. A stripper is placed on the punch to strip the component and also to revert back the punch to its normal position. When power cylinder assembly is operated in reverse direction the cable connected to the cylinder is pulled while in turn pulls the one edge of the wedge, because of wedge movement the punch moves in longitudinal direction. When power cylinder assembly is operated in forward direction the cable pulls the other end of the wedge and creates space for accommodation of the punch and the punch is retracted back by rubber stripper.

PREAMBLE TO THE DESCRIPTION
The following Complete specification particularly describes the invention and the manner in which it is to be performed.

Field of invention:
This invention relates to pierce selector system used in Press Tools (Dies and Punches) and more particularly relates to pierce selector system by using power and mechanical linkages.
Background of invention:
Conventional pierce selector unit uses, separate power cylinders and related ancillaries for each punch for its operation, which makes the system as a whole more cumbersome and large in size which is shown in fig.lA and IB.
Fig.lA shows a conventional pierce selector unit when punch is in extended position and fig. IB shows a retracted (normal) position.
Referring fig.lA and IB.
The punch "A" moves with the help of wedge "B" which is operated by wedge "D" and in turn the said wedge "D" is powered by a separate power cylinder "C" and springs "E" pushes the punch "A" to its normal position when wedge "D" is pulled back by the power cylinder "C".
The following are the draw backs associated with the above said conventional pierce selector unit:
1. More space is required to mount the punches on die.
2. Requires steel stripper for stripping.
3. Punches can't be removed without disturbing selector unit assembly.
2

Brief description of invention:
A pierce selector system consists of power cylinder assembly, cable, wedge, punch, and punch holder, key, rubber stripper. Where the wedge is slidably placed inside the punch holder and connected to the power cylinder assembly with the help of cables and the said wedge has a taper surface on one side and the punch also has a corresponding taper surface at one end on its base to have a sliding movement with respect to wedge. Longitudinal motion of the punch beyond the required stroke and rotational movement is prevented by providing a key in the punch holder and a slot on the punch. A rubber stripper is placed on the punch to strip the component and also to revert back the punch to its normal position.
Reverse Movement:
When power cylinder assembly is operated in reverse direction the cable connected to the cylinder is pulled which in turn pulls the one edge of the wedge, because of wedge movement the punch moves in longitudinal direction.
Forward movement:
When power cylinder assembly is operated in forward direction the cable pulls the other end of the wedge and creates space for accommodation of the punch and the punch is retracted back by rubber stripper.

Objects of invention:
The main object of mis invention is to provide a compact pierce selector system.
Yet another object of this invention is to provide a pierce selector system having simple construction and cost effective.
Yet another object of this invention is to provide a pierce selector system working on independent of steel pads.
Yet another object of this invention is to provide a pierce selector system having facility of removal of punch without disturbing to punch selector system assembly.
Summary of Invention:
The present invention relates to a improved pierce selector system comprising of at least one punch holder having a provision to slidably hold a wedge in its position, said wedge have a taper surface on one side, and a punch having a corresponding taper surface at one end on its base to have a slidable motion with respect to said wedge is slidably held in said punch holder, wherein both the ends of said wedge are connected to power cylinder assembly through a cables, and a rubber stripper is provided on a said punch.
Improved pierce selector system in accordance with this invention wherein said punch holder is provided with a key and punch is provided with a slot in order to prevent undesired movement of the punch.
