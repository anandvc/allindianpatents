Title of Invention

"PROCESS FOR PURIFYING A LIQUID"
Abstract Process for purifying a liquid which liquid originates either from the dimerization of acrylonitrile in the presence of catalyst containing phosphorus, or from the hydrocyanation reaction of butadiene in the presence of catalyst containing phosphorus, by extracting one or more trivalent and/or pentavalent organophosphorus compounds from said liquid which process comprises contacting said liquid with alumina and/or titanium oxide, the alumina having a specific surface equal to or greater than 70 m2/g and a total pore volume equal to or greater than 0.3cm3/g to obtain the purified liquid.
Full Text The present invention relates to a process for purifying a liquid by extraction (disposal) of organophosphorus compounds contained in an industrial charge which is a gas or liquid.
It is known to use organophosphorus compounds as catalysts, in particular as catalyst Uganda. However, during the step in which these catalysts are . employed, it is often found that they beoome partially degraded, with the result that traces of these catalysts are present in the reaction products. This is the case, for example, during the dimerization reaction of acrylonitrile to form 1,4-butenedinitrile, as described in patent US-A-4,952,541 .
Depending on the way in which these products will later be used, it is often preferable to dispose of the catalydt residues which they contain. This is because these residues may, for example, poison and therefore deactivate the catalysts used in later reaction steps.
It may also be reoonunended to dispose cÂ£ these residues in such a way that they do not lead to the release of environmentally controlled compounds into the atmosphere or into residual water and/or solvents.
It is also known to use organophosphorous compounds as extracting agents during purificaticn .
operations involving liquid/liquid extractions the product to be purified, initially in aqueous solution with the impurities, is transferred into the extracting solvent based on these organophosphorus compounds, while the impurities remain in the aqueous phase. This gives rise to a purified product in the organic phase which is then back-extracted into the aqueous phaue. During this back-extraction, some of the organophosphorus-based organic phase can enter th(s aqueous phase. The product which is obtained, although purified, then contains undesirable organophosphorus traces.
One conventional way of purifying the products consists in distilling the medium containing the phosphorus residues, but an operation of this type is expensive in terms of investment and operation. Furthermore, it is not always possible and, lastly, decomposition of the products to be purified is sometimes found to occur.
In certain oases, an additional liquid/].iquid extraction may be carried out, but the solvent enriched with phosphorus compounds then needs to be treated before being discharged or re-used.
It has also been proposed to use ion-exchange resins, such as for example in patent GB-A-2,212,155. These resins have the drawback of being expensive, as well as of exhibiting acidity which may lead to undesired degradation.
in patent US-A-4,952,541, the phosphorus compounds are disposed of by bringing the reaction medium into contact with an oxidizing agent. However, depending on the nature of the medium to be treated, it may become degraded by the oxidizing agent.
One object of the present invention is therefore to provide a novel process for the disposal of organophoBphorus compounds contained in a liquid or gaseous medium.
Another object is to provide a process of this type which does not degrade a liquid or gaseous medium.
To this end, the invention provides a process for extracting one or more trivaient and/or pentavalent organophosphorous compounds which comprise at least one carbon atom from a gas or a liquid, in which the said gas or liquid is brought into contact with alumina and/or titanium oxide.
The process of the invention therefore consists in bringing the organophosphorus compounds into contact either with alumina or with titanium oxide, or with a mixed compound of alumina and titanium oxide. The reaction involved is an adsorption and/or uptake reaction.
Accordingly, the present invention relates to a process for purifying a liquid which liquid originates either from the dimerization of acrylonitrile in the presence of catalyst containing phosphorus, or from the hydrocyanation reaction of butadiene in the presence of catalyst containing phosphorus, by extracting one or more trivalent and/or pentavalent organophosphorus compounds from the liquid which process comprises contacting the liquid with alumina and/or titanium oxide, the alumina having a specific surface equal to or greater than 70 m2/g and a total pore volume equal to or greater than 0.3 cm3/g to obtain the purified liquid.

Formation by rotary technology involves aggregation of alumina carried out by contaot and rotation of alumina by itself. Exampl es of machines which may be used for this purpose include rotary granulators and rotating drum machines.
This type of process makes it possible to obtain beads having controlled dimensions and pore distributions, these dimensions and distributions being in general created during the aggregation step.
The porosity can be created in various ways, for example selection of the particle size distribution of the alumina powder or the aggregation of several alumina powders with different particle size distributions. Another method consists in mixing a so-called pore-forming compound with the alumina powder before or during the aggregation step, this compound disappearing completely as a result of heating ar.d thus creating porosity in the beads.
