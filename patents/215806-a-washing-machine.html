"A WASHING MACHINE"
Abstract The present invention provides a washing machine comprising an outer tub, a rotatable tub rotatably mounted in the outer tub; an agitator rotatably mounted in the rotatable tub; a brushless motor provided for direct driving at least one of the rotatable tub and the agitator and having windings of plurality of phases, characterized in that the brushless motor comprising a rotor; a rotor position detecting element detecting a rotational position of the rotor of the brushless motor, thereby delivering a position signal representative of the rotational position of the rotor, the rotor position detecting element being smaller in number than the winding phases of motor; an energizing signal forming means for forming a sinusoidal wave energizing signal on the basis of the position signal delivered from the rotor position detecting element, and driving means for energizing the windings of the plurality of phases on the basis of the energizing signals delivered from the energizing signal forming means. Full Text This invention relates generally to a washing machine including a rotatable tub for accommodating laundry with water, an agitator for agitating the water, and an electric motor for direct driving the rotatable tub and the agitator, and more particularly to drive control of the motor in such a washing machine.
In washing machines of the type described above, a rotatable tub serving both as a wash tub and as a dehydration basket is rotatably provided in an outer tub as well known in the art. An agitator is rotatably mounted on the bottom of the rotatable tub. The rotatable tub and the agitator are rotated by an electric motor. when a wash operation is executed, a rotational speed of the motor is decreased and the agitator is rotated in normal and reverse directions alternately repeatedly with the rotatable tub being stopped by a braking force. In execution of a dehydration operation, the rotational speed of the brushless motor is not decreased with the rotatable tub being released from the braked state and both the rotatable tub and the agitator are rotated at high speeds.
To drive the rotatable tub and the agitator as described above, a belt transmission mechanism, a clutch mechanism, a gear reduction mechanism including planetary gears, etc. are provided in a rotation transmission path from the motor to the rotatable tub and the agitator. However, these driving mechanisms increase the weight and the height of the washing machine. Furthermore, the gear reduction mechanism produces loud noise during operation.
The above-described problems could be solved by the employment of a direct drive mechanism in which the agitator and the rotatable
tub are direct driven by the motor. In the direct drive mechanism, a clutch would be provided for switching between an operating mode in which the agitator is driven directly by the motor repeatedly alternately in the normal and reverse directions with the rotatable tub being stopped and an operating mode in which both of the agitator and the rotatable tub are driven directly by the motor at high speeds . The direct drive mechanism could eliminate the belt transmission mechanism and the gear reduction mechanism and consequently, could reduce the weight and the height of the washing machine. Furthermore, the noise produced by gears could also be eliminated.
An electric motor used with the direct drive mechanism needs to produce high torque and its rotational speed needs to be variable in a broad range from a low speed to a high speed. A brushless DC motor meets these requirements. The brushless DC motor is driven by an inverter device. The brushless DC motor is generally provided with, for example, three Hall ICs for detecting a rotation position of a rotor. Based on position signals delivered from the Hall ICs, the inverter device generates three-phase rectangular wave voltages having phase difference of 12.0 degrees in electrical angle from one another, supplying the voltages to the brushless DC motor.
Torque varies with the switching of a winding phase to be energized or commutation when the rectangular wave voltages are supplied to the brushless DC motor to drive the same. The torque variations result in vibration of the motor. The vibration of the motor produces noise. The provision of the direct drive mechanism would reduce noise to a large extent particularly in the above-proposed washing machine. Accordingly, the noise due to torque variations in the brushless DC motor could not be ignored.
Furthermore, the vibration of the brushless DC motor would resonate the outer tub of the washing machine, increasing the noise.
Therefore, an object of the present invention is to provide a washing machine in which vibration and noise produced during the steady operation can be reduced and the number of detecting elements of the rotor position detecting means can be reduced.
Another object of the invention is to provide a washing machine in which occurrence of vibration due to nonunif ormity in the position signals delivered by the rotor position detecting means can be reduced.
Further another object of the invention is to provide a washing machine in which occurrence of vibration at a motor starting time can be reduced.
The present invention provides a washing machine comprising an outer tub, a rotatable tub rotatably mounted in the outer tub, an agitator rotatably mounted in the rotatable tub, a brushless motor provided for direct driving at least one of the rotatable tub and the agitator and having windings of a plurality of phases, the brushless motor including a rotor, a rotor position detecting element detecting a rotational position of the rotor of the brushless motor, thereby delivering a position signal representative of the rotational position of the rotor, the rotor position detecting element being smaller in number than the winding phases of the motor, energizing signal forming means for forming a sinusoidal wave energizing signal on the basis of the position signal delivered from rotor position detecting element, and driving means for energizing the windings of the plurality of phases on the basis of the energizing signals delivered from the energizing signal forming means.
According to the above-described washing machine, the brushless motor is controlled on the basis of the sinusoidal energizing signal formed on the basis of the position signal. This control manner reduces the torque variations in the brushless motor to a large extent, resulting in reductions in the vibration and noise due to the operation of the washing machine. Thus, the number of rotor position detecting element can be reduced as compared with the case when it is equal to the number of winding phases.

The energization of the brushless motor can be controlled in synchronization with the rotor being located at each position generally when a plurality of rotor position detecting elements are provided. A desired rotation control can be achieved particularly in a period of low speed rotation or in periods of acceleration and deceleration. However, a plurality of position signals used in the control sometimes becomes nonuniform when an accuracy in the mounting positions of the rotor position detecting elements are low or when motor fluxes are nonuniform. The nonuniformity in the position signals sometimes produces a cogging torque when the control is switched at a switching time of the position signals. The cogging torque appears more conspicuously in a high speed range.
However, the above-described washing machine is provided with the first energizing signal forming means forming the energizing signal on the basis of the position signals form a plurality of rotor position detecting means and the second energizing signal forming moans forming the energizing signal on the basis of the position signal from one of the rotor position detecting means. The selecting means selects either energizing signal forming means. Consequently, the motor can be controlled according to its rotating state, and the vibration can further be reduced.
