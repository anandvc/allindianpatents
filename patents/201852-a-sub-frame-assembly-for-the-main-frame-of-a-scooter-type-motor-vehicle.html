Title of Invention

A SUB FRAME ASSEMBLY FOR THE MAIN FRAME OF A SCOOTER TYPE MOTOR VEHICLE
Abstract A subframe assembly for a scooter type of motor vehicle, comprising a first strip member of substantially U-shaped configuration, the said strip member terminating in two fastener plates attachable to the rear portion of the main frame of the vehicle; first and second brackets respectively fixed to the two sides of the said strip member for mounting the pillion handle of the vehicle; a third bracket fixed to the outer side of the U-bend portion of the said strip member for mounting the top portion of the rear lamp assembly of the vehicle; a fourth bracket fixed to the inner side of the U-bend portion of the said strip member for mounting the fuel tank of the vehicle; an auxiliary strip member, surrounding the said U-bend portion and fixed to the main strip member, for mounting the rear fender assembly along with the bottom portion of the rear lamp assembly of the vehicle; a fifth bracket for mounting the fuel tank; sixth and seventh brackets respectively fixed to two sides of the auxiliary strip member for mounting the side panels of the vehicle.
Full Text

This invention relates to a subframe assembly for the main frame of a scooter type motor vehicle. Although the expression "scooter type motor vehicle" is used in this specification it is to be construed as including any other type of motor vehicle as well, to which this invention is applicable.
The scooter type of motor vehicle has a main frame on which various parts of the vehicle are mounted, such as, the pillion handle, rear lamp assembly and so on* Such parts are mounted on the main frame of the vehicle during assembly of the vehicle as a whole, rendering the assembly-line procedure tedious and cumbersome, inasmuch as such small parts will have to be individually mounted on the assembly line, consuming time which can otherwise be profitably utilised. It is therefore an object of this invention to reduce time and labour while assembling the vehicle as a whole by providing a subframe assembly as an integrated unit which is constructed independently of the main assembly of the vehicle. Thus, during assembly of the vehicle, the subframe

assembly is quickly and readily attachable, as an integral unit, to the main frame of the vehicle, on the assembly line. Whenever required, as for instance, during maintenance and repair jobs, such assembly is as quickly and readily detachable, as an integral unit, from the main frame. Thus, not only during manufacture, but also during post-manufacture jobs to be carried out on the vehicle, this invention is beneficial from several points of view.
The subframe assembly for a scooter type of motor vehicle, according to this invention, comprises a first strip member of substantially U-shaped configuration, the said strip member terminating in two fastener plates attachable to the rear portion of the main frame of the vehicle; first and second brackets respectively fixed to the two sides of the said strip member for mounting the pillion handle of the vehicle; a third bracket fixed to the outer side of the U-bend portion of the said strip member for mounting the top portion of the rear lamp assembly of the vehicle; a fourth bracket fixed to the inner side of the U-bend portion of the said strip member for mounting the fuel tank of

the vehicle; an auxiliary strip member, surrounding the

sixth and seventh brackets respectively fixed to two
sides of the auxiliary strip member for mounting the
side panels of the vehicle.
