Title of Invention

IMPROVEMENTS RELATING TO PHARMACEUTICAL COMPOSITIONS
Abstract A process for the production of a composition comprising a water-insoluble statin which comprises the steps of: a) providing a mixture comprising: i) a water-insoluble statin ii) a water soluble carrier, iii) a solvent for each of the statin and the carrier, and b) spray-drying the mixture to remove the or each solvent and obtain a substantially solvent-free nano-dispersion of the statin in the carrier.

'Emulsion' Preparation Method: 5
In one preferred method according to the invention the solvent for the water-insoluble statinis not miscible with water. On admixture with water it therefore can form an emulsion. 10
Preferably, the non-aqueous phase comprises from about 10 % to about 95 % v/v of the emulsion, more preferably from about 20 % to about 68 % v/v.
15 The emulsions are typically prepared under conditions which are well known to those skilled in the art, "for example, by using a magnetic stirring bar, a homogeniser, a sonicator or a rotational mechanical stirrer. The emulsions need not be particularly stable, provided that they do not undergo
20 extensive phase separation prior to drying.
Homogenisation using a high-shear mixing device is a particularly preferred way to make an emulsion in which the aqueous phase is the continuous phase. It is believed that 25 this avoidance of coarse emulsion and reduction of the
droplet size of the dispersed phase of the emulsion, results in an improved dispersion of the 'payload' material in the dry product.

There is a directional relation between the emulsion droplet size and the size of the particles of the 'payload' material, which can be detected after dispersion of the 10 materials of the invention in aqueous solution. We have

Sonication is also a particularly preferred way of reducing the droplet size for emulsion systems. We have found that a 25 Hert Systems Sonicator XL operated at level 10 for two minutes is suitable.
