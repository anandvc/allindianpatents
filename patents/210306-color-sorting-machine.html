Title of Invention

COLOR SORTING MACHINE.
Abstract There is disclosed a color sorting machine for sorting granular materials on the basis of the difference in color comprising a feeder (2) for supplying granular materials; a descent slope (4) through which said granular materials run down; a detector (7) which comprises a reflection plate (15) and a photosensitive apparatus (101) being operationally integrated with each other; a transparent plate (14) being covered to protect the inside, an air shower system to clean said transparent plate, and an ejector (8) equipped with a slot type air spray nozzle (19) to blow out granular materials which are sorted by said detector and the air spray nozzle.

[Field of the invention]
The present invention relates to a color sorting machine.
More particularly, the present invention relates to a color sorting machine comprising a feeder having a guiding groove formed, a descent slope with a round u shape channel and a heater attached at the rear, an air shower system to clean transparent plate of detector, an ejector having a slot typed air spray nozzle to blow out inferior goods.
Until recently, most of the color sorting machines have generally separated a specific granular material from other granular materials on the basis of its color. These have been used for color sorting of various granular materials such as cereals, plastic pellet, tablets, etc.
However, these kinds of conventional color sorting machines have some problems. That is, granular materials are not evenly inputted from the feeder into a descent slope because an operating condition is not always consistently maintained. Also, a dropping speed can be changed because descent slope condition is getting changed depending on the humidity among the air and the temperature. Furthermore, transparent detecting plate is readily polluted by rice bran, dust etc.

[Detailed description of the invention]
FIG. 1 is a cross-sectional view of a color sorting machine of the present invention. Referring to Fig. 1, a color sorting machine 10 0 comprises a hopper 1 for containing granular materials in the upper part and a feeder 2 under the hopper. The feeder 2, as shown in Fig. 3, has a structure in
-3 -
which the front end is inclined to allow granular materials to be easily run down evenly to a descent slope 4, and a guide groove 17 in V or U shape is formed (See upper drawing of Fig. 3). The guide groove 17 is coincided with each channel of a descent slope 4 to allow granular materials to be inputted evenly and precisely into a descent slope 4. A vibration element 3 is located beneath the feeder 2. Under the feeder 2, several channels are formed, and each channel comprises descent slope 4 having the bottom in U or V shape. Under the air conditions, such as high percent of humidity condition, descent slope 4 gets humidified and is easily polluted by dust, which affects on a descending speed and cause occurring product with poor quality. In order to solve this, the condition of descent slope 4 should be consistently maintained. For this purpose, a heater of which temperature can be consistently controlled, is attached to the rear of the descent slope 4 to make respective channel dry and prevent moisture or dust from being adhered. Since granular materials can show different flowing resistances depending on the shape of descent slope 4, a conventional rectangular shape 41 (see Fig. 5) or V shape 4 3 (see Fig. 5) is replaced with a round bottom U shape 43 as shown in Fig. 5 to keep a speed consistently.

The signal for inferior goods separated by photosensitive apparatus 101 is transmitted to ejector 8 and air is suddenly blown to send inferior goods to outlet for inferior goods. The dropping speed of granular materials is a high speed, i.e. several pieces per second. Since conventional sorting machines mainly employ a circular type air spray nozzle, air is sprayed over broad area to cause a lot of desired goods to be lost and the sorting accuracy to be decreased. As shown in Fig. 4, in order to solve aforesaid problems, the color sorting machine of the present invention employs a slot type nozzle rather than a circular one to increase the accuracy.
