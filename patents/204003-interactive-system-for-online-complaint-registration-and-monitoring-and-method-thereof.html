INTERACTIVE SYSTEM FOR ONLINE COMPLAINT REGISTRATION AND MONITORING AND METHOD THEREOF.
This invention relates to the system for online registration of public complaints. More particularly the invention relates for complaints of public by networked registration and monitoring of complaints of the citizens by the police, with no or minimal human interface.
The traditional method of getting the grievances of the people registered by the police department is very tedious, dilatory, non transparent and inconvenient to the people, whom the police claims to serve. Moreover, the process involves substantial human interface and discretion, which makes the process vulnerable to the curse of corruption and nepotism.
It is common knowledge that often a complainants) visiting a police station and complainants(s) is made to wait for hours and sometimes for even months or years to lodge the complaints and follow up. The complainant(s)/public is treated rather shabbily and even harassed for doling and monetary favour to the policemen. Further with motive to keep the crime statistics artificially low, the police station officers often resort to burking or minimization of offences and the complainant(s) are sent back without getting his/her complaint properly registered. Even if the complainant(s) manages to get the complaint registered, there is no viable monitoring mechanism for complainants to monitor and track the status or fate of his/her complaint. This, an ordinary citizen is caught in a vicious cesspool mired by the following problems:
(a) Non-registration or burking of complaints.
(b) Non-transparent and non-accountable traditional process of registration of complaints.
(c) Too much discretion in the hands of police station officers, which may lead to corruption or favoritisms.
(d) The traditional process does not provide for effective, prompt and comprehensive supervision by senior police officials.
(e) In the traditional process/method there is no convenient mechanism available to the citizens/public for effective monitoring or tracking of the action on his/her

All the aforesaid lacunae in the traditional process/method of manual registration of citizens complaints by the police are substantially over come by the present invention, which provides for online-interactive registration of complaints of the citizens/public by the police.

The operationationalisation of the inventions according to inventive interactive system can be done by either of the following methods.

Police Network (of Kiosk / Computers) based method (figure 2).
In this method a police network is established first, whereby terminal computers located at all police stations are interconnected via optical fibers cable or through V-SAT connectivity. This LAN / WAN of police offices / department is further extended by interconnecting with police network kiosks (front end terminals). The police network kiosks (front end terminal) are created at several locations, these network kiosks are accessible to public (Users) wherein the pubic can come and key in their complaints online. The interactive system generates the instant user ID and password at the time of key in of complaint. The user(s) can use system generated user ID and password for future monitoring purpose. The invented system automatically sorts the complaints jurisdiction wise and directs it in particular folder/account of to the terminal computers of the concerned police station connected via the police optical fiber or V-SAT network. The police officer (end user) designated to operate particular account of respective terminal computer of that police station. This channelising of complaints to the concerned police officer is given as the initial status of the complaint. The concerned police officer then checks his/her folder/account in the police network and acts on the online complaint(s). The police officer periodically posts the status of the complaints along with the details of final disposal of complaints to the central server of the police network.. The central server is located at central complaint cell. The central server can be accessed discretely by the complainant(s) through any of the police network kiosks using the password generated by the system, when the complaint was initially registered, (figure 2.).

A salient feature of the invention is that it provides the process/method which facilitates periodic checking of the status- quo of the complaint by the complainants. Accordingly , the invention acknowledge the receipts of the complaints and assign a unique password to the complainant. This interactive system according to present invention facilitates:
1. Reduction of costs (traveling and opportunity costs)
2. Greater transparency and accountability in police working.
3. Comprehensive supervision by the senior police officials vis-a-vis the disposal of public complaints by the cutting edge level police officers.
4. It is a bulkwark against red tapism and corruption in poice functioning.
5. Ensure citizens friendly policing.
6. It is a step in the direction of paperless police administration "where no paperwork is required."
A summary of the invention:
The invention pertains to a system and method based on the novel mechanism of getting the grievances or complaints of the ordinary citizens registered online by the police department. An aggrieved citizen can register his/her complaint from the convenience oh his/her home of office. The system also allows for online monitoring and tracking of the status-quo of the complaint by the complainant. It can also be used by the police department to secure valuable feedback, intelligence and secret information directly from the members of the public. The system ensures anonymity to the informer (based on the principle of sharing of information on 'need to know' basis). By using this system features such as transparency, accountability, promptness of response, fair registration of public complaints, proper monitoring of complaints etc. are injected into the functioning of the police. The system is operationalised by either establishing an optic fibre/VSAT based police network (LAN/WAN) of computers or by creating the enabling website on the Internet.
