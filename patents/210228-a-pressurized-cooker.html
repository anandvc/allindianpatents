Title of Invention

A PRESSURIZED COOKER
Abstract This invention provides a pressurized cooker comprising a vessel and a lid, characterized in that said lid is provided with an opening and means are provided for fitting a resilient plate made of a material such as rubber over the said opening, said means for fitting the said plate comprises a cap capable of being applied against the said plate towards the periphery of the latter on to the edges of said opening on the cooker, the base of the said cap is provided with an opening or is positioned spaced from the said plate with means provided for the space between the cap and the plate to communicate with the interior of the cooker when the cap is fitted internally or with the exterior when the cap is fitted externally.
Full Text

This invention relates to a pressure cooker or a pressure sterilizer. More particularly this invention relates to an additional safety device which would prevent an accidental explosion of the pressure cooker or pressure sterilizer.
The invention is aimed principally at and would be described with reference to pressure cookers, ^ But it can also be applied to pressure sterilizers, the vent weight or a spring biased safety valve for' letting out excess steam within the cooker can get stuck. The fusible alloy pellet safety valve can also fail in some cases particularly when it is a spurious one. Pressure cooker explosions do occur causing injury to the operator. It is against this background that in-built safety devices have been thought of and proposed by many. Thus Indian Patent No.149522 proposes a gasket release system (GRS) for the lid outside the body type of pressure cookers. The value of this GRS has been well recognised and the invention adopted by several manufacturers. But a not so well recognised defect of these lid outside the body type of pressure cookers with a GRS is that leaks are frequent at the safety slot on the lid during the routine working of the appliance (with the weight valve functioning adequately) especially with an aging gasket as the gasket is not supported adequately at the slot. But the safety feature is of supreme importance and this has avoided many a pressure cooker explosion in these
lid outside the body type of pressure cookers. The GRS is however not available for the lid inside the body type of pressure cookers.

A safety feature related to such a narrow ring as the gasket may not function 9. properly at the crucial moment. The allowable width of the gasket and consequently of the slot on the lid is a limiting factor for pressure release mechanisms related to the gasket and this severely limits its use particularly for the lid inside the body type of pressure cookers. As such the need is felt for an extra safety device which does not predispose to leaks during the routine functioning of the cooker but which becomes operative when the pressure reaches dangerous levels. Certain pressure cookers of the prior art do have a small hole on their lid fitted with a rubber disc cap having a biflanged edge which engages on to the edge of the said hole on the lid. The centre of this rubber disc cap is provided with a fusible alloy pellet. In case the alloy pellet does not melt the rubber disc cap would be ejected out of the lid when the pressure reaches dangerous levels. Besides the possibility of leakage at the edges of the rubber disc cap during routine working, the main defect of this existing safety valve is that the size of the hole on the lid of the cooker cannot be increased as the rubber disc cap would then be ejected out during the routine working of the cooker. Such a small rubber cap sealing a small hole may not be ejected out at the critical moment and the failed ejection mm mechanism may predispose to an explosion. The object of this invention is to propose a pressure release mechanism independent of the gasket, which does not predispose to leaks during the routine working of the cooker but becomes unfailingly operative when the pressure reaches dangerous levels, which can be put into practice economically

and which can be applied to both the lid outside and lid inside the body type of pressure cookers.

In the routine working of the pressure cooker the steam pushes the'rubber disc outwards, but the steam pressure would not be sufficient to rupture the stretched disc. There is thus no risk of leakage at this extra safety valve during the routine working of the cooker as the disc can be well pressed all around. But when the pressure reaches dangerous levels, the rubber disc ruptures to let out the steam and the steam escapes out through the openings M and P. A new rubber disc is inserted once the old one has ruptured and the cooker used again. In cases where the openings P or M are closed externally by a press fitting cap, this cap is ejected out by the excess pressure of steam. The size of the opening on the lid of the cooker and of the mouth M, and the sixe of the opening or of the raised portion on the base of the said cap C, and the area and thickness of the rubber disc K can be suitably adjusted so thst the disc can withstand the ordinary workiag pressures of the pressure cooker but would give way when the pressure reaches dangerous levels. The cap C also performs the aesthetic function of concealing Idwt the rubber disc from the operator. For most ordinary cookers the rubber disc is exposed for an area of at least 2 sq.cm.
