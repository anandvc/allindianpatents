Full Text FIELD OF THE INVENTION
The present invention relates to a cosmetic hair care composition and a process for preparing such composition.
BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
Till the present time, alopecia i.e. baldness is considered as a hair problem in human beings. In spite of much scientific research in the field of cosmetics, there has been no solution to the problem of baldness in human beings or animals. Baldness as such is not a disease which could cause pathological conditions in the mammalian body such as human body. In other words, baldness is not a pathological condition. However, baldness spoils the appearance of the face of a human being.
Causes of baldness :
There are several reasons for the occurrence of baldness on mammalian skin or human scalp. A few of the reasons being, (I) it is a hereditary problem inherited from the parents. It is presumed that there are a few genes responsible for the baldness and such genes are 'expressed predominantly in males. Though, these genes are dormant in females, nevertheless as carriers of such genes they can pass such genes to the male off springs without showing any occurrence of baldness on themselves; (ii) the extreme weather conditions such as desert area or presence of certain chemicals such as chlorine or calcium in the water used for washing hair; (iii) hypertension; (iv) reaction to drug such as chemotherapy; (v) hormonal disturbances; (vi) menopause and its accompanying disturbance; and (vii) glandular disturbances and abnormal thyroid functioning.
Normally, the part of the scalp where hair grows is known as hair follicle. Due to several reasons, the size of the hair follicle shrinks to the extent that it does not allow the hair to grow. When this process of follicle shrinkage persists for a long time then the problem of baldness begins . Beneath each hair follicle, there are certain types of cells where hair begins life. These cells are directly involved in manufacturing and controlling the growth of hair shaft. When these cells become inoperative/inactive/dead, then the peison faces the problem of baldness.
The appearance, growth and fall of hair is a continuous process. Usually, the life of hair is between 3 to 4 years. A person loses between 50 to 150 hairs per day and new shoots come out of these follicles. Due to such continuous process, old hairs fall down after a stipulated period, these hairs are replaced by new hairs . As stated earlier, once the size of the hair follicle shrinks to the extent as explained above and cells lying undetneath become inoperative .and old locks of hair which have already fallen off are not replaced by new growth, then the problem of baldness begins.
Scientists all over the world are busy finding a solution to solve the problem of baldness in human being but till date ao one has achieved a clear break through in this field. This view is also supported by British Science News No.249, Page 19 "Spectrum" Nov-Dec.1995
PRIOR ART DISCUSSION
Some years ago, scientists in the USA developed an anti-hypertension medicine comprising 2% minoxidal. While administrating this drug to the patients of hypertension, the doctors surprisingly noticed that such administration triggered
hair growth in the body of the patients. Many patients noticed the growth of cosmetically accepted hair on their bodies. Unfortunately, the treatment of minoxidal was accompanied by several side effects. Further, the application of minoxidal on the scalp also triggered growth of facial hair. In addition, the process of growth of hair on the bald scalp was co-terminus with the process of application of minoxidal. . In other words, once the application of minoxidal is slopped, the hair also stops growing or falling off. Further, the growth of hair on the bald scalp was not satisfactory and complete. Therefore, the above application could not mitigate the problem of baldness.
There are several hair treating compositions or. oils proposed by different companies or groups but none has given authenticated proof regarding the efficacy of the products.
There are many methods by which the bald scalp can be covered with either artificial or natural hair and a few of such methods are listed herebelow: a. Hair transplant: This treatment involves obtaining tuffs of hair from different parts of the body and transplanting them on the bald scalp. The tuffs of hairs thus transplanted on the bald scalp start growing and thereby cover the bald scalp to a certain extent. However, this treatment is not a solution to the problem of baldness because it is very costly and creates ugly lumps or accumulation of flesh on and around the hair tuffs oil the scalp. Moreover, this treatment is a painful one and only a.limited number of hairs can be transplanted on the bald scalp. In addition, after the transplant, the scalp requires to be properly conditioned or protected from undue exertion or weather conditions.
b. Hair weaving: It is simply a means of concealing baldness. It is also costly
and does not give permanent solution to baldness because it has no connection or
relationship with the roots or its cells which are the foundation of hairs.
c. Wig.: It is a device made of artificial or natural hair having one or more
fixing means whereby the wig can be superficially fixed on the bald scalp. This
does not provide a permanent solution and at the same time it is most inconvenient
for the wearer.
d. Injection of some hormones: This treatment is painful and does not
provide pennanent cure for baldness. In addition, the hormones injected create
many unwarranted side-effects.
e. Providing cuts on the scalp;
f. Local application of razor several times on the scalp to induce growth of
new hair;
g. Local application of kerosene oil;
h. Local application of powdered elephant tusk (ivory);
i. Local application ofc fish oil;
j. Local application of coconut oil;
k. Local application of onion extract;
I. Local application of tincher/sulphur on the scalp;
The various factors of the body relating to cure of baldness depend on :
The number of years a person has been bald or has been suffered from loss of hair.
The extent of baldness in the scalp of the person
The age and general health conditions of the person..
SUMMARY OF THE INVENTION
As a result of an intensive research based herbal medicine, the applicant ESCUBE LABORATORIES Dehra Dun - 248 001, India has now come out with a novel composition for hair care on mammalian skin preferably, on human skin, wherever the hair follicles are present.
The present invention also relates to a process for the preparation of a novel composition for application on hair present on mammalian skin such as a human being.
Accordingly, the present invention provides a novel cosmetic herbal hair care composition, said composition comprising Emblica offinicalis 9 to 35 wt%, Terminal Chebala 3-15 wt.%, Sapindus mukorossi 8-30 wt%, Acacia concinna 15-45 wt%, Nardostachye sp. 5-20 wt%, Ecupta Alba 10-35 wt%, and optionally 2-25% of conventional additives such as preservatives and/or flovouring agents and/or rose water.
The novel composition of the present invention is a synergistic composition exhibiting surprising properties. In other words, the unique property of the composition has not been envisaged in any of the individual ingredients of the composition. The composition is a herbal cosmetic preparation. The inference of applying the novel composition establishes that it has the unique distinction of being an authentic and effective solution for maintaining and caring hair. This composition helps in setting (hair setting) the hair. Further, the dark colour of the composition, upon application on hair, provides a sort of block dying effect to the hair. This composition is also provides shine to the hair. This composition is not an anti bacterial agent.

Steps of preparation:
The novel composition can be prepared by mixing the ingredients identified above with water in any known manner. Preferably, the herbs identified above can be reduced into power and the power so obtained mixed with soft or distilled water and the essence of the herbs is obtained by filtration. Any part of the plants can be used for preparing the herbal extracts.
Most preferred manner of producing the composition :
Preferably, there can be "ghan satva" of each of the six ingredients, preferably, 1 gm each of the ingredients mixed with 1§ times of soft water and left in sunlight under glass or in shadow for 3 days. Theft it is boiled till the soft water remains upto 4 to 2 times more in the mixture. It is filtered and mixed with original power
of ingredient and such mixture is kept fox some time so that power will absorb the excess water. This process is repeated several times. At last, the original power of ingredients does not absorb it even after it is left for some time. Then it is called "ghan satva".
Later, the "ghan satva" of each ingredient mixed together and also with 9 times soft water with total amount of all ingredients. It is left in this form for 4 days. Thereafter, it is boiled 2 hours per day for 2 days. After that it is filtered and boiled till it achieves a great density that water. Subsequently, it is allowed to be cooled down. Finally, optional ingredients are mixed and the novel composition is ready for use.
