COMMUNICATIONS METHOD AND APPARATUS RELATED TO USER SCHEDULING OF TRAFFIC DATA TRANSMISSION IN A PEER TO PEER COMMUNICATIONS SYSTEM
Abstract Methods and apparatus related to peer to peer communications are described. A first peer to peer wireless device, having transmitted a request to a second peer to peer device to transmit traffic data using a corresponding air link resource, monitors during a monitoring period for a response from the second device and for responses from other devices. The first device makes a decision whether or not to transmit as a fimction of the received power of the received response signals. At times, the first device, although authorized to transmit by the second device, decides not to transmit and yields the traffic air link resource. The second device monitors to receive transmission requests, determines a power level of a request from the first device to transmit to the second device and a request to transmit to a third device, and determines whether or not to authorize the first device to transmit.

Various embodiments are directed to methods and apparatus for wireless communication and, more particularly, to methods and apparatus for use in peer to peer wireless communication.

BACKGROUND

[0002]
In a cellular communications network, in which wireless terminals communicate via a base station attachment point, typically a centralized control node or base station node schedules competing users, e.g., wireless terminals, to use available traffic air link resources and/or manages interference in the system. However, in a peer to peer communications system, in which a centralized node and/or base station is no longer in control, the problems of efficient management of traffic air link resources and interference control become more complex. Accordingly, there is a need for methods and/or apparatus for providing management of traffic air link resources and/or interference control to support efficient use of air link resources in such systems.

[0003]
In a peer to peer communications system multiple pairs of connected peer to peer communications devices may desire to use the same air link resource for traffic signals. For some situations, an intended receiver of traffic signals in a connected pair of peer to peer to communications device is in a better situation to decide if transmission should be authorized. For some other situations, an intended transmitter of traffic signals in a coimected pair of peer to peer communications devices is in a better situation to decide if transmission should be authorized. Methods and apparatus that provide an opportunity for an intended transmission device to veto a traffic transmission request would be beneficial. Similarly, methods and apparatus that provide an opportunity for an intended receiving device to veto a traffic transmission request would be beneficial. Methods and apparatus that take into consideration potential concurrent peer to peer signaling firom other coimected pairs, which would like to use the same air link traffic resource concurrently, when determining whether to proceed with or veto a traffic transmission request corresponding to first connected pair would be beneficial.
