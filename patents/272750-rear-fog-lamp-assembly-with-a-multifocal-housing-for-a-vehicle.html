Field of Invention:
The present invention relates to a rear lamp assembly, particularly, but not exclusively for use on a motor vehicle.
Background:
The rear fog lamp is a high wavelength illumination lamp that penetrates thick fog
or mist and assists vehicle's identification in foggy conditions.
Conventionally, there is a lamp assembly comprising a hollow body including a coloured lens, a reflex reflector within the hollow body attached with the lens for reflecting the incoming light to the bulb, a bulb holder for mounting the bulb on the body, and a fluorescent bulb fixed on the bulb holder for illuminating light.
In the above mentioned design the intensity and effect of the colour during fog is not effective and doesn't give a clear view of the illuminating light.
Thus, to overcome the aforementioned deficiencies it is desirable to improvise on the lamp design to improve the illumination of the light emitted by the rear fog lamp.
Objects of the Invention:
An object of the present invention relates to intensifying the illumination of the rear fog lamp.
Another object of the present invention is to increase the vision of the rear fog lamp.

Description:
To overcome the above deficiencies present in the conventional rear fog lamp assembly, the bulb used in the lamp assembly is coloured red instead of the red coloured lens which is turned transparent. This helps in improving the intensity of illumination and visibility of the red colour emitted by the rear fog lamp.
The present invention relates to intensifying the illumination and visibility of the rear fog lamp. The use of coloured bulb instead of the florescent one and transparent lens instead of coloured one helps in improving the intensity and visibility of the rear fog lamp.
Fig. 1 exemplarily illustrates an isometric view of the lamp assembly without the lens in accordance with one embodiment of the present invention. A housing 600 covering a red coloured bulb 100 includes a bulb holder 700 attached to housing 600. The coloured bulb 100 fixed on the bulb holder 700. The housing is attached to the vehicle body by three fixing holes 500 integrated on the housing.
Fig. 2 exemplarily illustrates a bottom view of the lamp assembly in accordance with one embodiment of the present invention. The housing 6 includes a transparent lens 200 and reflex reflector 400 placed on the lower side of the lens to reflect the incoming light. Also there is a cable with sleeve to connect two terminals 800 and 900.
