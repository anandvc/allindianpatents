BACKGROUND AND PRIOR ART REFRENCES OF THE INVENTION The mammalian egg is surrounded by an acellular translucent envelope, the zona pellucida (ZP), comprised of acidic glycoproteins, which are synthesized and secreted during oocyte growth and follicular development (Wassarman. vol 57, .Ann Rev Biochem 415-442, 1988). ZP glycoproteins mediate critical steps in fertilization process including initial attachment followed by tight binding of spermatozoa to the ZP, induction of the acrosome reaction, and affecting a block to polyspermy. This critical role in reproduction together with their tissue specific nature have made the ZP glycoproteins potential candidate antigens for immunocontiaceptive vaccine.
To test the efficacy of ZP glycoproteins for immunocontraception. female subjects from several species were immunized with pig zona pellucida glycoproteins isolated from ovaries. Such an immunization leads to block of fertility (Skinner et
al., vol 115, Endocrinol 2418-2432, 1984, Mahi-Brown et al., vol 32. Biol Reprod 761-772, 1985; Dunbar et al, vol 52. Fertil Steril 311-318, 1989. Paterson et al. vol 46, Biol Reprod 523-534, 1992). However, it is invariably associated with either a transient or irreversible alteration in the cyclicity, hormonal profile and follicular development in the ovary Impairment of ovarian function is considerably reduced, if not completely eliminated, by the use of purified ZP glycoproteins along with permissible adjuvants such as alum, sodium phthalyl derivative of lipopolysaccharide and muramyl dipeptide instead of complete Freund's adjuvant (Sacco et al.. vol 21, Am J Rep Immunol 1-8, 1989; Bagavant et al.. vol 102, J Reprod Fertil 17-25, 1994). Active immunization studies with glycosylated and deglycosylated porcine ZP antigens, in rabbit model system have atrributed ovarian pathology to glycosylation (Keenan et al., vol 44, Biol Reprod 150-156, 1991; Jones et al, vol 95, J Reprod Fertil 513-525, 1992). Molecular characterization of ZP glycoprotems from various species led to the identification of three major proteins termed as ZP1 (ZPB), ZP2 (ZPA) and ZP3 (ZPC) (Harris et al.. vol 4, DNA Sequence 361-393, 1994; Castle and Dean, vol 50. J Reprod Fertil Supp 1-8. 1996). On the basis of the size of mRNA's ZP2 is the longest and ZP3 smallest [Harris et al., vol 4, DNA Sequence 361-393. 1994]. ZP1 has been cloned and sequenced from mouse (Epifano et al., vol 270, J Biol Chem 27254-27258. 1995), human (Hams et al.. vol 4. DNA Sequence 361-393. 1994), rabbit (Schwoebal et al.. Vol 266, J Biol Chem. 7214-7219. 1991). pig
(Yurewicz et al., Vol 1174, Biochimica et Biophysica Acta 211-214, 1993) and cat (Harris et al., vol 4, DNA Sequence 361-393, 1994). In mouse, three zona transcripts have been detected uniquely in the ovary, where dieir expression is restricted to oocytes [Ringuette et al., vol 83, Proc Natl Acad Sci USA 4341-4345. 1986; Liang et al.. vol 10, Mol Cell Biol 1507-1515, 1990; Epifano et al.. vol 121. Development 1947-1956, 1995]. However, in cynomolgus monkey ZP2 and ZP3 transcript is present in oocytes at all stages of folliculogenesis including granulosa cells [Martinez et al.. vol 50, J Reprod Fertil Supp 35-41. 1996]. However. ZPI transcript is present in oocytes in secondary follicles and to lesser extend in tertiary follicles but absent in primordial, primary or antral follicles and granulosa cells [Martinez et al., vol 50, J Reprod Fertil Supp 35-41, 1996]. To avoid or minimize ovarian dysfunction for a ZP glycoprotein based immunocontraceptive vaccine for human application, it will be desirable that the candidate antigen is not present on the primordial/primary follicles and granulosa cells. To design a vaccine for human use, it will be desirable to either use human zona proteins or those having a high sequence homology with human zona proteins so as to elicit an immune response capable of inhibiting more efficiently the human sperm-egg interaction. So far full length ZPI gene has not been cloned from any . non-human primate. The present invention for the first time describes the full length ZPI cDNA clone isolation from bonnet monkey (Macaca radiaia) and its complete nucleotide and deduced ammo acid sequence. Zona pellucida (ZP), an extracellular matrix, surrounds the mammalian oocyte /P
serves as a site for the species-specific recognition and binding of the spermatozoa to the oocyte, induces acrosome reaction in the ZP bound spermatozoa, atfects avoidance of polyspermy and protects the pre-implanted blastocyst The ZP is composed of three biochemically distinct glycoproteins, which have been classified as ZP1, ZP2 and ZP3 The cloning and characterization of bonnet monkey (Mac at a radiata) ZP1 (bmZPl) provided exciting and novel approaches for using bmZPl as an antigen to control fertility in humans and other mammals A vaccine tor fertility inhibition can be produced by combining either recombinant or synthetic bmZPl or a portion theieof with a suitable carrier protein for administration to a subject foi immunization The carrier protein help m providing 1 -cell help which in turn help in generating immune response against the ZP protein/peptide The carrier can bt selected from diphtheria toxoid or rabies glycoprotem-Cj Alternatively peptides corresponding to promiscuous T-cell non B-cell epitopes from malaria parasite tetanus toxoid etc can also be used as earner The bmZPl- carrier protein conjugate are used with or without adjuvant(s) to immunize female subjects (bonnet monkey oi other mammals) The antibody response thus generated is monitored by assay thai uses bmZPl as antigen Antibodies thus produced against bmZPl will bind to the oocyte and thus disrupt fertilization Alternatively, antibodies against bmZPl can lead to degenerative changes in ZP, inhibition in the growth of the tollicles and thus affecting ovulation These effects of immunization will lead to inhibition of fertility in the immunized animals It may be noted that the recombinant or synthetic bmZPl oi a
portion thereof with a suitable carrier protein have a synergistic relationship in that the immunity to the animal is achieved only by a combination of these ingredients and not otherwise. Use of single ingredient gives no effect to the animal
