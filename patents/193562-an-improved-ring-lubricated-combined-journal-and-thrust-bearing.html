Title of Invention

"AN IMPROVED RING LUBRICATED COMBINED JOURNAL AND THRUST BEARING"
Abstract An improved ring lubricated combined journal and thrust bearing for use in primary air fans in boiler auxiliary equipment of thermal power plant comprising a combined journal and thrust bearing having a plurality of thrust pad sections (2) supported by a correspondingly numbered runners (9) mounted on a rotating shaft (3); and an oil ring (1) mounted on a rotating journal of said combined journal and thrust bearing, characterized in that said oil ring (1) is fabricated with a trapejoidal cross section having a plurality of grooves on an inner diameter made of a bronze material, and in that each of said thrust pad section (2) is given a taper in a rotational direction of said bearing.
Full Text This invention relates to improvements in ring lubricated combined journal and thrust bearings used in primary air fans in boiler auxiliary equipment of different rating at thermal power stations.
U. S. Patent No. 3544179 describes a ring lubricated combined journal and thrust bearings. However/ such bearings are self - contained/ oil ring lubricated, water cooled and spherical seated and can take both radial and thrust loads. The radial load is due to self weight of the fan rotor and axial load is due to reaction forces on the fan blades. The magnitude of these loads are of the order of 2 tonnes both radial and thrust bearing in a typical 7" dia PA Fan used in 210 MW thermal units.
Ring lubricated bearings are most economical and reliable compared to pressure-fed bearings, as there is no seprate oil circulation system. In ring lubricated bearing, the oil ring mounted on the journal rotates when the rotor starts rotating and picks up oil from the sump beneath it and feeds to the bearing. Oil delivered by the oil ring depends upon ring material/ geometry of the ring, oil viscosity and depth of immersion.
Disadvantage associated with the oil ring used in U.S I Patent No. 3544179 is that it is restrictive from the point of view of ring geometry/ weight and material of the
ring. Therefore/ this oil ring needs improvement
to deliver more quantity of oil to the bearing, so that the
excessive bearing temperatures can be reduced.
Another disadvantage is that the thrust bearing used in combined journal and thrust bearing of U.S. Patent No. 3544179 is a flat plate design and in this thrust bearing, there is no geometrical wedge and hence it is only suitable for smaller thrust loads. If the loads are excess, the flat plate design may fail due to rubbing of thrust plate with thrust collar. Therefore, the flat plate design of thrust bearings needs improvement for reliable operation of the bearing .
An object of the present invention is to propose an improvement in ring lubricated combined journal and thrust bearings used in primary air fans in boiler auxiliary equipments and capable of taking additional thrust load .
Yet another object of the present invention is to propose an improvement in the oil ring exhibiting a better performance.
Still another object of the present invention is to propose an improvement in oil ring having a greater oil delivery for the bea'ring and reduction in operating temperature.
Further object of the present invention is to propose an improvement in the oil ring wherein the weight of the modified ring is a greater than the present design
thereby giving better life.
Still further object of the present invention is to propose an improvement in the oil ring having improved hydro-dyanmic pressure distribution properties in the thrust bearing clearance.
According to this invention there is provided a ring lubricated combined journal and thrust bearings used in primary air fans in boiler auxiliary equipments having an oil ring with trapezoidal cross section and multiple grooves.
