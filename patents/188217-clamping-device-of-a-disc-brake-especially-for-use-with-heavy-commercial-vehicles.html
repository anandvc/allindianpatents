Title of Invention

CLAMPING DEVICE OF A DISC BRAKE ESPECIALLY FOR USE WITH HEAVY COMMERCIAL VEHICLES
Abstract . A clamping devices of a disc brake, especially for use with heavy conmercial vehicles, comprising - at least one plunger (30) which is displaceable in the direction of a plunger axis (C) for applying a brake pad to a brake disc, - at least one eccentric (24) which is rotatable about a transverse axis (B) - a roll body (28) carried on the eccentirc(24) for trans¬mitting actuating forces to the plunger (30), - at least one adjusting member (70) which is rotatable about an adjusting axis (D) extending at an angle to the transverse axis (B) to compensate for lining wear, and - a pin (76) fixed to the eccentric (24) and cooperating with the adjusting member (70) to transmit the torque for effecting anadjustment to compensate for lining wear, characterized in that the pin (76) forms part of {76, 98) by which the roll body (28) is prevented frcm inadmissibly moving in the direction of the transverse axis (B) with respect to the eccetntric.(24).

The invention relates to a clamping device of a disc brake, especially for use with heavy commercial vehicles, comprising

A clmaping device having an angular drive of the kind mentioned above is known wherein two radially projecting pins are fixed to the rotary body and a drive lever is arranged on the adjusting member, the outer contour of which is similar to a toothing having three teeth and two gaps. Each of the gaps is engaged by one of the two pins, respectively, fixed to the rotary member. Thereby the adjusting member is connected to the rotary member by a kind of mangle gear which has been known since ancient times for angular drives and which, compared to modern toothings, has the drawback that there is only point contact between pins and teeth resulting in high surface pressures that cause wear. Similar statements apply in respect of the clamping device known which also belongs to the afore described kind comprising an angular drive. There, a pin extending radially beyond a rotary member is carried on the latter such that it is pivot able about an axis crossing the axis of the rotary member. Again, a lever is fixed to the adjusting member that is to rotate upon rotations to the rotary member, said lever having a recess into which the pin extends. It cannot be avoided here either that the pin contacts the edge of the associated recess only pointwise at least when the rotary member and the adjusting member occupy certain positions.
In another clamping device having been known and belonging as well to the afore-described kind comprising an angular drive, the pin fixed to the rotary member has a spherical head which is embraced in a fork-lime manner by a lever mounted to the adjusting member. In the most favourable case it is possible here, however only in the area of a center position of the pin with respect to the lever, to achieve a line contact between pin and lever.
More favourable contacting is achieved with a known clamping device in which also a rotary member rotatable about a first axis for actuating a brake is connected to an adjusting member rotatable about a second axis to compensate for lining wear, said two axes intersecting at a right angle. The said two members directly engage each other via beveL gear toothings

The two bevel gear toothings contact each other at least along one contact line in every operative position. Said comparatively favourable contacting demands however the high price of relatively high manufacturing costs.
According to the second aspect as well the invention has there¬fore as object to design a clamping device comprising an adjusting member for compensating lining wear in a disc brake in such a way that it can be manufactured with a minimum of expenditure.
Starting from a clamping device of the kind mentioned above the object according to the second aspect of the invention is achieved in that a sliding block is rotatably supported on the pin which is fixed to one of the said two members, said sliding block having a profile which is complementary to the sectional shape of the recess in the other one of the two members and being slidingly guided along said recess.
According to a third aspect the invention relates to a clamping device of a disc brake, especially for use with heavy commercial vehicles, comprising
at least one plunger which is displaceable in the'direction of a plunger axis for applying a brake pad to a brake disc,
a rotary member which is rotatable about a transverse axis and includes at least one eccentric for displacing the plunger,
at least one adjusting member adapted to be rotated by the rotary member about an adjusting axis.

- an adjusting drive which is adapted to be driven by the adjusting member to compensate for lining wear at the brake pads,
a sliding clutch arranged between the adjusting member and the adjusting drive, and
a pressure member for transmitting an axial force determining the torque which can be transmitted by the sliding clutch.
In practice, over-adjustments have sometimes been observed with clamping devices of the above-mentioned kind. Therefore, the third aspect of the invention has as object to design a clamping device with adjusting members to compensate for linging wear in a disc brake at low structural expenditure, in such a way that over-adjustments are prevented.
Starting from a clamping device of the above-described kind the object of the invention according to the third aspect is achieved in that the adjusting member and the pressure member engage telescopically without contacting each other and are supported independently of each other.
