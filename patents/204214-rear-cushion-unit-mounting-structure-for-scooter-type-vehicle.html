Title of Invention

REAR CUSHION UNIT MOUNTING STRUCTURE FOR SCOOTER-TYPE VEHICLE
Abstract 1. A rear cushion unit mounting structure for a scooter-type vehicle in which a rear wheel (WR) is rotatably supported on a power unit (P) which is swingably supported by a main frame (21) and a lower end portion of a rear cushion unit (33) is connected to said power unit (P), i and a cushion side bracket (86) provided on the upper end of said rear cushion unit (33) is mounted on a cushion mounting portion (88) provided on a frame main portion (24a) as a main rear portion of said main frame (21), characterized in that: an opening portion (87) allowing said cushion side bracket (86) to pass therethrough is provided in said frame main portion (24a), and said cushion mounting portion (88) is provided on said frame main portion (24a) in such a manner as to be raised upwardly from said frame main portion (24a) while crossing said opening portion (87).

[Detailed Description of the Invention] [0001]
[Technical Field to Which the Invention Pertains]
The present invention relates to an improvement in a structure for mounting a rear cushion unit to a main frame in a scooter-type vehicle in which a rear wheel is rotatably supported on a power unit which is swingably supported by the main frame and a lower end portion of the rear cushion unit is connected to the power unit, and a cushion side bracket provided on the upper end of the rear cushion unit is mounted on a cushion mounting portion provided on a frame main portion as a main rear portion of the main frame. [0002]
[Prior Art]
The body frame of this type has been conventionally disclosed, for example, in Japanese Patent Laid-open No. Hei 10-218065. [0003]
[Problem to be Solved by the Invention]
The above-described prior art vehicle is configured


such that a cushion mounting portion is provided on a frame main portion in such a manner as to project downwardly from the frame main portion. Accordingly, the length of the rear cushion unit in the vertical direction is relatively short, so that the stroke of the rear cushion unit becomes relatively small. To improve the driver's riding comfort, it is expected to make large the stroke of the rear cushion unit. [0004]
In view of the foregoing, the present invention has
been made, and an object of the present invention is to
provide a rear cushion unit mounting structure for a
scooter-type vehicle, which is capable of making large
the stroke of a rear cushion unit, thereby improving the
driver's riding comfort.
[0005]
[Means for Solving the Problem]
To achieve the above object,
there is provided a rear cushion unit mounting structure for a scooter-type vehicle in which a rear wheel is rotatably supported on a power unit which is swingably supported by a main frame and a lower end portion of a rear cushion unit is connected to the power unit, and a cushion side bracket provided on the upper end of the rear cushion unit is


mounted on a cushion mounting portion provided on a frame main portion as a main rear portion of the main frame, characterized in that an opening portion allowing the cushion side bracket to pass therethrough is provided in the frame main portion, and the cushion mounting portion is provided on the frame main portion in such a manner as to be raised upwardly from the frame main portion while crossing the opening portion. [0006]
With this configuration, the cushion side bracket provided at the upper end of the rear cushion unit can be located at a higher position. Accordingly, it is possible to make large the stroke of the rear cushion unit and hence to improve the driver's riding comfort. [0007]
According to an invention in
addition to the configuration of the invention
., a sub-frame constituting a body frame in coÂ¬operation with the main frame is fastened to the frame main portion at a position near a connection portion between the cushion mounting portion and the frame main portion. With this configuration, the strength of the cushion mounting portion raised upwardly from the frame main portion can be reinforced by the sub-frame. [0008]

The function of this embodiment will be described below. The body frame F of the scooter-type vehicle is formed by fastening the front frame 23, rear frame 24, and sub-frame 22 to each other. At least one of the frames 23, 24 and 22 is formed by casting (the front frame 23 and rear frame 24 are formed by casting in this embodiment).
