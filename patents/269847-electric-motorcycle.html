Title of Invention

ELECTRIC MOTORCYCLE
Abstract To easily secure weight balance of an electric motorcycle, including a battery, a power drive unit, and a driving power generating motor, in the left-and-right direction or in the frontward-and-rearward direction. [Solution] An electric motorcycle 1 includes a power drive unit 18 and a driving power generating motor 16 which are mounted to a swing arm 13 swinging about a pivot shaft in an upward-and-downward direction. A rear wheel WR is driven by the power generating motor 16. The electric motorcycle includes a battery 9 to supply electric power to the power generating motor 16. A center of gravity (center-of-gravity position BC) of the battery 9 is located at the side opposite to the power drive unit 18 and the power generating motor 16 about a central line CX of a motorcycle body.

[Techinical Field]
[0001]
The present invention relates to an electric motorcycle that is driven by an electric motor (a power generating motor), and, more particularly, to a component arrangement structure of the electric motorcycle wherein the electric motor is supported by a swing arm.
[Bac] [0002]
Generally, an electric motorcycle includes a battery, a power generating motor to generate a drive force for the electric motorcycle based on electric power supplied from the battery, and a power drive unit to control the supply of electric power from the battery to the power generating motor. A drive power current is input from the battery to the power generating motor via the power drive unit to drive the power generating motor, with the result that a rear wheel is driven. Consequently, the electric motorcycle is driven (for example, see Patent document 1).

[Patent Document 1] JP Patent No. 3317 560 [Patent Document 2] JP-A No. H7-112617 [Disclosure of the Invention] [Problem to be Solved by the Invention] [0004]

footrest space in consideration of balance of the electric motorcycle body in order to achieve a low center of gravity, for example, in Patent document 2. Further, it is necessary to sufficiently consider arrangement of these components of the electric motorcycle so as to secure weight balance of the electric motorcycle in the left-and-right direction or in the frontward-and-rearward direction.
Therefore, it is an object of the present invention to easily secure weight balance of an electric motorcycle, including a battery, a power drive unit, and a driving power generating motor, in the left-and-right direction or in the frontward-and-rearward direction of the motorcycle. [Meaning for Solving the Problem] [0005]

Next, weight balance in the left-and-right direction will be described.
In this embodiment, the PDU 18 and the power generating motor 16 are mounted to the swing arm 13, which swings about the pivot shaft 12 in the upward-and-downward direction.
Power from the battery group 9X including the plurality (two in this embodiment) of batteries 9 is supplied to the power generating motor 16 via the PDU 18.
Here, the center of gravity (the center-of-gravity position BC) of the plurality of the batteries 9 is located at the side opposite to the PDU 18 and the power generating motor 16 about the central line CX (see Fig. 2) of the motorcycle body. [0049]
More specifically, the center-of-gravity position SC of the swing arm 13, to which the PDU 18 and the power generating motor 16 are mounted, is located at the left side about the central line CX of the motorcycle body.
On the other hand, the two batteries 9 constituting the battery group 9X are accommodated in the two battery accommodating spaces 19X1 and 19X2, apart from the central line CX of the motorcycle body, among the battery accommodating spaces 19X1 to 19X3 in the battery box 19. As a result, as shown in Fig. 2, the center-of-gravity position BC of the batteries of the battery group 9X is located between the two batteries 9. That is, the

center o'f gravity position BC is located at the right side about the central line CX of the motorcycle body, which is the side opposite to the PDU 18 and the power generating motor 16 about the central line CX of the motorcycle body.
As a result, the center-of-gravity position of the assembly of the battery group 9X and the swing arm 13 is closer to the central line CX of the motorcycle body than the center-of-gravity position BC of the batteries of the battery group 9X and the center-of-gravity position SC of the swing arm 13.
Consequently, it is possible to easily secure weight balance of the electric motorcycle in the left-and-right direction. [0050]
