Title of Invention

A BOBBIN TUBE SUPPORT FOR SPINNING OR TWISTING SPINDLES
Abstract This invention relates to a bobbin tube support for spinning or twisting spindles, the support being capable of taking along and exact centering of the bobbin tube on the spindle upper part by means of a multipoint support, characterized in that the multipoint support (5, 6, 7) has positive fit.
Full Text FIELD OF THE INVENTION
The present invention relates to a bobbin tube support for spinning or twisting
spindles, in which support the taking along and the centering of the bobbin tube
on the spindle upper part is achieved by means of a multipoint support and
without the application of additional movable coupling elements.
BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
It is known that the bobbin tube is centered by means of spring-mounted
elements and, at the same time, taken along. It is further known that the
centering and taking along of the bobbin tube can be effected by coupling
members under the action of centrifugal forces. The coupling of the bobbin tube
and the spindle by means of conically shaped bearing surfaces is also known.
The above mentioned solutions have the disadvantage in that the exact
centering of the bobbin tube is not guaranteed, as the bobbin tube is disposed,
due to unbalance and the effect of the centrifugal forces, to one side of the
spindle. Because of this off-center seat, the unbalance forces of the spindle
bearing are increased.
A bobbin tube support of the above mentioned type for avoiding the above
mentioned disadvantages is known from German Democratic Republic published
patent 102 169. Here the bobbin tube is centered, without play, on the spindle
upper part and made fast by means of a multipoint support, whereby the bobbin
tube is slightly deformed in the area of this multipoint support when placed onto
the upper spindle part. Thus a frictional line contact is established between the
bobbin tube and the spindle upper part.
The disadvantages of the known bobbin tube support is the fact that as a result
of diametrical tolerances of the bobbin tube, a slip-free friction connection of the
bobbin tube support is no longer guaranteed.
OBJECT OF THE INVENTION
It is an object of the present invention, while retaining the advantages of the
above mentioned bobbin tube support, to eliminate the disadvantageous
influence of the diametrical tolerances.
SUMMARY OF THE INVENTION
This object of the present invention has been achieved in that the multipoint
support has a positive fit.
Due to the positive fit between the bobbin tube and the spindle upper part, the
dependence on the slight deformation of the bobbin tube in the area of the
multipoint support is eliminated, so that diametrical tolerances are no longer of
any importance.
In an embodiment of the present invention, the spindle upper part of the
multipoint support in cross section - is designed as an equilateral polygon, to
which corresponding cams on the inner circumference of the bobbin tube are
arranged. At least three polygon surfaces must be present. When the vertical
centre lines of the individual polygon surfaces intersect with the spindle axis, the
bobbin tube is exactly centered in relation to the spindle. Under these
circumstances, for example, an equilateral triangle is possible.
When the bobbin tube is placed on the spindle upper part, the bobbin tube falls
into a defined position. Insertion bevellings are advantageously provided on the
spindle upper part.
In order that the bobbin tubes do not have to turn too far until the cams are
disposed on the individual polygon surfaces when the spindle starts to run, in
practical embodiments at least six polygonal surfaces are provided on the spindle
upper part. The number of cams should, however, be limited to three, so that,
with regard to the arrangement of the cams on the respective polygonal
surfaces, there is no overdetermination which could lead to friction. The number
of polygonal surfaces must be divisible by three.

DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF THE INVENTION
The bobbin tube support shown in Figures 1 and 2 between a spinning or a
twisting spindle 1 and a bobbin tube 2 onto which yarn is wound is located in the
upper area 4 of a spindle upper part 3. In the case of this bobbin tube support,
the taking along and the centering of the bobbin tube 2 on the spindle upper
part 3 takes place without the application of additional movable coupling
elements. For the coupling of the bobbin tube 2 to the spindle upper part 3, a
multipoint support, 5, 6 and 7 is provided, in the present case a three-point
support.
While the spindle upper part 3 below the bobbin tube support is, in the known
way, cylindrical or slightly conical in design, it is in the upper area 4 in cross
section designed as a polygon 8 having, in this case, six polygon surfaces 9
altogether. The enveloping surface of the polygon 8 is, in diameter, slightly
smaller than the inner circumference 13 of the bobbin tube 2.
To ensure the symmetry and to achieve a good centering of the bobbin tube 2 in
relation to the relevant axis of the spinning or twisting spindle 1, the polygon 8 is
equilateral.
The bobbin tube 2 has on its inner circumference 13 three cams 10, 11 and 12
altogether, of which one is arranged to a polygonal surface 9. In order to avoid
overdetermination and thus friction, however, only three symmetrically arranged
cams 10, 11 and 12 are provided, even though there are more than three
polygonal surfaces 9 - in the present case six - present.
An optimum centering is achieved when the vertical centre lines 15 to each
polygonal surface 9 intersect at one point, namely on the axis of the spindle
upper part 3.
In order to facilitate the placing of the bobbin tube 2 on the spindle upper part 3,
the spindle upper part 3 is provided with insertion bevellings 14.
When the bobbin tube 2 is placed on the spindle upper part 3, the procedure is
such that the bobbin tube 2 is let fall down the last 50 mm. This can be done in
the case of either manual doffing or automatic doffing.
When the spinning or twisting spindle 1 starts to run, the bobbin tube 2 turns
until the cams 10,11 and 12 are disposed in the area of the bobbin tube support
on the respective polygonal surfaces 9. Thereafter, this point of contact on all
cams 10, 11 and 12 lies on a circle, whose centre point is the centre of the
spinning or twisting spindle 1. Thus, the bobbin tube 2 is always exactly centered
on the spindle upper part 3 and is, at the same time, taken along having positive
fit.
Due to the embodiment according to the present invention, the bobbin tube 2
cannot travel to an off-centre position because of unbalance forces or as a result
of action of centrifugal forces. The bobbin tube 2 cannot wander from the axis of
the spinning or twisting spindle 1.
We Claim
1. A bobbin tube support for spinning or twisting spindles, the support being
capable of taking along and exact centering of the bobbin tube on the
spindle upper part by means of a multipoint support, characterized in that
the multipoint support (5, 6, 7) has positive fit.
2. Bobbin tube support as claimed in claim 1, wherein the spindle upper part
(3) in the area of the multipoint support (5, 6, 7) is designed in cross
section as an equilateral polygon (8), to which corresponding cams (10,
11, 12) on the inner circumference (13) of the bobbin tube (2) are
arranged.
3. Bobbin tube support as claimed in claims 1 or 2, wherein the multipoint
support (5, 6, 7) is provided in the upper area (4) of the spindle upper
part (3).
4. Bobbin tube support as claimed in claim 3, wherein the spindle upper part
(3) is provided with insertion bevellings (14) for guiding the bobbin tube
(2) to the multipoint support (5,6, 7).
5. Bobbin tube support as claimed in any of the claims 2 to 4, wherein three
cams (10, 11, 12) are provided, independent of the number of polygon
surfaces (9).
6. Bobbin tube for a bobbin tube support for spinning or twisting spindles,
whereby the bobbin tube is taken along and centered on the spindle
upper part by means of a multipoint support, characterized in that the
inner circumference (13) of the bobbin tube (2) is provided with cams (10,
11, 12) for the multipoint support (5, 6, 7) having positive fit on the
polygon surfaces (9) of the spindle upper part (3).
