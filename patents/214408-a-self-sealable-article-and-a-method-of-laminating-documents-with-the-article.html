Full Text The present invention relates to a self-sealable article and a method of laminating documents with the self-sealable article. The self-sealable article includes sheets rolls and folders.
The invention further comprises a composition constituting the film. The invention also relates to a technique by which instantly certificated document, maps, identity cards are laminated without the aid of any equipment or machinery.
The lamination process finds a very unique application as single side lamination, windows cut lamination, inner page of books lamination.
The folders for lamination are available in various sizes such Al, A2, A3, A4 and identity card sizes.
Therefore it is the primary object of invention to develop a laminating article by means of which through a simple process, varieties of documents can be laminated.
It is further an object of invention to invent a simple process of lamination.
It is another object of invention to invent a laminated sheet / material which is sturdy and long lasting.
A further object of the present invention is to prevent the formation of rainbow coloured interference fringes in the laminated documents. This is achieved by the present lamination system.
Further object of the invention will be clear described in the following description. The invention will be fully and particularly described in the following complete specification.
In the conventional heating method, matter is inserted between two films. The entire two films and matter are placed between thick board and heat is applied during film formation.

The disadvantages of the conventional methods are:
1. Destruction of original documents due to overheating
2. In the case of power failure during the lamination process original gets damaged.
3. The documents should match specification of machine.
The present invention is addressed to overcome the problem associated with prior art technique method or process.
The inventor has now invented a lamination film which obviate the defects associated with prior art technique.
The invention further envisages a simple process for lamination of documents.
The conventional machine, technique or process are very expensive. This novel invention overcomes the problem associated with prior art.
The salient features therefore according to the invention are as follows:
1. The lamination is carried out without aid of any machine.
2. Lamination is carried out without application of heat.
3. Lamination of any sized document is permissible
4. The capital investment is practically nil.

It may be noted that the following specification particularly describes and ascertains the nature of this invention and the manner in which it is to be performed. As stated earlier, the present invention relates to a simplified method of lamination to help everyone to laminate their certificates, documents, plans etc., of their own without any powered or non powered machineries. In the known art a document which is to be laminated is kept in between a BOPP or PVC film and again kept in a thin board and then it is inserted into a lamination machine. The machine should be set according to the films specification and the document will get laminated by heat. In such machinery systems a huge investment on machinery is a must, and again power consumption, training the operators, and the help of service Engineers are unavoidable. Safely precautions during operations is a must.
In machine lamination system, often the comers of the original documents get burned and one side lamination, inner books lamination, lamination of heat sensitive materials are also not possible.
In machine lamination system, size of document, and thickness of document should be as per the condition of machine. In machine lamination system, variety lamination such as a glass and mettle is also not primary.
-5-

In machine lamination system, after lamination the document cannot be folded, since it will be rigid. Hence, the object of the present invention is to have a system to overcome the above said defects and to have a safe method of lamination of documents.
It is to realise further the object of the invention to have a very simple, easy method for lamination of documents. Thus according to this photolam (self-sealable article) laminating system for laminating a document or a certificate is placed on a smooth table and the photolam processed film after releasing the release paper is placed on the document to be laminated and the film is gently rubbed so that the sheet gets stuck to the documents (this constitutes one side lamination). For laminating the document on both sides, the same process is repeated. The edges are then trimmed. The laminated document is tear proof and water proof
The article is also available as folders where the document is placed in the folder after the removal of the releasing paper and rubbed on both sides for sealing. Further the article is also available as rolls, which can be cut into desired size and used for lamination for single and double side of the documents.

7. Self-sealable article is also available in standard sizes of Identity card, A4, Full scape, A3, A2, Al, AO sizes.
HOW TO USE THE SELF-SEALABLE ARTICLE
1. Release the film from the release paper.
2. Place it so that the coated side is facing upwards, on a smooth cleaned surface.
3. Take the original to be laminated.
4. Carefully place it on the coated side of the film without any shrinkage.
5. Gently rub on the top of the original.
6. Trim the edges and the document is now laminated on one side.
7. The same process is repeated on the other side of the document to have both sides laminated.
