Title of Invention

"AN IMPROVED DIE CASTED RAZOR ASSEMBLY"
Abstract An improved die casted razor assembly comprising a blade assembly (10), and a razor handle assembly (20), a cap member (11), a top blade (12), a bottom blade (14), a spacer member (13), a guard member (15), a cam means (100) and two side guide pads (95,96), the said razor handle assembly consisting a body member, a handle head portion (25) with recess (30) in which is disposed a moulded plastic head portion (40) comprising two arms (50,60) in the said recess there is disposed a coil spring (70) and a plunger member (80) wherein the said coil spring biases the said plunger member in the direction of a free end (90) of the plunger member in the said blade assembly (10), a leaf spring (110) is disposed in the said moulded plastic head portion so that the said two arms (50,60) are biased outwardly, two shell bearings (120,130) are provided to connect the said blade assembly (10) to the said handle assembly (20) and the said plunger member (80) is provided with oppositely extending arms (140,150) each having upstanding their from the detents, the said coil spring (70) urges the said plunger member to move upwardly the said detents so that the detents enter the opening to lock the said two arms in a inwardly flexed position.
Full Text - 2 -Field of the Invention
The present invention relates to shaving implement and is directed more particularly to a razor blade handle which facilitates the blade assembly is pivotally movable on the handle assembly during the shaving operation. Description of the Prior Art
It is known in the art to provide a razor assembly, which may be connected to and used in conjunction with a razor handle to facilitate shaving operations. In a blade assembly in which blade means are held between blade assembly surfaces adopted to engage the surface being shaved in front of and behind, such surfaces are generally referred to as a guard and cap.
It is further known that shaving efficiency of such a safety razor assembly may be improved if the blade assembly is adopted to pivot on the razor handle during a shaving operation, permitting the blade assembly to more closely follow the contours of a surface being shaved.
The razor handle parts mainly tail portion was made out of plastic or metal pressed components and head portion is assembled with pivotally movable parts on the assembly machine.

Referring now to the accompanying drawings of Figure 1 to 9 and particularly in Figure 9, it will be seen that the illustrated die casted razor handle assembly comprises a razor handle (20) made out of aluminum or like material in a pressure die casting process and the handle head portion (25) is provided with recess (30) in which is disposed a moulded plastic head portion (40) comprising two arms (50, 60) joined by connecting portion secured in the handle.

- 7 -In the handle recess there is disposed a coil spring (70) and a
plunger member (80) the coil spring biasing the plunger member in
direction of a free end (90) in the blade assembly (10) is connected to
the handle assembly (20) the free end of the plunger member (80) is
urged by the coil spring (70) into engagement with the blade assembly
cam means (100). During pivot operation of the blade assembly (10) the
plunger member free end (90) bears agains the cam means (100) to urge
the blade assembly to a given position. The position in which the
plunger member free end rest at an apex of the cam means.
The two arms (50, 60) extend beyond the sides of the handle and are accessible to an operator. The arms are biased outwardly by a leaf spring(110) disposed in head portion. To connect the blade assembly to the handle assembly the operator presses on two arms inwardly towards each other against the bias of the coil spring (70), moving the two shell bearings (120, 130) towards each other.
The plunger member (80) is provided with oppositely extending arms (140, 150) each having upstanding their from the detent. Each of the shell bearings(120, 130) is provided with an opening adopted to receive restructively the detents. As the two shell bearings are pressed inwardly towards one another the opening align with the detents respectively. Upon such alignment the coil spring (70) urges the plunger member (80) and thereby the detents move upwardly such that the detents entering the opening to lock the two arms in an inwardly flexed position. Upon release of the two arms by the operator the arms remain locked in the inwardly flexed position.

- 8 -The two shell bearings (120, 130) are then pressed against the
blade assembly under two side guide pads (95, 96) forcing the plunger
member downwardly and removing the detents from the opening, the
arms being thereby released. Upon release of two arms (50, 60) the arms
springs outwardly in opposite direction away from each other under
influence of this coil spring (70), the two shell bearings entering the two
side guide pads.
The spring biased plunger member engages the cam means (100) seeking the apex of the cam means and thereby urging the blade assembly to a neutral position. During shaving operation the blade assembly as a whole pivots on the handle following the contours of the surface being shaved.
When it is desired to discard used blade assembly (10) the operator need only to compress the two arms removing the shell bearings from the guide pads and releasing the blade assembly.
The grip portion of the razor handle assembly (20) is provided with serrated rubber pads (26, 27) on both sides. These pads secured in the recess (170) provided in the aluminum die casted handle for better grip during shaving operation.
