Title of Invention

"A TYRE TO BE MOUNTED ON A RIM"
Abstract A tyre which is intended to be mounted on a rim of axial width L, with rim seats forming an angle &#947; with the axis of rotation such that 0° < &#947; < 160, and rim flanges with rounded hooks, comprising a tread joined to two beads (3) by two sidewalls (2) and at least one carcass reinforcement (1) formed of at least one ply of reinforcement elements, characterised in that each bead (3) is without a bead wire, said carcass reinforcement (1) having in each bead (3) a meridian profile which is curved axially and radially from the outside to the inside to form an axially inner edge located in an angle a open axially and radially towards the inside, one of the edges of which is parallel to the axis of rotation, and of a value of at most 250, said carcass reinforcement (1) being reinforced in each bead (3) at least radially to the outside and axially to the inside by at least one first continuous additional layer (6), of meridian profile substantially parallel to the meridian profile of said carcass reinforcement (1) in the bead (3) and comprising at least one first section composed of at least one ply (61, 62, 63) of inextensible reinforcement elements forming an angle of between -2.5° and +2.5* with the circumferential direction.
Full Text The invention relates to a tyre comprising at least one tread joined to two beads by means of two sidewalls and a carcass reinforcement, said tyre being able to be used with or without an independent inner tube and being intended, after mounting on a standardised rim and after inflation, to be fitted on any vehicle capable of rolling.
The tyres may be mounted on several types of standardised rim. Said rims may have frustoconical seats inclined relative to the axis of rotation, forming with said axis an angle which may be between 4° and 16°. Among the latter, there are integral rims which are then referred to as hollow rims, semi-hollow rims or even drop-centre rims, or rims made of several sections which are referred to as interim or "advanced". The rims in question may also have rim seats parallel to the axis of rotation and are generally referred to as flat-based.
The most frequently used rims, referred to as hollow or drop-centre rims, comprise a mounting groove, the diameter of which is distinctly less than the nominal diameter of the rim. This rim groove diameter is considered by users to be too small, and it does not make it possible to select brake drums of dimensions suitable for braking effectively vehicles which are becoming more and more powerful.
It is furthermore highly desirable, whatever the reasons (reducing the pressures exerted by the tyre on the ground, increasing the transverse stiffness of the tyre, increasing the available internal space of the rim, lowering the centre of gravity of certain types of machinery, to name but a few), to produce tyres, the

H/S form ratio of which is reduced, H being the height of the tyre on its rim and S its maximum axial width.
The development of tyres with reduced form ratios, more particularly .intended to be fitted on heavy vehicle-type vehicles, such as lorries, buses, subway trains and tractor-trailer units, or of the very large heavy-vehicle type, such as loading, transport and earthmoving machinery used in civil engineering, or aircraft machinery, is very delicate; in particular, the endurance of the beads becomes significantly less as the H/S ratio decreases, the inadequacies in endurance being found at the level of the ends of upturns of the carcass reinforcement or the ends of reinforcement plies located in the beads of the tyre, the structure of said beads being a conventional structure with, in each bead, a carcass reinforcement upturn and reinforcement plies, the meridian profiles of which are substantially parallel to the meridian profile of the carcass reinforcement in the zone of said beads. Furthermore, such form ratios, despite the possible reduction in the inflation pressures, involve a notable lack of comfort, which may be quite unbearable in the case of tyres travelling long distances on relatively bumpy roads.
In order to overcome such disadvantages and to facilitate the production of tyres, Application FR 94/14688 proposes a tyre of form ratio of less than 0.8, intended to be mounted on a hollow rim or a drop-centre riin, the seats of which are inclined relative to the axis of rotation by an angle which may be between 4° and 16". Said tyre has a carcass reinforcement, surmounted radially by a crown reinforcement, the carcass reinforcement being composed of at least one ply of cords or cables and being turned up in each bead about an anchoring bead wire, passing from the outside

to the inside to form an upturn, said upturn being reinforced by at least one additional reinforcement ply of metal cables, oriented by an angle of between 0" and 20° relative to the circumferential direction, the value 0° being included in said range, and the meridian profile of said reinforced upturn, in the tyre not mounted on its rim, being substantially parallel to the meridian profile of at least the portion of the seat located axialLy to the inside of the projection of the centre of the circle circumscribed on the bead wire on said rim seat::.
Although the production methods of a tyre are currently very highly developed, the use of bead wires requires separate production and treatment of said bead wires (coating, for example), the transport and positioning of the same bead wires on a building drum or on a given support for manufacture of the bead, all operations which require time and multiple precautions, and which are therefore costly. Furthermore, since the current tyres are subjected to ever-increasing driving torques, the compromise between clamping on the rim which is necessary to civoid inopportune rotations and maximum clamping to permit mounting on the rim is difficult to control by the person skilled in the art in the current structures of tyres with bead wires around which a carcass reinforcement is wound.
The Applicant, has unexpectedly noted that the distribution of the contact pressures between the rim and the tyre at: the level of the respective rim and bead seats could be controlled and rendered as regular as possible i.n a very simple and reproducible manner whilst permitting an improvement in the resistance to unwinding of the carcass reinforcement in the event of a high degree of heating of the beads.
