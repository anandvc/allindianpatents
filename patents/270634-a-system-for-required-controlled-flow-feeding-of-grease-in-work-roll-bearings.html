Title of Invention

A SYSTEM FOR REQUIRED CONTROLLED FLOW FEEDING OF GREASE IN WORK ROLL BEARINGS
Abstract A device for feeding of grease in work roll bearings and, in particular, to a system for required controlled flow feeding of grease in work roll bearings. The system basically involves a dose feeder means adapted to feed said desired selective amounts of grease in bearings; means to operate the dose feeder means at high pressure to ensure uniform distribution of grease inside the bearing; and monitoring device adapted to monitor the exact amount of grease feeding through the said dose feeders. Importantly, the system for feeding of grease in work roll bearings in adapted to ensure feeding of exact amount of grease in the bearings. The system can be advantageously used to feed grease in roll choke bearings and in other rolling mill areas such as finishing strands in Hot Strip Mill, Skelp Mill and the like.

BACKGROUND ART
It is well known that work rolls are interchanged in Taddem Cold MilI (TCM), Double Cold
Reduction (DCR) and Skin Pass Mill (SPM) in Cold Rolliog Mill complexes in steel plants.
Usually, the work rolls are changed on a schedule basis, after proccssing a fixed no, of
colls and subsequently are brought bo Roll Grinding and Bearing Shop (RGBS) for
inspection and maintenance. Two numbers of four row taper roller grease lubricated
bearings are mounted on the roll necks. Usually, such bearings are required to be dosed
with specific amount of grease before they are again mounted in the mills for rolling.
The amount of grease to be fed into individual bearings is pre-calculated. Feeding more
amount of grease into bearings than the required amount lead to causing flooding which
restrict the movement of taper rollers in the raceways. Inadequate greasing on the other
hand lead to starvation in the bearing and the lubricating, film thickness between the
races and rollers are not available which lead to metal-to-metal contact in the raceways,
which in turn finally fead to failure of the bearings.
It is apparent from the above that the lifee of a bearing depends largely on the amount of
grease it contains. In roll grinding and bearing shops, feeding of grease to the bearings
take place with the help of centralized grease pumps. Usually, about two numbers of
gear pumps are put into service to feed the grease into the bearings. The roll choke
housing by not dismended from work rolls but ports are provided to feed grease in the
bearings by inserting a flexible high pressure hose. Usually low pressure high discharge
gear pumps are employed to carry out the greasing of the bearings.
As would be apparent from the above state of the art on feeding of grease in work roll
bearings, while it is important to ensure that the amount of grease is fed to the desired
level and also to take care the bearings do not suffer from low levels of grease, the
system of feeding grease in bearings as presently followed in the art do not serve
effectively to take care of the above requirements of gneasing of bearing. Thus, it is
usually difficult to monitor the grease feed into the bearing and also to continuously

such as bearing failures. Tnere is thus also a need to check the contamination of grease
being fed into the bearings to ensure effective working life of bearings.

of grease through the dose feeder means. The system is adapted such that the
feeding operation cycle can be selectively completed in a desired time frame.
A system as claimed in anyone of claims 2 to 5 comprising flexible hose/pipe means
operatively connecting the dose feeders to the bearing.
A grease transfer pump is provided to transfer grease from a grease drum/source to
a pumping station with dose feeders provided downstream of the pumping station.
Preferably, a double acting reciprocating grease pump.
