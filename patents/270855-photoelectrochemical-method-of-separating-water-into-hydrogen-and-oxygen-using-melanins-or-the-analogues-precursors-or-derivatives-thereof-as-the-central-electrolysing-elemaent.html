Title of Invention

"PHOTOELECTROCHEMICAL METHOD OF SEPARATING WATER INTO HYDROGEN AND OXYGEN, USING MELANINS OR THE ANALOGUES, PRECURSORS OR DERIVATIVES THEREOF AS THE CENTRAL ELECTROLYSING ELEMAENT"
Abstract Photoelectrochemical process to generate energy, that comprises; water, at least one water soluble electrolyzing material such as melanin, mobile electron carriers, and a source of energy; the electrons excited by the source of energy are taken from the exterior of the electrolyzing material to the interior, followed by the generation of high energy electrons, from low energy electrons; these high energy electrons go toward the reaction centers of the electrolyzing material through carriers, where they are caught by an acceptor and transferred onto another primary acceptor of electrons, these ionic interactions depending on pH, this electron transfer liberates energy which is used to establish a gradient of protons, i.e. a positively charged or oxidant agent and a negatively charged or reducing agent are formed; in this way the photoelectrolysis starts in a billionesimal of second after the excitement; the reaction is reversible, i.e. for the production of the molecule of 02 and 41-T, the photoelectrolysis requires the removal of four electrons coming from two water molecules, the removal of four water electrons requires the absorption of four photons, one for each electron; this reaction is exergonic and consumes energy; on the other hand, water is generated when the photons reduce oxygen atoms with hydrogen atoms forming water molecules and liberating energy; a better benefit of the reaction is obtained when the electrolyzing material is doped; the temperature of the process may range between 2 to 45 °C.

DESCRIPTION
FIELD OF THE INVENTION
This invention relates to the processes or methods for obtaining alternative energy, particularly the ones known as photoelectrochemical processes, through which hydrogen and oxygen atoms are obtained by means of the separation or partition of water molecule with which we generate hydrogen and oxygen atoms. Moreover, high energy electrons are generated, and very possibly this method can be applied to the reduction of carbon dioxide, nitrate and sulphate molecules.
Because the reactions occur in both ways, our invention can also be applied to electricity generation, for our method permits to bind hydrogen and oxygen atoms forming water molecules, and collaterally generating electrical current.
BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
About the related art, nowadays, the known processes used up to now to separate the water molecule in hydrogen and oxygen atoms are, among others: a).-The application of intense electrical currents. b).-The heating of water until two thousand degrees centigrade. c).-The separation of water molecule by solar electrochemical method: (photoelectrochemical), which integrates a semi-conductor material and a water
electrolyzer in a monolithic design to produce hydrogen directly from water using light as
the unique energy source. Simple in concept, the challenge was to find a material or base that could support the whole process, and up to now, the ideal or the most adequate material had not found because some materials are very expensive, some are polluting, others are inefficient; most of them decompose fast, others are damaged with water and some others require exceedingly strict work conditions; that is why cost-effectiveness has not been feasible up to now from an economical, environmental and political point of view, and others are not appropriate for large scale application, their usefulness being thus reduced to some specific and small processes d).-Another method to separate water is by solar energy concentration (with mirrors for example), with the object to elevate water temperature until two thousand ° C. This is the required temperature used in laboratory to divide the water molecule, e) One further method is by using photosynthetic microbes as green algas and cianobacterium, those produce hydrogen from water as part of metabolic activities using light energy as main source. This photobiological technology is promising, but as oxygen is produced as well as hydrogen, the technology must solve the limitation that is the sensibility to oxygen in the enzymatic systems. Besides, hydrogen production from photosynthetic organisms is currently too low to be economically viable. f).-Another method is water electrolysis, using electricity to separate the water molecule in its compounds (hydrogen and oxygen atoms). At present time, two kinds of electrolyzers are used for commercial production of hydrogen: the alkaline, and the membrane of protons interchange, but these approaches cannot compete now from an economic point of view with the hydrogen produced from natural gas.(Source: U.S.

A natural material that can also divide or separate the water molecule and that has been studied is chlorophyll but because its affinity with light is between 400 nm and about 700 nm the rest of the light energy is lost. That is why it is estimated that 80 per cent of used energy is wasted. Moreover, its production is complex and expensive, requiring for example temperatures of -8 ° C. EXAMPLES
We conducted small scale experiments. Once we inferred these interesting properties of melanins according the structure activity relation, we placed soluble synthetic melanin in water, forming a 1% solution in five 20 ml transparent, high density polythene flasks, at room temperature. We measured the pH before and after lighting them during 30 minutes with visible light of natural source (sun) not concentrated; measuring the pH, we obtained in average a decrease of two decimals of unit of pH (from 7.3 to 7.1), we consider it significant because melanins have buffering property per se, so the change must be larger, but is hidden by melanin intrinsic buffering property, and thus we only detected part of this pH modification, a change of pH the magnitude of which is related to the biological system, because if it were greater, it would probably severely destroy or damage the cell, but a change of this size is enough to induce biological changes that involves said extraordinary compound. To determine the biological magnitude of a decrease of 0.2 units of pH, we will mention that, in the case of blood, this reduction increases more than 10% the calcium concentration.
Besides, the total blood pH ranges from 7.38 to 7.44, the arterial blood pH ranges from 7.36 to 7.41, and the vein blood pH ranges from 7.37 to 7.45, i.e., the variations are within a very narrow margin, and thus a difference of 2 decimals of unit of pH is really significant in a biological system.

l.-Photoelectrochemical process to generate energy, that comprises; water, at least one water soluble electrolyzing material such as melanin, mobile electron carriers, and a source of energy; the electrons excited by the source of energy are taken from the exterior of the electrolyzing material to the interior, followed by the generation of high energy electrons, from low energy electrons; these high energy electrons go toward the reaction centers of the electrolyzing material through carriers, where they are caught by an acceptor and transferred onto another primary acceptor of electrons, these ionic interactions depending on pH, this electron transfer liberates energy which is used to establish a gradient of protons, i.e. a positively charged or oxidant agent and a negatively charged or reducing agent are formed; in this way the photoelectrolysis starts in a billionesimal of second after the excitement; the reaction is reversible, i.e. for the production of the molecule of 02 and 41-T, the photoelectrolysis requires the removal of four electrons coming from two water molecules, the removal of four water electrons requires the absorption of four photons, one for each electron; this reaction is exergonic and consumes energy; on the other hand, water is generated when the photons reduce oxygen atoms with hydrogen atoms forming water molecules and liberating energy; a better benefit of the reaction is obtained when the electrolyzing material is doped; the temperature of the process may range between 2 to 45 °C.
The photoeledjochemical process to generate energy according to claim 1, wherein
the electrolyzing material can be melanin, melanin precursors, melanin derivatives, melanin analogues, melanin variants, electroactive, with particle size from 1 angstrom; they can be in suspension, in solution, in gel, natural or synthetic, from vegetal, animal, or mineral origin, pure or mixed among them or with organic or inorganic compounds, i.e., doped, with concentration in the system ranging from 0.1% to 100%.
3.- Photoelectrochemical process to generate energy according to claim 1, wherein the electron carriers can be organic, such as quinones, and inorganic acceptors.
4.- Photoelectrochemical process to generate energy according to claim 1, wherein the energy source is natural light or any other source with a wavelength ranging from 200 to 900 nm, concentrated, coherent or disperse, otiier sources of energy that can a used is the kinetic energy, such as the mechanical movement; sound, or ultrasound in the interval of one MHz or magnetic fields and others.
5.- Photoelectrochemical process to generate energy according to claim 1, wherein pH interval ranges from 2 to 9, preferably around 7.
6.- The use of melanin, melanin precursors, melanin derivatives, melanin analogues, melanin variants as electrolyzing material to generate energy in a Photoelectrochemical process obtaining alternatively reduction products.
7.- The use of melanin according to claim 6, wherein a photoelectrochemical process
generates electrical energy or store it as a battery or accumulator.
8.- The use of melanin according to claim 6, wherein a photoelectrochemical process generates alternatively the reduction of carbon dioxide, sulphates and n'trates.
9.- The use of melanin according to claim 6, wherein a photoelectrochemical process generates molecular 02 and, alternatively through incomplete reduction of oxygen, superoxide anion, hydrogen peroxide and hydroxyls can be obtained.
10.- A device to perform a photoelectrochemical process of production or storage of energy through an electrolyzing agent such as the melanin, comprising: a cell that integrates a semiconductor material and a water electrolyzer inside a monolithic design, a source of energy, with electrodes, that may have lens to allow the concentration or selection of the energy source, magnetic fields from low to significant intensity can be applied to the instrument.
11.- A device to perform a photoelectrochemical process of production or storage of energy through an electrolyzing agent such as the melanin according to claim 10, wherein the cell is made of a material that permits the transmission of electromagnetic waves for the activation of the reactant system, such cell can be segmented, the materials used having redox properties; the volume can be at least 1 microliter, the partial pressures of gases inside the cell can range from 0.1 mm Hg to 4 atmospheres of pressure.
12.- A device to perform a photoeiectrochemical process of production or storage of energy through an electrolyzing agent such as the melanin according to claim 10, wherein lenses can be biconvex microlens on one side (the side exposed to light to concentrate it) and flat on the other side, the power of this microlens ranging from 0.1 to 100 dioptres.
13.- A device to :perform a photoeiectrochemical process of production or storage of energy through an/electrolyzing agent such as the melanin, according to claims 10 to 12, that when storing electrical energy works as a battery or accumulator.
