Title of Invention

A FRONT PANEL MOUNTING MEMBER FOR A SCOOTER TYPE OF MOTOR VEHICLE
Abstract 75 MAS 2003 A front panel assembly mounting member for a scooter type of motor vehicle A front panel assembly mounting member for a scooter type of motor vehicle comprising a metallic member M blanked out from sheet metal to form at one end thereof, a curved portion C cooperating with the pivot tube T of the vehicle main frame; a first raised support SI for mounting the horn H of the vehicle; a second raised support S2 for mounting the relay R for the direction indication lamps of the vehicle; a third raised support S3 for mounting the regulator N of the vehicle; an A aperture for allowing wiring harness to pass therethrough, the sides B of the aperture having a smooth contour, said metallic member having two drilled holes T for mounting the front panel of the vehicle.
Full Text

This invention relates to a toggle link device for mounting the engine of a scooter type vehicle on the frame thereof. The term "scooter type vehicle" in this specification not only means the scooter type two wheeler vehicle as it is popularly known but also includes any other vehicle to which this invention is applicable.
Conventionally, the toggle link device has one or two connectors for taking the load of the engine.
However, the known toggle link device has the disadvantage that with the two connectors that are used, the overall strength of the toggle link has to be increased without sacrificing the ridfr handling of the vehicle.
Secondly, the known toggle link device has stoppers provided which are independent of the connectors that are used and, therefore, the connectors do not play any role in the stopping mechanism of the toggle link.
On the other hand, the toggle link device proposed herein is provided with three spaced

connectors, whereby the load to be borne is distributed between them. Accordingly, the load bearing strength of each connector is reduced, thereby reducing the cost.
Furthermore, in the proposed device, the spaced configuration of the connectors furnishes a wider span of support for the engine, thus improving ridfi^i;: handling of the vehicle.
In the proposed toggle link device a stopper is provided for at least one of the end connectors. Such connector(s) not only serves the purpose of load bearing, but also serves to take on impact forces, by reason of its dual function as a load bearer and stopper.
The toggle link arrangement for mounting the engine of a scooter type vehicle on the frame thereof, according to this invention, comprises a swivelable member carrying three spaced connectors fixed to the engine; a stopper bracket, provided with a cushion, for at least one of the end connectors; and two bushes provided for the end connectors for being clamped to the frame.
This invention will now be described with reference to the accompanying drawings which illustrate

by way of example, and not by way of limitation, one of various possible embodiments of the device proposed herein, wherein
Fig.l illustrates a view of the said embodiment in perspective and
Fig.2 illustrates a part of the vehicle in which the said embodiment is installed.
A swivelable member S carries three spaced connectors Cl C2 and C3 fixed to the engine. Two bushes G, preferably made of rubber, provided at the ends of the member S are fixed to the frame of the vehicle at A for facilitating the swiveling action.
The centre connector Cl is provided with a tube T. The engine E is pivotably supported by a supporting rod R, attached to the engine, the rod passing through the tube T and the holes H in the connectors C2 and C3, to mount the engine pivotably.
During movement of the vehicle, the engine tends to move up and down, pivotably at the tube T and the connectors Cl C2 and C3, thus causing the connectors to move swivelably at the swivelable points

of support A of the member S. This movement is limited by the stopper M, shovm as being provided for the connector C3. The stopper M, at the end of its travel, butts against the frame, to limit the swivelling movement of the member S.
The stopper comprises a stopper bracket B provided with a cushion C, such as, a rubber pad.
