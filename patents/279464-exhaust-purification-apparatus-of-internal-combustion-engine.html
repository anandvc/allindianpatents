Title of Invention

"EXHAUST PURIFICATION APPARATUS OF INTERNAL COMBUSTION ENGINE"
Abstract In an internal combustion engine, a NOx selective reduction catalyst (15) is disposed in an engine exhaust passageway, and a urea water stored in a urea water tank (20) is fed to the NOx selective reduction catalyst (15) to thereby conduct selective reduction of NOx. Whether or not the interior of the urea water tank (20) has been replenished with urea water is judged by detecting opening/closing of a urea water replenishment port (40) of the urea water tank (20). When at engine driving immediately after replenishment of the interior of the urea water tank (20) with urea water the NOx purification rate is judged as having lowered to below an acceptable level, it is determined that the replenished urea water is abnormal.

TECHNICAL FIELD
The present invention relates ft) an exhaust purification device of an internal combustion engine.
BACKGROUND ART
Known in the art is an internal combustion engine arranging an NOx selective reduction catalyst in an engine exhaust passage, feeding an urea aqueous solution stored in an urea aqueous solution tank to the N0X selective reduction catalyst, and using the ammonia produced from the urea aqueous solution to selectively reduce the N0X contained in the exhaust gas, in which internal combustion engine a urea aqueous solution concentration sensor is arranged in the urea aqueous solution tank for detecting an abnormality of the urea aqueous solution (for example, see Japanese Patent Publication (A) No. 2005-83223).
However, this urea aqueous solution concentration sensor is expensive. .It is desirable to use another more inexpensive method.
DISCLOSURE OF THE INVENTION
An object of the present invention is to provide an exhaust purification device of an internal combustion engine able to detect an abnormality in a urea aqueous solution inexpensively and reliably.
According to the present invention, there is provided an exhaust purification device of an internal combustion engine arranging an NOx selective reduction catalyst in an engine exhaust passage, feeding a urea aqueous solution stored in a urea aqueous solution tank to the NOx selective reduction catalyst, and using ammonia produced from the urea aqueous solution to selectively reduce N0X contained in an exhaust gas, wherein urea aqueous solution refill judging means for judging if the
urea aqueous solution has been refilled in the urea aqueous solution tank and N0X purification rate judging means for judging if the N0X purification rate has fallen to an allowable level or less are provided and, when it is judged that the N0X purification rate has fallen to the allowable level or less at the time.of engine operation right after the urea aqueous solution has been refilled in the urea aqueous solution tank, it is judged that the refilled urea aqueous solution is abnormal.
An abnormality of the urea aqueous solution, that is, a drop in concentration of the urea aqueous solution, for example, mainly occurs when a nonstandard urea aqueous solution is refilled or a liquid other than a urea aqueous solution is refilled. On the other hand, a drop in concentration of the urea aqueous solution is expressed as a drop in the N0X purification rate at the time of engine operation. Therefore, it is possible to detect an abnormality of the urea aqueous solution from a drop in the N0X purification rate at the time of engine operation right after the urea aqueous solution is refilled and therefore possible to detect an abnormality of the urea aqueous solution by an inexpensive method.

The oxidation catalyst 12 for example carries a precious metal catalyst such as platinum. This oxidation catalyst 12 acts to convert the NO contained in the exhaust gas to NO2 and acts to oxidize the HC contained in the exhaust gas. That is, NO2 is more oxidizable than NO, therefore if NO is converted to N02, the oxidation reaction of the particulate matter trapped on the particulate filter 13 is promoted and, further, the reduction reaction by the ammonia at the N0X selective reduction catalyst 15 is promoted. As the particulate filter 13, a particulate filter not carrying any catalyst can be used. For example, a particulate filter carrying a precious metal catalyst such as platinum can also be used. On the other hand, the N0X selective reduction catalyst 15 can be comprised from an ammonia adsorption type Fe zeolite having a high N0X purification rate at a low temperature and can be comprised from a titania-vanadium-based catalyst having no ammonia adsorption function. The oxidation catalyst 16 for example carries a precious metal catalyst comprised of platinum. This oxidation catalyst 16 acts to oxidize the ammonia leaking out from the N0X selective reduction catalyst 15.
