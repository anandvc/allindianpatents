Title of Invention

"PROCESS FOR ZEOLITE SYNTHESIS FROM A SYNTHESIS MEDIUM".
Abstract Process for zeolite synthesis from a. synthesis medium containing especially a source of trivalent aluminium, a source of tetravaleat silicon, at least one alkali or alkaline-earth metal cation in hydroxide form and water in a reactor, characterized in that the said synthesis medium is circulated in the reactor as at least two flows in opposite direction solely by means of a solid helical rotor driven in rotation at a speed lower than 500 rev/min in a guide tube which, motionless in the reactor, defines a space which is internal and a space which is external to the said tube, the first flow moving through the internal space and the second flow moving through the external space in the opposite direction, the circulating synthesis medium being maintained at a temperature necessary for at least one of the operations of maturing or of crystal growth of the zeolite to be synthesized.

The present invention relates to process for zeolite synthesis from a synthesis medium containing especially a source of trivalent aluminium a source of tetravalent silicon, at least one alkali or alkaline-earth metal cation in hydroxide form and water, performed in a reactor, characterized in that the said synthesis medium is circulated in the reactor as two flows in opposite direction solely by means of a solid helical rotor driven in rotation at a speed between 20 rev/min and less than 500 rev/mm m a guide tube which, motionless in the reactor, defines a space winch is internal and a space which is external to the said tube, the first flow moving through the internal space in one direction and the second flow moving through the external space in the opposite direction, the circulating synthesis medium being maintained at a temperature between 50'C and 150C necessary for at least one of the operations of maturing or of crystal growth of the zeolite to be synthesized.

The circulation of the said synthesis medium is ensured by the continuous or noneoutinuous driving in rotation of the solid helical rotor. It is obvious; that it is also possible to cause the medium to circulate by deans of ei number of solid helical rotors, each being driven in rotation in a guide tube held motionless in the
reactor. '
« The dimensional characteristics and the speed of
rotation of the solid rotor are adapted to the synthesis medium, especially to its viscosity, in order to establish a nonturbulent flow regime in the reactor, that is to say a laminar or intermediate flow regime corresponding to a stirring Reynolds number lower than 25,000 (see the collection entitled Mixing by Nagata, Wiley, 1375).
In the course of the syntheses of zeolites in static medium, seeds appear randomly in the synthesis jn.ed.ium, that is to any in places which are unpredictable and at various times,, and this produces crystal siae distributions which are broad and not very reproducible and results in the presence of a number of intermediate crystalline or amorphous phases. In a conventional stirred medium the medium has a shear rate which is variable and often high in the region of stirring and virtually nil in comparison outside the stirrer zone, and this interferes with homogeneous germination. "With the present invention the medium is made homogeneous by tSe Controlled stirrin of the medium, that is to say by setting all of the medium in motion in the guice tube(s} and outside there of. A consequence of this homogeneous flow is that; shear rates, temperatures and compositions are obtained which are virtually ident,ical_at ,all points, resulting in an equal chance of obtaining an identical germination process at the same time, which cannot be
obtainedusing conventional stirring processes or even in a. statio medium,
Alao, besides; the use of a solid helical rotor in order to make the synthesis medium circulate without perturbing the flow regime and hence without appreciably varying its shear rate, theguidetubeincJLudes at least one orifice in its lower part the shape of which has
appreciably only a small influence on the shearing of the gel. An orifice is here intended to mean the space between the lower end of the guide tube and the bottom of the reactor or & plurality of holes cut out in the lower end of the guide tube resting in the bottom of the reactor. It is easy for a person skilled in the art to give any desired shape to these; holes, as their edges must not promote the nucleation of undesirable zeolite seeds, that is to say a heterogeneous nucleation.
Within the scope of the present invention the axis_ of at least one helical rotor coincides with the axis of the reactor and that of the guide tube which contains it.
In a preferred embodiment of the invention the helical rotor is inscribed within a cylinder of revolution such that the ratio of the diameter of this said cylinder of revolution to the diameter of the guide tube which contains it varies from 0.4 to 0.99, and such that the ratio of the diameter of this cylinder of revolution to the diameter of the reactor varies from 0.3 to 0.9.
In addition, In the reactor, the height of the guide tubes is chosen such that it is smaller than that of the reactor, but preferably such that the ratio of the height of the said guide tube to the height to which the reactor is filled by the synthesis gel or solution is smaller than or equal to 0.95.
To implement the process according to the invention, the solid helical rotor necessary for circulating the synthesis medium in the reactor will be chosen from the group consisting of an Archimedes' screw, twisted banes or any other solid rotor which has at its peripheryat least one external ridge exhibiting a helical shape over the whole of its length, capable of establishing a nonturbulent flow regime, by driving in rotation. The preferred rotor of the invention is an Archimedes' screw which can be driven in a rotary motion by means of a motor.
The use of an Archimedes' screw in the case of which there is no pitch limit makes it possible to ensure not only a homogeneous flow in the reactor at virtually constant shear, but also permits an immediate transposition of the process of the invention to any scale and chiefly to an industrial scale.
In order to make use of the process of the invention, the ratio of the speeds of the flows in opposite directions in each reactor varies between 0.5 and 2.
In a particular embodiment of the invention the synthesis medium passes through & succession of reactors arranged in series or in parallel and connected together, the gel rerireulating in each react ox. before being directed towards the next reactor. With this particular embodiment the stages of nucleation and of growth or of maturing o£ the gel, and its crystallization, can be separated.
