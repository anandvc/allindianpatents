Title of Invention

"SEAT DEVICE FOR A MOTORCYCLE"
Abstract [Object] To provide a seat device for a motorcycle in which an upper opening portion of a helmet containing box is opened/closed with a seat, which is capable of lowering the height of the seat, thereby improving the easy-riding when a rider gets on or off the motorcycle. [Solving Means] In a seat device for a motorcycle in which an upper opening portion of a helmet containing box is openably/closably covered with a seat 30, an opening portion 34 is provided in part of a bottom plate 31 of the seat, and a vertically movable plate 38 is provided in the opening portion 34 separately from the bottom plate 31 of the seat.
Full Text [Detailed Description of the Invention]
[0001]
[Technical Field of the Invention]
The present invention relates to a seat device for a motorcycle in which an opening portion of a helmet
containing box is opened/closed with a seat.
[0002]
[Related Art]
Conventionally is known, for example, from Japanese Utility Model Publication No. Hei 6-30627, a motorcycle having a structure in which a helmet containing box is provided under a seat and a helmet is accommodated or taken in or out of the helmet containing box by opening/closing the seat.
In this related art structure, the openable/ closable seat is arranged over an upper opening portion of the containing box and a foldable stopper plate is raised to support a cross-plate under the seat at a high position in the helmet containing state, whereby when a driver sits on the seat in the helmet containing state, the seat can be prevented from being sunk by the cross-plate.
[0003]
[Problem to be Solved by the Invention]
Incidentally, a helmet has a large dimension in the height direction. In particular, a helmet of a full-face type for covering the entire head is designed to cover from the vertex of the head to the neck, and therefore, it has a larger dimension in the height direction.
As a result, in a helmet containing box provided

under a seat, the dimension in the height direction tends to become larger. Accordingly, the height of the helmet containing box becomes larger, and thereby the height of the seat arranged on the helmet containing box inevitably becomes larger.
[0004]
On the contrary, in a scooter-type motorcycle, it is desirable to set a seat at a low position for enhancing the easy-riding when a driver is get on/off the motorcycle.
Meanwhile, for example, in a power unit swing type motorcycle, a vertically swingable power unit is arranged under a containing box, and accordingly, if a bottom portion of the containing box is set at a lower position, it becomes difficult to ensure the vertical swing stroke of the power unit. On the other hand, in relation to a helmet to be accommodated in a containing box, it is difficult to set the height of the containing box shorter.
Accordingly, in the case where a seat is provided in such a manner as to opening/closing an upper opening portion of a containing box, it is difficult to set the seat at a low position.
[0005]
The present invention has been made to solve the above-described problems.
An object of the present invention is to provide a seat device for a motorcycle in which a helmet containing box is opened/closed by a seat arranged over the helmet containing box, which is capable of reasonably solving the above-described inconsistent two requirements by making the height of the seat as low as possible for improving the easy-riding when a driver gets on or off the motorcycle (first requirement) while setting the position of the bottom of the containing box at the same height as that of the related art motorcycle in relation to equipment such as a swing type power unit arranged under the bottom of the containing box (second requirement).
[0006]
[Means for Solving the Problem]
To achieve the above object, according to an invention described in claim 1, there is provided a seat device for a motorcycle in which an upper opening portion of a helmet containing box is openably covered with a seat, characterized in that an opening portion is provided in part of a bottom plate of the seat, and a vertically movable plate is provided in the opening portion separately from the bottom plate of the seat.
[0007]
With the configuration of the invention described
in claim 1, when a helmet is accommodated in the containing box, only the movable plate provided separately from the bottom plate of the seat is moved upward to ensure a space for containing a helmet in height direction, with a result that it is possible to make the height of the seat lower.

According to an invention described in claim 2, in addition to the invention described in claim 1, the movable plate is configured such that the outer peripheral portion thereof is overlapped onto the inner peripheral edge of the opening portion provided in the bottom plate of the seat.
With the configuration of the invention described in claim 2, when a load of a driver sit on the seat is applied to the movable plate in a state in which the helmet is taken out of the containing box, such a load is supported by the bottom plate of the seat.
[0009]
According to an invention described in claim 3, in addition to the invention described in claim 1, a soft protective member is provided on the lower surface of the movable plate.

A disk-type movable plate 38, which is made from the same material as that of the bottom plate 31, is vertically movably arranged in the opening portion 34 separately from the bottom plate 31.
To be more specific, a peripheral portion 38a of the movable plate 38, which is formed into a stepped portion higher than the main portion of the movable plate 38, is placed on the rack portion 36 provided around the inner periphery of the opening portion 34 such that the
outer peripheral portion 38a of the movable plate 38 is overlapped to the inner peripheral portion of the opening portion 34 via the rack portion 36. The outside diameter of the movable plate 38 is set such that a gap is provided between the outer periphery of the movable plate 38 and the inner periphery of the guide portion 35.
[0019]
A radially intermediate portion of the movable plate 38 is slightly tilted upward toward the center of the movable plate 38, and has at its central portion a large-diameter circular hole portion 39. The inside diameter of the hole portion 39 is set such that part of the vertex of a helmet H accommodated in the containing box 21 provided thereunder is fitted into the hole portion 39. The reinforcing sheet 37 provided at the portion, corresponding to the opening portion 34, of the bottom of the cushion member 32 is placed on the movable plate 38.
A protective member 40 made from sponge rubber or the like is stuck on the lower surface of the movable plate 38. In the example shown in the figure, the ring-shaped protective member 40 is provided, on the lower surface of the movable plate 38, at the peripheral portion of the hole portion 39.
[0020]
Fig. 4 shows a state in which the helmet H is accommodated in the containing box 21 and the seat 30 as a lid body is closed. The opening portion 34 formed in the sheet bottom plate 31 and the movable plate 38 are arranged such that the vertex Hi of the helmet H accommodated in the containing box 21 as shown in Fig. 4 is positioned directly under the movable plate 38.
When the seat 30 is closed as shown in Figs. 4 and 5, that is, the helmet H is accommodated in the containing box 21, part of the vertex HI of the helmet H put in the containing box 21 is brought in contact with the movable plate 38, more specifically, the hole portion 39 of the movable plate 38. In other words, by closing the seat 30 from top to bottom, the movable plate 38 is brought in contact with the vertex HI of the helmet H placed under the movable plate 38, and is then lifted up. [0021]
The upward movement of the movable plate 38 by the pushing-up action of the vertex HI of the helmet H tends to occur, but is easily allowable by the deformation of the cushion member 32. The flow of intake or exhaust air of the cushion member 32 by the deformation of the cushion member 32 is smoothly performed because the reinforcing sheet 37 stuck on the cushion member 32 has permeability as
described above. As a result, the cushion member 32 is smoothly and easily deformed.
Also for the upward movement of the movable plate 38 as described above, the radial movement of the outer peripheral portion of the movable plate 38 is restricted by the guide portion 35, that is, the vertical movement is restricted for smooth operation. Thus, it prevents the movable plate 38 from freely moving within the horizontal plane in the opening portion 34, but is vertically movable at the fixed position.
[0022]
As a result, referring to Fig. 4, specifically, to Fig. 5, the movable plate 38 is lifted up by a specific height S by the contact with the vertex HI of the helmet H. In other words, even if the overall height of the seat 30 is made low by the height S, the helmet H can be accommodated in the containing box 21. That is to say, according to the present invention, the overall height of the seat 30 can be made lower by the height S.
Since the soft protective member 40 made from sponge rubber or the like is stuck on the lower surface of the movable plate 38, even if part of the vertex HI of the helmet H is slid in contact with the movable plate 38, it is protected by the protective member 40, so that the
surface of the helmet H is prevented from being damaged.
[0023]
Fig. 6 shows a state in which the helmet H is taken out of the containing box 21 and then the seat 30 is closed. The opening/closing of the seat 30 is performed, as described above, with the pivot 25 at the front end taken as a hinge.
The movable plate 38 is pushed down by the returning action of the cushion member 32 to the original shape, and it is held in a state in which the peripheral portion 38a is placed on the rack portion 36 at the peripheral portion of the opening portion 34 of the seat bottom plate 31.
When a driver is sit on the seat 30 in the state in which the helmet H is taken out of the containing box 21, a load of the driver is applied not only to the seat 30, but also to the movable plate 38.
[0024]
Since the movable plate 38 is configured such that the peripheral portion 38a is vertically overlapped on the rack portion 36 provided around the inner periphery of the opening portion 34 as shown in Figs. 3 and 6, the load of the driver applied to the movable plate 38 is supported by the sheet bottom plate 31.
Accordingly, although the movable plate 38 is provided in the opening portion 34 in such a manner as to be vertically movable, and allowed to move upward when the helmet H is accommodated in the containing box 21, it is possible to sufficiently ensure the function of the seat. [0025]

[Effect of the Invention]
The present invention having the above configuration exhibits the following effects:
According to the invention described in claim 1, there is provided a seat device for a motorcycle in which an upper opening portion of a helmet containing box is openably/closably covered with a seat, characterized in that an opening portion is provided in part of a bottom plate of the seat, and a vertically movable plate is

provided in the opening portion separately from the bottom plate of the seat. As a result, when part of the vertex of the helmet is positioned under the seat bottom plate in a state in which the helmet is accommodated in the containing box and the seat is closed, only the movable plate provided separately from the seat bottom plate is brought in contact with part of the vertex of the helmet and is moved upward.
Accordingly, the height of the seat bottom plate can be lowered to the extent that the vertex of the helmet interferes with the seat bottom plate and is sunk in the seat. As a result, since part of the helmet containing space in the height direction can be ensured over the seat bottom plate, the seat can be set at a lower position.
[0028]
With the configuration of the invention described in claim 1, therefore, the height of the seat of the scooter-type motorcycle is made as low as possible to improve the easy-riding, when a rider gets on or off the motorcycle, while the height of the bottom of the helmet containing box is set at the same required height as that of the related art structure to ensure the motion of the unit swing type power unit without changing the arrangement of equipment, to thereby ensure the function of the equipment.
[0029]
According to the invention described in claim 2, in addition to the invention described in claim 1, the movable plate is configured such that the outer peripheral portion (peripheral portion) thereof is overlapped onto the inner peripheral edge of the opening portion provided in the bottom plate of the seat. As a result, when a load of a driver sit on the seat is applied to the movable plate in a state in which the helmet is taken out of the containing box, such a load is supported by the bottom plate of the seat.
Accordingly, when a driver sits on the seat after the helmet is taken out of the containing box, the load of the driver is supported by the seat bottom plate and thereby the seat is prevented from being sunk. In this way, although the opening portion is provided in the seat bottom plate and the vertically movable plate is provided in the opening portion, it is possible to sufficiently ensure as much function of the seat as the prior art structure gives. [0030]
According to the invention described in claim 3, in addition to the invention described in claim 1, a soft protective member is provided on the lower surface of the
movable plate. As a result, in the state in which the helmet is accommodated in the containing box, the vertex of the helmet or its neighborhood is brought in contact with the movable plate via the protective member made from sponge rubber or the like provided on the lower surface of the movable plate.
Accordingly, when the vertex of the helmet is brought in contact with the movable plate, the surface of the helmet is prevented from being damaged by the protective member.
