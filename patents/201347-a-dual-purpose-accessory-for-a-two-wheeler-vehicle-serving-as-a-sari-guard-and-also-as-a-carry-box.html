Full Text This invention relates to a dual purpose accessory for a two wheeler vehicle serving as a sari guard and also as a carry-box.
The sari guard for two wheeler vehicles is known to the art. This device is used to prevent the sari of a woman pillion rider from getting entangled in the rear wheel of the vehicle when in motion.
An object of this invention is to utilise the space available adjacent the sari guard to provide a carry-box for carrying various articles, such as, a briefcase, a market-bag and so on, whenever there is no pillion rider on the vehicle. However, whenever a person desires to ride on the pillion, the carry-box can be rendered collapsible and put away by the side of the sari guard, flush with it, so that there is no inconvenience caused to the pillion rider.
As a corollary of the foregoing object of this invention, another object is to make the best of an existing accessory to furnish another accessory with .minor additions to the former accessory.

According to this invention, the dual purpose accessory for a two wheeler vehicle serving as a sari guard and also as a carry-box, comprises a first frame attachable to the side of the vehicle, at its rear, said first frame being provided with a footrest and a Bcreen serving as a sari guard; a second frame loaded by a spring and pivotably attached to the first frame, to enable the second frame to be folded, to lie flush against the first frame, and unfolded, transversely to the first frame, to an extent determined by a stopper, thus creating a carry-box structure defined by the first and second frames, for receiving within said structure, articles to be transported along with the vehicle, the said spring exerting a force on the second frame tending to bring it to its folded position, thereby also tending to keep any article in the said structure, which is hi contact with the second frame, securely in portion under such force.
