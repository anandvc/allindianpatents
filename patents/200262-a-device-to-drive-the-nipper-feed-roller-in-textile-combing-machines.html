Title of Invention

A DEVICE TO DRIVE THE NIPPER FEED ROLLER IN TEXTILE COMBING MACHINES
Abstract This Invention relates to a drive device for nipper feed rollers in a textile combing machines. An actuating means with multiple fulcrum points is provided. One end of the actuating means Is connected to the top nipper which is pivoted to the bottom nipper. The other end of the actuating means is connected to the feed gear through a drive gear. An internal ratchet and pawl arrangement is also provided which lies in the axle of the feed gear when the backward movement of the top nipper starts, the fulcrum of the actuating means is manually changed to reverse the feed roller movement
Full Text

This invention relates to a device to drive the nipper feed roller in textile combing machines.
In textile processing, combing operations are carried out to prepare cotton and wool fibres for spinning by separating and straightening the fibers. Combing also removes fibres below a specific length. Lap which is a rolled sheet of fibres produced at the delivery end of opening machinery is fed into nipper gripping plates at a predetermined length, through a feed roller. This feed roller is actuated by means of ratchet pawl arrangement and the top nipper movement. An external ratchet and pawl arrangement is used for feeding lap while the nipper moves forward towards the detaching rollers. This movement is termed the forward feed. An internal ratchet and pawl arrangement is used for feeding lap while the nipper moves away from the detaching rollers. This is termed as the backward feed. These operations are carried out manually and depend on the materials to be processed. It is evident that such manual operations reduce productivity. High speed operation of the machine also poses problems.
This invention seeks to solve the above discussed problems by developing a device for driving the nipper feed roller with the help of a actuating arrangement for positive drive and easy change over mechanism from forward to backward feed in the nipper feed roller.

In a combing machine, the top nipper is pivoted to the bottom nipper. The top nipper closes and opens as per the requirements based on the type of lap and fibre. The top nipper is driven by a driving gear which drives the feed gear in clockwise direction.
This invention provides an actuating means one end of which is pivoted to the top nipper and the other end to the driving gear. As the top nipper approaches the bottom nipper plate the drive gear is rotated to an anticlockwise direction by the rotation of drive gear. An internal ratchet and pawl arrangement connected in axis to the feed roller, rotates the feed roller in an anticlockwise direction resulting in feeding of predetermined length of the lap into the nipper plates. After a certain period, the top nipper moves away from the bottctfn nipper plate thereby making the actuating means to rotate the dirve gear in anticlockwise direction and to rotate the feed gear ions clockwise direction. The internal ratchet arrangement ships during this rotation to stop the movement of the feed roller.
