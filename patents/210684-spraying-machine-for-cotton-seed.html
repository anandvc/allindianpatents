Title of Invention

SPRAYING MACHINE FOR COTTON SEED
Abstract The Miniature Spraying System for Cottonseeds has been fabricated from mild steel and all other parts with stainless steel. The machine has a sturdy stand of mild steel with stainless steel top. A Hopper feeder, which can accommodate about 25 kg cottonseeds, is taken out from the bottom to an enclosed screw conveying system. A 3 HP geared motor is provided with a speed of 1440 rpm, the power of which is transmitted to the screw conveyor with an output of 38 rpm. Provision is there for an overhead tank, which can store about 10 liters of water or any solution. The solution is sprayed on to the cottonseeds with the help of automizers while passing through the conveyor at two places with the help of a pump. Both the motor and pump are operated with the help of a panel. The treated seeds are collected through a drain in a bin. It is possible to adjust the speed of the screw conveyor and also the rate of spary. The unit can be used for spraying any seed with chemicals, enzymes etc.

TECHANICAL FIELD
This invention relates to spraying system for cotton seeds, more particularly spraying system for cotton seeds
BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
Cottonseeds obtained during ginning of seed cotton are considered an important by-product.
The cottonseed contains about 18-25% oil depending on the quality of seed and the species. The oil is primarily seed as a medium for frying and for manufacture of hydrogenated vegetable products, cooking, salad dressings and for production of soap. This is one of the important edible oils and is much superior in its nutritional value to many of the traditional oils. As per an estimate, about 60 lakh tonnes of cottonseeds produced in India can yield approximately 10 lakh tonnes of oil. The nutritional value of cottonseed is around 9 kcal/g. the average digestibility is around 97% and could be compared with that of soybean, safflower and sunflower. The refined oil with practically no gossypol is pale yellow in color and rich in Vitamin-E and can be used directly as a cooking medium and also for the manufacture of Vanaspati. The keeping quality of oil is also quite good and is comparable with other edible oils.
Utilization of cottonseed oil for human consumption should receive immediate attention in our country for meeting, the shortage of edible oils. It contains more than 50% of poly - unsaturated fatty acids and is very ideal as a component in human diet. This oil is very popular in USA. However in India, it is being used to a very small extent. Therefore efforts are to be made on a war footing to popularise its use in our country which can result in stoppage of import of other edible oils.

The seeds being rich in oil and protein are not handled properly as it should be in ginneries. When they are stored in open, there is every possibility of microbial infestation particularly when the humidity is high. The aflatoxin producing fungi makes the meal unfit for consumption by animals and this also affects its exportability; There are many methods of preventing the microbial contamination and aflatoxin production. Propionic acid spray has been found to be very effective. A miniature spraying system has been developed in order to make a uniform spray on a bed of cottonseeds.
