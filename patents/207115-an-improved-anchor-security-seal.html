In an ongoing lookout for a low-cost, yet effective device, a security seal has been designed which is an improvement over and modification of the device described in the complete specification of my main Patent Application The device of the present invention is capable of being used as an effective seal to prevent pilferage, tampering and / or theft of water, gas, petrol, diesel and electric maters and also as a means for preventing adulteration of edible substances during storage, transit and / or distribution.
Heretofore mainly lead seals were used with G.I. wire to make facilities pilfer-proof. These seals normally comprised of two metal bodies having recesses in between for insertion of wire. Such wire was passed through bodies, installations or equipments which were to be sealed and in turn was allowed to be inserted through the recess provided between two sheets of metal bodies. On being pressed with an instrument bearing logo or insignia of the concern or company handling such matters, the seal was put on. Such sealing devices suffered from a number of drawbacks, e.g. the seal being essentially two soft and malleable metal bodies, one could easily force them open with the tip of a sharp instrument like knife and reset by applying pressure, without leaving discerning evidence of tampering. Soft and malleable metal bodies could be restored to their original position and such sequence of steps may be repeated many times over, particularly by people who have had access to the equipment for applying pressure with insignia or logo marked on the working lips thereof.
Apart from the foregoing, the conventional sealing means also posed a potential health hazard, being principally made from lead or lead-bearing alloy. The workmen handling such toxic material run the risk of suffering from lead poisoning.

The sealing wire means mentioned above may be selected from plastic coated ¦ galvanized steel (G.I.) wire, double-stand stainless steel or in-built plastic sealing thread, the last-named being sealingly attached to the bottom of the base of the said anchor-like body. Double strand or ribbed stainless steel wire ensures greater strength, resistance to atmospheric condition corrosion resistance and hence greater safety, since the wire cannot be drawn out even by applying force without damaging or destroying the sealed body.
Fabrication of the components parts may be done by moulding using suitable dies, and raw materials and machineries are indigenously available.
The component parts of the device other than metallic sealing means may be made from opaque or transparent plastic materials to which colour of different shades may be imparted by incorporating therein such as, for instance, blue, green, red, yellow, violet or any other suitable / desirable shade.
The anchor sealing means is designed for single use and once the anchor-like body is inserted in the capsule and pressed, it cannot be opened unless destroyed by a cutting device. In case of moulded end product with a transparent appearance, any instance of tampering will be easily detected.
Printing of logo and use of non-repetitive numerals make each seal uniquely non-vulnerable which cannot be tampered with or removed even by the erring employees of the service providers. However, serial numbering serves as a deterrent and it is virtually impossible to reproduce a seal with the same features and get-up.

For putting the anchor seal to use, the sealing wire is to be inserted through the hole or recess of the object to be secured. Next the sealing wire is routed down the channel on one side of the arrow and up the channel on the other side, followed by bending the link and inserting the anchor through the opening of the capsule. On being pushed firmly by pressing on the base of the anchor, there will be a 'click' signalling that the seal is locked upon insertion of the anchor, its legs or barbs expand and get permanently locked behind the inner stops provided on the narrow walls of
9
the capsule, and thereafter anchor cannot be pulled out. It is advisable to check that the seal is properly locked by pulling the handle provided below the main body of the capsule.
The anchor seal of the present invention has another notable feature which may be regarded a distinct improvement over the main Patent Application. In the later, steel wire was hermetically sealed with the capsule, whereas in the present instance the sealing wire is detachable and can be selected from different cross sections depending on the end use. Moreover, the user may exercise his choice in selecting the wire material and also the wire length according to necessity. As mentioned above, the wire is secured on the channel by passing one end of it through one of the holes, preferably the one adjacent the shorter barb or leg of the anchor, and bending it down the channel and passing it through the other hole after forming a loop or seal through the slot or recess provided on the body of the object to be secured. Repeated trails have established that after the anchor with sealing wire is pushed down inside the capsule, neither the wire nor the anchor can be pulled out without disturbing or destroying the seal.
The anchor seal of the present invention finds application in diverse areas, some of which are, inter alia, as denoted hereunder:
(i) electric meter, (ii) gas meter, (iii) taxi meter, (iv) water meter, (v) pay phones, (vi) ATM cassettes, (vii) cabinets, (viii) tankers, (ix) vehicle doors, (x) valves, (xi) locking rings, (xii) fibre drums, (xiii) zipper bags, (xiv) documents, (xv) milk cans, (xvi) demand meters and the like.
