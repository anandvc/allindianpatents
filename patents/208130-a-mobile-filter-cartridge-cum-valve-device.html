FIELD OF THE INVENTION
This invention relates to a water purifier. More particularly, the invention relates to a mobile filter cartridge cum valve device adaptable as water purifiers.
BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
Water purification devices are known in the art. However, most of these devices suffer from the drawbacks that the filtration cartridge is of fixed depth and it is made up of just two layers of a single composite layer. In the. prior art cartridges, one layer comprises component which is generally claimed to make the water odour-free but is used in quantity which is insufficient to achieve the claimed effect. The other layer comprises a component which is a bactericidal and often an iodine based compound and therefore not totally for human body if used for long duration. Further, balanced quantity of either components is normally not used to achieve the claimed results.
Further in order to make the water flow through the nozzle, in the inverted position, the device of the prior art has to be squeezed. When the pressure is released air flows into the bottle through the nozzle and the cartridge means. This makes the distribution of the chemicals containing in the layers non-uniform which phenomenon in fact stands in the way of using the required quantity particularly the chemicals being in granular form which contain as layer the bacterial medium. Thus, there remains a high probability of the bacteria in ambient air being sucked inside the device and disposed on the lower surface of the filter pad forming a lower boundary of the bactericidal layer. SUMMARY OF THE INVENTION
According to this invention there is provided a mobile filter cartridge-cum valve device adaptable as water purifier, comprising a bottle (8) having a lid cover (9); a filter cartridge means (1) releasable interposed in the lid cover (9), the lid cover (9) disposed on the top portion of the bottle (8) to encases the device, a valve means (11-18) Interposed in the middle portion of the bottle (8) to allow

inflow of ambient air inside tlie bottle, the valve means having an unidirectional flap (18) which allows exit of filtered water via an exit nozzle (19) upon squeezing of the bottle (8) in an inverted position characterized in that the filter-cartridge means (1) having a plurality of filtering layers (2-5) to purify the water Inputed in the device, and in that atleast top two layers (2, 3) of the plurality of filtering layers (2-5) comprises water-softening compound, the third layer having a compound to remove the odour, and the last layer (4) containing a bactericidal compound.
The device comprises a bottle made of compressible material, a lid cover to close the bottle, a cartridge having a plurality of layers, preferably four layer, being separated by filter pads and accommodated in the lid cover. The layers .are fitted one above the other along the inner wall of the cartridge. According to an exemplary embodiment, the number of layers is four, wherein the topmost layer contains a water softening compound such as for example an Anion resin used for demineralization of water. The second layer contains a water softening compound such as a cationic resin. The third layer contains a compound for making odour-free. This compound is an absorbent such as activated coconut charcoal. The fourth layer which is the most critical feature of the cartridge feature of the cartridge device contains a bactericidal compound. A typical bactericidal compound used is silver oxide, although the invention is not limited thereto.
The layers are supported on filter pads typically made from non-woven filter cloth, although not restricted thereto. The layers are supported by a wire mesh at the top of the first layer and below the last layer. Such a wire mesh is constructed from food grade plastic or any similar material. The valve means

provided at the inner wall of the bottom of the purifier device which allows water to flow out in the inverted position of the bottle without having to squeeze the latter and prevents back flow of water. An axial hole is disposed externally at an upper portion of the bottle to allow air-inflow when the bottle is squeezed invertedly.
A typical valve means comprises a tube having a horizontal and vertical section. The vertical section of tube has a flap disposed at an end portion. The opening of the flap is unidirectional and thus prevents the back flow of water.
The air flow into the purifier device through an extremity of the horizontal section of the valve which includes a plurality of washers and a tortoise-back shaped screw provided through an axial hole.
The screw is releasably coupled to the wall of the purifier device and secures the washers to the tube of the valve means.
