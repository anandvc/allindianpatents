Full Text This invention relates to a brush assembly of an electric machine, more particularly, though not exclusively, a brush assembly for automotive starter motors.
In the known automotive starter motor, brushes in contact with the commutator of the motor wear off during use. However, the brushes are not utilised to the extent that they could, because the connecting leads of the brushes interfere with the brush box and prevent the brushes from protruding farther out of the holders.
It is therefore the principal object of this invention to propose a brush assembly as aforesaid which permits the brushes to protrude farther beyond the holders than possible in the conventional design.
The invention proposed herein thus enhances the life of brushes to the extent of full use of the brushes up to the point of connection of their connecting leads, resulting in considerable saving of brush material and greater economy.

The brush assembly such as, in an automotive starter motor comprises
a base plate B on which a plurality of brush holders H are mounted.
The base plate B has a central hole A for receiving the commutator of the
said starter motor.
Each holder H is provided with first and second side slots S1 and S2.
The second side slot S2 receives a spring T for retaining the brush B in
position within the holder H and applying pressure on the brush.. The side
slot S2 is closed at the commutator end C.
Each holder H receives a brush B with its connecting lead L seated in the
first side slot SI.
In the known brush assembly, the side slot which receives the brush and
connecting lead is closed at the commutator end C. This construction
restricts the movement of the brush beyond the point of connection of the
brush to the connecting lead, since the movement of the said lead (and
therefore of the brush) would be restrained by the connecting lead fouling
against the closed end of the said side slot.
On the other hand, in this invention, the side slot S1 is open at the
commutator end C whereby the movement of the brush towards the

commutator is not restrained by the connecting lead and the brush is enabled to move out farther than the brush in the known brush assembly and thus be utilised up to a point close to the connecting lead (Fig. 3)

and being closed at the commutator end, each holder receiving a brush

which is closed at the commutator end, is open at its other end.
