Title of Invention

A NOVEL PRESSURE COOKER WITH REINFORCED SHORT LIP AND A PROCESS OF MAKING THE SAME
Abstract A novel pressure cooker having a reinforced short lip with a protrusion length from the inner body wall is in between 10 to 13mm and having thickness greater than the thickness of the input raw material. The enhanced density and strength of lip is achieved by drawing the material from the body side of the pressure cooker and adding the material at the lip before the lip formation stage.
Full Text This invention in general relates to household appliances. In particular this invention is concerned with pressure cooker. More particularly this invention relates to a novel construction of pressure cooker made of wrought Aluminium/Aluminium alloy. Especially this invention relates to a novel technology used in the process of manufacturing pressure cooker. Therefore this invention relates to a novel technology, a novel process and a novel product.
Aluminium pressure cookers already known are made from heavy gauge material with long lip, which tends to gradually deform or distort during usage leading to weakening of the mouth and early obsolescence of Pressure Cooker. Moreover some companies manufactured pressure cooker in thinner gauge material with long lip keeping the view of cost reduction which further increased the rate of defect frequently.
Pressure cooker (Fig.l) consists of a body (1), a lid (2), a gasket (3), pressure regulating device known as vent weight or weight valve (4), handle (5) and other safety features. The size of the body and the matching lid decide the capacity of the pressure cooker. The capacity indicates the inside volume of the pressure cooker when measured in the closed condition.
While using the pressure cooker for cooking purpose, water and the food material to be cooked are kept inside and put on a stove, so that the heat is applied to the base The steam generated inside is not allowed to escape until the required steam pressure is developed. The excess pressure is controlled by the pressure regulating device by letting it out and closing. Due to the steam under pressure the food gets cooked faster than open vessel cooking. The pressure developed inside acts on all the sides and would fend to separate the fid from the bottom. The lid is held in its position until the lip of the bottom or locking Up of the lid or both give way. The maximum pressure held by the pressure cooker depends on the strength of the locking faces, referred as bursting strengh of the Pressure Cooker.

From the study of our R & D all conventional Aluminium pressure cookers including our own brand have long lip (Fig.2) generally 15 +/- i 0mn with thickness as per the table below.

The long lip thickness is nearly equivalent to the thickness of raw material used to produce the Pressure Cooker torn wrought aluminium disc. In these kinds of pressure cookers the long lip generally tend to deform in long usage as illustrated in Fig, 3. Ultimately there could be the chance of reduction in bursting strength as specified by Bureau of Indian Standards through IS2347 which stands for safety of consumer. So this type of Conventional Pressure Cooker may
1. Reduce the Span life of usage of Pressure Cooker.
2. Lead to a potential danger to the user after long usage.
OBJECT OF THE INVENTION
The object of the invention is to identify the problem associated with the Pressure Cooker of long lip and invent a novel pressure cooker with reinforced short lip, which will not have above drawbacks.
The primary object of the invention accordingly is to overcome such defect in pressure cooker with long lip made out of Heavier/Thinner gauge raw material and construct a novel pressure cooker with reinforced short lip by usage of least input material with lower gauge thickness-
Another object of the invention is to invent a novel method of maufacturing pressure cooker with reinforced short lip.

Another object of invention is to invent a pressure cooker with reinforced short lip, which is sturdy and economical

TEST PROCEDURE OF PRESSURE COOKER TO ASSESS BURST STRENGTH IN ACCORDANCE TO rS.2347 :1995.
The cooker shall be coupled to a hydraulic test pump provided with a Calibrated pressure gauge, suitably graduated. AH the remaining openings in the body and lid of the cooker shall be suitably sealed.
A gradually increasing hydraulic pressure shall be applied to the vessel of the cooker until:
a) a rupture takes place, or
b) a deformation occurs so that an appreciable leakage takes place at the joint of the lid or else where.
The maximum gauge pressure obtained shall be taken as the bursting pressure of the cooker for the purpose of this standard IS 2347, which is 6 Kg. per square cm.
