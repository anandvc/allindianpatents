A PROCESS FOR THE PREPARATION OF 1,4,7,10-TETRAAZACYCLODODECANE-1-ACETIC ACID
Abstract The process for the preparation of 1,4, 7, 10-tetraazacyclododecane-l acetic acid of formula V comprising hydrolysing 1,4,7,10-tetraazabicyclo (8.2.2) tetradecane-2-one of formula I In basic conditions in a basic acqueous solution at a temperature from 60° to 100°C and at a pH higher than 12 in a known manner and recovering said compound of formula V from the reaction mixture in a known manner.

Contrary to what described in literature by Weisman (Tetrahedron Letters, 1980, 21, 33 5) and by Kolinski (Tetrahedron Letters, 1981, 22, 2217) , compound (II) is highly reactive also in hydrolytic conditions.
The conversion of (II) to (I) involves heating (II) in aqueous solution at temperatures from 80 to 100°C, at pH slightly acidic, neutral or slightly basic (pH 5-9), and in the presence of piperazine, added in amounts

ranging from 2 to 20 mol/mol of (II).
At the end of the reaction, the mixture is alkalinized, concentrated to dryness and extracted with toluene. The toluene solution is partially concentrated and cooled to separate piperazine, which is filtered and can be purified and recycled.
Toluene mother liquors are further concentrated, to obtain (I), which can be recrystallized, for example, from toluene.
Alternatively, at the end of the reaction, the inorganic anions can be removed by ion-exchange. Eluates are suitably concentrated and added with orthophosphoric acid to pH 6.5, thereby precipitating piperazine H3PO4 salt. After filtration, the residual phosphate ions are removed from mother liquors by a second ion-exchange, eluates are concentrated to a residue which is recrystallized from toluene, to obtain (I) at high purity.
