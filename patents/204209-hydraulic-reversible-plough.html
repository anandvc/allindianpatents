HYDRAULIC REVERSIBLE PLOUGH
The invention relates to a reversible plough having a hydraulic turning cylinder engaging with the swivel and the plough turret and having pivotal device for raising the plough frame end. The conventional system of plough is operated manually by levers which gets clugged due to wet mud during ploughing and therefore it is difficult to turn the plough due to the heavy weight of the plough as well as insufficient force applied by the lever. Therefore, the objective of the ploughing system is defeated.
Further due to the undue vibrations/jurk the hydraulic linkage of the tractor is impaired and thereby frequent replacement of the link rod due to the wear and tear is inevitable. Thus maintenance cost increases. Therefore, the prior art device has became unsatisfactory . The inventor has carried out lot of alterations to overcome the above defects and come out with an improved reversible plough with hydraulic turning cylinder which obviateSthe aforesaid drawbacks.
The principle object of this inventor is therefore to provide a reversible plough having a hydraulic turning cylinder which can operate efficiently.

Still further object of this invention is to provide a reversible plough having a hydraulic turning cylinder which effectively plough the field with minimum maintenance thereby cost effective.
Accordingly there is provided a reversible plough comprising a plough head carrying hemessing means and a re'ar carriage carrying the movable parts wherein the plough head carries a fix ring inside which pivots a movable ring fixly connected to the rear carriage.

1. A hydraulic reversible plough comprising a head stock 1 to be attached with a tractor by means of linkages 12, 13 and 14 wherein a double acting hydraulic cylinder 4 with hydraulic valve system 5 is connected to a shaft 3 rotably mounted inside a chassis 2, the other end of the shaft is connected to the plough member 15 consisting of fixing two reversible plough member mounted diametrically opposite to each other on a framing 16 fixed to the rotating shaft 3; two stopper 8 and 9 are positioned on either side of the hydraulic cylinder having tow swivel joints 6 and 7 so as to convert the vertical to and fro movement of the double acting cylinder into rotatary movement of the shaft thereby providing 180 movement of the reversible plough.
