Title of Invention

A METHOD AND APPARATUS FOR TANDEM SUBMERGED ARC WELDING USING ONE WIRE FEEDER
Abstract This invention relates to an improved apparatus for performing tandem submerged arc welding, comprising; a single wire feeder (2) capable of simultaneously feeding two wires; at least two spools (1a, 1b) feeding the wires into two torches (3A, 3B), the spools (1a, 1b) being driven by a wire-feed motor (2); a control box (9) controlling the wire - feed motor (2); at least two power sources (10,11) respectively providing via the first terminals to the first and second torches (3A, 3B); a job (12) connectable to the second terminals of the power sources (10, 11); the wires fed to the two torches (3A, 3B) coming-out of each contact tip (4) to create an arc (5) under flux blanket (6) to form a single weld pool (7).

FIELD OF INVENTION
This invention relates to an improved apparatus with a single wire feeder for
Tandem Submerged arc welding of steel plates.
BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
In submerged arc welding (SAW), the arc is hidden by a blanket of granular flux.
Normally a single wire is used as filler material. It is also possible to use two or
more wires to increase productivity. When two wires are used (Tandem
Submerged Arc Welding), one wire is connected to a DC power supply and the
other wire is connected to AC power supply. Alternatively, both wires are
connected to AC power source. Hence, there will be two power supplies, two
wire feeders and two torches employed in tandem Submerged arc welding
process. In this case two wire feeders independently controlled through separate
controls have to be employed. Conventional tandem submerged arc welding
requires welding current (wire feed speed) and arc voltage to be pre-set
independently to achieve the desired bead geometry. However, the prior art
systems are not capable to operate with one feeder and associated controls with
a synchronized single point control for the wire feeders. Thus, automatic
selection of welding current from the second power source is not feasible in the
prior art system.
US patent US patent US 7166818 discloses a Process development for making
Tandem submerged arc welding.

According to the cited invention, an electric arc welding apparatus comprising at
least a first consumable electrode and a second consumable electrode movable
in unison along a welding path between the edges of two adjacent, mutually
grounded plates, a first power supply for passing a first welding current at a first
low frequency between the first electrode and the two plates, a second power
supply for passing a second welding current at a second low frequency between
the second electrode and the two plates, where each of the power supplies
includes a three phase voltage input operated at line frequency, a rectifier to
convert the input voltage to a DC voltage link and a high frequency switching
type inverter converting the DC voltage link to a high frequency AC current, an
output rectifier circuit to provide a positive voltage terminal and a negative
voltage terminal, and an output switching network operated at a given low
frequency for directing a pulsating welding current at the given low frequency
from the two terminals across one of the electrodes and the plates, and a circuit
for independently adjusting the given low frequency so the value of the first low
frequency of the first power supply is different from the second low frequency of
the second power supply.
Further, US patent US 7220940 discloses a Process development for system for
welding with multiple arcs.
This invention describes a system for welding with a first and second arc

OBJECTS OF INVENTION
An object of the present invention is to propose an improved apparatus with a
single wire feeder, for tandem submerged arc welding of steel plates, which can
drive two separate filler wires at the same speed.
Another object of this invention is to propose an improved apparatus with a
single wire feeder, for tandem submerged arc welding of steel plates, which
enables reduction in the number of parameters required to be set.
Yet another object of the invention is to propose an improved apparatus with a
single wire feeder, for tandem submerged arc welding of steel plates, which both
power sources is capable of delivering same level of current.
A still another object of the invention is to propose an improved apparatus with a
single wire feeder, for tandem submerged arc welding of steel plates, which can
achieve the same filler metal deposition rate from both wires.

SUMMARY OF INVENTION
Accordingly, there is provided an improved apparatus for performing tandem
submerged arc welding, comprising a single wire feeder capable simultaneously
feeding two wires; at least two spools feeding the wires into two torches, the
spools being driven by a wire-feed motor; a control box controlling the wire -
feed motor; at least two power sources respectively providing via the first
terminals to the first and second torches; a job connectable to the second
terminals of the power sources; the wires fed to the two torches coming-out of
each contact tip to create an arc under flux blanket to form a single weld pool.
DETAIL DESCRIPTION OF THE INVENTION
As shown in figure - 1, the apparatus consists of one wire feeder, which feeds
two wires required for tandem submerged arc welding simultaneously. Wires

6
from two spools (la, lb) are fed by the wire feed motor (2) in to the torches 3A
and 3B. The wires come out of contact tips (4) to create an arc (5) under flux
blanket (6) to form single weld pool (7). The wire feeder (2) is driven by a motor
(8) which is controlled by a control box (9). with the power drawn from power
source (10). One of the torches 3A is connected to a Direct Current power source
(10), while the other torch, 3B is connected to an AC power source, (11).
Alternately, both the welding power sources can be AC type. The other terminal
of the power source is connected to the job (12). The torches are isolated from
each other electrically.
