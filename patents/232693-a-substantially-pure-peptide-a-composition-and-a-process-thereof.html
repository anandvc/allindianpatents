Field of invention
The present invention pertains to the filed of pharmacologically useful compounds that modulate sodium channels.
Background
Conotoxirts, a group of pharmacologically active peptides produced by diverse species of Canus snails act with a high degree of specificity on different classes of channels and receptors in excitable cells (Myers, R.A., Cruz, L.J., Rivier, J.E. and OLivcra, B.M. (1993) Chem. Rev. 93, 1923-1936, Olivers, B.M, Rivicr, J., Claris C. Ramilo, C.A., Corpus, G.P., Abogadie, KC, Mena. E.E., Woodward, S.R., Hillyard, D.R. and Cruz, L.J. (1990) Science 249, 257-263). The evolution of conotoxins in the venom of predator snails may be influenced by selective pressures imposed by the nature of the prey, with peptide mixtures from molluscivorous, piscivorous and vermivorous snails exhibiting differences (Olivers, B.M. (1997) Mol. Bio], Cell 8, 2101-2109). Systematic elucidation of structure-activity relationships for all components in a conotoxin mixture is impeded by the difficulties in isolating and identifying every individual peptide. Conoioxins are characterized by multiple disulfide bridges which provide a relatively rigid peplide backbone framework, upon which amino acid side chains, important for interaction with the pharmacological receptors, are arrayed (Wakamatsu, K., Kohda, D., Hatanaka, H., Lancelin, J.M., lshi- da, Y., Oya, M., Nakamura, H., Inagaki, F. and Sato, K. (1992) Biochemistry 31, 12577-12584). The classification of conotoxins has relied on the distribution of Cys residues in the primary sequence, the nature of the disulfide pairing topology and the functional attributes of the peptides {Mclntosh, J.M., Olivcra, B.M. and Cruz, L.J. (1999) Methods Enzymol. 294, 605-624; Gray, W.R. and Olivera, B.M. (1998)

Annu. Rev. Uiochem. 57, 665-700). As many as 14 classes of conotoxins have thus far been identified (α ,αA, δ,ε,γ,k1, λ1,λ/x,μ,μO,p, σ,ω and ψ). The δ-conotoxins have been shown to inhibil voltage-gatedμ Na+ channel inactivaiion. The specific role of the peptide K PVIA in combination with a K+ channel antagonist K PVIIA has been shown to be critical for prey capture in the fish-hunting snail, Conus purpurascens. Peptide combinations (cabals), which act in concert al distinct target sites, have been suggested to be important in rapid immobilization of prey (Tcrlau, H., Shon, KJ., Griilcy. M., Stockcr. M, Summer, W. and Olivera, B.M. (1996) Nature 38 U 148-151). The δ-conotoxins identified thus far have polypeptide cliain lengilis of 27-32 arnino acids and have three disulfide bridges with a pattern (1-4; 2-5; 3-6), where 1 to 6 indicates the six Cys residues starting from the N- terminus. The only other class of conotoxins characterized thus far that target Na+ channels are the δ-conotoxins, which share a similar disulfide-bonding pattern, but have a relatively shorter polypcptidc chain length of 17-22 amino acids. The isolation of δ-conotoxins from complex mixtures is rendered difficult due to their hydrophobicity.
