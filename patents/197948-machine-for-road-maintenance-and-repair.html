Title of Invention

MACHINE FOR ROAD MAINTENANCE AND REPAIR
Abstract ABSTRACT The invention in general related to a new mechanized hydro and pneumatic machine for maintenance and re co-conditioning work of concrete and bitumen roads. This machine is a four wheel mobile mechanical equipment with 2/4 wheel drive powered by 16 HP/20 HP diesel engines for achieving mobility and comprising steering system for steering feasibility, electrical equipment for activating and controlling the units, Hydraulic system for operating the front and rear buckets, pneumatic system for compressor for cleaning of pot holes and other accessories including tar boiler and tools etc. In the process of repair work, the machine is driven to the spot where the pot hole is identified and then pothole trimming is done by using pneumatic chipper and the pit is leaved out clean by pneumatic blower. Then the flame torch is used to dry the pit surface and mix is filled in the pit at the right temperature and then seal coat is filled and compacted well using pneumatic rammer and then the debris is removed and dumped in the debris bucket. Thus this one machine can carry out a perfect road reconditioning work,

This invention in general relates to a machine for maintenance and repair of pot holes found in concrete and bitumen roads.
The transportation system depend mainly on good vehicles and good roads. Good roads play a vital role in enhancing and improving the transportation system, The criteria is not just ensuring laying of well designed and good quality roads, but it largely depends on the pattern and quality of continuous maintenance and improvement relevant to the traffic load and other environmental factors.
The wear and tear on the road surface is natural and inevitable. The damage is mainly on the top surface of the roads. The damage leads to uneven surface of the roads and pot holes on the road.
The main causes of the damage done to the road are;-
a. Due to excessive load on the road - which leads to cracks on the
surfaces,
b. Due to poor construction of the road Itself i.e. poor quality raw materials
and poor process of laying.
c. Due to debonding of top layer fi-om the bottom bearing layer.
d. Due to failure of the top bitumen mix layer.
e. Due to seepage of water underneath.
Hazards due to the existence of damaged roads and pot holes
a. High Accident rate due to the existence of potholes and uneven road
surfaces.
b. Mechanical damage to the vehicles - uneven wear and tear of the tyres,
c. Uncomfortable travel for the passengers due to jolting and random
braking.
d. Hampers traffic movement.

In earlier days length of road was less, the quality of workmanship was better, vehicles using the road, the number, speed and laden weight etc., were less, the quality of maintenance was better, thus potholes never reached a dangerous level of attention in the past.
Nowadays, the length of road has increased, the quality of workman ship has decreased, the number of vehicles using the roads have increased and also the vehicle load carrying capacity and speed, thus leading to failure of road and more potholes and with less and improper maintenance it has reached a dangerous level of national debate and attention.
How it was prepared in the past, present and by the new system are being explained briefly below;

Z. r>OTHOLE TRIMMING
a) Earlier, In the manual system, the pothole pits used to be properly trimmed and cleaned.
b) Nowadays. In the manual system this is not being done and just some filling material of WBM or bitumen mix is filled In and Ijeft to the vehicles passing over to do the job of consolidation.
c) In our mechanical system, the pothole side surface and the bottom will be properly chipped with mechanical tools, brought to some proper geometric shape and made fit to receive further stages of work.
II. PIT CLEANING
a) In the earlier manual system, the pits were broomed and cleaned and the debris removed.
b) Presently, In the manual system, the same Is not being done due to lac) c) In our mechanical system the surface will be cleaned using cleaning brushes and the dust particles are removed using Pneumatic air blower, thus making the surface absolutely dean,
III. PIT'S SURFACE PREPARATION
a) In the earlier manual system, if the surface was wet there was no way to make it dry. Further, since bitumen will not stick to a wet surface, mud and jelly etc, will be filled and allowed to get compacted with vehicle movement.
b) Presently, in the manual system, cold bitumen mix is filled in pits and allowed to get consolidated with vehicle movement. Further this will not stick to the surface due to the present of moisture.

c) In our mechanical system, the surface shall be dried using flame torch,
heated and dried and brought to the perfect condition required for bonding of new bitumen/tack coat.
IV. PIT FILLING MATERIAL
a) In the older days, in the manual system, jelly and bitumen used to be mixed, heated to a warm condition, filled in pits and tamped to some level surface.
b) Presently, in the manual system, warm/cold asphalt mix Is filled and left for passing vehicle to do the job of consolidation. The warm/cold mix asphalt performances will not be as good as hot mix asphalt and will not stick to the old surface.
c) In our mechanical system, hot asphalt mix is carried to site and kept warm in the bucket. This shall be filled in the pit, heated with a flame torch and compacted using pneumatic rammers which does a perfect job, reaching compaction level similar to a Road Roller passing over the surface. The top surface can further be sealed using seal coat mix laid, heated and tamped mechanically, llius the top surface is well sealed.
