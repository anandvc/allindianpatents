Background of the Invention
Several cytotoxic drugs such as, e.g. fluorouracil (5-FU), doxorubicin and caraptothecins, cause damage DNA or affect cellular metabolic pathways and thus cause many cases, an indirect block of the cell cycle. Therefore, by producing an irreversible damage to both nonnal and tumor cells, these agents result in a significant toxicity and s effects.
In this respect, compounds capable of being highly specific antitumor agents by selectively leading to tumor cell arrest and apoptosis, with comparable efficacy but redu toxicity than the cuirentiy available drugs, are desirable,
It is well known that progression through the cell cycle is governed by a series o checkpoint controls, otherwise refened to as restriction points, which are regulated by a family of enzymes known as the cycUn-dependent kinases (cdk).
In their turn, the cdks themselves are regulated at many levels such as, for instan binding to cycUns.
The coordinated activation and inactivation of different cyclin/cdk complexes is necessary for noimal progression through the cell cycle. Both the critical Gl-S and G2-J transitions are controlled by the activation of different cyclin/cdk activities. In Gl, both cyclin D/cdk4 and cyclin E/cdk2 are thought to mediate the onset of S-phase. Progressic through S-phasc requires the activity of cyclin A7cdk2 whereas the activation of cyclin

A/cdc2 (cdkl) and cyclin B/cdc2 are required for the onset of metaphases. For a general reference to cyclins and cyclin-dependent kinases see, for example, Kevin R. Webster et al. in Exp. Opin. Invest. Drugs, 1998, Vol, 7(6), 865-887.
Checkpoint controls are defective in tumor cells due, in part, to disreguiation of cdk activity. For example, altered expression of cyclin E and cdk"s has been observed in tumor cells, and deletion of the cdk inhibitor p27 KIP gene in mice has been shown to result in a higher incidence of cancer.
Increasing evidence supports the idea thai the cdks are rate-limiting enzymes in cell cycle progression and, as such, represent molecular targets for therapeutic intervention. In particular, the direct inhibition of cdk/cyclin kinase activity should be helpfiil in restricting the unregulated proliferation of a tumor cell,
