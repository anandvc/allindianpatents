FOLDABLE TWO-WHEEL VEHICLE
Abstract A seat 13 capable of opening and closing an opening portion of a storange net is disposed at an upper portion of the strong net, a stopper release lever for opening and closing the seat 13 is provided at a front portion of the seat 13, a handle post 23 and a handle 17 are disposed on the front side of the stopper release lever so as to be foldable to the rear side, and the handle post 23 is provided with a main switch for locking in a folded condition, whereby the handle post 23 and the handle 17 folded to cover a front portion of the stopper release lever are locked with the main switch to thereby make the stopper release laever unoperatable. When the front portion of the seat is covered with the handle means, the seat cannot be opened or closed, prevention of mischief upon the stronger net under the seat can be contrived, the two-wheel vehicle can be made compact, and since the wheels are not locked, the two-wheel vehicle can be moved and transported.

[Detailed Description of the Invention]
[0001]
[Technical Field to which the Invention pertains]
The present invention relates to a foldable two-wheel vehicle which can be moved or transported even in a folded condition, for example, in the case of being
mounted on a vehicle, which is contrived to be compact,
and which is provided with a measure against theft and
mischief.
[0002]
As a two-wheel vehicle in which a handle can be
folded, there is known, for example, Japanese Patent
Laid-open No. Hei 10-129556 "Antitheft Bicycle".
FIG. 2 of the above publication shows an antitheft
bicycle in which a handle 1 (Symbols described in the
publication are used here as they are) is folded and
fixed between spokes 2, 2 of the front wheel, and a lock
3 for preventing the handle 1 from being returned to its
original condition is provided.
[0003]
[Problems to be Solved by the Invention]]
When the handle 1 is folded and fixed between the
spokes 2, 2 of the front wheel as mentioned above, it is
difficult to move or transport the bicycle in this
condition. However, in the case of transporting such a
bicycle by mounting it on a four-wheel vehicle or the
like, the bicycle must be put into the four-wheel vehicle
by, for example, lifting it up; therefore, a further
improvement is required.
In addition, in the case of mounting such a bicycle


or other two-wheel vehicle on a vehicle, compactness is demanded for effectively utilize a limited space.
Furthermore, it is desired that the two-wheel vehicle is provided with a contrivance for preventing mischief or the like.
[0004]
Accordingly, it is an object of the present invention to enable a foldable two-wheel vehicle to be moved or transported even in a folded condition in the case of, for example, mounting it on a vehicle, to contrive compactness, and to provide a contrivance for, for example, protection of a luggage container under the seat.
[0005]
[Means for Solving the Problems]
In order to attain the above object, claim 1 is characterized in that a seat capable of opening and closing an opening portion of a storage portion is disposed at an upper portion of the storage portion, a seat opening/closing lever for opening and closing the seat is provided at a front portion of the seat, handle means is provided on the front side of the seat opening/closing lever so as to be foldable to the rear side, and the handle means is provided with lock means


for locking the handle means in a folded condition, whereby the handle means folded so as to cover a front portion of the seat opening/closing lever is locked with the lock means so that the seat opening/closing lever cannot be operated. [0006]
In the condition where the two-wheel vehicle with the handle folded is mounted on a four-wheel vehicle, the seat cannot be opened or closed, so that protection of luggage disposed under the seat can be contrived. Further, a lock is not required for opening or closing the seat in a riding condition, and the operations of opening and closing the seat are very easy.
Besides, the two-wheel vehicle can be made compact by folding the handle means. Further, since the wheels are not locked, the two-wheel vehicle can be moved and transported. [0007]
Claim 2 is characterized in that the seat is fitted to a vehicle body so as to be slidable to the front and rear sides, and the seat can be held by a holding mechanism at a most front side position where the seat is slidden to the most front side, a riding position provided on the rear side of the most front side position


for the rider to ride at the time of running, and an open position where the seat is moved to the most rear side so as to fully open the opening portion of the storage portion. [0008]
By sliding the seat to the most front side position, the two-wheel vehicle with the seat opened can be made compact, and by sliding the seat to the open position, the opening portion of the storage portion can be made large, resulting in that luggage can be easily put in and taken out. In addition, since the seat is of a slide type, opening and closing of the seat and putting luggage into and out of the storage portion can be performed where the rider is not seated on the seat but standing astride the seat, so that convenience in use can be enhanced. [0009]
Claim 3 is characterized in that a front portion of the seat opening/closing lever is covered at the most front side position.
The foldable two-wheel vehicle can be made compact, and the luggage under the seat can be protected. [0010]
Claim 4 is characterized in that the seat opening/closing lever is disposed at a front portion of

the seat and on the lower side of a seat bottom plate, whereby a hand cannot be inserted to the lower side of the seat bottom plate in the condition where the handle means is folded.
The seat opening/closing lever can be made unoperatable, with a simple constitution. [0011]
Claim 5 is characterized in that a tail lamp is integrally fitted to a rear portion of the seat.
Since the tail lamp can be slidden forward together with the seat, the seat and the tail lamp are brought near to the center of the vehicle body, and compactness of the two-wheel vehicle can be further contrived. [0012]
Claim 6 is characterized in that the handle means includes a left-right pair of handle bar portions foldable in the vehicle width direction, and a handle post portion for supporting the handle bar portions, and in the condition where the handle bar portions and the handle post portion are folded, the front side of the seat is closed in a roughly U-shaped form with an upper portion of the handle post portion and the left and right handle bar portions.
The front side of the seat can be covered with the

handle post portion without a crevice and the handle bar portions, so that protection of the luggage under the seat becomes more favorable. [0013]
Claim 7 is characterized in that the lock means functions also as switch means for turning ON and OFF a power source of the vehicle.
With the lock means functioning also as the switch means, the number of component parts can be reduced as compared with, for example, the case where switch means is specially provided independently of the lock means; therefore, parts cost and assembly cost can be suppressed. In addition, since the power source would not be turned ON at the folded position, useability with carefree manner is ensured. [0014]
Claim 8 is characterized in that the lock means is provided on the front side of the handle means and in the vicinity of a folding shaft for folding the handle means to the rear side of the vehicle body.
Since the distance between the lock means and the folding shaft is short, the lock means and the folding shaft or the vicinity thereof can be easily connected to each other, and the structure of the lock means can be


made simple. In addition, since the lock is located on the front side of the handle means, a series of operations of, for example, unlocking and returning the handle means to its original position can be easily carried out. [0015]
Claim 9 is characterized in that, in the two-wheel vehicle according to claim 5, a front wheel support member for supporting a front wheel is fitted to the vehicle body so as to be foldable to the rear side, and a rear wheel support member for supporting a rear wheel is fitted to the vehicle body so as to be foldable to the front side.
In addition to the features that the handle means is foldable to the rear side and the seat is slidable to the front and rear sides, the front wheel support member is foldable to the rear side and the rear wheel support member is foldable to the front side, whereby more compactness of the two-wheel vehicle can be contrived, and the two-wheel vehicle becomes extremely favorable for being mounted on a four-wheel vehicle.
