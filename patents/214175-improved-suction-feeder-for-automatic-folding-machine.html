An Improved Suction Feeder for Automatic Folding Machine
The invention relates to an Improved Suction Feeder for Automatic Folding Machine. More particularly the invention relates to the feeding of the bottom most sheets without any obstruction automatically in the folding machine without any interruption.
The conventional machine, available where the stack of paper being feed in the folding machine by means of suction arrangement wherein the lifting & feeding the bottom most sheet was not precisely done & therefore, are some operational problems & the smooth functioning of the machine was affected. The initial flow of the bottom most sheet was delayed & therefore, not up to the satisfaction of the customer, therefore the present inventor has invented machine by incorporating a mechanism to detect the presence of the bottom most sheet & lifting the stack of paper when feeding the bottom most paper without any obstruction.
The machine operates through the controller & interface where initial data is fed. During the feeding operation the encoder senses the travel of sheets & sends the data of sheet length, gap length & suction length to the controller which is compared by a comparator & then operates the suction valve for the smooth functioning of machine.
The principal object of the improved suction feeder for automatic folding machine to provide an accurate feeding of sheets to the folding machine.

An improved feeder uses a solenoid valve (6) , uses a sheet presence sensor (7) mounted in the direction of the travel of paper, an encoder / transmitter device (8) /which senses, the distance traveled by the sheet, a controller (9) to decide timings required to control the operation of suction line of the feeder as explained in fig. (2) The data related to sheet length (17), suction length (18), and gap length (19) is entered into controller (ref. Figure 3). Encoder / sheet travel sensor (8) informs the controller about travel of sheet. The sheet presence sensor (7) informs that sheet suction has been affected and sheet moving in the direction of folding station has traveled a fix distance between stopper and sensor (20). A program decided in the controller gives the control outputs to solenoid valve to control the timing of valve to achieve controlled suction.
The reservoir (10) used in the suction line builds the vacuum in the reservoir tank when solenoid valve is off. Thus better utilizing the suction pump (11) capacity. This also improves the amount of immediate suction air flow, when valve is opened. This gives better suction over traditional suction arrangements made earlier. The suction affected with this arrangement ensures feeding.

Feeding cycle: (Ref figure 5) initially the valve is opened to start suction and bottommost sheet is fed. A sheet sensed by the sensor mounted in the path ensures that suction is affected and sheet has started traveling in direction of fold station and is already on conveyor belt (13). At this point the distance measurement starts (22). Fixed distance (20) between stopper band sensor is subtracted from suction length. After the length corresponding to the suction length (18) is traveled, valve is closed. Balance sheet length and gap length (21) is then measured to start the new cycle again.
An additional blower nozzle called air pressure nozzle (1) is provided from inside the front stopper as shown in figure (1). This blower blows the air on to the front end of paper stack (4,5) exactly above the area where rotating vacuum drum (2) sucks the bottom most paper (3). The blowing of air pushes the bottom most papers (4) of the stack towards rotating drum and at the same time lifts the upper papers (5) in the stack of paper. This facility improves the adhesion of bottom most paper of the stack to rotating drum, thus enabling easy removal of bottom sheet of stack with rotation of drum.
