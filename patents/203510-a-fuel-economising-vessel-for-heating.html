A FUEL ECONOMISING VESSEL FOR HEATING

This inventtoo relates to a fuel economisiag vessel for heating various substances, iuchiding fluids, solids and semi-solids, more particularly, though not exclusively, food substances. The said vessel will be provided with a lid.
In one case the lid will be such as to cover the vessel tightly and enable steam pressure to build up within the vessel. In such a case the lid will be provided widi die usual pressure release valve. The vessel m this cfuie will serve as a pressure cooker.
In another case, the lid of the vessel will serve to contain the steam witiiin the vessel. The steam pressure in such a case will not be as high as that in a pressure cooker, but will still be sufficient for steam heating or cooking purposes.
Known vessels used for steam heating various substances, such as those indicated above, generally suffer from the drawback that a sttbstffiitial amount of the heat energy applied is wasted.
For instance, in the known vessel used for steam cooking food, the bottom portion of ihe vessel gets heated by a heat source. The heat absorbed by the said bottom portion is conveyed, by conduction, to the sides of the vessel, and to the inside top portion of the vessel. Such heat conducted to the sides, is ultimately wasted by radiation from the sides of the vessel to atmosphere. If such conduction of heat can be prevented, then, to that extent there is substantial saving of heat energy.
