Title of Invention

"A PROCESS FOR PREPARATION OF AN ENVIRONMENTALLY SAFE SMOKE COMPOSITION"
Abstract This invention relates to a process for preparation of an environmentally safe smoke composition. The composition produces extremely low toxicity smoke. According to the process a binder is prepared by mixing a polyester resin, an accelerator and a catalyst. Pyrotechnic smoke composition is prepared by mixing ammonium chloride, potassium chlorate containing 1% MgO, Anthracene and polyester binder and binder and pyrotechnic smoke composition is mixed.
Full Text FIELD OF INVENTION
The present invention relates to a process for preparation of an environmentally safe smoke composition
suitable for fire fighting training, NBCD training and industrial use such as for testing the leakage of pipes.

STATEMENT OF INVENTION
According to this invention there is provided a process for preparation of an environmentally safe smoke composition which produces extremely low toxicity smoke wherein the process characterised by the steps of:-
(a) preparation of a binder by mixing a polyester resin in 100 gms, an
accelerator in 0.75 ml to 1.5 ml and a catalyst 0.75 to 1.5 ml.
(b) preparation of pyrotechnic smoke composition by mixing ammonium
chloride (40-50 parts), potassium chlorate (30-45 parts) containing 1%
MgO, Anthracene (10-20 parts) and polyester binder (5-10) parts.
(c) mixing of binder and pyrotechnic smoke composition
According to the present invention there is provided a process for preparation of an
environmentally safe composition which has extremely low toxicity and is therefore
suitable for fire fighting training, NBCD training and industrial use. In its simplest form
the smoke generating composition comprises of at least one oxidizer, a fuel, a
vaporizable constituent which is non toxic and a polyester
binder. The composition of the present invention comprises of
Ammonium chloride as a vaporizable constituent, Potassium Chlorate (with 1% magnesium Oxide) as oxidizer, Anthracene as fuel and suitable parts of a polyester binder.

Required quantity of ingredients (excluding polyester resin which has been utilised for the preparation of binder) are loaded in a planetary mixture of 5 litre capacity and the comb shaped blade is used for the mixing. The mixture is first run at low speed (20 r p. m) for 5 minutes and thereafter for 25 minutes at high speed (40 r.p.m). The batch is unloaded and spread in an aluminum tray.
Addition of Binder
Uniform solution of binder of the above preparation is spread over the smoke composition. The composition is mixed with a wooden spatula manually and loaded in the above mentioned planetary mixture. The composition is mixed for 30 minutes at low speed (20 r.p.m) and is taken for filing within 24 hours.
It has been observed that the above composition when ignited using matchstick produces smoke of ammonium chloride particulate which is of low toxicity and within environmentally acceptable limits and can therefore be utilised for fire fighting training and NBCD Training in enclosed areas and industrial use. The smoke is produced for a duration of four to five minutes and fills up 150 m3 of enclosed space.
WORKING EXAMPLE;
For preparation of 1 kg of Environmentally safe smoke composition, the ingredients were taken as under:-
Ammonium Chloride 450 g
Potassium Chlorate 400 g
(Containing 4 g Magnesium Oxide)
Anthracene 150g
Polyester binder 70 g
(HylacHSR8lll)
Required quantity of ingredients (excluding polyester binder was loaded in a planetary mixer of 5 Litre capacity and the comb shaped blade was used for mixing. The mixer was first run at a low speed (20 rpm) for 5 minutes and thereafter for 25 minutes at high speed (40 rpm). The batch was unlaoded and spread in aluminium trays. Solution of 70 g of polyester resin HSR-8111 was taken in a mixing bowl and 0.7 cc of accelerator Q-8021 and 0.7 cc of catalyst Q-8013 were added with continuous stirring. The composition was mixed with a wooden spatula manually and loaded in the planetary mixer. The composition was mixed for 30 minutes at low speed (20 rpm) and was taken for filing within 24 hours.
300 ± 10 g of smoke composition was weighed along with 30 g of ignition composition (Smoke Composition without polyester binder and pressed at 100kg/cm2 using suitable 79 mm dia plunger (into a paper tube of dia 80 mm and which had its lower end turned over) using supporting mould.
The above process was carried out in a room where the RH was maintained at 50 ± 5% using dehumidifier and ambient temperature about 25°C.
