Title of Invention

"AN IMPROVED CRANE"
Abstract This invention relates to an improved crane comprising a front member (9) adapted to be secured with a rear member (10) by articulation and oscillation means wherein the articulation and oscillation means (11) having a link member (15) secured with the base plate (16) of the frame (12) of the rear member (10) at lower end of the plate, a pivot pin (17) on the other end of link member (15) is provided to hold the connecting plate of front member (9) at the upper end, characterized in that an operating cabin is provided at front member together with the controls and boom assembly.
Full Text FIELD OF INVENTION
This invention relates to an improved crane.
BACKGROUND OF INVENTION
Cranes are known in the an: to have a front and rear section. However, such members were provided with cranes having a front and rear section. The cabin is mounted or the rear section together with the controls consisting of hydraulic, mechanical and electrical controls. Further, the engine and transmission is also provided with the rear section of the crane. The front section of the crane supports the boom assembly. The front section and the rear section are jointed together by means of an articulation member, which comprises a pivot pin passing through a bush mounted on the upper plate of the frame provided with the rear section of the crane such thai to connect the linkage of the from section (boom assembly) and rear section (operator cabin) therewith pivotly.
Several disadvantages are associated with the known construction of cranes having the cabins mounted on the rear section. One such disadvantage is that the

front section supporting the boom assembly has a plurality of fastening means to hold the plurality of components and subassemblies. Thus the front section.has a yoke held through its base to a main frame through a first fastening means. The arms of the yoke are held to a cradle through second and third fastening means. A cradle is supported on the main frame through a fourth fastening means. A frame joins the front arms of the main frame through a fifth fastening means. The main frame pivotally supports the boom of the crane. The frame provides at the fourth fastening means a first point of articulation. The second point'of articulation is provided at the first fastening means.
OBJECTS OF THE INVENTION
An object of this invention is to propose an improved construction of a crane to obviate the disadvantages associated with the prior art.
Another object of this invention is to propose an improved construction of a crane having improved articulation and oscillation means provided therewith.

Yet another object of this invention is to propose an improved construction of the articulation and oscillation means for use with a crane which obviates the disadvantages of a plurality of fastening means.
Still another object of this invention is to propose an improved construction of the articulation and oscillation means for use with a crane and wherein the cabin may be provided with the front section.
Further objects and advantages of this invention will be more apparent from the ensuing description.
According to this invention there is provided an improved crane comprising a front member adapted to be secured with a rear member by articulation and oscillation means wherein the articulation and oscillation means having a link member secured with the base plate of the frame of the rear member at lover end of the plate, a pivot pin on the other end of link member is provided to cold the connecting plate of front member at the upper end, characterized in that an operating cabin is provided at front member together with the controls and boom assembly.

In accordance with this invention, the crane comprises a front and rear section, as known in the art. However, in the present invention, the front section has the cabin mounted thereon together with the boom assembly and controls. The rear section has the engine and transmission. Further the articulation and oscillation means has a link member adapted to be secured with the base plate of the frame of the rear member at the lower surface thereof by means of a first pivot pin passing through a spherical bearing provided at the corresponding end of the link member. A second pivot pin being provided at the other end of said link member such that to hold the connecting plate of the front member at the upper surface thereof. A spherical bearing is provided in the passes of the connecting plate of the front section for allowing the passes of the second pivot pin therethrough.
