Title of Invention

A STEAM PRESSURE REGULATING DEVICE FOR A PRESSURE COOKER
Abstract A steam pressure regulating device for a pressure cooker comprises a valve housing made of a flexible material for insertion into, and engagement with the rim of, an aperture in the lid of the cooker, the housing having a passage with a mouth at the first end thereof and an opening at its second end. There is at least one lateral hole running through the wall of the passage, connecting the passage to atmosphere; a valve stem having a plug at its first end and a collar at its second end, the circumference of the plug and that of the collar being larger than that of the mouth and that of the opening,' but smaller than that of the passage. The arrangement is such that the collar is forcibly insertable through the mouth and passage, and thence through the opening, to emerge thereat, the stem being thus located within the passage, leaving the plug and collar outside the passage.

This invention relates to a steam pressure regulating device for a pressure cooker.
An objective of the device proposed herein is to close ah aperture in the lid of the pressure cooker, to enable normal steam pressure build-up at the start of the cooking operation. In the event of subsequent excessive build-up of steam pressure within the pressure cooker, the known dead weight pressure release valve opens to let out steam| however, if this valve malfunctions, the said device operates to let out steam
and thus relieve the cooker of excessive pressure.

created within the cooker, the said device permits air
from atmosphere to enter the cooker and thus equalize
the pressure.
Thus, the said device regulates the pressure within the cooker in conditions of excessive positive or negative pressure.

Such location of the stem in the passage serves to achieve free and guided movement of the stem.
With the lid L of the pressure cooker closed and the cooker in operation, the normal build-up of steam in the cooker causes the plug T (which is outside the passage P and exposed to the interior of the cooker) to push the plug against the mouth M to close the said mouth. Consequently, no steam can enter the passage P and the pressure build-up of steam within the cooker continues to take place in the normal way.

Once the plug enters the passage P, the gap between the plug and the wall W of the passage causes steam, from the cooker, to enter the passage and escape through the holes B to atmosphere, thus relieving the cooker of excessive pressure.
