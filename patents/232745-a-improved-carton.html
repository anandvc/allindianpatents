Title of Invention

"A IMPROVED CARTON"
Abstract This invention relate to an improved carton characterized by an outlet adapted to be opened or closed by a spout member (6), said spout member comprising a pair of side walls (7a, 7b) spaced from each other and having a base plate (8) there between, said spout member having an angular movement with respect to said carton so as to cause an opening or closure of said outlet.
Full Text This invention relates to an improved carton for the storage of liquidores and particulate material. In particular, though not limited thereto, this invention relates to a carton having an integrally formed discharge spout for discharge of the materials contained therein.
Cartons for storage and dispersing of particulate or granular materials are known in the art. Such cartons normally comprises a rectangular or square container with flaps at the upper or lower and. Normally, the lower and flaps are sealed, whereas the upper and flaps can be opened to allow a discharge of the material. However, a disadvantage associated with such a construction of a carton is that an opening of the upper flaps allows an ingress of moisture within the carton.
To obviate such a disadvantage, a carton is also
known know having both the upper and lower and
flaps in a sealed relationship, and that it becomes necessary to pierce a hole in a sidewall or the upper flaps so as to allow a discharge of the material. As the hole remains in an open state, it also allows an ingress of moisture within the carton.
An object of this invention is to propose an improved construction of a carton having a discharge spout which no longer allows an ingress of moisture within the carton.
Another object of this invention is to propose a carton having a discharge spout which can be actuated into an open and closed status.
According to this invention there is provided an improved carton characterised by an outlet adapted to be opened or closed by a spout member (6), said spout member comprising a pair of side walls (7a, 7b) spaced from each other and having a base plate (8) therebetween, said spout member having an angular movement with respect to said carton so as to cause an opening or closure of said outlet.

Outlet 10 is normally formed by piercing a hole or opening in container 1.
