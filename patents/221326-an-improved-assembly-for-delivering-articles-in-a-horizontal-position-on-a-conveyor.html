Title of Invention

AN IMPROVED ASSEMBLY FOR DELIVERING ARTICLES IN A HORIZONTAL POSITION ON A CONVEYOR
Abstract A drum assembly for delivering articles on a conveyor comprising: a rotary drum having longitudinal slots for receiving the articles vertically; an inner guide member having a tapering groove on circumference and being placed within the drum wherein said tapering groove has a narrower head portion having uniform depth to receive the article passing through the slot and a broader tail portion connected to the narrower head, having slant depth gradually decreasing to zero for tilting the articles in a direction parallel to the rotational axis of the rotary drum; an outer guide member being placed outside said drum, said outer guide member has slanting portions for titling the articles in a direction parallel to the rotational axis of the rotary drum and holding the tilted articles; wherein rotary drum delivers the received article on the conveyor in a position parallel to the rotational axis of the drum.

FIELD OF INVENTION
The present invention relates to orientation and delivering of articles such as capsules, and the like for band sealing machines, capsule filling machines, capsules sorting machines, capsule inspection machines and the other pharmaceutical machineries.
BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
Capsules are usually made in two sections, a body and a cap which is used for containing pharmaceutical or nutraceutical product in solid form or in a liquid form. However, when liquid products such as pharmaceutical, nutraceutical and the like are to be filled, it is known in the art to seal for the purpose of avoiding leak of the contents and ensuring tamper proof product. Conventionally employed methods for sealing the body and cap of the capsules wherein each capsule body is formed with either one of a radially outwardly extending circumferential projection and recess so that, when the capsule cap is mounted on the capsule body to provide the complete capsule, the circumferential projection can be received in the circumferential recess; the application of gelatin or any other solution to the overlapping joint of each closed capsule to permit the open end of each capsule cap to stick to the capsule body upon drying; and the application of gelatin or any other solution to form a binder layer at the overlapping joint of each closed capsule. For applying such a band seal or for other purposes such as for example packaging, printing etc, the closed capsules are required to be oriented at a predetermined posture and are successively supplied for applying band seal or for packaging etc.


US4266477 describes an apparatus and process for transporting and orienting materials that have dissimilar ends, like capsules, to and in a predetermined orientation and position. The apparatus includes two rotatable drums a feeding drum and a transferring drum in tangential arrangement. Each drum includes a plurality of peripherally spaced pockets sized and shaped to receive and carry individual capsules. Each drum is provided with an air jet which orients certain preselected capsules from a radial to a longitudinal position with a selected end trailing, and the capsules are then delivered to a conveyor for further processing, like printing, such as spin-printing.
However, in the existing apparatus and methods the capsules are to'be oriented first and then delivered which is time consuming.
US Patent No. 5, 802, 804 discloses an a system for feeding articles to blisters of a blister band for automatic packaging of articles such as capsules, pills, tablets including at least one magazine formed by basically vertical channel with open bottom containing a pile of articles to be introduced into relative blisters of blister band that is located under this magazine and driven in a straight direction; the said system being characterized in that it also includes a placement means for taking cyclically at least one article from the bottom of said magazine and for introducing it into a respective blister; said placement means being located between the magazine and the blister band and rotated in a direction that is in accordance with movement direction of said band and in phase relation therewith; plurality of depressions made on an outer surface of said placement means, a motor controlled by an electronic control unit for driving said placement means into rotation; feedback control means,

electronically connected with said control unit for detecting the position of the blisters and for operating said motor to carry out a precise introduction of the articles into respective blisters.
Although the US Patent 5802804 uses a simple placement means for feeding articles, the capsules should be oriented prior to the delivery of the capsules to the placement means for delivering to the blister band. Further, the articles have to be fed horizontally to fit into the depression of the placement means. The mechanism involves two drums, the first drum tilts the capsules having body in upward position and the second drum tilts the capsules having body in downward position in order while capsules are loaded in second drum where capsule body gets upward position and gets tilted. The orientation of the capsules is done is two steps before delivering the capsule.
SUMMARY OF INVENTION
It is the object of the present invention to simplify the system and method for orienting and delivering the capsules.
It is another object of the present invention to orient and deliver the capsules simultaneously to reduce the time of orienting and delivering the capsules.
According to one embodiment of the present invention provides a drum assembly for delivering articles on a conveyor comprising a rotary drum having longitudinal slots for receiving the articles vertically; an inner guide member having a tapering groove on circumference and being placed within the drum wherein said tapering groove has a narrower head portion having

uniform depth to receive the article passing through the slot and a broader tail portion connected to the narrower head, having slant depth gradually decreasing to zero for tilting the articles in a direction parallel to the rotational axis of the rotary drum; an outer guide member being placed outside said drum, said outer guide member has slanting portions for titling the articles in a direction parallel to the rotational axis of the rotary drum and holding the tilted articles; wherein rotary drum delivers the received article on the conveyor in a position parallel to the rotational axis of the drum. The inner guide member and the outer guide member are stationary for orienting said articles received on the slots of said drum. The diameter of the slots on the drum is bigger than the diameter of the body of the capsule and smaller than the diameter of the cap thereby receiving the article in vertical position.
According to another embodiment of the present invention, a system for orienting and delivering articles comprising a hopper for holding articles; a reciprocating magazine for transferring the articles from said hopper to a drum assembly; the drum assembly according to an embodiment of the present invention for orienting and delivering the articles to conveyor; said assembly being located between the reciprocating magazine and a conveyor of the band sealing apparatus; said conveyor having pockets for receiving the articles and being located under the drum assembly; wherein the articles are simultaneously oriented and delivered to the pockets of the conveyor by rotating the drum.

The present invention provides a drum assembly and a system for achieving orientation and delivery of articles such as capsules and the like in a single step. The drum assembly of the present invention is a single drum assembly for orienting and delivering the articles. As the orientation and delivering of the articles are done simultaneously, it saves a lot of time and labor involved in the process. The drum assembly according to the present invention is of a Simple construction involving fewer components and shall be incorporated into the existing system with little modification.

DESCRIPTION OF THE INVENTION
The drum assembly according to the present invention as shown in Figures 1, 2, 3 comprises an drum (20) having longitudinal slots (21) for receiving articles such as capsules (11); an inner guide member (22) having a tapered

groove (23) that is tapering towards one end for orienting the capsule (11); an outer guide member (24) having slanting portions (25) for holding the capsules (11) in horizontal position. The longitudinal slots (21) on the drum and the tapering groove (23) on the inner guide member (22) are aligned in such a manner that the tapering groove (23) and the slanting portions (25) on the outer guide member tilts the capsule (11) received on the slot of the drum in a vertical position to a horizontal position as shown in Figure 4. Referring to Figure 7, the tapering groove (23) on the inner guide member (22) has a head portion (30) and a tail portion (32). The head portion is narrower with uniform depth. The tail portion (32) is broader connected to the head portion (30) and having a slant depth gradually decreasing to zero. The tapering groove (23) is positioned perpendicular to the slots on the groove. The inner guide member (22) and the outer guide member are stationary that is they are fixed to a shaft. The drum (20) is a rotating drum that is driven by a motor and a shaft. The drum (20) rotates in a direction opposite to the direction the conveyor. Preferably, the drum (20) rotates about a rotational axis (not shown) in anticlockwise direction.
The capsules (11) are received in vertical position on the slots (21) provided on the drum (20) due to the diameter and alignment of the slots (21) and grooves (23) on the inner guide member (22) with respect to the diameter of the body (11 A) and cap (11B) of the capsule (11). When the capsules (11) are fed to the drum assembly randomly, the capsules (11) are received in a vertical position as mentioned aboye either with the body (11 A) of the capsule

(11) down or with the body (11 A) of the capsule (11) up. When the capsules (11) are received with the body (11 A) of the capsule (11) down, the body (11 A) of the capsule (11) passes through the slots of drum as the diameter of the slot is bigger than the body diameter of the capsules (11) and reaches the tapering groove (23) of the inner guide member (22). Therefore, when the drum is rotated, the tapering groove (23) on the inner guide member (22) tilts the body (11A) of the capsule (11) towards the slot (21) and the capsules (11) turn horizontally in the slot (21) of the drum (20) as shown in Figure 4. Such oriented capsules (11) are further held on a horizontal position by the guide member (24).
When the capsules (11) are received on the slots with the body (11 A) of the capsule (11) up and the cap (11B) of the capsule (11) down as shown in Figure 4, the diameter of the slots being smaller than the diameter of the cap (11B) of the capsule (11), the capsule (11) remains in upward position and may not reach the tapering groove (23) of the inner guide member (22). Therefore, when the drum is rotated, the outer guide member (24) tilts the capsule (11) to an axial direction thereby orienting the capsule (11) and simultaneously delivering the same in a position parallel to the rotational axis of the rotary drum on the conveyor.
Thus, on rotating the drum, the received capsules (11) are oriented and delivered in a single step i.e. simultaneously to the conveyor (17) or for any other purpose such as for example packaging, printing etc.
