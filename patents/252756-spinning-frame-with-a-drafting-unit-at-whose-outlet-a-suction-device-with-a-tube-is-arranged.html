Title of Invention

SPINNING FRAME WITH A DRAFTING UNIT AT WHOSE OUTLET A SUCTION DEVICE WITH A TUBE IS ARRANGED
Abstract SPINNING FRAME WITH A DRAFTING UNIT AT WHOSE OUTLET A SUCTION DEVICE WITH A TUBE IS ARRANGED In a spinning frame with a drafting unit at whose outlet a suction device with a tube (30) is being arranged wherefrom an opening (dl) of the tube is directed towards a yarn (12b) leaving the drafting unit, the opening (dl) at the side facing a bottom roller (28) of the drafting unit is being provided with an edge which runs parallel to the axis of the bottom roller, while the side of the opening (dl) opposing said edge, is converging to form a peak. The outline of the opening (dl) is preferably triangular or pentagonal. It is sized at least that large that a slightly twisted fibre band with a circular cross section of 5 mm diameter can be led into the opening (dl) and thus into the tube. With a tube in the suction device being configured in the described way the operating behaviour of a spinning frame can be improved. (Figure 1)

The present invention relates to a spinning frame with a drafting unit at whose outlet a suction device with a tube is arranged.
In the German patent application DE 37 08 113 a suction device for broken threads on a spinning frame is being disclosed, with a tube which conically diverges in flowing direction of the suctioned air, whereat the plane of the suction opening of said tube is basically being positioned parallel to the thread path. The opening of the tube is shown below the axis of a bottom roller of the drafting unit at a distance from the thread which amounts to about D/2 of the bottom roller. In order to be able to securely suck off a broken thread or a fibre band respectively which has hardly been twisted because of an initial thread break, a sufficiently high flowing speed of the suction air has to be provided within the tube. Therefrom results a high energy consumption of the spinning frame.
It is the object of the present invention to provide a tube for a suction device which even at relatively low subpressure within the suction system securely picks up a fibre band or a thread respectively. This task is being solved by a suction device of the present invention as described herein.
Accordingly the present invention provides a spinning frame with a drafting unit at whose outlet a suction device with a tube is arranged wherefrom an opening of the tube is directed toward a yarn leaving the drafting unit, characterised in that the opening at the side facing a bottom roller of the drafting unit is being provided with an edge which runs parallel to the axis of the bottom roller, and that the side of the opening opposing said edge, is converging to form a peak.

With a tube according to the invention the operating behaviour of a spinning frame, a ring spinning frame in particular can be considerably improved. Contamination and clogging within the tube are being avoided by the configuration according to the claims. Also the tendency of winding of the two bottom rollers in the drafting unit can be reduced considerably, as well as the rate of thread breaks. Preferably the body of the tube is being formed with a constant conicity and a circular cross section. The inlet opening directed towards the bottom roller preferably includes an angle of 45Â° towards the thread path. The quantity of suction air and thus the energy consumption of the spinning frame can be achieved by a cross section of the opening which is as small as possible. The opening is being sized so that a nontwisted roving and small fibre flakes, coming from the drafting unit 12 are being suctioned without deformation. The width of the suction opening of the tube has to correspond with the thread traversing stroke. The basically triangular or pentagonal shape of the opening results from the requirement that the opening has to be as close to the bottom roller as possible, further by the necessity of a minimum width as mentioned and an inner clearance of at least 4 mm. The slanted opening of the tube, that is its angular shape of approximately 45Â° in relation to the thread moving path takes into consideration the fall-off direction of the fibres during a thread break.
