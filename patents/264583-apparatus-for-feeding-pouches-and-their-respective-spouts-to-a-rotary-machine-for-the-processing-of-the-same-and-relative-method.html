Title of Invention

APPARATUS FOR FEEDING POUCHES AND THEIR RESPECTIVE SPOUTS TO A ROTARY MACHINE FOR THE PROCESSING OF THE SAME AND RELATIVE METHOD
Abstract Apparatus (1) for feeding pouches (102) and their respective spouts (103) to a rotary machine (R) for the processing of the same, comprising at least one magazine (2) for the pouches (102) and at least one magazine (3) for the spouts (103). The apparatus (1) originally comprises a handling unit (4) designed to pick up a pouch (102) and a spout (103) from their respective magazines (2, 3) and feed them simultaneously, and in continuous mode, to the rotary machine (R) that processes them. Said handling unit (4) comprises the means for picking up a pouch (102) and the means for picking up a spout (103). Method for feeding pouches (102) and spouts (103) to a rotary machine (R) for the processing of the same.

As is well known, drinks, and in particular fruit juices and sports drinks, arc
increasingly more frequently sold and preserved in flexible containers
commonly known as "pouches".
Generally, these flexible containers comprise a pouch, normally made of
polyethylene (PE) and have a spout, to cnabic the product contained inside the
container to be poured out
Pouring of the product is particularly fecthtated by the possibility to exert
pressure on the beverage by squeezing the pouch.
The flexible containers, or pouches, arc made from. a pair of polycthylcuc sheets
the edges of which are welded together leaving an opening.
Into this opening a portion of the spout is inserted and secured by means of
welding In particular, this welding concerns the edges of the opening and is
performed by applying heat to partially melt said edges to that they adhere to
the portion of the spout inserted in the pouch.
Generally, the spout is inserted inside the opening before the container is filled.
It is well known that systems already exist for the processing of pouches
comprising rotary machines with starwheels for picking up and transferring the
