Problems; that the Invention is to Solve
However, 5-aminolevulinic acid phosphate hitherto known has a melting point of only a little over 100°C and not resistant to common heating sterilization at 121°C. Moreover, when heated, the phosphate is converted into a dinter of 5-aminoievulinic acid molecules to form 2,5-pyrazinedipropionic acid (PDPA) , resulting in an impurity-containing one. Accordingly, an object of the invention is to provide a novel crystal of 5-aminolevulinic acid phosphate having a high thermal stability, a high melting point, and an excellent moisture absorption resistance as well as a process for producing the same.

Advantage of the Invention
The crystal of 5-aminolevulinic acid phosphate of the invention has a high melting point and an excellent moisture absorption resistance and also is remarkably titele in the content of the impurity (PDPA) to be formed by heating and thus excellent in thermal stability, so that the crystal is useful as an active ingredient for
