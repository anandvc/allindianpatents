Title of Invention

"WASHING MACHINE"
Abstract A washing machine has a washing-cum-dewatering tub ( 10) installed rotatably inside of a water holding tub ( 12), the top of which is covered with a water holding tub cover ( 23), and a packing material ( 27) such as sponge, rubber and the like disposed between the water holding tub(12) and the water holding tub cover (12) to block a water leakage, thereby having a washing water located between the washing-cum-dewatering tub (10) and the water holding tub (12) elevated between the washing-cum-dewatering tub (10) and the water holding tub cover (23) by pumping action caused by rotating the washing-cum-dewatering tub (10) with a motor (13), discharged from above towards near the center of the washing-cum-dewatering tub (10) and circulated.
Full Text Field of the Invention.
The present invention relates to a washing machine for washing items to be washed that are placed in a washing-cum- dewatering tub. Background of the Invention.
In recent times, the washing capacity of a washing machine tends to be increasing and not a small number of washing machines are found to have the washing capacity of 8 kg or more. Accordingly, a prior art washing machine structured as described in the above requires a stronger rotational force and an enhanced agitation force in order for the entire items to be washed to be wel1 soaked in a washing water and also to be exposed to the rotation of an agitator. As a result, there have arisen such problems as damaging the items to be washed while getting rid of stains, and also entangling the items to be washed.

Summary of the Invention.
The present invention deals with the problems involved with the prior art washing machine as described in the above, and the objective thereof is to do washing by circulating a washing water without exerting a mechanical force from an agitator and the like to items to be washed contained in a washing-cum-dewatering tub, thereby causing no damages and tangles to the items to be washed.
In order to attain the objective as described in the above the present invention discloses a structure for a washing machine

a packing material such as sponge, rubber and the like placed between the water holding tub and the water holding tub cover to prevent water from spilling over.

When items to be washed are placed in the washing-cum-dewatering tub and rotated in a washing water, the washing water is elevated inside of the water holding tub because of the centrifugal force caused by the rotation of the washing water, changes its direction at the water holding tub cover located atop and is discharged through the clearance between the washing-cum-dewatering tub towards near the center of rotation of the washing-cum-dewatering tub.
The discharged washing water passes through the items to be washed and then returns to the outside of the washing-cum-dewatering tub doing a repeat of circulating through this path. At this time, water pressure is applied to the place, where the water holding tub and the water holding tub cover meet each other, because the washing water elevated along the inner surfaces of the washing-cum-dewatering tub hits the water holding tub cover to change its direction. However, by having a packing material inserted between the water holding tub and the water holding tub cover and preventing water from spilling over, water can be stopped from bursting forth, splashing and the like. Fig.11 is a longitudinal cross-sectional view of a prior art washing machine. Detailed description of the Invention.
A structure of a prior art washing machine will be described with reference to Fig.11 in the fallowing:
As shown in Fig.11, a washing-cum-dewatering tub 1 is made rotatable around the center axis thereof, provided with an agitator 2 on the bottom thereof, the agitator 2 being made freely rotatable, and also provided with a water holding tub 3 on the outside thereof . A motor 4 for driving the washing-cum- dewatering tub 1 and the agitator 2 is installed on the bottom of the water holding tub 3 to drive the washing-cum-dewatering tub 1 via a clutch 5 for controlling a driving unit. The water holding tub 3 is linked to an external framework 6 via a suspension 7 in such a way as vibrations are isolated. There is a liquid balancer 8, containing a liquid, fixed on the top of the washing-cum- dewatering tub 1. The top of the water holding tub 3 is covered with a water holding tub cover 9.
In the washing process of the washing machine structured as described in the above, a washing water is poured in the water holding tub 3, items to be washed (clothes) placed in the
9.

washing-cum-dewatering tub i are agitated by the agitator 2, the items to be flashed are soaked in the washing water and crumpled to get rid of stains. In the rinsing process, fresh water is poured in the water holding tub 3 and the items to be washed are agitated by the agitator 2 to rinse the items to be washed. In the dewatering process, the rotation of the motor 4 is transferred to the washing-cum-dewatering tub 1 by switching the clutch 5 and the rotated washing-cum-dewatering tub 1 causes the water content in the items to be washed to be extracted by centrifugal force.
These processes are control led in succesion to perform each respective process of washing, rinsing and dewatering automatically.

As shown in Fig.l, a washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 has its side walls provided with many small holes (not shown in Fig.l) and an agitator 11 rotatably mounted on its bottom. This washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 has a water holding tub 121 installed on its outside.

The water holding tub 12 has a motor 13 (a driving means ) attached to its bottom for driving the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 and agitator 11 in such a way as a clutch 14 can switch in driving between the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 and the agitator 11.
The wash holding tub 12 is linked to an external framework 15 via a suspension 16 so as to isolate vibrations. A water channel cover 17 is fixed to the inner wall of the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 to form a water circulating channel 18, which is connected between a pumping chamber 20 formed around the outer periphery of a lower wing 19 that is formed integrally with the agitator 11 on its bottom side and a lint filter 21 located in the upper section of the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10.
A liquid balancer 22 containing a liquid is fixed to the upper section of the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10. The top of the water holding tub 12 is covered with a water holding tub cover 23. A packing material 27 is placed between the top of the water
11.

holding tub 12 and the water holding tub cover 23 to ensure watertight sealing.
A water draining valve 24 is for draining a washing water from the water holding tub 12 and a water feeding valve 25 is for feeding a washing water to the water holding tub 12. A controller 26 controls the operating status of the motor 13, water draining valve 24, water feeding valve 25 and the like, thereby controlling a series of the processes of washing, rinsing and dewatering in succession. In case where items to be washed are delicate, the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 is rotated at 300 rpm, for example, for each respective process of washing and rinsing to have the washing water located between the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 and the water holding tub cover 23 elevated by pumping action caused by the rotation of the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 and then discharged downward to near the center of rotation of the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10.
In the structure as described in the above, when the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 is rotated after items to be washed have been placed in the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 and a washing water has been fed by opening the water feeding valve 25, upward motion is produced by centrifugal force due to rotation, thereby causing the washing water to ascend the inner surface of the water holding tub 12, and the washing water thus ascending between the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 and the water holding tub 12 hits the water holding tub cover 23 and changes its direction, and then bursts forth from the clearance between the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 (the top of the liquid balancer 22) and the water holding tub cover 23 downward to near the center of rotation of the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10.
After passing through the items to be washed, the

discharged washing water passes again through many small holes (not shown in the drawing) formed on the side walls of the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 and circulates through channels that shift in position between the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 o and the water holding tub 12, thereby allowing stains to get rid of from the items to be washed while the washing water passing through the items to be washed. It is noteworthy that the washing water can be infiltrated effectively throughout the items to be washed by centrifugal force since the washing water bursts forth between the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 and the water holding tub cover 23 downward to near the center of rotation of the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10.
The washing water ascending the inner surface of the water holding tub 12 caused by the centrifugal force produced by the rotation of the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 hits the water holding tub cover 23, changes its direction and bursts forth between the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10 and the water holding tub cover 23, resulting in an application of a high pressure to the fit between the water holding tub 10 and the water holding tub cover 23. In order to prevent a leakage of the washing water due to this high pressure, a packing material 27 is employed to be placed therebetween. Accordingly, there is little concern for the leakage of the washing water due to the high pressure caused by bursting forth of the washing water, thereby making it possible to perform the washing of the items to be washed without damages and tangles by circulating the washing water and without applying a mechanical force caused by the agitator 11 and the like to the items to be washed in the washing-cum-dewatering tub 10.
The packing material 27 of the above includes sponge, rubber, putty and the like, and the effectiveness of each respective
