Title of Invention

A TUBULAR RIVET CONNECTION FOR COMPONENTS OF A TRANSPORT AND STORAGE CONTAINER FOR LIQUIDS AND FLOWABLE MATERIAL
Abstract A tubular rivet connection is provided for assernblinq a transport and storage container that has a support frame, an inner plastic container with a filling socket and a discharge socket, and an exterior jacket. The support frame for the inner container .has corner and center legs mounted on a .base frame. The bottom of the support frame and the exterior jacket are attached to the corner legs and center legs by the tubular rivet connection. that has a tubula~ rivet stem connected to a first component; the tubular rivet stem is formed as a. monolithic part of the first component or is a s.eparate part welded to the first component. A second component to be riveted to the first compopent has a rivet hols. A free end of the tubular rivet stem that projects past the second component after insertion into the rivet hole is deformend to a driven head.
Full Text BACKGROUND OF THE. INVENTION 1'Field of the Invention
•The invention, relates to a tubular rivet connection for connecting a component of metal or plastic material to a component of metal, plastic material or wood, in particular for assembling transport and storage containers for liquids and flowable material (bulk material). The '. transport and storage containers are provided with a pallet-like support frame, an inner container of plastic- material resting on the support frame and comprising a-, closable.filling socket and a discharge socket to be connected'to a discharge fitting, and with an exterior jacket in the form of a grid jacket or sheet metal jacket. The support frame made of sheet metal, plastic material or wood has a bottom for supporting the inner container and also has corner legs and center legs that ' are mounted on a base frame or skids, wherein the bottom of the support frame as well as the.exterior jacket of the inner container are attached to the corner legs and the center legs.
2. Description of the Related Art
When assembling transport and storage containers for liquids of the aforementioned kind as disclosed in DE 100 62 088 C2, the underframe of the metal grid basket and the sheet metal bottom of the pallet-like support frame on which the inner plastic container for liquids rests are screwed to the corner or center legs of the support frame and the legs are welded to the base frame of the underframe. Because of the employed different connecting techniques and the resulting significant assembly expenditure as well .'as the small parts required for mounting, e.g., sheet-metal or self-tapping screws and washers, the manufacturing costs of the liquid container are accordingly high.
SUMMARY OF THE INVENTION
It is an object of the present invention to develop a connection technique for workpieces and components of metal, plastic material or wood that enables a simple assembly of objects and devices of different kinds.
In accordance with, the present indention, this is.
i • •
achieved in that a tubular rivet connection is provided wherein the rivet stem, either formed as a unitary or monolithic part of a first component or provided as a separate part that is welded to the first component, is provided for insertion through a rivet hole of a second, component that rests against the first component and is to be riveted to the first component, wherein the free end of the tubular rivet stem of the first component projecting past the second component after insertion into the rivet' hole can be deformed to a driven head resting against the second component.
The dependent claims disclose how the individual components of a transport and storage container for. liquids of the aforementioned kind are furnished with the tubular rivet connections according to the invention.
The gist of the"invention resides in that the tubular' rivets and the matching rivet holes are integrated into the workpieces and components of different kinds of metal, plastic material or wood so that for connecting the workpieces and components only one joining technique, i.e., riveting, is employed during assembly and separate tubular rivets with a rivet head are no longer required. In this way, the assembly expenditure' is reduced and' manufacturing costs are lowered.

The tubular rivet connection illustrated in Figs, la to lc of two components 1, 2 of sheet metal comprises a rivet stem 3 that is formed as a part of the first component 1 and to be inserted through the rivet hole 4 of the second component 2 to be connected to the first component 1, wherein the second component 2 rests against the first component 1. . When riveting the two components 1, 2, the free end 3a of the rivet stfem 3 that'is inserted through the rivet hole 4 in the .second component 2 and projects past the second component 2 is deformed by a hammer or a special'riveting tool to a driven head S. ' resting against the second component 2.
The rivet stem 3.can be roll-formed, for example, by . a slide, on the first component 1.
In a different embodiment of the afore described tubular rivet connection, the rivet stem 3 is welded as a separate component to the first component 1.
