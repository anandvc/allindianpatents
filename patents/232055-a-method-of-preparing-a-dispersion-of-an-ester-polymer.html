Title of Invention

"A METHOD OF PREPARING A DISPERSION OF AN ESTER POLYMER"
Abstract An ester polymer dispersion in at least one organic liquid, said ester polymer having a hydrocarbon backbone and at least a first type of side chain which is a long chain aliphatic group of at least 14 carbon atoms, and especially at least a second type of side chain, which is an alkyl of 1-13 carbons and the polymer is present in said dispersion in at least 10%, preferably 20-30% by weight. The organic liquid component of the dispersion is a branched or linear aliphatic hydrocarbon of 6-16 carbon atoms and/or an aromatic hydrocarbon of 6-10 carbon atoms. The dispersions are of lower viscosity than corresponding solutions of the polymers in hydrocarbons and may be used to reduce or inhibit deposition of wax from waxy oils, e.g. crude oil.
Full Text The present invention relates to a dispersion of an ester polymer and a method of preparing a dispersion.
The present invention relates to dispersions of ester polymers, and for their preparation and use, particularly in petroleum industry pipelines.
Crude oils are complex mixtures comprising hydrocarbons of varying types and molecular weights. One class of hydrocarbon present in the oils is paraffins which are linear, branched chain or cyclic hydrocarbons having at least 18 carbons which can form waxy solids. The solubility of these waxy solid forming components in the crude oils is predominantly temperature-dependant. They are usually soluble in the crude oil under down-ho'e conditions i.e. high pressures or high temperatures. However as the oil is brought to the surface its temperature and press jre are reduced. As a result the wax may begin to precipitate out and rray form deposits on any cooler surface with which it comes into contact. These wax deposits can cause problems, such as blockage of pipelines, valves and other process equipment. The wax may also deposit in pipelines subsequently used to transport crude oil or fractions derived from the total product brought up from down-hole, such as fractions comprising gas, e.g. natural gas, and/or water, as well au a liquid hydrocarbon body, e.g. crude (or black) oil or "condensate"; the p.pelines may thus be hydrocarbon lines or multi phase transportation lines with oil, gis and/or waiter. The wax may deposit on surfaces of metal e.g. of ferrous metal.
The contents of the transport pipelines are often cooler than the contents of liaes on oil platforms or in refineries, This cooling is especially critical in respect of lines from offshore oil fields to the land and land lines in cold territories such as
Wax deposition may be reduced in a number of ways, including keeping the lines hot, diluting the oil with solvent, or using special additives which interfere with wax crystal growth.
The special additives may usually be added to the pipeline upstream of the location of any potential cold area likely to result otherwise in deposition of wax. Thiy may be added as a concentrate for ease of handling and are diluted by the oil to :he requisite level for use. However some additives are difficult to form as concentrates for this purpose, because the concentrate is either too viscous or solid at :he concentration required, especially at low temperatures, such us 10°, 5° or -1°C, conditions that may be experienced by the concentrate during its travel to the pipeline eg from an offshore platform.
