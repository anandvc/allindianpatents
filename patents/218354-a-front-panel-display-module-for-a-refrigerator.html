Title of Invention

"A FRONT PANEL DISPLAY MODULE FOR A REFRIGERATOR"
Abstract This invention relates to a front panel display module for use with a refrigerator. A control panel is provided with a time switch, select switch and three-indicator lamps together with related circuitry means which together indicate operational status of refrigerator. These include built in alarm, indication for power ON/OFF, indication for compressor ON/OFF, indication for door open/closed, door open luzzer incase the door of the refrigerator is open for more than 2 minutes or is not properly closed leading to loss of cooling. An overload protector and relay disconnect the compressor when the temperature inside the refrigerator exceeds the predetermined temperature.
Full Text FIELD OF INVENTION
This invention relates to a front panel display module for use with a refrigerator. In particular, this invention relates to a front panel display module to provide a visual display of the operational status of the refrigerator
BACKGROUND OF INVENTION
It is presently known to provide a relay and overload protector, which disconnects the compressor of the refrigerator from the power source in the instance of the temperature of the compressor exceeding a predetermined value. Further, a thermostat is normally provided inside the cabinet of the refrigerator, which regulates the temperature within the cabinet. Thus, whenever the temperature inside the cabinet is lower than the set value of the thermostat, the compressor is disconnected from the power source. A disadvantage in the known refrigerator is that no display means were provided to indicate as to whether the compressor is in an operative status.
In the known refrigerators, a lamp is normally provided inside the cabinet and connected to the power source through a door switch. Thus, only when the door was in an open status, the cabinet lamp was connected to the power source. A disadvantage of such a construction is the absence of a display to indicate the status of the door. Yet another disadvantage is that no signal means were provided in the event that the door was unintentionally left in an open status.
Another disadvantage in the known refrigerators is that no display means were provided to reflect as to whether power was present in the power source.
OBJECTS OF THE INVENTION.
An object of this invention is to propose a front panel display means for providing the operational status of a refrigerator.
Another object of this invention is to propose a front panel display means to provide a visual status as* to whether the compressor of the refrigerator is connected to the power source.
Still another object of this invention is to propose a front panel display means to provide a visual status on the opening or closure of the refrigerator door.
Yet another object of this invention is to propose a front panel display means to provide a visual status which is efficient.
Still another object of this invention is to propse a front panel display means to provide a visual status as to whether the door is in an open status and further includes audio means whicms operable in the event that the door is open beyond a prescribed period.

(SS) and three indicator lamps (L2, L3, 14), wherein lamp (1,2) is

of keys for real time in hours and minutes, alarm time in hours and
minutes, and alarm ON/OFF;
(b) a relay (RLi) through which compressor (CoM) is connected to
power source (PS) wherein said relay is adapted to disconnect the
said compressor when its temperature exceeds a predetermined

temperature and due to overload protector (OP1);
(c) a power supply battery back up module (MPS) comprising live terminal and neutral terminal connected to refrigerator circuit, the said terminals being connected to isolating transformer (T) which is connected to rectifier (R) connected to a filter (F) and regulator (RG) which is further connected to a frequency compensator (FC) connected to back up supply (BS) through back up switch over control (BSC), input terminals being connected to output terminals of back up supply through Current Limiter (CL) and Isolator (I) being provided to connect the back up power supply for the compressor switch/thermostat (T1) and door switch (DS), and wherein the output terminal of said back up switch over control (BSC), isolators (I) and sound transducer (ST) are adapted to be connected to the display module.
In accordance with this invention, the refrigerator circuit is connected to the first lamp through said module having a current limitor and isolator. The first lamp provides the status of the compressor and as to whether it is disconnected from the power source due to overload or by the thermostat provided in the cabinet of the refrigerator. For this purpose, the known refrigerator circuit normally employed in a refrigerator is employed.
The second lamp provides an indication as to whether the door of the refrigerator is in an open or closed status and also connected to the known refrigerator circuit through said module having a current limitor and isolator. Additionally, a sound transducer is connected to the display module to provide an audio signal in the event that the door remains unintentionally open.
The display module has a clock connected to the main power source through the module having a step down transformer and circuits normally provided for operation of the clock.
A further lamp is provided for an indication as to whether power is present in the power source.
