Title of Invention

"A BOOM FOR USE WITH A CRANE"
Abstract An improved boom for use with a crane comprising a mother boom having first and second extensions disposed therein a manual extension member being provided with the last hydraulic operating extension member of the main jib of a crane, Characterised by a push rod having its distal end secured to said manual extension member and the proximal end to said hydraulic operated extended member being provided to stop the retracting/backward movement of said manual extension so as to adjust the height of the boom.
Full Text This invention relates to an imployed boom for use with a crane. In particular, this invention relates to a power pinned manual extension member of said boom.
Cranes presently known in the art have normally a boom of fixed length. Due to such a fixed
length of the boom, hooks are provided at different lengths of the boom depending upon the weight of the
load to be lifted by the crane. Such hooks are held to the boom through hook plates rigid1y he1d to the boom
at said different lengths.
The disadvantage associated with such a known construction is that the boom is of a fixed length and some time it. is difficult to lift a particular weight.
An object of this invention is to propose an improved construction of a boom for use with a crane,
Another object of this invention is to propose a boom having a power pinned manual extension member„
Still another object of this invention is to propose a boom of an adjustable length which is efficien t.,,
According to this invention there is provided an improved boom for use with a crane comprising a mother boom having first and second extensions disposed therein a manual extension member being provided with the last hydraulic operating extension member of the main jib of a crane, characterised by a push rod having its distal end secured to said manual extension member and the proximal end to said hydraulic operated extended member being provided to stop the retracting/backward movement of said manual extension so as to adjust the height of the boom.
Specifically in according with this invention a manually operated extension member is provided with the first hydraulic operating extension member and is locked by means of a locking pin provided with said hydraulic operated extension member. In operation, the hydraulic operated member is extended outwardly. A locking pin is provided in a hole provided at the distal end of the hydraulic operated extension member and is adapted to be removed so as to lock the manually operated extension member with the hydraulic extension member.
