Title of Invention

"HEMORRHOID TREATMENT DEVICE"
Abstract A hemorrhoid message device has a shaft with a first end section, a middle section and a narrow section. The middle section is positioned between the first end section and the narrow section. The narrow section has a concavity extending around the diameter of the shaft. The narrow section has a first end separated from a second end by between one-half inch and three-quarters inch diameters of the first end and the second end of the narrow section are equal. The shaft has a length between one and a half inches and four inches. The middle section can be of straight cylindrical, concave or convex shape.

FIELD OF THE INVENTION
[0001] The present invention relates to apparatus for the treatment of hemorrhoids. More particularly, the present invention relates to devices for massaging internal and external hemorrhoids by the self-movement of the device. The present invention also relates to devices that are held within the anal canal and are driven by the peristaltic activity of the surface of the anal canal for the purpose of massaging internal and external hemorrhoids.
BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
[0002] There are several anal treatment dilators and various mechanical devices, which are intended to facilitate the treatment of hemorrhoidal tissues. For example, U.S. Patent No. 3,675,642-issued on July 11,1942 is a post operative stationary device for enlargement of the anal canal. This device having more than 1.25 inches diameter and a shoulder at the bottom of the device that restricts free movement of the device. The device's big diameter induces the evacuative response of the anorectal area. Also the diameter of the device narrows by less than 1.5 inches from the base flange toward the end of the device. This large area of the narrowing tapered surface toward a bland end of the device receives a downward thrust force from the lateral pressure of the anorectal area. Some support is necessary to keep this device inside the anal canal.
[0003] US Patent No. 4,263,914 massages the anal canal manually. US. PatentNo. 4,583,542 issued on April 22,1986 to Z.R. Boyd, describes a stationary device with free self movement restricted by a steep circumferential shoulder of the head and a large flange associated with the device. This device is intended to stay in a stationary position. U.S. PatentNo. 5,797,950 to the present inventor utilizes anal sphincter contractions to massage the prostate by reciprocal linear movement of the apparatus held by a rod set against the perineum region.
[0004] U.S. Patent No. 6,589,193 to the present inventor utilizes the entire anal canal's static and dynamic lateral pressure and the peristaltic activity of the anal canal to massage the internal and external hemorrhoids. With the exception of U.S. patent No. 6,589,193, no previous invention mobilizes the apparatus to massage the internal and external hemorrhoids by the activity of the pecten and the peristalistic movement within the anal canal.
