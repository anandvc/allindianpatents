Title of Invention

"A MUSCLE STIMULATOR"
Abstract This invention relates to a muscle stimulator characterized in that is a transformer, voltage selector, diodes, a capacitor, an I.C., a voltage selector switch and a pair of sensors wherein transformer provided for low voltage required to operate the muscle stimulator, the output terminals of said transformer connected to the input terminals of a voltage selector having lower and upper terminal; diodes being connected between the upper terminals of first, second and third contact and also between lower terminal of first, second and third terminal, a capacitor being connected between the lower terminals of fourth and fifth contacts, an I.C. is connected to the lower terminals of sixth, seventh and eighth contacts, a voltage selector switch is connected to the upper terminals of the sixth and seventh contacts, a pair of sensors being connected to the common points of I.C. and the upper terminal of the seventh contacts and to the common point of the capacitor and lower terminal of fourth contact, so as to identify sphincter muscle of the anus
Full Text This invention relates to a muscle stimulator for use by the Doctors during the operation. Particularly this invention relates to a muscle stimulator for the identification of sphincter muscle.
Sometime babies are born without a normal anal orifice. The operation done for the correction of this anomaly is called Posterior Sagittal Anorectoplasty. In this operation the blind ending rectum is opened and re-located at the proposed site of the anul verge. The whole operation requires very precise definition of the anatomy so that the body can control the passage of stools at will. This can. only be achieved if this muscle which control the passage of stool are identical with precision and which is a very difficult job.
The main disadvantages is that the operation is done on the basis of Pre-sumptions/guess and therefore such operation are not always successful.
Therefore, the main object of this invention is to propose a muscle stimulator to identify the muscles which control the passage of stool.
According to this invention there is provided a muscle stimulator characterized in that is a transformer, voltage selector, diodes, a capacitor, an I.C., a voltage selector switch and a pair of sensors wherein transformer provided for low voltage required to operate the muscle stimulator, the output terminals of said transformer connected to the input terminals of a voltage selector having lower and upper terminal; diodes being connected between the upper terminals of first, second and third contact and also between lower terminal of first, second and third terminal, a capacitor being connected between the lower terminals of fourth and fifth contacts, an I.C. is connected to the lower terminals of sixth, seventh and eighth contacts, a voltage selector switch is connected to the upper terminals of the sixth and seventh contacts, a pair of sensors being connected to the common points of I.C. and the upper terminal of the seventh contacts and to the common point of the capacitor and lower terminal of fourth contact, so as to identify sphincter muscle of the anus.
In accordance with this invention the muscle stimulator has a transformer adapted to be connected to the A.C mains supply. The output terminals of the transformer are connected to a voltage/current selector through an on-off switch such that to select the current to supply to a pair of sensors provided to sense/identify the sphincter muscles of the anus. A control switch is provided with the selector to select the desired voltage/current to be supplied to the pair of sensors. The current to be supplied to the sensors is between 50 to 280 ma and the voltage is between 15 to 20 volts. An indicator is connected to the selector to indicate the working of the selector.
