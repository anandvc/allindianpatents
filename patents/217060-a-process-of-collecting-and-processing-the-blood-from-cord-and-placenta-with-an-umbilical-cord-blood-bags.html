Title of Invention

A PROCESS OF COLLECTING AND PROCESSING THE BLOOD FROM CORD AND PLACENTA WITH AN UMBILICAL CORD BLOOD BAGS
Abstract An Umbilical Cord blood bag system for a process of collection of blood from Cord and placenta, which comprises a triple bag system with a primary blood collection bag (A) containing coagulant, a first processing satellite bag (B) containing HES solution and a second satellite bag (C) alongwith a first transfer bag(D) containing cryo solution and a second transfer bag (E) containing wash solution all interconnectable suitably. The process comprises of admitting the umbilical blood into bag (A), transferring HES from bag (B) into bag (A), centrifuging bag (A) so as to push the upper leukocytes containing stems cell into bag (B), which bag is further agitated alongwith cryo solution admitted from the bag (D), such that undesired plasma is thrown out to bag (C), followed by wash solution admitted from bag E and then bag B is sealed and chilled.
Full Text The fourth objective is the processing of the collected blood in the closed system, by removing RBCs and plasma end getting the isolated stem cells in a small volume of cryo solution.
The fifth objective is to preserve the stem cells in the cryo solution at low temperature.
The sixth objective is to remove the cryo solution before use of stem cells and resuspend the stem cells in a solution safe for administration.

The above paras have described the various bags, packs, ports and tubes used as apparatus/means to achieve the object of the invention. We now give below the process and sequence involved for collecting umbilical blood, separation of stem cells and their further storage until use in recipients.
The process steps in sequence using the above system as per the invention are :-
1. Collection of Placenta
Collection of placenta after it has been delivered and the umbilical cord clamped and cut in the sterile plastic bag (X) and its transfer to a laminar flow in a sterile room.
2. Mounting of Placenta & Cord
The placenta with umbilical cord afler wiping with sterile cotton soaked in isopropanol is mounted on the stand (Y), with the cord hanging down. The first donor needle (1) from Collection Bag (A) is penetrated into cord vein such that the blood from umbilical cord falls via gravity into Bag (A) and further extraction of blood is done by repeating the above mentioned collection process with needle (2) of Collection bag (A) but only after removing first donor needle


WE CLAIM :
1. An Umbilical cord blood bag system for a process of collecting and
processing the umbilical cord blood to obtain stem cells and storing the stem cells and for using the stored stem cells, the said process comprising of following sequence and steps :-
1(a) Collecting umbilical cord blood from umbilical cord fitted onto
stand (Y) into the collection bag (A) and mixing the collected
umbilical cord blood with anti-coagulant solution present in the
collection Bag (A). 1(b) Admitting of H£S solution from Bag (B) into Bag (A) via the inter
connecting tube, within the closed sterile collection bag system
and thereby draining the Bag B empty. 1(c) Centrifuge at approximately 50 times gravitational force the
contents in Bag (A) so as to separate the blood into
(_) upper leukocyte rich plasma containing stem cells and;
(_) lower sedimented unwanted red cells. 1(d) Transferring the upper leukocyte rich plasma containing stem
cells from Bag (A) to Bag (B). 1(e) Centrifuge the contents in Bag (B) at a higher speed of 500 times
gravitational force so as to separate the plasma into 2 layers.
(_) Lower layer containing stem cells as pellet.
(_) Upper Layer mainly of unwanted plasma. 1(f) Transfer away the unwanted upper layer mainly of unwanted
plasma from Bag (B) to Bag (C) for disposal thus leaving behind
stem cells as pellet back in Bag (B), 1(g) Transferring the cryo solution from transfer pack (D) into bag (B)
and mixing contents in Bag (B) to obtain stem cell suspension. 1(h) Sealing of Bag B and C from bag A at collection temperature of 15 -
20" C.

l(i) Cooling the sealed bag (B) with bag C at the rate of 1- 2° C per minute until -40oC for storing purposes for short term storage or to liquid nitrogen temperature (- 196oC) for long term storage.
l(j) Using the stored stem cells suspension stored in Bag (B) when the need arises, following the sequence steps just prior to as mentioned herein below :
A. Thaw the stored Bag (B) containing stored stem cell
suspension, centrifuge and remove unwanted supernatant to
bag C.
B. Adding wash solution contained in transfer pack (£) to Bag
(B) and mixing.
C. Centrifuge the contents of stored bag (B) for separation of
suspended stem cells and transfer the unwanted
supernatant to bag C. Suspend stem cell pellets in wash
solution (sterile buffered saline containing 0.2 % solution)
from transfer pack E, for use on the recipient.
D. Resuspend the stem cells pellets in required volume of wash
solution from transfer bag E. The suspend stem cells are
now be used in the recipient.
