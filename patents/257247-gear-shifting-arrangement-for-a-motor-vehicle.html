Title of Invention

GEAR SHIFTING ARRANGEMENT FOR A MOTOR VEHICLE
Abstract 1. A gear shifting arrangement for a motor vehicle comprising a micro controller electrically coupled to a DC motor which in turn is coupled mechanically to the gearing, the said micro controller being actuated by controls operated manually, such that when the controls are actuated, the micro controller constrains the DC motor to rotate precisely to a predetermined point or points necessary for effecting a corresponding gear change.
Full Text This invention relates to a gear shifting arrangement for a motor vehicle, more particularly, though not exclusively, for two wheelers and three wheelers where the gear changing controls are hand operated or foot operated. Of course this invention can also be used in other motor vehicles as well, to which this invention is applicable.
Hand operated controls for gear changing are connected to the gearing of the vehicle by cables and linkages which require substantial effort in twisting the wrist from time to time. Foot operated controls are operated by the foot. This is also a case where manual effort is called into play from time to time.
In present day traffic such manual operation of the controls leaves the user, at the end of the day, tired and irritated at the frequent number of hand and foot related exercise he will have to resign himself to. It is therefore an object of this invention to propose a gear shifting arrangement which enable the user to carry out gear shifting effortlessly, with no strain on his body.
The gear shifting arrangement, according to this invention, comprises a micro controller electrically coupled to DC motor which in turn is coupled mechanically to the gearing, the said micro controller being actuated by controls operated manually, such that when the controls are actuated, the micro controller constrains the DC motor to rotate precisely to a predetermined point or points necessary for effecting a corresponding gear change.
The DC motor is powered by the power source provided for the vehicle such as the battery with which the vehicle may be provided or a separate DC source.
Reference will now be made to the accompanying drawings which illustrate, by way of example, and not by way of limitation, one of possible embodiments of the invention.
