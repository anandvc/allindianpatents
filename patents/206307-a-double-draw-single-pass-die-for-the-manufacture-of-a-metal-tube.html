This invention relates to double draw single pass die for the manufacture of a metal tube.
The conventional system consists of two independent single piece dies. The material passes through one die to obtain a tube of a given reduction and thereafter the drawn tube, after heat treatment, is again drawn through a second die for further reduction. The conventional system is thus two pass two stage.
On the other hand, with the die proposed herein, sizes reduction, which is conventionally achievable in two stages, can be achieved in one stage.
With the die proposed herein, the amount of reduction achievable in a single stage is more than that achievable according to the conventional system involving two stages.
With the die proposed herein, the heat treatment process involved in the conventional system is eliminated.
Wim the die proposed herein there is increase in productivity and saving in process time and man hours.
With the die proposed herein the equipment capacity can be released to process other orders.
There is also a re duct, on of consumables and material movement.
Various other features of this invention will be apparent from the following further description thereof.
According to this invention, there is provided a double draw single pass die for the manufacture of a metal tube, comprising primary and secondary die bodies provided with mandrel means, me primary die body being intended for the initial reduction of the cross-sectional area of the tube and the secondary die body for further reduction of the said area, both die bodies having a relieving angle at each outlet, characterized in that the said bodies are fastened together end to end, to form a single unit, with the outlet of the primary die body abutting the inlet of the secondary die body; the entry angle at the mouth of the primary die body is substantially 17 degrees, while the secondary dm body does not have any entry angle but onfy a curvature at its

mouth; and the outlet of the primary die body has a predetermined relieving distance for preventing the drawn material from getting deformed while leaving the primary die body and before entering the secondary die body.
