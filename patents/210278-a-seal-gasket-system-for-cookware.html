Title of Invention

A SEAL GASKET SYSTEM FOR COOKWARE
Abstract The invention relates to a seal gasket system for cookware, specifically for domestic cookware system, which is made of an elastomer or polymer, which provides a seal between the body and the lid of the cookware when placed such that its grooves fit into lid, which lid is mated with body and the lower skirt portion of the said gasket will protrude and extend against the inner periphery of the body of the cookware.

BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
This invention in general relates to cookware gasket. More especially this invention relates to novel gasket for domestic cookware. There are many varieties of the gaskets available in the market. The art of using gasket for domestic cookware Is yet unknown in the market.
This invention relates to a system that provides a Soft Seal between the top of any cookware and the edge of its lid. This system can be used for the sealing-in of vapours and steam generated in various vessels such as, but not limited to, domestic cookware including the category commonly known as non-stick cookware. This is a complete gasket system which is made to match and mate with the edge of any lid made of any appropriate materials like glass, metal or plastic on the one hand and having a soft skirt on the lower portion that fits inner top edge of the cookware matching the lid on the other hand.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RELATED ART
The disadvantage of the existing lids is that they have a rigid edge. Even when they have a vertical lip fitting inside the top of the cookware this lip is not in complete juxtaposition with the inner surface of the cookware. Because of this, during use the vapours or steam generated inside the vessel do not get sealed. The hot vapours tend to escape randomly around the edge of the vessel. Further such a rigid lid damages the inner nonstick surface when used in conjunction with nonstick cookware.
Another disadvantage with existing cookware systems is that during normal use or inadvertently by being dropped from the hand the edge of the lid tends to develop nicks or other distortions, which aggravates the initial poor sealing.

A further disadvantage is that in case of lids made of glass, ceramic or other brittle material any accidental knock or fall tends to break the lid, sometimes in a hazardous manner. In the interest of easy handling and less breakage of cookware lid during handling, the preferred embodiment provides for gasket shape and size such that the gasket act as a shock absorber to the lid during a fall.
0B3ECT OF THE INVENTION
The object of the invention is to contrive a novel gasket to be used in domestic cookware. Consequence of this is that when used in cookwares, the rigid material does not adjust to imperfect edges and profile of the lids and the containers. This results in gaps in certain parts resulting in escape of vapour from the cookware at all such open spots. At certain spots, there will be pressure on the lid to fit into the profile of the cookware. The portion of the lids thus pressed additionally will cause damage to the lid and the cookware.
The invention consisting of a soft seal gasket system obviates these disadvantages in addition to providing some extra advantages. It takes care of the profile of the lid and container. The concept of the system is such that it is adaptable to all manner of lids made of a variety of materials like glass,ceramic, metal or plastic or a comibanation of these. It is adaptable to all manner of shapes of the lid such as round, square or any other shape. Hence the container, lid and gasket are capable of being used without causing undue damage to all three components and sealing in the vapours within the cookware.

According to this invention, the enhanced efficiency and safety factor originates from the composition and shape of the gasket, which has been developed by the inventors,
A gasket as per the invention comprises of soft shape with a profile designed to mate with the edge of the lid with top groove designed to accommodate onto the edge of the lid and the lower skirt designed to accommodate on to the edge of the lid and the lower skirt designed to accommodate into the inner periphery of the top of the vessel.
