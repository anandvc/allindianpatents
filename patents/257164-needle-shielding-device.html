Title of Invention

NEEDLE SHIELDING DEVICE
Abstract NEEDLE SHIELDING DEVICE A needle assembly with a needle shield is disclosed. The shield includes a lumen slidable on the needle, a holder and a needle blocking object which moves at least partially into the lumen in order to block distal movement of the needle. A spring biases the blocking object towards the needle axis. Proximal movement of the needle relative to the shield is limited by a restraint in the form of a discontinuity on the needle which abuts the shield, a tether or a tube abutting the needle hub. When the needle shield moves from a non-shielding position to a shielding position, the needle blocking object moves at least partially into the lumen. The needle blocking object is radially and axially constrained in the shielding position. The holder also prevents radial, longitudinal and circumferential movement of the blocking object. In one embodiment, the needle blocking object is a ball. A catheter adapter and tube may be threaded over the needle. The needle blocking object may also lock the catheter adapter to the needle shield, allowing it to be released only when the needle is shielded.

BACKGROUND This patent application relates to medical devices using needles such as spinal needles, intravenous catheter introducers, blood collection devices and syringes. It includes a device for shielding needles used in such devices.
SUMMARY OF THE INVENTION A first embodiment of the invention is a medical device - a needle assembly (for example a catheter introducer, a syringe, a winged needle or a Huber needle). The assembly includes a needle and needle shield slidable on the needle. A housing, forming part of the shield carries a blocking object (preferably a ball), which is biased by a spring. The housing has an internal

lumen into which the needle fits so that the housing can slide along the needle. In a first, non-shielding position, in which the tip of the needle is exposed, the spring biases the blocking object towards the longitudinal axis of the needle. In a second shielding position, in which the needle tip is shielded, the blocking object lies at least partially in the internal lumen of the housing thereby blocking emergence of the sharp distal end from the needle shield assembly. A restraint prevents movement of the needle shield assembly off the sharp distal end of the needle. The restraint may be in the form of an abutment between a discontinuity on the needle and the needle shield, a tube abutting a flange on the needle hub or a tether secured to the needle hub.
The part of the shield which constitutes the blocking object carrier is configured to limit movement of the blocking object towards, around or along the longitudinal axis of the needle. To that end the blocking object is carried in an opening in the first housing which is suitably shaped and dimensioned and extends from the outer wall to the internal limen. A cap at least partially covers the first housing.
The cap is provided with a side hole, through which, the ball protrudes in thefirst non-shielding position. The spring is a compression spring, but may also be a torsion or other type of spring. It is preferably coaxial with the housing and needle and is mounted on the outside- of the housing, generally parallel to the longitudinal axis of the needle.
An overhang is provided which also constrains the blocking object in a radial direction relative to the longitudinal axis of the needle in the second, shielding position. Alternatively or additionally, the spring constrains the blocking object in a radial direction relative to the longitudinal axis of the needle. The shield is also provided with a wall, distal of the blocking object, which constrains the blocking object in the longitudinal direction in the second, shielding

position. The opening in the blocking object earner is configured to prevent the blocking object from fully entering the lumen. The lumen supports the needle opposite the blocking object in the second, shielding position. In the second shielding position, the blocking object lies at least partially in the himen and the blockmg object axis is offset from the needle axis.
The invention also involves a method of shielding the tip of a needle. A blocking object, carried by a housing having a lumen, is maintained in a non-shielding position, displaced radially relative to, and biased towards, the longitudinal axis of the needle. The needle slides relative to the blocking object The needle shield moves from the non-shielding position to a shielding position such that the tip of the needle passes the blocking object, such that the blocking object enters the lumen at least partially, thereby blocking the sharp distal tip of the needle. In the non-shielding position, the blocking object touches theouter surbce of the needle and the spring biases the blocking object against the outer surface of the needle. Radial, longitudinal and/or circumferential movement of the blocking object are constrained. In the shielding position the axis of the blocking object is offset from the longitudinal axis of the needle.

DETAILED DESCRIPTION The following is a description of embodiments of the invention as applied to catheter introducers, a syringes and other needle based devices. It is not intended to limit the scope of the invention.
The invention may be applied to a wide variety of needle based devices such as catheter introducers, syringes, winged needles and Huber needles. In almost all cases, shielding a needle involves providing a needle shield and ensuring that it does not come off the sharp distal end of the needle or move proximally, thereby re-exposing the sharp distal end. Some sort of locking mechanism or mechanisms must therefore prevent distal and proximal movement of the shield once the needle is shielded.
IN the present invention, proximal movement of the shield is prevented by the assembly shown in Figs. lA, B and C and Fig.2. Assembly 1 comprises needle 10, havin g a longitudinal axis 11, an outer surface 12 and a sharp distal end 15. Needle shield assembly 90 has an internal lumen 93, which is coaxial with needle 10. Needle shield assembly 90 is shown inside a catheter

adapter or hub 23 in Fig. 1 A. Shield assembly 90 is made up of a first housing 95 which is covered with a cap 100, First housing 95 has a stepped area or area of reduced diameter 105 onto which spring 2 is threaded. First housing 95 has an opening 18 which with first housing 95 forms a holder: or carrier for ball 3. Opening 18 extends from outer wall 19 through to lumen 93, Opening 18 is configured such that ball 3 can move in it but the movement of ball 3 is restricted radially, longitudinally and circumferentially relative to axis 11.
In the non-shielding position shown in Fig. 1 A, ball 3 protrudes through hole 21 ia cap 100. Spring 2 exerts a force on ball 3 which has an axial component and a component radially towards axis 11. In the non-shielding position, ball 3 touches outer siuface 12 of needle 10. The biasing force of spring 2 thus makes ball 3 tend towards axis 11.
As shield assembly 90 slides along needle 10, it approaches distal end 15, The biasing force in spring 2 forces baU 3 at least partially into lumen 93 and it leaves hole 21 and moves radially towards axis 11 in opening 18, as the beveled of needle 10 passes ball 3. Due to the geometry of opening 18, when the bevel has passed by, ball 3 lies at least partially in lumen 93. Axis 23 of ball 3 lies offset from axis 11. Radial movement of ball 3 is restricted by spring 2 and by top wall 20 of cap 100. Axial movement of the ball is also restricted by front wall 22 of opening 18. Distal movement of needle 10 forces ball 3 against wall 22. If shield 90 now slides proximally (ie. needle 10 slides distally), needle. 10 will be blocked by ball 3, which lies at least partially in lumen 15 and movement of which is limited by spring 2 and walls 20 and 22,
Referring to Fig. 3, wall 20 of cap 100 forms an angle a tangential to ball 3 when ball 3 is moving into its position at least partially occluding lumen 93. This angle a is set at a value less than the smallest bevel angle p of needle tip 15. 'In the embodiment described here, the

angle a between wall 20 and ball 3 is about zero degrees. If that angle is made too large relative to angle p, ball 3 will not be trapped.
The above operation is described in greater detail and with sUght variations in the remainder of this specification in the context of catheter introducers, syringes and other needle-based medical devices. Three types of catheter introducer are shown, In the first, distal movement of the needle shield off the sharp end of the needle is restramed by means of an abutment between the needle shield and a discontinuity on the introducer needle. In the second, the needle shield is on the end of a tubular member, distal movement of which is restrained by an abutment with a member attached to the needle hub. In all cases, proximal movement and hence pulling back of the needle to expose the sharp end of the needle, is prevented by the device described above.
