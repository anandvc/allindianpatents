Figure 1 shows the frequencization technique apparatus. It illustrates glass pot (1), laboratory glass test tube (2), glass cork (3), sand (4), magnets (5), herbal / electropathic / homeopathic medicine (6) & hair (7).
A glass pot / flask is filled with cleaned and washed sand gravel approximately up to half its height. Another glass laboratory test tube with a glass cork is placed vertically at the bottom of the glass pot / flask. Around this test tube, a number of magnets (having north and south poles) are placed such that it is surrounded by the magnets. The height of the glass test tube is more than that of the height of the sand filled in the glass pot / flask. Hair (or any part belonging of the body whose physical composition does not change) of the client is placed in the test tube. The test tube is filled with Herbal, Electropathic or Homoeopathic medicine as per the prescription of the practitioner. The glass cork is placed on the mouth of the test tube. Precaution should be taken before closing the cork that one end of the hair must remain outside the test tube in the open atmosphere. The other end and a major part of the hair should remain immersed in the liquid medicine (having odd force / vital force / natural energy). This complete apparatus is called as frequencization technique apparatus; this apparatus which should always be kept in a room in normal open atmosphere. Heat should be avoided. The hair immersed in the liquid medicine is obtained from the body of the client. When the client changes, the hair will also be replaced. The liquid medicine will also be replaced as per the requirements of the client (complaints of the patient and disease if any) and as per the prescription of the practitioner. Vertical strokes (upward and downward strokes by hand) will be given to the test tube as per requirements. The client gets healthier in due course of time.
