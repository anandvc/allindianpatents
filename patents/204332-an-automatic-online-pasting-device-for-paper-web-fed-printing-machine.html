Title of Invention

AN AUTOMATIC ONLINE PASTING DEVICE FOR PAPER WEB FED PRINTING MACHINE
Abstract An automatic online pasting device for paper web-fed printing machine comprising: i. a wheel assembly comprising atleast a first wheel and a second wheel; said first wheel is larger than the second wheel; ii. said first wheel adapted to rotate when in friction contact with a web being fed from the printing machine; iii. said second wheel disposed in contact with an adhesive source and adapted to relatively rotate along with the first wheel wherein upon rotation of the first wheel, second wheel transfers the adhesive source to the first wheel for applying the adhesive on the web being fed.

FIELD OF INVENTION
The present invention relates to an automatic on-line pasting device for paper web fed printing machine. The device according to the present invention, is constructed to suit the configuration of the web printing machines used for printing purpose.
PRIOR ART
In prior art no such automatic on-line device is present and used for pasting the paper bunch. There are other methods used, these are as follows :
1. Stapling the paper bunch after completion of printing operation either manually or with separate machine.
2. Stitching the bunch of paper after completion of printing operation either manually or with separate machine.
3. Some times the papers are also pasted separately after completion of printing operation.
The above mentioned methods have certain drawbacks.
1. Additional requirement of manpower results in consumption of extra labour hours.
2. More handling of job resulting in to wastage of material.

3. Another machine has to be installed to staple or to stitch the
paper bunch after completion of printing operation. Therefore more consumption of power resulting in increase in initial cost.
OBJECTS OF THE INVENTION
It is an object of the present invention to provide an automatic online pasting device for paper web fed printing machine to suit the configuration of a web fed printing machine.
It is an object of the present invention to develop an automatic on line pasting device which is economical and which does not require any special mounting arrangement and can be mounted on the existing bars of the machine as per the pasting requirements.
SUMMARY OF THE INVENTION
According to the most salient feature of the present invention an automatic on line pasting device does not require any special driving arrangement as it is self driven using the friction force of the web being fed.
According to this invention therefore, an automatic on line pasting device for paper web fed printing machine comprises of a wheel assembly having atleast a pair of wheels, one relatively larger than other, placed preferably in inclined position with the help of bush and bearing in the centre such that the larger wheel is preferably on the upper side and the walls are provided on either side of the said wheel assembly. An over flow drain tray is provided at the bottom having an outlet, a vertical

clamp is connected to the said wheel assembly by means of threaded bolt along with the spring, the slide is provided below the said wheel assembly to fix the position and to avoid the play.

DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF THE INVENTION AND PREFERRED EMBODIMENTS THEREOF
The present invention discloses a n automatic online pasting device for a web fed printing machine.
An automatic on line pasting device comprises of a wheel assembly (1) having atleast a first wheel (1a) and a second wheel (1b) wherein preferably, the first wheel (1a) is relatively larger than the second wheel (1b). The wheel assembly (1) is mounted on a main clamp (2) in inclined position preferably with first wheel (1a) on the upper side, using bush and bearing in the centre of both the wheels such that the first wheel (1a) is set in light pressure contact with the second wheel (1b). The second wheel (1b) is driven by the first wheel (1a) wherein the first wheel (1a) is driven by the friction contact of the web paper (15) being fed from

the printing machine (not shown). An adhesive (4) is supplied to the second wheel (1b) with the help of an adhesive supply tube (3) provided above the second wheel (1b). As the second wheel (1b) receives the drop of adhesive (4) continuously, and is driven by the first wheel (1a), the transfer of adhesive takes place from the second wheel (1b) to the first wheel (1a) and automatically to the web paper (15) which is set in contact with the first wheel (1a).
Walls (5) are provided on either side of said wheel assembly (1) so that the adhesive (4) does not splash on the web paper (15). An over flow drain tray (6) is provided at the bottom of the said wheel assembly (1) having an outlet (7). A vertical clamp (8) is connected to the wheel assembly (1) by means of a threaded bolt (9) which is integral part of wheel assembly (1) along with the spring (10). The threaded bolt (9) is mounted through the vertical clamp (8) at lower end with nut (11) on the end.
A grub screw (12) is provided on upper side of the said vertical clamp (8) for mounting and fixing the said wheel assembly (1) on the existing bars of the web fed printing machine (not shown). A slide (13) is provided below the said wheel assembly to fix the position and avoid the play.
An in built recess tank (14) is below the smaller wheel such that the smaller wheel is partly dipped in the tank with adhesive (4) which ensures the supply of adhesive (4) to the second wheel (1b), in case of the supply of adhesive from the adhesive supply tube (3) trips.

The present invention proposes to achieve the following adventages:
1. Very easy to mount as the device is very light in weight.
2. Setting capability for friction contact because of the nut and spring arrangement.
3. Over drain facility for cleanliness of operations.
4. Positive operation by friction driven wheels for application of adhesive.

I CLAIM
1. An automatic online pasting device for paper web-fed printing machine
comprising:
i. a wheel assembly comprising atleast a first wheel and a second wheel; said first wheel is larger than the second wheel;
ii. said first wheel adapted to rotate when in friction contact with a web being fed from the printing machine;
iii. said second wheel disposed in contact with an adhesive source and adapted to relatively rotate along with the first wheel wherein upon rotation of the first wheel, second wheel transfers the adhesive source to the first wheel for applying the adhesive on the web being fed.
2. The device as claimed in claim 1 wherein the first wheel and said second wheel are mounted at an inclined position relative the web feeding direction.
3. The device as claimed in claim for 2 wherein walls are disposed on either side of the wheel assembly, to prevent splashing of the adhesive.
4. The device as claimed in any one of the claims 1 to 3 wherein a drain tray having an outlet is disposed on the underside of the wheel assembly, to collect the excess adhesive from a tank to the second wheel.

5. The device as claimed in any one of the claims 1 to 4 wherein a drain tray having an outlet is disposed on the underside of the wheel assembly, to collect the excess adhesive after transfer.
6. The device as claimed in any one of the foregoing claims wherein the adhesive source is a tube which supplies adhesive from a tank to the second wheel.
7. The device as claimed in any one of the claims 1 to 5 wherein the adhesive source comprises a supplement tank in which the second wheel in partially dipped, for sourcing adhesive when adhesive supply tube trips.
8. The device according to any one of the foregoing claims wherein a nut and spring arrangement in the wheel assembly enables setting of degree of friction contact between the first wheel against the paper web.
