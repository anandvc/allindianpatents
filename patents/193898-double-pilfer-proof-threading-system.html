Title of Invention

DOUBLE PILFER PROOF THREADING SYSTEM
Abstract A double pilfer-proof threading system, in general relates to pilfer-proof bottles and more particularly to pilfer-proof threading bottle neck of the kind which once unscrewed or the closure is opened leaves behind evidence of prior opening and the bottle cannot be used for adulteration or refilling or diluting the contents since the bottle cannot be capped again. In the present invention the exterior wall of the neck portion of the bottle is provided with double threading and triple threading features at two places with Top Circumferential supporting beads and a bottom supporting bead and a middle ring at specified distances and measurements such that when the cap body member with circular extended web is crimp fitted on to the bottle neck after filling in the liquid the edges of the extended circular web of the cap turns slightly inwards and the interior surface of the extended circular web firmly grips the double thread surfaces exterior and the other features of the neck on the bottle mentioned herein above similarly. Hence tampering of the cap leaves behind the hold ring which is not removable and prevents adulteration, misuse and shows tampering.

The present invention relates to a double pilfer-proof threading system for glass containers used in Bottling units to prevent the liquid being diluted, adulterated, contaminated or misuse of the contents of the bottle. Tampering of the cap, leaves behind as an evidence a hold ring of the extended circular web cap which cannot be lifted off the neck.
In most of the industries where the contents are required to be preserved in tact for consumption, particularly in the Distilleries, Soft-drinks, Pharmaceuticals, food and vinegars for household, a number of devices have been used to secure the contents by means of closures. A number of devices for closure of bottles have been made, as an on-going business exercise, to improve and secure the containers. In the early days of the business, the use of corks, plastics, crown corks, metals and also pilfer proof caps came into vogue as and when the growth and technology of bottle closure industry improved. The progress of the bottle closure or capping industry which commenced with manual operations by hand for making the devices is now coping with advanced automatic machines.
When metallic bottle closure became popular, tampering of bottle, hit the reputed brands of containers. Due to the increasingly high occurrences of tampering of bottles in the Distilleries and other places, many attempts have been made in recent years towards producing tamper-proof bottle closures or caps which could signal or alert potential consumers, that tampering or unauthorized access of the container has transpired. A number of ways or methods have been adopted by the industry such as tear-away strip, caps and other devices, including cap and bottle neck configuration to provide easy on and off use of the cap by the bottler and ultimately by the end user of the bottled product.
Pilferproof devices of the caps for bottles has been the subject of a continuous research for many years and many patents have been granted for various pilfer proof bottle. The quest for search for a new system of device to achieve the set objectives mentioned herein is distinct from the prior art.

Amongst the Patents granted, mention might be made of U.S.Patent No.5,711,443 and U.S.Patent No.6,332,55061. In these devices there are many set backs in manufacturing or disadvantages and suffer from some drawbacks or other. For instance the cited Patents granted are mainly a Tamper Evident Container closure whereas the present invention is a double pilfer proof threading system comprising of the threads on the upper and lower parts of the neck of the bottle with top circumferential beads at the places in combination with the extended cap body member with circular web extended downwards crimp fitted on to the bottom circumferential bead in such a way that the hold ring cannot be taken off from the neck of the bottle.
The U.S.Patent No.6,332,55061 is a closure cap consisting of an outer tamper evident cap engagable over an inner container sealing cap; cap retaining prongs on the outer cap for placement on the container neck, a tear strip for severing the outer cap between its upper and lower ends, the former holds on to the bottle and the later enables to open the bottle by severence. All these innovations are concerned and rotates round the cap structure alone. It is noteworthy that the outer cap is used as a cup for drinking or for drinking medicine. The U.S.Patent No.5,711,443 is also a closure cap with a cap retaining prongs on the outer cap and also a tear strip for severing the outer cap. In the cited Patents greater emphasis is made of the tear strip removal and of cap retaining prongs.
The present invention has overcome many of the disadvantages of the existing cap and neck devices and has come out with a double pilfer proof threading system wherein the tampering is made evident and re-filling of the bottle or misuse of the contents is prevented as the broad band or hold-ring which forms part of the extended circular web of the cap member prevents being lifted off the neck of the bottle when the cap is unscrewed.
In the present invention, the combination of the extended web downwards with the features on the top neck portion of the bottle with circumferential band with the tear of web and the circumferential ends made engagable by crimp fitting inwards, that holds the hold ring or broad band onto the bottle bending inwards. At the same time the grip of the hold ring is so firm due to crimp fitting which gives a firm hold on to the bottle neck.

The container sealing cap may, at a glance, gives an impression of similarity but a closer look at the U.S.Patents and the present invention have many dissimilarities. Basically the U.S.Patents are for a container closure with tamper evident hold. After the tear off the cap could be lifted off easily as there are no threads on the bottleneck. Cap retaining prongs on the outer cap which permits placement. Sealing cap is cylindrically curved to an external radius. The cap is externally sized to have either a relatively snug fit or a somewhat loose or sliding fit within the upper portion of the Cap. The ribs are in pairs with longitudinal spike like ribs. Further the closure cap have an outer cap and inner cap including a circumferential side walls having upper and lower ends and the cap has an inner circumferential shoulder.
From the above description it is obvious that the tamper-evident cap in both the cited Patents are mainly manufactured in a mould and fitted on to the glass bottle having all the features described including an outer and inner cap. The tearing up seal and hold ring are also embodied therein.
In spite of the commonality of the hold ring, tear up strip or the tearing webs, the present invention is entirely different in formation of the various parts and functions. This is due to the combination of the feature of the threading system on the bottle neck as described hereunder and the firm grip and support to the inner surface of the circular web extended downwards and to help edges of the circular web of the cap to turn inwards and prevents lifting of the hold ring due to crimp fitting of the edges.
The novelty of the present invention resides in the formation of the threads on the upper and the lower parts of the neck of the bottle with the top circumferential bead in between and with the bottom thread at the lower portion in combination with the circumferential bead which forms the hold ring and the extended cap body member with circular web extended downward which is crimp fitted on to the neck portion of the glass bottle when the interior of the said circular extended body of the web holds on to the exterior shape of the features on the top of the neck with a firm grip and when the cap is opened or tampered the holding cap prevents being lifted off the neck of the bottle and is left behind as proof of tampering.

Accordingly the main objectives of the present invention are as under:
a) To provide a double pilfer-proof threading system to prevent misuse of the contents or adulteration of the contents, such that, the closure of the container is made impracticable by means of the broad based band or hold-ring being made a permanent fixture and not removable
b) Another object of the invention is to provide consumers with a genuine product.
c) Other objects are in part obvious and will in part, appear hereafter.
Accordingly, the present invention of a double pilfer-proof threading system for glass bottles consisting of the essential features positioned in a sequence on the exterior of the top neck portion of the said bottles in combination of a cap body member with circular web extended downwards comprises of:
a) a top threading portion on the exterior of the glass bottle on the neck portion.
b) a top circumferential broad band, a means for the resting of the rupturable circumferential webs of the extended web of the cap or tear off portion.
c) a middle ring a means for a tight grip for the interior surface of the body of the circular extended cap when crimp fitted on the neck of the bottle.
d) a bottom threading portion, a means for the interior surface of the cap web to hold a firm grip on the bottom thread and prevent being taken off the hold ring of the bottle.
e) a bottom circumferential bead, a means for the interior surface of the cap web to hold firm grip and support and to help the edges of the circular ribs of the cap to turn inwards when crimpped and prevent lifting of the holding after removal of cap upper portion.

. f) a tear off portion or a rupturable circumferential web portion situated above the said middle ring on the surface of the top circumferential bead, a means for tear off and facilitate removal of the cap from the bottle
g) when the said circular cap body member is crimp fitted on to the neck of the bottle, the exterior of the body closely and firmly grips on to the neck and take the shape of the features of the neck portion and
h) the edge of the circular cap body member extended downwards turns inwards and in the process firmly holds the bottom circumferential bead which acts as a means for preventing the hold-ring being lifted off from the neck of the bottle.
i) The formation of the threads on the upper and the lower parts of the neck bottle with the top circumferential bead in between with the circumferential bead which forms the hold ring and the extended cap body member with the circular web extended downwards crimp fitted on to the neck of the glass bottle, hold the bottom circumferential bead which prevents the hold ring being taken off from the neck of the bottle.
