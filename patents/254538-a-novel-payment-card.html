Title of Invention

"A NOVEL PAYMENT CARD"
Abstract The body of the inventive payment card is made of a precious or rare metal, bone or fine wood. The elements of a payment system feature, an emitting bank features and protection and personalization elements are also made from said materials and precious stones by engraving and incrustation. An area for a replaceable standard chip is also provided. Said invention makes it possible to increase the period of use and the reliability of the protection level of the payment card against imitation and counterfeit cards. Fig.l
Full Text Field of the invention
The present invention relates to a novel payment card that is an integrated financial product enabling to use it for making a variety of payments, in particular for managing money resources for the purposes of guaranteed payments to the organization, which has issued the card, e.g., for managing money at a personal account.
Prior Art
It is known that at present a wealthy and privileged part of clients provides a major part of bank income derived from issuing plastic cards; on an average, 10 per cent of such clients effect a half of the total number of transactions.
It is necessary to note that strategies of many companies are aimed at providing banks with financial products capable of satisfying needs of privileged clients in full. For example, cards of the Premium category enable the cooperating banks to:
• increase the loyalty of their existing clients;
• increase the volume of their cashless expenditures;
• attract new clients.
As an example, Europay International Company, being the biggest international pay system with the strategic goal to become the most preferable international pay system in Europe, offers to banks three types of cards included into the Premium category; each of them being intended for a definite category of privileged clients and is clearly positioned in the respective market segment.
The segmentation of the Premium product is supported by the establishment of the minimum servicing standards for each type of cards and forms the basis for strengthening relations between banks and clients.
• Eurocard/MasterCard Platinum card is a card for the "upper level" in the segment of privileged clients. It is intended for businessmen making frequent business trips and interested in various bonus programs. Such clients account for 15-20 per cent of the total client base of a bank. The minimum company's standards for a Platinum card guarantee that their holders will enjoy a higher level of servicing than holders of the Gold cards.
• Eurocard/MasterCard Gold card is a card for clients, who need higher, as compared to standard, limits of expenditure and a higher level of servicing; such clients account for about 25 per cent of the total client base of a bank. The minimum range of services, as offered to holders of such cards, is inevitably higher that the offering level for the Eurocard/MasterCard Standard cards.
• Word Signia is a card for clients enjoying the right to individual banking servicing, whose income is at least US $ 160'000.- per year. The number of such clients does not exceed 5 per cent of the total client base.
The minimum servicing standards of the Word Signia card holders guarantee that the client enjoys a higher level of servicing, as compared to that of other card holders.
Thus, various types of payment cards are known, which comprise a body, a microprocessor device, elements relating to features of the respective pay system and the issuing bank, protection and personalization (New Payment Technologies. Ed. by A.I. Grizov, M. AOZT "Recon", 2001, p. 35.)
All the products known in this field (payment cards of various pay systems) have, due to their mass production, an insufficient set of features providing for individual preferences of their holders, including the requirements of uniqueness, protection, art design, etc.
However, there has always existed and will continue to exist a category of clients, where each client would like to get even more attention from banks, originality, good showing and attention to his/her person, as well as the uniqueness of a pay instrument evidencing a definite social status.
Summary of the Invention
The objective of this invention is create a V.I.P payment card that would ensure increased period of use and higher reliability of the device's level of protection against imitation and counterfeiting and, thus, would enable to expand its functionality, improve its specifications and enlarge the set of technical means for creating a pay instrument complying with wishes of privileged clients in banks.
For the purpose of achieving the stated objective three main variants of implementing the invention are possible.
According to the first variant, in a known payment card comprising a body, a microprocessor device, elements of features relating to the respective pay system and the issuing bank, those of protection and personalization, in accordance with the invention the body is made of a precious or rare metal, the elements of the features relating to the respective pay system and the issuing bank and those of protection and personalization being made of precious and/or semi-precious stones in combination, or without it, with finewood and/or bone.
According to the second variant, in a known payment card comprising a body, a microprocessor device, elements of features relating to the respective pay system and the issuing bank, those of protection and personalization, in accordance with the invention the body is made of finewood, and

the elements of the features relating to the respective pay system and the issuing bank, those of protection and personalization being made of a precious and/or rare metal in combination, or without it, with precious and or semi-precious stones and/or bone.
According to the third variant, in a known payment card comprising a body, a microprocessor device, elements of features relating to the respective pay system and the issuing bank, those of protection and personalization, in accordance with the invention the body is made of bone, and the elements of the features relating to the respective pay system and the issuing bank, protection and personalization being made of a precious and/or rare metal in combination, or without it, with precious or semiprecious stones and/or finewood.
Additional embodiments of the device are possible, wherein it is expedient that:
- gold and/or platinum and/or silver are used as precious metals;
- brilliants and/or sapphires and/or rubies and/or emeralds are used as precious stones;
- nephrite and/or lazurite and/or chalcedony are used as semi-precious stones;
- redwood and/or box-tree and/or sandal wood and/or black wood are used as finewood;
- horns and/or tusks are used as bone;
- a microprocessor device is fixed in the body with the possibility of being replaced;
- elements of features relating to the respective pay system and the issuing bank, protection
and personalization are engraved and/or inlaid and/or threaded and/or electroformed;
- elements of protection and personalization are made as fragments of heraldry, the family
coat of arms, other attributes and are arranged on the face side and/or on the reverse side of the body;
- a place for signature be provided on the reverse side of the payment card body;
- titanium and/or lithium and/or molybdenum and/or metals similar to them are used as rare
metals;
- the body is made in the form of a monolithic plate or a piece having the geometrical form of
a plate made as an open work piece and/or an engraved piece and/or a cast piece and/or a forged
piece.
The above advantages as well as the specific features of this invention will be further explained by its preferred embodiments taken with references to the appended drawings.

Description of Preferred Embodiments
An example of a payment card according to the first embodiment is shown in a photo of a mock-up (FIG. 1), where on the face side and the reverse side shown are: the body 1, the elements 2, 3 of the features relating to the pay system and the issuing bank, the microprocessor device 4, the elements 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11 of protection and personalization (Fig. 1, 2), the dielectric element 12 made as a frame (FIG. 1).
In the shown example the body 1 of the payment card is made of a precious metal; the elements 2, 3 are made of precious stones; the microprocessor device 4 is a standard electronic chip and is installed in a definite place; the elements 5, 6 are author's art elements of protection and are made of a precious metal and precious stones, elements of protection also being made on the reverse side by engraving and inlaying; 7 - a hallmark, if it is required by law, 8 - the author's stamp, 9 - a manufacturer's trademark; 10 - the place for the signature of the payment card holder; 11 - a place for individual attributes; the element 12 is made of bone or finewood.
According to the second embodiment the body 1 of the payment card is made of finewood; the elements 2, 3 are made of precious or semi-precious stones; the microprocessor device 4 is a standard state-of-the-art electronic chip available at the time of making a payment card in accordance with this invention; the elements 5, 6 are inlaid works made of a precious metal and/or precious stones; 7, 8, 9 are places for a hallmark, if it is required by law, the author's stamp, a manufacturer's trademark, these may be made as inserts of a precious metal; 10 - the place for the signature of the payment card holder, it may also be made as a precious metal insert; 11 - a place for individual attributes; the element 12 is made of bone or a precious metal.
According to the third embodiment the body 1 of the payment card is made of bone; the elements 2, 3 are made of precious stones; the microprocessor device is a standard unit; the elements 5, 6 are made of a precious metal and/or precious stones; 7, 8, 9 are places for a hallmark, if it is required by law, the author's stamp, a manufacturer's trademark; 10 - the place for the signature; 11 - a place for individual attributes; 12 is a deepened spot in the body, which is inlaid with finewood and a precious metal.
As far as the working of the invention is concerned, according to the first embodiment the body 1 is made of gold, silver, platinum, rare metals, such as titanium, lithium, molybdenum or similar metals.

Soft metals are melted, poured into a form, rolled to the required thickness; hard metals are cut and machined; precious stones are fixed with the use of corners, which are cut in advance in the body by special burins. Recesses or holes are made along the contour of a stone for semi-precious stones, which then are fixed according to a known technology (a glue, a setting, etc.).
Then everything is turned, grounded and polished according to the thickness of the body 1; the element 12, which is inlaid with bone or finewood, apart from ensuring art perception, performs the function of a dielectric material for the microprocessor 4, bone and finewood are glued into elements of deep engraving and curled for ensuring reliable fixation.
According to the second embodiment the body 1 is made of finewood by sawing and treating raw finewood to required dimensions; the other materials (a precious metal, precious stones, etc.) are glued into the places prepared in advance or put into cuts and fixed on the body 1, then they are grounded and polished. The microprocessor device 4, as in the first embodiment, is installed into the place intended for it. The element 12 (a deepened spot with a frame) is inlaid with bone and a precious metal.
According to the third embodiment the bone 1 is also made of bone by sawing and treating to required dimensions; then it is manufactured in accordance with the previous embodiments for the purpose of fixing precious stones, a precious metal or finewood on it; the microprocessor device 4 is installed into the recess 12, the said element 12 being inlaid with finewood and a precious metal.
As precious metals gold, silver, platinum of standard fineness (so-called jewelry alloys) are used, which contain additives of copper, brass, bronze, lithium.
Treated diamonds, sapphires, rubies, emeralds etc. are used as precious stones; and nephrite, lazurite, malachite, chalcedony, etc. are used as semi-precious stones; thus, for example, on the mock-up photo the element 5 and the body 1 are made of gold of 583 standard fineness; the element 2, a pigeon, which is shown in the hologram of one known pay system, is made of brilliants; the element 3, the logotype of the pay system, is made of sapphires and brilliants.
Red wood, box-tree wood, sandal wood, black wood and similar kinds of wood are used as finewood in all the three embodiments. They may be used, for example, for making the dielectric frame 12 for the microprocessor device 4 according to the first embodiment of a payment card under this invention, wherein the frame 12 is both a decorative element and the dielectric substrate.
Horns of an argali, a rhinoceros, tusks of a walrus, a mammoth or similar animals may be used as jobbing bone.

For the microprocessor device 4 a container or guides may be made; in such a case it may be made replaceable.
Certain elements on the payment card body 1, as, e.g., the elements 5, 10, 11, may be made by embossing, engraving, electrotyping.
If a payment card holder wishes so, his/her family coats of arms, heraldry elements, other attributes may be used as personalization elements; moreover, they may be made of the above materials and by different technical methods.
A place for signature is provided for on the reverse side of the body 1; according to the first embodiment the signature is engraved with a special tool directly on the body 1, in a case where the body 1 is made of bone or fmewood, a precious metal insert may be made, e.g., of gold, and fixed in the place intended for signature.
Lithium, molybdenum, titanium or similar metals may be used as rare metals.
Stones of Groups I, II or III, which are suitable for proper treatment, may be used as precious or semiprecious stones, depending on the social status of a payment card holder.
The cited embodiments of this invention are not intended to limit the scope of claims of the applicant, but serve only as illustrations to the preferred embodiment of the technical solution.
It is necessary to note that as protection and personalization for the purposes of this invention the creation of a set of identifying features on the body 1 is understood, including the features established by the respective pay system and the issuing bank.
It is also necessary to state that the proposed payment card fully conforms to the international standards ISO 7810, ISO 7813, ISO 7816 as well as to the Russian standard COST R 50809.
