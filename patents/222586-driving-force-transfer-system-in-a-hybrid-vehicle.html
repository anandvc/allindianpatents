Title of Invention

"DRIVING FORCE TRANSFER SYSTEM IN A HYBRID VEHICLE"
Abstract In a hybrid vehicle including an engine 61 and a generator-motor 43 as a drive source, a driving force transfer system 60 is constituted by disposing a centrifugal clutch 62, a transmission 48 and a one-way clutch 65 in a driving force transfer path from the engine 61 up to a driving force joining point 53 where the driving force of the generator-motor 43 and that of the engine 61 join together. [Effect] By switching the generator-motor into the motor, the vehicle can be moved forward by both engine and generator-motor, while by switching the generator-motor into the generator, batteries can be charged while electric power is generated by the engine, and the vehicle can be moved forward or backward by the generator-motor alone with the engine OFF. Thus, the vehicle can be moved backward using the generator-motor as a drive source, and batteries can be charged through the generator-motor using the engine as a drive source.

[Technical Field of the Invention]
The present invention relates to an improvement of a driving force transfer system in a hybrid vehicle. [0002]
[Prior Art]
As a power system in a hybrid vehicle there is known, for example, "a hybrid propulsion system for a vehicle, especially for a streetcar" which is disclosed in Japanese Patent Laid Open No.218430/92.
As shown in Fig.l of the said unexamined publication, the above known system is provided with an

internal combustion engine 10 and a motor 20. A driving
force generated by the internal combustion engine 10 is
transmitted to a shaft 16 through a belt 25 and a clutch 14, then from the shaft 16 it is transmitted to a vehicle wheel 26 through gears 17, 18 and a shaft 19. On the other hand, a driving force generated by the motor 20 is transmitted to the shaft 16 through a free wheel 24 which is a one-way clutch to assist the internal combustion engine 10. [0003]
[Problems to be Solved by the Invention]
However, in the prior art referred to above, since the driving force of the motor 20 is transmitted to the shaft 16 through the free wheel 24 (one-way clutch), it is impossible to transmit the driving force of the internal combustion engine 10 to the motor 20 side. Thus, the motor 20 cannot be used as a generator and it is impossible to charge batteries with use of the motor 20.
Nor is it possible to rotate the vehicle wheel 26 in the opposite direction with the motor 20 to move the vehicle backward. [0004]
Accordingly, it is an object of the present invention to provide a driving force transfer system in a

hybrid vehicle capable of moving the vehicle backward with
»>
use of a generator-motor as a drive source and also capable of charging batteries through the generator-motor with use an engine as a drive source. [0005]
[Means for Solving the Problems]
In order to achieve the above-mentioned object, according to claim 1 of the present invention, in a hybrid vehicle including an engine and a generator-motor as a drive source, a driving force transfer system is constituted by disposing a centrifugal clutch, a transmission and a one-way clutch in a driving force transfer path from the engine up to a driving force joining point where the driving force from the generator-motor and that from the engine join together. [0006]
By switching the generator-motor into the motor, the vehicle is moved forward by the engine and the generator-motor, and by switching the generator-motor into the generator, batteries are charged while electric power is generated in the motor by the engine. Where required, the engine is stopped and the vehicle is moved forward or backward with the generator-motor alone. [0007]

Accordingly, the present invention relates to a hybrid vehicle including an engine and a generator-motor as a drive source, a driving force transfer system characterized in that a centrifugal clutch, a transmission and a one-way clutch are disposed in a driving force transfer path from the engine up to a driving force joining point where the driving force from the generator-motor and that from the engine join together.

The operation of the driving force transfer system 60 in the hybrid vehicle 1 (see Fig.l) described above will

be explained below with reference to Figs.5 to 7.
Figs.5(a) and (b) are first operation explaining
diagrams of the driving force transfer system according to the present invention.
Fig.5(a) shows a case where the rear wheel 14 is driven with a combined force of both engine 61 and motor 43.
The engine 61 drives the rear wheel 14 like an arrow ® successively via the inner 62a and outer 62b of the centrifugal clutch 62, cone type continuously variable transmission 48, one-way clutch 65, first transfer gear 66, second transfer gear 67 and engine-side first and second helical gears 68, 69 which are mounted on the motor shaft 53 as a joining point of the engine-motor driving forces, further via the output gear 75, propeller shaft 76, differential 78 and rear axle 51. [0013]
On the other hand, the motor 43 drives the rear wheel 14 like an arrow successively via the motor shaft 53, motor-side first and second helical gears 69, 72, output gear 75, propeller shaft 76, differential 78 and rear axle 51.
The driving force of the engine 61 and that of the motor 43 are combined together at the motor shaft 53.

[0014]
When the hybrid vehicle 1 (see Fig.l) is to be started with the engine 61, the vehicle can be started by smooth and gradual transfer of torque through the centrifugal clutch 62.
Since the centrifugal clutch 62 is disposed before the cone type continuously variable transmission 48, a smaller clutch capacity will do than that when the clutch is disposed after the transmission 48. Conversely, when viewed from the cone type continuously variable transmission 48 side, the said arrangement provides a protection for the transmission 48 because the transmission need not directly undergo an excessive torque of the engine 61. Particularly, where the clutch is used as a wet clutch, the contact pressure becomes small, so a larger clutch capacity becomes necessary if the centrifugal clutch 62 is disposed after the transmission 48, resulting 'in an increase in size of the system.
Since the cone type continuously variable transmission 48 is connected to the outer 62a of the centrifugal clutch 62 through the torque limiter 63, the engine can avoid receiving a back torque from the rear wheel 14. [0015]

Fig.5(b) shows a case where the rear wheel 14 is driven with only the motor 43.
The motor 43 drives the rear wheel 14 like an arrow (3)successively via the motor shaft 53, motor-side first and second helical gears 69, 72, output gear 75, propeller shaft 76, differential 78 and rear axle 51.
With the engine 61 OFF, the one-way clutch is open.
Since the one-way clutch 65 is disposed just before the engine-motor driving force joining point, when the rear wheel 14 is driven with the motor 43 alone, there does not occur any follow-up rotation of the load-side transmission 48 and centrifugal clutch outer 62b. Consequently, it is possible to save consumption of the batteries and a longer operation time can be ensured. [0016]
Figs.6(a) and (b) are second operation explaining diagrams of the driving force transfer system according to the present invention.
Fig.6(a) shows a case where the rear wheel 14 is driven with the only the engine 61.
The engine 61 drives the motor shaft 53 like an arrow successively via inner 62a and outer 62b of the centrifugal clutch 62, cone type continuously variable transmission 48, one-way clutch 65, first transfer gear 66

and second transfer gear 67. Thus, it is possible to let the motor 43 operate as a generator to charge the batteries (see Fig.1).
Further, the engine 61 drives the rear wheel 14 like an arrow (5) successively via the engine-side first and second helical gears 68, 71, output gear 75, propeller shaft 76, differential 78 and rear axle 51. [0017]
Fig.6(b) shows a case where the hybrid vehicle 1 (see Fig.l) is moved backward with the motor 43.
The motor 43 is rotated reverse and this rotating force is transmitted to the rear wheel 14 like an arrow (6) successively via the motor shaft 53, motor-side first and second helical gears 69, 72, output gear 75, propeller shaft 76, differential 78 and rear axle 51 to rotate the rear wheel in the reverse direction.
Although the engine 61 is OFF, the motor 43 is rotating in the reverse direction, so that the one-way clutch 65 is engaged and the driving force of the motor 43 is transmitted to the cone type continuously variable transmission 48 and the outer 62b of the centrifugal clutch 62 like an arrow (7), but there does not occur a follow-up rotation of the engine 61 by the centrifugal clutch 62. [0018]

Fig.8 is a sectional view of a torque sensor unit used in the driving force transfer system according to the present invention.
The torque sensor unit 88 comprises the counter shaft 73 referred to previously, pressure sensors 74a and 74b mounted on both sides of the counter shaft 73, engine-side second helical gear 71 and motor-side second helical gear 72 both mounted on the counter shaft 73, engine-side first helical gear 68 and motor-side first helical gear 69 which are in mesh with the gears 71 and 72, respectively.

The operation of the torque sensor unit 88 will be
described below with reference to the following figures. [0020]
Figs.9(a) and (b) are explanatory views explaining the operation of the torque sensor unit.
Fig.9(a) shows the operation of the torque sensor unit 88 during acceleration.
In acceleration, the driving force from the engine 61 (see Fig.3) or from the motor 43 side is transmitted to the rear wheel 14. That is, the engine-side first helical gear 68 and the motor-side first helical gear 69 serve as the driving side, while the engine-side second helical gear 71 and the motor-side second helical gear 72 serve as the driven side, so that the gears 71 and 72 give rise to a stress Fa in the counter shaft 73 like an arrow "a". The stress Fa is detected by the pressure sensor 74a.
Fig.9(b) shows the operation of the torque sensor unit 88 in deceleration.
During deceleration, a driving force is transmitted from the rear wheel 14 side to the motor 43 side. That is, the engine-side second helical gear 71 and the motor-side second helical gear 72 serve as the driving side, while the engine-side first helical gear 68 and the motor-side first helical gear 69 serve as the driven side, so that the gears

68 and 69 give rise to a stress Fb in the counter shaft 73
»
like an arrow "b". The stress Fb is detected by the
pressure sensor 74b.
[0021]
Thus, the magnitude of driving force and the direction of transfer are detected by the pressure sensors 74a and 74b, followed by feedback control, and they are combined with the driving force of the engine 61 or the motor 43 (see Fig.2) as a drive source, whereby the hybrid vehicle 1 (see Fig.l) cab be driven efficiently.
Since the torque sensor unit 88 is composed of the counter shaft 73, pressure sensors 74a and 74b mounted on both ends of the counter shaft 73, engine-side second helical gear 71 and motor-side second helical gear 72 both mounted on the counter shaft 73, and engine-side first helical gear 68 and motor-side first helical gear 69 meshing with the gears 71 and 72, respectively, it is possible to realize a compact and highly reliable torque detecting mechanism. [0022]
[Effect of the Invention]
Since the present invention is constructed as above, the following effects are attained.
According to claim 1, by switching the generator-

motor into the motor, the vehicle can be moved forward by both engine and generator-motor, while by switching the generator-motor into the generator, the batteries can be charged while electric power is generated by the engine, and if necessary, the vehicle can be moved forward or backward by only the generator-motor with the engine OFF. Using the generator-motor as a drive source, the vehicle can be moved backward, and using the engine as a drive source, the batteries can be charged through the generator-motor. Therefore, the use of the hybrid vehicle becomes more convenient.
