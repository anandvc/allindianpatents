FIELD OF THE INVENTION:
This invention relates to blood pump, More particularly, this invention relates to a centrifugal blood pump for extracorporeal applications.
BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION:
Blood pumps are integral part of each and every cardiopulmonary bypass circuit. Traditionally, roller pumps are used in the extracorporeal circuit to pump blood due to its pulsatile flow similar to that of human heat. Such pumps are attended with the difficulties of blood damage, after load sensitivity and are complicated and after unreliable during operation.
OBJECTS OF THE INVENTION:
It Is therefore an object of this invention to propose a blood pump for extracorporeal applications.
It is a further object of this invention to propose a blood pump which imparts insignificant blood damage.
Another object of this invention to propose a pump which dissipates very low heat energy.
Yet another object of this Invention to propose a pump which is pressure sensitive.
A further object of this invention to propose a pump which is single use disposable.

DESCRIPTION OF THE INVENTION:
According to this invention is provided a blood pump for extracorporeal applications comprising a housing, a magnetically driven impeller balanced in pivot bearing with an axial port for blood inlet and a radial port for blood exit.
The centrifugal blood pump for extracorporeal applications described herein has a housing consisting of top cover (1). bottom cover (2) and an impeller (3) all made of rigid plastics like polycarbonate. Blood enters axially through an inlet port (4) and leaves radial through an outlet port (5). The Impeller Is semi-open type with multiple straight or curved vanes(6). The impeller has a multi-pole circular ring magnet (7) embedded in it. The impeller has a top pivot (8) and a bottom pivot (9). The pivots are made of materials like stainless sfeel ^nd /or with suitable coating like nitirding or diamond like carbon coating. The top pivot rests on a top bearing (10) where as the bottom pivot rests on a bottom bearing (11).

The bearings are made of materials like ultra high molecular weight polyethylene. The top bearing has sufficient gap for smooth blood flow. The impeller has through holes (12) and back vanes (13) in order to reduce blood damage and thrombus formation.
The blood pump described herein before propels fluid by converting potential energy generated by the electromagnetic forces into kinetic energy, which is imparted to the fluid through the forced rotation of an impeller. It imparts insignificant blood damage and dissipates very low heat energy. It is pressure sensitive so that there won't be any tube rupture even in the case of pump occlusion. Further, it does not pump air that may be inadvertently induced into the circuit and in which air tends to remain at the center of the pump. These and other advantages of the blood pump according to the invention make it a unique one of its kind.
