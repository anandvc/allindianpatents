Title of Invention

"AN IMPROVED DUNNAGE MATERIAL FOR USE AS FLOOR COVERING IN STORES, GODOWNS AND WAREHOUSES AND A PROCESS THEREOF"
Abstract An improved dunnage material for use as floor covering in stores, godowns, warehouses, comprising of five layers, the first layer being of plastic film, the second layer being of hessian cloth, third layer being of bitumen coating, fourth layer being of hessian cloth and fifth layer being of plastic film wherein plastic is selected from PP, LDPE, LLDPE, HDPE and any combination thereof and bitumen is applied to hessian cloth. The process of preparation of the dunnage material comprises steps of preparing two separate laminates of plastic and hessian cloth by laminating plastic film over a hessian cloth wherein said plastic is selected from polypropylene (PP), low density polyethylene (LDPE), linear low density polyethylene (LLDPE), high density polyethylene (HDPE) and any combination thereof, binding two separate laminates thus obtained by using a layer of bitumen between said two laminates wherein bitumen is pre-heated to a temperature of 150-350Â°C.
Full Text FIELD OF INVENTION
This invention relates to an improved dunnage material for use as floor covering in stores, godowns and warehouses and process for preparation the material. This material is resistant to water, mildew and moisture and therefore helps in preventing spoilage of food products by preventing ingress of moisture and dampness in stored consumable items which includes food grains, tea, coffee, cement, sugar, fertilizers, tobacco.
PRIOR ART
Traditionally wooden crates, bamboo mats, black polyethylene etc are used in different godowns, stores for storage of food products. These traditional materials have several drawbacks.
A drawback of the above material is that black polyethylene used in the godowns gets easily ripped thereby exposing the stored food products to moisture and dampness.
Another drawback of the above material is that nails used in wooden crates, come out after certain period, which tear the gunny bags kept over the wooden crates, which in turn causes spillage and wastage of food products.
Still another drawback of the bamboo material used in flooring in godowns is that during removal of food grains from the stacks, bamboo/pataramats get mixed with the grains, which cause wastage of food grains.
Further drawback of the above is traditional flooring materials is that these materials if partially damaged, the damaged portion is not easily replacable.
Yet further disadvantage of the above materials is that these materials cannot be reused.
Even further drawback of the above material specifically black polyethylene is that it is not durable enough to withstand the pressure of load of stacks of food products and therefore it necessitates periodic replacement.
Still further drawback of the above material is that these materials cannot be used on walls. The stacks of food grains in godowns, are therefore kept away from walls as safety zone to prevent ingress of moisture creeping into the storage bags form the walls. The storage space in the godowns, warehouses therefore remains partially unutilised to the extent of about 5%.
Yet further drawback of the above material is that excessive discarding of used polyethylene etc. is not eco-friendly.
Even further drawback of the above material is that laying down of t] materials on the floor is time-consuming.
Still further drawback of the above materials is that these materials not cost-effective as these have relatively lower life and necess: periodic replacements.

DESCRIPTION OF INVENTION
According to this invention, the improved dunnage material for use as floor covering in stores, godowns and warehouses, comprises of five layers, the first layer being of plastic, second layer being of Hessian cloth, third layer being of bitumen coating, the fourth layer being of Hessian cloth and the fifth layer being of plastic film.
Further according to this invention the floor covering material is prepared by the process comprising following steps :
(a) Preparing laminates of plastic and Hessian cloth
Plastic film using any of polypropylene (PP), low density polyethylene(LDPE), linear low density polyethylene (LLDPE), high density polyethylene (HOPE) or any combination thereof, is laminated over a hessian cloth. The thickness of film is kept such that the laminated material having a width of 82-124" and length of 1m has a weight in the range of 160-220g. Lamination is carried out at 150-300Â°C under a pressure of 8.5-9kg. In this way two separate laminates of plastic film and Hessian cloth are prepared.
(b) Preparation of dunnage material
The two separate laminates of plastic film and Hessian cloth obtained by step (a) are bonded together using bitumen. The bitumen is pre-heated to a temperature of 150-350Â°C and then applied between the said two laminates in the quantity range varying from 0.70 g to 3 g per square foot.
WORKING EXAMPLE
The invention will now be illustrated with a working example, which is intended to be a typical example to illustrate the working of the invention
and is not intended to be taken restrictively to imply any limitation on the scope of the present invention.
Jute/Hessian cloth measuring 82 " in width is laminated with LDPE film, the weight of film being 200 gms on jute/hessian cloth measuring Imtr. in length and 82" in width. Two such rolls measuring 100 mtr in length are prepared by above mentioned method. The laminated jute/hessian cloth from these two rolls are then bonded together by bitumen which is preheated upto a temperature of about 275 degrees celcius and subsequently pressed together. The said process is continued till the two laminated jute/hessian cloth rolls of 100 mtrs each in length are finished. As a result a roll of 100 mtr of floor covering material is obtained.
The material obtained restricts the moisture level to a very low level in the range of 3 to 6%
