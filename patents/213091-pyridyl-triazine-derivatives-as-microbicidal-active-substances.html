Title of Invention

PYRIDYL-TRIAZINE DERIVATIVES AS MICROBICIDAL ACTIVE SUBSTANCES
Abstract Triazine derivatives of formula (1) wherein R<sup>1</sup> is C<sub>1</sub>-C<sub>20</sub>alkyl; C<sub>3</sub>-C<sub>7</sub>cycloalkyl; or C<sub>1</sub>-C<sub>20</sub>perfluoroalkyl; R<sup>2</sup> is hydrogen; C<sub>1</sub>-C<sub>20</sub>alkyl; or C<sub>3</sub>-C<sub>7</sub>cycloalkyl; and R<sup>3</sup> is hydrogen; C<sub>1</sub>-C<sub>20</sub>alkyl; C<sub>3</sub>-C<sub>7</sub>cycloalkyl; C<sub>1</sub>-C<sub>20</sub>perfluoroalky; C<sub>1</sub>-C<sub>20</sub>alkyl-carbonyl; C<sub>3</sub>-C<sub>7</sub>cycloalkyl-carbonyl; C<sub>1</sub>-C<sub>20</sub>perfluoroalkyl-carbonyl; or phenylcarbonyl; are described. The compounds exhibit pronounced action against gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria and also against yeasts and moulds. They are also suitable for treating or preventing biofilms on human tooth surfaces and oral mucosa.
