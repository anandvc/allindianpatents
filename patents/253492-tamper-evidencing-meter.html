Title of Invention

"TAMPER EVIDENCING METER"
Abstract The present invention provides a tamper evidencing meter comprising a casing comprising a front cover (1), a rear cover (2) and optionally an intermediate cover (6), characterized in that said casing comprises: a frangible portion (3) formed on the front cover (1) or on the intermediate cover (6) at a location which is easily observable for retaining a permanent evidence indicative of attempt to tamper and/or tamper; at least one element (4) engageable with a portion (5) of the said casing, being located so that any force applied to disengage the engagement there between is directly transferred to the frangible portion.
Full Text Field of invention
The present invention relates to a meter, more particularly to a tamper evidencing meter.
Background of invention
Power meter is being used in electric power distribution system and is located at the subscriber's or customer's premises. The power meter measures the amount of electric energy supplied by the subscriber/customer.
Various components of the power meter are encased inside a casing in order to (a) provide protection to the components, (b) to enhance the ease of mounting of the power meter at the user's premises and (c) to avoid access of the components. By avoiding access of the components of the power of the power meter, tampering to the meter can be reduced.
The casing of the power meter is made in two or three parts and the parts are joined together. In one of the recent techniques adopted, the components of the power meter are placed inside a plastic casing, which is formed in at least two parts, and the parts are ultrasonically welded with each other.
Although the above mentioned construction of the power meter overcomes previously faced problems, in the recent time it has been noticed that dishonest customers still attempt to manipulate or tamper the meter that are formed in the above-mentioned manner by cutting the sealed cover and gluing the cover back after tampering. Once the plastic casing is glued, it is difficult for the representative of the power company to detect the tampering. The situation is further aggravated when the glued portion is not easily noticeable or detectable. This results in the power companies a low revenue collection from the dishonest customer for electric consumption. Thus there exists a need to provide a meter in which it is easier to detect tampering.

Figure 1 shows a tamper evidencing meter according to a first embodiment of the present invention. As can be noticed from figure 1, the meter comprises two covers, a top cover (1) and a bottom cover (2). The top cover (1) has at least one weaker or frangible portion (3) located at a portion of the top cover which is easily visible or in other words, observable. The frangible portion provided on the top cover carries a permanent and nonerasable mark or evidence of any attempt to tamper or tampering.
The top cover is provided with at least one element (4) engageable with a corresponding portion (5) of the bottom cover when the said top cover is secured to the bottom cover. Particularly, the element (4) engages with the portion (5) when the top cover and the bottom cover are joined / sealed together. The top cover and the bottom cover can be sealed or joined by any suitable joining process e.g., by fastening, gluing or ultrasonic welding. The element (4) and portion (5) are located in such a manner that by cutting the cover at any location, the element (4) or the portion (5) or the engagement between the element (4) and the portion (5) is not disturbed. In other words, even by cutting the sealed cover, the element (4), the portion (5) and the engagement between the element (4) and the portion (5) remain intact, due to which, free access inside to cover is not available.
Thus, if any unauthorized person tries to access the components inside the cover, he would be required to apply further force and disengage the engagement between the element (4) and the portion (5). The location of the element (4) and the portion (5) is such that any force applied to disengage the engagement there-between is directly transferred to the frangible portion. By way of example, the element (4) can be located on the frangible portion (3) or in vicinity of the frangible portion (3) and the portion (5) can be located substantially beneath the element (4). The further force required for disengaging the engagement between the element (4) and the portion (5) is larger than the force required for breaking the frangible portion (3). Thus, whenever the unauthorized person tries to access the components inside the cover, the frangible portion (3) of the top cover
(1) breaks, thereby leaving a permanent and non-erasable mark or evidence of the attempt to tamper or tampering. Once the frangible portion is broken, it cannot be rejoined.
By way of example, the element (4) can be engaged with the portion (5) by gluing, by means of a locking device, a magnetic lock, magnetic hook or snap lockable device.
Figure 2 shows a tamper evidencing meter according to a second embodiment of the present invention. As can be noticed from figure 2, the meter comprises a top cover (1), a bottom cover (2) and an intermediate cover (6) located between the top and the bottom covers. The intermediate cover (6) is arranged to be secured with the bottom cover (2) and the top cover (1) is also arranged to be secured with the bottom cover (2). Similar to the first embodiment shown in figure 1, the top cover (1) has at least one weaker or frangible portion (3) located at a portion of the top cover which is easily visible or in other words, observable. The frangible portion provided on the top cover carries a permanent and non-erasable mark or evidence of any attempt to tamper or tampering.
The top cover is provided with at least one element (4) engageable with a corresponding portion (5) of the intermediate cover (6) when the said top cover (1) is secured to the bottom cover (2). Particularly, the element (4) engages with the portion (5) when the top cover and the bottom cover are joined / sealed together. As mentioned in the first embodiment, the top cover and the bottom cover can be sealed or joined by any suitable joining process e.g., fastening, gluing or ultrasonic welding. The element (4) can be adapted to comprise of a snap-lockable device (7) and portion (5) can be adopted to comprise of slots (8) that interact with the snap-lockable device. The snap lockable device is located in such a manner that by cutting the cover at any location, the snap-lockable device (7) or the engagement between the snap lockable device (7) and the slots (8) is not disturbed. In other words, even by cutting the sealed cover, the snap-lockable device (7) and the engagement between the snap lockable device (7) and the slots (8) remain intact, due to which, free access inside to cover is not available.
Thus, if any unauthorized person tries to access the components inside the cover, he would be required to apply further force and disengage the engagement between the snap
lockable device (7) and the slots (8). The location of the snap lockable device (7) and the slots (8) is such that any force applied to disengage the engagement there-between is directly transferred to the frangible portion (3). By way of example, the snap lockable device (7) can be located on the frangible portion (3) or in vicinity of the frangible portion (3) and the slots (8) can be located substantially beneath the snap lockable device (7). The further force required for disengaging the engagement between the snap lockable device (7) and the slots (8) is larger than the force required for breaking the frangible portion (3). Thus, whenever the unauthorized person tries to access the components inside the cover, the frangible portion (3) of the top cover (1) breaks, thereby leaving a permanent and non-erasable mark or evidence of the attempt to tamper or tampering. Once the frangible portion is broken, it cannot be rejoined.
In a third embodiment, which can be considered as an alternative to the second embodiment, the portion (5) can be adapted to comprise of a snap-lockable device (7) and the element (4) can be adopted to comprise of slots (8) that interact with the snap-lockable device. Thus, in this alternative embodiment, the slots are formed inside the frangible portion (3) on the front cover (1) and the snap-lockable device may be formed in the intermediate cover (6).
In a fourth embodiment, which can be considered as an alternative to the second and/or the third embodiment, the top cover could be formed to include at least one transparent portion and the intermediate cover (6) comprises at least one weaker or frangible portion (3) located at a portion which is easily visible or in other words, observable. Preferably, the transparent portion included in the top cover overlaps with the frangible portion included in the intermediate cover.
The intermediate cover (6) may be provided with at least one element (4) engageable with a corresponding portion (5) of the bottom cover (2) when the said intermediate cover is secured to the bottom cover. In an alternative, the bottom cover (2) may be provided with at least one element (4) engageable with a corresponding portion (5) of the intermediate cover (6) when the said intermediate cover is secured to the bottom cover.
n another alternative, the intermediate cover (6) may be provided with at least one element (4) engageable with a corresponding portion (5) of the top cover (1) when the said top cover is secured to the bottom cover. In yet another the top cover (1) may be provided with at least one element (4) engageable with a corresponding portion (5) of the intermediate cover (6) when the said top cover is secured to the bottom cover.
The Applicants would like to add than when the top and the bottom covers are secured with the help of externally mounted fasteners, at least one of the fasteners is covered by a cap element (9) to avoid any unauthorized access to the fastener. By avoiding access to the fastener, the chances of tampering can be reduced. As shown in figure 3, the top cover (1) is arranged to be secured to the bottom cover sealing screws. However, instead of using sealing screws, nut-bolts or screws may be used as fasteners. The cap element (9) could be located above the fastener and sealed to the top cover. The fastener can be sealed by ultrasonically welding the cap element or by gluing the cap element to the top cover. As shown in figure 3, the cap element is optionally provided with a snap lock arrangement (10). The cap element (9) is optionally surrounded by a wall type structure (11) which prevents insertion of screw driver or any other tools to break open the cap element which snugly fits inside the wall type structure (11). If the fastener used is a sealing screw, an aperture (12) can be provided in the wall type structure (11) for inserting a sealing wire.
All the above-mentioned embodiments are primarily directed towards leaving a permanent and non-erasable mark or evidence of any attempt to tamper or tampering on the meter itself which is easily observable, which is the novel and the inventive aspect of the present application.
