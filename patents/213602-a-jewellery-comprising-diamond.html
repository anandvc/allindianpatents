Title of Invention

A JEWELLERY COMPRISING DIAMOND
Abstract A cut design for diamonds having a crow of substantially a frustum shape and substantially a conic pavilion directly under the frustum part, the diamond being enhanced in the brilliancy and scintillation of its crow part, the brilliancy and scintillation twinkling, and bluish lights being returned as reflected lights. A crown angle and a pavilion angle pare so determined that lights coming in the table facet and emitted from crown facets and emitted the observer at the same time. Further, it is preferable to determine the crown angle c and the pavilion angle p so that the angles formed by incident lights and emitted lights should be between or equal to 0.60 and 0.33, more preferably not more than 0.55 and even more preferably not more than 0.38, in ratio to the girdle diameter.
