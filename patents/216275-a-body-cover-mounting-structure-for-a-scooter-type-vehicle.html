Title of Invention

"A BODY COVER MOUNTING STRUCTURE FOR A SCOOTER-TYPE VEHICLE"
Abstract A body cover mounting structure for a scooter-type vehicle in which a containing box (10) opened upwardly is provided behind a low-level type floor (14) located between a front wheel (1) and a rear wheel (8), and an upper end portion (95) of said containing box (10) is covered with a seat (11) such that the upper opening of said containing box (10) is opened and closed with said seat (11), characterized in that: an upper end portion of a body cover (17, 18 and 19) is mounted on and in engagement with the upper end portion (95) of said containing box (10), and that the upper end portion of said body cover (17, 18 and 19) mounted on said containing box (10) is covered with said seat (11).
Full Text [Detailed Description of the Invention]
[0001]
[Technical Field to Which the Invention Pertains]
The present invention relates to a body cover mounting structure provided at the rear body of a scooter-type vehicle. [0002]
[Prior Art]
The scooter-type vehicle including a containing box is known, for example, from Japanese Utility Model Publication No. Hei 3-43911, in which the periphery of the rear body including the containing box is covered with the body cover, and the upper end portion of the body cover is treated in such a manner as to be overlapped to the underside of a flange portion formed around the opening of the containing box. [0003]
[Problem to be Solved by the Invention]
The above-described structure, however, has a problem. Namely, it takes a lot of labor to remove the body cover for maintenance or the like because the containing box must be removed from the body frame by which the containing box is supported before removal of the body cover. Accordingly, an object of the present invention is to provide a body cover mounting structure capable of simply removing a body cover upon maintenance while preventing the body cover from being removed in an
unintentional state.
[Means for Solving the Problem]
The present invention relates to a body cover mounting structure for a scooter-type vehicle in which a containing box opened upwardly is provided behind a low-level type floor located between a front wheel and a rear wheel, and an upper end portion of said containing box is covered with a seat surch that the upper opening of

said containing box is opened and closed with said seat, characterized in that: an upper end portion of a body cover is mounted on and in engagement with the upper end portion of said containing box, and that the upper end portion of said body cover mounted on said containing box is covered with said seat.
To solve the above problem, according to the present invention, there is provided a body cover mounting structure for a scooter-type vehicle in which a containing box opened upwardly is provided behind a low-level type floor provided between a front wheel and a rear wheel and the opening of the containing box is opened/closed with a seat, characterized in that an upper end portion of a body cover is mounted on an upper end portion, to be covered with the seat, of the containing box, and the mounting portion of the body cover mounted on the containing box is covered with the seat. In the above structure, the mounting of the body cover to the containing box may be performed by vertical engagement between a projection formed on one of the body cover and the containing box and a recess formed in the other.
[Effect of the Invention]
Since the upper end portion of the body cover is mounted on the upper end, to be covered with the seat, of the containing box, the body cover can be removed without removing the containing box from the body frame when the
seat is opened. When the seat is closed, the body cover cannot be removed unless the seat is opened again. In addition, since the commercially available vehicle of this type is generally provided with a seat lock, the seat cannot be unintentionally opened. [0006]
Accordingly, there can be obtained a body cover mounting structure capable of improving the maintenance characteristic by simply removing the body cover upon maintenance, and preventing the body cover from being easily removed in an unintentional state. [0007]
By adopting the structure in which the mounting of the body cover to the containing box is performed by vertical engagement between a projection formed on one of the body cover and the containing box and a recess formed in the other, it is possible to simply mount/dismount the body cover only by shifting the body cover in the vertical direction.

The containing box 10, which is molded from a resin into one-body, requires a very large strength because the containing box 10 supports the rear extension portion 31 and the rear fender 13 which have been conventionally supported by the body frame. To ensure such a very large strength, the containing box 10 is molded by a flow molding method using as a raw material a
laminated seat composed of a resin layer made form a thermoplastic resin such as polypropylene and a fiber layer made from glass long-fibers. [0027]
To be more specific, the flow molding method is performed by laminating about 60 wt% of a polypropylene film (which is previously subject to heat
resistant/weather resistant treatment) to about 40 wt% of a needle punched swirl mat of glass long-fibers, to form a laminated sheet; cutting out a specific blank material from the laminated sheet; heating the blank material at a temperature higher than the melting point of the resin, for example, ranging from about 200°C to about 220°C by a heater or the like, to melt the resin for fluidizing the resin; and transferring the blank material thus heated in a die and immediately closing the die to press the blank material. The function and effect of the above embodiment will be described. As is apparent from Fig. 3 and Figs. 10 to 13, the left rear cover 18 constituting part of the body cover is mounted in accordance with the following manner. It should be noted that the mounting of the right rear cover 19 is the same as the mounting of the left rear cover 18, and therefore, the explanation thereof is omitted. The seat 11 is opened, and the left rear cover 18 is kept in a state being slightly shifted upwardly from the final mounting position, and in such a state the hooks 87 of the engagement portions 80 of the left rear cover 18 are inserted, from the side of the containing box 10, into the slits 89 of the front cover 17. After that, the whole of the left rear cover 18 is moved downwardly.
[0051]
Along with the downward movement of the left rear cover 18, the engagement portions 88 at the leading ends of the hooks 87 are engaged with the lower edges 89a of the slits 89, and also the flat portion 93 of the upper end of the left rear cover 18 is overlapped on the flange portion 95 of the upper end of the containing box 10 and the projections 94 of the engagement portions 82 are engaged in the grooves 96 opened in the upper surface of the flange portion 95.
[0052]
The left rear cover 18 is thus positioned to the containing box 10 in the width direction of the vehicular body, and the front and rear fastening portions 81a and 81b are overlapped to the slant portion 91 of the front cover 17 and the nut portion 92 of the containing box 10, respectively. The front and rear fastening portions 81a and 81b are then fastened to the mating members with the small screws. [0053]
At this time, on the rear side of the left rear cover 18, the engagement portion 83 projecting downwardly in the shape of the rib is engaged in an engagement hole formed in a longitudinally intermediate portion of the
side edge of the central cover 20 previously mounted on the rear extension portion 31, and the lower rear fastening portion 85 is overlapped to a fastening portion provided at the lower end of the center cover 20 and is fastened thereto with the small screw 86. Further, on the front side of the left rear cover 18, the lower projection 84 is fixed to the floor 14 with the small screw. [0054]
The seat 11 is then closed, and at this time, the seat under cover 16 constituting part of the seat 11 in the state being integrated with the bottom portion of the seat 11, which is sufficiently larger than the main body of the seat 11 as is apparent from the figures, covers the mounting portion of the left rear cover 18 located on the flange portion 95 of the containing box 10 as shown in Fig. 12. Accordingly, after the seat 11 is locked with the seat catch 60, the mounting portion cannot be exposed unless the seat 11 is opened by releasing the locking state of the seat catch 60. [0055]
Since the mounting portion of the left rear cover 18 covered with the seat under cover 16 constituting part of the seat 11 cannot be removed after the seat 11 is
closed and locked with the seat catch 60, the left rear cover 18 is prevented from being removed in an unintentional state. [0056]
When the seat 11 is opened by releasing the locking state of the seat catch 60, the mounting portion of the left rear cover 18 can be exposed. As a result, since the engagement state of the engagement portions 80 and 82 is released by removing the small screws from the fastening portions 81a and 81b and shifting the left rear cover 18 upwardly, the left rear cover 18 can be simply removed. At this time, it is not required to remove the containing box 10 from the rear frame 6, so that it is possible to improve the maintenance characteristic. In addition, the fastening state of the fastening portions 84 and 85 on the lower side of the left rear cover 18 are previously released. [0057]
Further, since the positioning of the left rear cover 18 in the width direction of the vehicular body by the engagement portions 80 and 82, there can be adopted the structure in which the fastening portions 81a and 81b having no necessity of positioning the left rear cover 18 in the width direction of the vehicular body are provided with the slits 90 opened on one side. Such a structure is advantageous in molding the left rear cover 18. [0058]
The present invention is not limited to the above embodiment, and changes and variations may be made without departing from the spirit or scope of the present invention. For example, in the above embodiment, the mounting structure between the body cover and the containing box is configured as the vertical engagement between a projection formed on one of the body cover and the containing box and a recess formed in the other, which structure is advantageous in that the body cover can be simply mounted/dismounted only by shifting the body cover in the vertical direction; however, such a mounting structure may be configured as a fastening structure using small screws or the like. In addition, the mounting portion of the body cover to the containing box is covered with the seat under cover integrated with the seat; however, it may be covered with a seat of a type including no seat under cover.
