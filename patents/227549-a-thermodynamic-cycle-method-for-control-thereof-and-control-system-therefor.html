BACKGROUND
Present refrigeration cycles reject heat to the atmosphere. In some cases a portion of the energy which would otherwise be rejected may be recovered from the cycle, thereby increasing the overall efficiency.
Figure 1 shows a diagrammatic representation of a heat pump circuit of the prior art. Hot, high pressure refrigerant liquid enters a throttling device often referred to as a Tx valve, which reduces its pressure and temperature at constant enthalpy. The heat absorbing vapour is passed through a heat exchange or "evaporator" which absorbs heat from ambient temperature air blown across its surfaces by an fan, cooling the air the thereby providing the refrigeration effect and causing it to expand. The acquisition of heat causes the liquid to flash to vapour and expand.
The heat laden working fluid vapour is then passed into an accumulator which has an internal structure designed to allow any remaining liquid to boil off prior to entering the compressor.
The energy rich warm working fluid vapour enters a compressor, which as a result of a work input, compresses the vapour thus raising its temperature and pressure. A significant portion of the work input into the compressor re-appears as the heat of compression thus superheating the working fluid vapour.
i he superheated working fluid vapour thus has its temperature elevated above that cf the ambient temperature of the environment and enters a condenser, which has a structure similar to that of the evaporator. A heat exchange then occurs between the superheated working fluid vapour and the environment which is al a lower temperature. The heat exchange continues until sufficient heat is removed from the woridng fluid to cause a change of state from hot vapour to hot liquid.
The hot working fluid liquid enters a reservoir, usually referred to as a "receiver" which has a sufficiently large volume lo support the requirements of the thermodynamic cycle ancf withstand the high pressure in the discharge line of the compressor. The hoi high pressure refrigerant liquid then enters the TX vafve to complete the thermodynamic cycle.
