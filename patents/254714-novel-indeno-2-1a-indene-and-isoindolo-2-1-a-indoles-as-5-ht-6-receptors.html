Those compounds of general formula (I) which have an asymmetric carbon and as a rule are obtained as racemates can be separated one from the other by the usual methods, or any given isomer may be obtained by stereospecific or asymmetric synthesis. Example -15 : FOOD INTAKE MEASUREMENT
Male Wistar rats (100-270 g) obtained from (National Institute of Nutrition, Hyderabad, India) are used. The animals are acclimatized to the animal facility for at least 7 days before they are subjected to any treatment. During this period the animals are housed (in groups of three) in translucid cages and provided with food and water ad libitum. At least 24 hours before the treatment starts, the animals are adapted to single-housing conditions.
The chronic effect of the compounds of general formula (I) on food intake in well-fed rats is then determined as follows. The rats were housed in their single homecages for 28 days. The rat is provided with ad libitum food and water. On 0, 7th, 14th, 21st and 28th day the rat is left with preweighed food. Food intake and weight gain is measured.
