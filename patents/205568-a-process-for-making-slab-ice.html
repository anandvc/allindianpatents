Title of Invention

A PROCESS FOR MAKING SLAB ICE
Abstract A method for making slab-ice in a hygienic way by completely avoiding human handling in a limited space for cost effective slab ice making comprising a ice making zone, a accumulator zone and a surge drum zone; the said ice-making zone comprising injecting refrigerant liquid into the ice maker having jacketed ice maker wherein thermosyphon, refrigeration arrangement are provided in the inner part of the ice-maker, having a freezing tank, where the partially cooled water through the accumulator will pass to the ice-maker and a ice-slab is produced due to the refrigerant which allows the water changing into ice, i.e. frozen water when latent heat of freezing is removed from water from 0°Centigrade/the water converts to ice without the use of brine tank and the ice slab thus produced is conveyed by the conveyor belt run though chain and sprocket arrangement and delivered on to the pallet and the ice slabs are covered hygienically.

A PLANT FOR MAKING SLAB ICE
The invention relates to a plant for making ice slab. More particularly the invention relates to plant for making hygenic ice slab.
In the conventional system of ice making wherein the plant occupies too much of space that is about by 12 meters * 12 meters to make 15 tons per day of block ice. The environment in which the conventional plant reduces are water which gets contaminated with brine solution or dipping water cans in brine solution . In the conventional system there is too much of human handling such as dirt from the shoes of operator and the environmental pollution which makes the process unhygenic.
Therefore there is need to develop a plant which requires less space and produces hygenic ice slab.
The object of the invention is therefore to produce hygenic ice-slab with minimum cost.
The invention is based on "water" changes to " ice " i.e frozen water, when latent heat of freezing is removed from water i.e. 80 Kcal heat removal at 0°C water converts water to ice. Invention to make "slab" ice from water without using conventional brine tank and dipping water cans in brine tank to freeze water as slab ice.
Conventional slab ice plant occupies too much space i.e. about 12 M * 12 M to make 15 tons per day ice where as new invention allows same production in 4 M * 12 M space.

The coventional plant produces dirty ice as water gets contaminated with brine and dirt from water gets contaminated with brine and dirt from shoes of the operator . New invention uses technique which allows ice fully hygenic.
The new invention is the stainless steel water channels of 30 to 100 mm width , 300 to 500 mm height and 1000 to 3000 lengths are made with one end closed and top open. Second end is closed with flat soft side head of the valve (closing the side with polyurethane / PVC / rubber covered flap , so that water does not leak out from freezing container channel.
The water is then taken in to this channel . This has jacket liquid refrigerant evaporation arrangement where liquid refrigerant is evaporated to gas taking away latent heat of freezing from water and converts water to ice. This is acheived by connecting refrigerant side to standard refrigeration system.
The drag chain is kept dipped in water gets embedded in slab of ice. Water for next cycles of freezing is taken in the channel , refrigerant is stopped by closing actuated refrigerant valve , hot gas valve is operated to push out liquid refrigerant to surge tank. The hot gas warms up walls of ice tank (channel) and releases the slab of ice because of this ice comes out of side opening.
- 3 -

The tank side door is opened . Then the drag chain drive is then started . This brings ice slab out of ice forming channel . The valve flap is then again closed and water spilled out in tray is pumped in to water channel and refrigeration valve is opened for freezing the water and the cycle of ice slab making repeats.
The slabs of ice are taken on roller to convey on to pallet . The "" star cool cozy " is used to cover the ice slabs to avoid melting loss in open air.
The benefits of the invention over the conventional one is of space saving and obtaining hygenic ice.

I CLAIM


1. A method for making slab-ice in a hygienic way by completely avoiding human handling in a limited space for cost effective slab ice making comprising a ice making zone, a accumulator zone and a surge drum zone; the said ice-making zone comprising injecting refrigerant liquid into the ice maker having jacketed ice maker wherein thermosyphon, refrigeration arrangement are provided in the inner part of the ice-maker, having a freezing tank, where the partially cooled water through the accumulator will pass to the ice-maker and a ice-slab is produced due to the refrigerant which allows the water changing into ice, i.e. frozen water when latent heat of freezing is removed from water from 0°Centigrade/the water converts to ice without the use of brine tank and the ice slab thus produced is conveyed by the conveyor belt run though chain and sprocket arrangement and delivered on to the pallet and the ice slabs are covered hygienically.
2. The method for making slab ice as claimed in claim 1, wherein the water pump is used to fill the freezing tank by water.
3. A method for making slab ice as claimed in claim 1, wherein the freezing of ice is done by cooling with -10°C to -13°C by passing cooling medium in the jacket of the ice-maker.
4. A method for making slab ice as claimed in claim 1, wherein ice slab is thawed by hot gas by employing refrigerant cycle.
5. A method for making slab ice as claimed in claim (1) and substantially herein described and illustrated in the accompanying drawing.
