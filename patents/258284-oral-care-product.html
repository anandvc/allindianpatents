The present invention relates to a polymeric material matrix comprising calcium silicate bioraaterial, the preparation of 5 such materials as well as the use of such a material to form a hydroxyapatite film on tooth surface for the remineralisation and whitening of the teeth.
A tooth is comprised of an inner dentin layer and an outer 10 hard enamel layer that is the protective layer of the tooth.
Due to today's lifestyles with increasing consumption of acidic drinks and Â£oodsr tooth erosion is becoming more prevalent and common, which is believed to be one of the
15 biggest threats to teeth in the 21s" century. Enamel is the hard, protective coating of the tooth, which protects the sensitive dentin underneath. Acidic drinks and foods, such as fruit and fruit juices, can make enamel softer and wear away by acid attack. If without intervention or effective
20 treatment, tooth erosion could further lead to tooth
sensitivity with severe pain, when the surface enamel is worn off and the inner dentin tubules are exposed.
Nowadays remineralisation is usually done by adding fluoride 25 ions according to the following reaction scheme

The enamel layer of the tooth is naturally an opaque white or slightly off-white colour; however, this enamel layer can
10 become stained or discoloured. The enamel layer of the tooth is composed of hydroxyapatite mineral crystals that create a somewhat porous surface. It is believed that this porous nature of the enamel layer allows staining agents and discolouring substances to permeate the enamel and discolour

Many substances can stain or reduce the whiteness of one's teeth; in particular, certain foods, tobacco products, and fluids such as tea and coffee. These staining and 20 discolouring substances are often able to permeate the enamel layer. This problem occurs gradually over many years, but imparts a noticeable discoloration of the enamel of one's teeth.

The present invention involves delivering mesoporous calcium silicate to the surface of the teeth and ultimately converting this material into hydroxyapatite in situ by reaction with phosphate ions present in the saliva and/or 5 added the teeth from a simultaneous or sequential applied source.
The in situ generation of hydroxyapatite results in the remineralisation of the teeth, potentially reducing the
10 likelihood of tooth decay and improving the appearance of the teeth, in particular their whiteness. The teeth may also appear smoother and shinier as a result. Since many "whitening" treatments result in a roughening of the tooth surface, the ability to whiten and yet reduce surface
15 roughness is a particular benefit of the present invention.
The benefits delivered by the present invention are principally targeting at the enamel; however, it is also expected that any exposed dentin may also be beneficially 20 affected.
