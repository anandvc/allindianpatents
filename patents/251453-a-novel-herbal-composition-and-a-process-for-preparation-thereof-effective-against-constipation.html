Full Text FIELD OF INVENTION
This invention relates to a novel herbal composition and a process for preparation thereof effective against constipation.
PRIOR ART
Constipation is a common complain all over the world. There is a very wide range of treatment and management of constipation but commonly laxation and purgatives are used frequently in modern medicine to avoid constipation and prolonged use of many laxatives produce many side effects such as abdominal colic hypokalaemia.
There are a number of formulations for Constipation available in the market. These are being manufactured and marketed by different pharmaceuticals.

OBJECTS OF THE INVENTION
The primary object of the present invention is to propose a novel herbal composition and a process for preparation thereof effective against constipation which is cheaper, more effective and free from side effects.
Another object of the present invention is to propose a novel herbal composition and a process for preparation thereof effective against constipation which is obtained from a plurality of herbs and minerals of indigenous origin growing abundantly all over India.
Further object of the present invention is to propose a novel herbal composition and a process for preparation thereof effective against constipation which has no toxicity or untoward effect after prolonged administration in children and also in pregnant women.
Still another object of the present invention is to propose a novel herbal composition and a process for preparation thereof effective against constipation which is superior than the drugs available in the market as it is more effective, easy to administer and has no side effects and also its effect lasts for long. Further, it is easier to take as compared to other drugs available in the market.
Yet another object of the present invention is to propose a novel herbal composition and a process for preparation thereof effective against constipation which acts as anti flatulent and relives abdominal pain.

DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF THE INVENTION
The herbal composition of the present invention comprising the following ingredients with specified amounts, which may be taken as an example without restricting the scope of the invention to the same.
i. Zanjabeel (Zingiber officinale Roxb). 125 -150 mg.
ii. Sana (Cassia angustifolia Vahl.) 125-140 mg.
iii. Namak Lahri (Lahori Salt) 30-40 mg.
iv. Gul-e-Surkh (Rosa damasena Mill.) 60-70 mg.
v. Bekh Jalapa (Ipomoea purge Hayne) 125-140 mg.
For the preparation of the herbal composition, all the four ingredients except Namak Lahori are pulverized to coarse powder in a pulveriser having coarseness of 40-70 mesh. The powder so obtained is extracted in alcohol such as Ethanol, methanol and water by refluxing wherein the ratio between alcohol and water is 30:70-50:50. In refluxing, method for maximum extraction with minimum loss of solvent is used. The extraction is continued till the complete discoloration of supernatant liquid. Total extracts thus obtained are pooled together and evaporated to
dryness under reduced pressure of 280-320 mg of Hg at the .temperature of 60-70째C in closed steam jacketed container. This forms first part.
Thereafter, Namak Lahori is dissolved in hot purified water, which forms second part. The two parts alongwith binding agent such as 10% of Gond Babool, 10% starch or 5% carboxy methyl cellulose are transferred to kneading mass, which is subjected to drying in a fluid bed drier for around 3 hrs at for example 105째C. The dried mass thus obtained is mixed with diluents and lubricants followed by compression to obtain tablet in which diluents and lubricants are kaolin and talcum powder.
