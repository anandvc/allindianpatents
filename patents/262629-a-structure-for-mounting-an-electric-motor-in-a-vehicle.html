Title of Invention

A STRUCTURE FOR MOUNTING AN ELECTRIC MOTOR IN A VEHICLE
Abstract A structure for mounting an electric mo tor in a vehicle, comprises: a mounting frame detachably fixed to a body component of the vehicle by a bolt; an upward-projecting bracket, which is upwardly projected from the mounting frame, being attached to the body component of the vehicle; a bolt hole, in which the bolt is inserted, being formed on the upward-proj ectingbracket; and a downward-proj ecting bracket, which is downwardly projected from the mounting frame, being connected to the electric motor. Both the mounting frame and the downward-projecting bracket are set apart from an axis of the bolt hole.

Now, the demerits of having a motor-roll center below a motor unit will be described using Fig. 6.
In this case, a roll-momentum MRi is generated at a motor unit 13' . When a vehicle 10' runs forward, an inertia force FI is generated at a center of mass GI of the motor-unit 13' . [0054]
Accordingly, at the motor unit 13', an inertia momentum MI centered at a motor-roll center RI is generated. The inertia momentum MT is obtained by multiplying a distance LI from the motor-roll center RI and the center mass GI, and the inertia force FI (Mi = LixFi) .
Namely, as shown in Fig. 6, assuming that the motor-roll center RI is located below the center of gravity GI of the motor-unit 13' and the vehicle 10' is accelerated, not only the roll-momentum MRI is generated, but also the inertia momentum MI at the motor-roll center RI . [0055]
Whereas, as shown in Fig. 5, according to the present invention in this embodiment, the motor-roll center R0 is located above the center of gravity Go of motor-unit 13. Accordingly, during acceleration of the vehicle 10, it is possible to suppress the roll-momentum MR of the motor unit 13 due to generating the counter momentum Mc (=L2xF) at the motor unit 13.
Further, the load applied to parts supporting the
motor unit 13 is reduced, and therefore, it is possible
to reduce costs and size of the parts.
