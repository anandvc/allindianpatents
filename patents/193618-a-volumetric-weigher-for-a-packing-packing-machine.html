Title of Invention

A VOLUMETRIC WEIGHER FOR A PACKING/PACKING MACHINE
Abstract (57) Abstract: A volumetric weigher for a packing/packaging machine comprises a bottom plate and a top plate.The bottom plate is fixed and the top plate is pivotally mounted on the said fixed bottom plate.A pneumatic actuator causes a to and fro horizontal swinging or curving motion to the top plate.The moving top plate has an aperture at a predetermined location. Mean are provided for adjusting the volume of the said aperture. The bottom plate is provided with a hole at a predetermined locationon the to and fro path of the said aperture.

This invention relates to a volumetric weigher for a packing/ packaging machine, in particular, but not exclusively, a ver¬tical Form-Fill-Seal type packaging machine used for packing tea, coffee, etc.
The existing volumetric weigher operates on the principle of volume of cttp to detennine the cpaantity to be packed. Different sizes of cups are required for packing various sizes of packs, viz, Rs 2/«, Rs 1^50/=, Rs 1/"* etc. Very fine adjustments for maintaining the correct pack weight is not possible especially when there are changes in blend formulation. The movable bottom portion of the cup is susceptible to wear and tear and therefore cups have to be replaced frequently. Further, due to the conti¬nuous motion of the feeding drum the tea tends to become brow¬nish which is not an acceptable characteristic. Furthermore, the bearing supporting the feeding drum fails frequently due to continuous rotation* Moreover, the large number of the movable parts in the system catases frequent brakedown due to wear and tear.
An object of the present invention is to avoid, if not, minimise, the abovementioned drawbacks.
Accordingly, the present invention provides a volumetric weigher for a packing/packaging machine, comprising a bottom plate and a top plate, the bottom plate being fixed and the top plate being

pivotally mounted on said fixed bottom plate, a pneiamatic actu¬ator to cause to and fro horizontal swinging or curving motion to said top plate, said moving top plate having an aperture at a predetermined location, means for adjusting the volume of said aperture, and the bottom plate being provided with a hole at a predetermined location on the to and fro path of said aperture.
The volume of the aperture can be adjusted by means of a block sliding within said aperture and a screw means to cause to and fro sliding movement to said block.

The volumetric weigher, as can be seen from the drawings, has two plates one mounted over the other. The bottom plate 1 is fixed to a stand 2. The upper plate 3 is rotatably mounted on a pivot 4 attached to the bottom plate. A pneumatic cylinder 5 is fixed to the bottom plate 1, and its moving rod is fixed to the top plate 3. The pneumatic cylinder when actuated cavtses the top plate to move to and fro almost in a semi-circular path. As the top plate is mounted on the pivot through a bearing 6, a free movement of the plate is achieved.
The top plate 3 has an aperture 7 which acts as a cup for measu¬ring the material, for example tea, to be packed, rhe volume of the aperture 7 can be adjusted by means of a screw 8. As the screw is turned clockwise ia, silding block 9 provided within the aperture moves forward and reduces the aperture volume and vice versa. Thus the volume of the aperture can be adjusted or pre¬set to any desired value. The volume of the aperture can be

directly read from the gauge 10 (see Fig. 6) .
The bottom plate 1 has a hole 11. The location of the hole is such that at a particular position of the top plate its opening (aperture) coincides or comes face to face with the hole 11.

A few advantages of the present volumetric weigher are:-
1. It can be used for weighing any quantity (say, from 25 grams to 1 kg) without using any add-ons like cups.
2. There are no motors and gear boxes unlike the existing volume-trie weigher.
3. Fine adjustments aire possible easily by using the screw provided.
4. Less moving parts and, therefore, less maintenance.
