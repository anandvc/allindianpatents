Title of Invention

AN EQUIPMENT FOR A CHARGE/DISCHARGE TEST OF A METALLISED FILM CAPACITOR TO DETERMINE ITS CURRENT HANDLING CAPACITY
Abstract The present invention relates to the charge/discharge test of a metallised plastic film capacitor by applying Direct Current (DC) voltage and discharging it through the selected resistor. The equipment comprises : an AC power supply unit, control unit, output unit and Alternating Current (AC) to Direct Current (DC) converter. An AC power supply unit generates 0 to 230 V variable AC power being converted to 400VDC variable as per the requirement of voltage. The control unit provides a fixed number of charge/discharge cycles for the test operation, The control unit comprises : two digit thumb wheel switch to set desired time for charge/discharge; and allows desired number of charge/discharge cycles pulses at the output, settable from 1 to 99; a flip-flop having "SET' and "RESET" terminals, to start and reset the timer; a pair of monostable multivibrator for providing square wave pulse outputs for setting and resetting the flip flop; foot switch is connected to "SET" terminal of the flip flop through one monostable multivibrator to start the counter; count over output is connected to reset terminal of the flip flop through the second monostable multivibrator to stop and reset the counter; solid state switch to ensure that only fixed number of pulses being decided by the control unit to be given to output unit. The output unit provides an amplitude controlled DC voltage to the capacitor for charging purpose, said output unit is connected to control unit, said output unit consists of variable transformer unit for providing variable amplitude DC voltage. The AC to DC converter is connected to AC power supply unit for providing DC power supply to control unit and output unit. Also a method of manufacturing a metallised plastic film capacitor with frequency clearing as one of the step is disclosed.

FIELD OF INVENTION
The present invention relates to check the current handling capacity of a metallised plastic film capacitor by inducing /passing desired amount of DC current through the capacitor. More particularly it relates to an equipment to simulate the field condition for CDI application of automobile. It also relates to method of manufacturing a capacitor which includes charge/discharge as one of the step to achieve desired characteristics of the metallised plastic film capacitor for CDI application.
PRIOR ART :
The self healing property and current handling capacity of the metallised plastic film capacitor offers a distinct advantage over the film foil capacitors. These properties depends upon the contact/adhesion between metal coating on the plastic film and the metal sprayed on both sides of capacitor element to form the two contact metal plates of the plastic film capacitor and result of the extreme thinness of the metallised electrode material. If the contact is weak then a contact resistance is formed. When current passes through this contact resistance, heat is generated resulting in shrinkage of metallised plastic film and not withstanding the required current. Wherever improvement in performance, withstanding current carrying capacity and avoidance of premature failure of a metallised plastic film capacitor charging/discharging is a must ie.e simulation of field condition for CDI explication..


The absence of Charge/Discharge test while manufacturing leads to:
1. Loose contact
2. Arcing
3. End spray metal on body which will reduce Insulation Resistance
4. Short circuit of capacitor
5. hjratic tan delta readings
6. More power loss across the capacitor leading to heating of capacitor
7. More capacitance loss leading to reduce life span of capacitor and CDI unit.
The above mentioned method of prior art discusses only the disadvantages such as premature failure, enatic change in tan delta, loss of power and electrical characteristics.
Thus the above mentioned method for testing current handling capacity of a metallised plastic film capacitor along with aforementioned disadvantages associated with them makes it necessary to provide a system/ equipment/device which will overcome the above drawbacks of the prior art and confirm specified current handling capacity of a metallised plastic film capacitor successfully.
