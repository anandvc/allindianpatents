This inventioa relates to an improved seat-ejector system for hinge type seat of two-wheelers and comprising the same in seat assembly of two-wheelers.
As known, in the front hinge-type of seat assembly of any 2-wheeler, one of the brackets is rigidly connected to the bottom base/panel of the seat and other is connected to the top surface of the vehicle frame. They are pivotally connected together by means of hinge pm fastened thereto. On the other (rear) end of the seat-assembly, the spring-loaded latch of a seat-lock is provided in at least in any one of the former and latter parts. The hook is provided in the other part for locking with seat-latch. The lockmg is normally capable of being done automatically against the spring-load of this seat-lock due to the own gravitational force on seat assembly, when the seat is dropped from the normal open-position for the customer's convenience.
When the seat-lock is opened by using one hand against its seat-lock-spring-load for the seat opening, the inevitable-primary problem found will be as follows. Until the seat-latch and its hook are just relieved from each other by keeping in open-condition of the seat-lock using one hand, the seat rear-end is to be simultaneously lifted by the other hand. The usage of both hands for opening the seat until both the seat-latch and the hook are just relieved from each other, is difficult at all times.
This problem has already been solved in known art by the seat-locking
system comprising a locking system and the ejector which are integrated

to each other to both open and eject/life the seat assembly while opening.
