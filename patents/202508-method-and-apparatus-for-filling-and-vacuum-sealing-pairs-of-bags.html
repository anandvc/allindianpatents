Title of Invention

METHOD AND APPARATUS FOR FILLING AND VACUUM SEALING PAIRS OF BAGS
Abstract A method of filling and vacuum sealing pairs of containers in the form of envelopes or bags provided with or without side and/or bottom bellows, made of flexible material, connected with each other by a central strip, comprising : a) feeding a filling and sealing apparatus with pairs of containers (3, 3') in the form of a continuous web (2) taken from a packaging box in which the web is Oconcertina-folded ; b) hanging the web from a horizontal rod-shaped support (6) to move forward ; c) spreading apart the upper openings of the containers d) filling the containers in a filling station, as said containers move ; e) heat-sealing the openings of the filled containers ; f) separating the sealed containers from each other; and g) collecting the containers in packaging means, is characterized in that step e) takes place in a chamber (32) which is opened to receive some couples of paired containers, whereafter the chamber is hermetically closed, a vacuum or a strong negative pressure is created therein and the container openings are heat-sealed.

METHOD OF AND APPARATUS FOR FILLING AND VACUUM SEALING PAIRS OF CONTAINERS IN THE FORM OF ENVELOPES OR BAGS
The present invention relates to a method of and an apparatus for filling and vacuum sealing of pairs of containers in the form of envelopes or bags fed in the form of a continuous web to filling stations.
In the field of packages, above all as far as goods of large consumption are concerned, widely used are envelopes of flexible plastic material that are directly heat-sealable or envelopes of aluminium foil made heat-sealable by the presence of a thin coating of plastic material.
In both cases the surface designed to remain externally visible is usually printed with inscriptions for the identification of the contained goods.
Of the different products marketed in containers of this type, a great number of them requires aseptic envelopes or bags to be vacuum sealed for better conservation in the course of time of the product held therein.
It is an object of the present invention to provide a method of and an apparatus for feeding and

The webs of paired containers facing each other that can be used in the filling and vacuum-sealing method according to the present invention can consist of any known type of paired containers, i.e. bags or simple envelopes/ envelopes with bellows-like bottom, envelopes with two side bellows, or envelopes with side bellows and simplified bottom bellows to allow vertical self-support of the container.
Envelopes with side bellows are preferred because they allow a greater automatic opening out of the flaps

This feature makes the production by the user customer easier because the latter must only attend to carrying out the final hermetic seal for closing the container after having filled the same. Thus also the final controls are reduced in favour of a more practical operation. From the point of view o£ surface contamination too, the closed bag ia more reliable than the material on a reel.

In the case under review, the apparatus ia fed with a web 2 of paired containers 3, 31 of flexible material joined to each other by an intermediate strip 20 and concertina-folded inside a packaging box 5, as supplied from the manufacturer of the containers.
The apparatus is provided with a rod-shaped support 6 descending gradually until close to the packaging box 5 where is centered the strip 2 0 that keeps in a connected relationship the web of paired containers 3, 31 folded up in the box itself (see also Figs. 2 and 3) . On proceeding towards the filling and sealing stations, the containers 3, 31 moved by a drive or transport unit 10 are arranged on either side of support 6 which, downstream, at position 8, progressively widens out until it becomes, at the transport unit 10, a track of practically the same transverse width as strip 20.
The transport unit 10 is preferably made up of an upper roller 11 provided with linear jutting out elements opposite which are the corresponding cavities of an underlying nip-roller so as to pluck the material of the union strip 2 0 as shown under reference 14 in Figs 2 and 3, thereby achieving a positive and regular forward movement of the containers 3-3 '.
