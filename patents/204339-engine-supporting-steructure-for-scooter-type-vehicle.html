Title of Invention

"ENGINE SUPPORTING STERUCTURE FOR SCOOTER-TYPE VEHICLE"
Abstract An engine supporting structure for a scooter-type vehicle in which a carburetor (35) and an engine (E) connected to said carburetor (35) are disposed under a seat (42) , and a pair of right and left links (682, 681) for connecting a body frame (F) to a pair of right and left engine side brackets (672, 67i) provided on said engine (E) are connected to each other by means of a cross-member (69), characterized in that: said cross-member (69) is formed into an approximately U-shape opened downwardly; and said cross-member (69) is disposed astride, on the front side from said links (682, 681) , an intake pipe (83) for connecting said engine (E) to said carburetor (35) disposed behind said links (682, 681).

[Detailed Description of the Invention]
[Technical Field to Which the Invention Pertains]
The present invention relates to a scooter-type vehicle in which a carburetor and an engine connected to the carburetor are disposed under a seat, and a pair of right and left links for connecting a body frame to a pair of right and left engine side brackets provided on the engine are connected to each other by means of a cross-member, and particularly to an improvement in a structure for supporting the engine to the body frame.

[Prior Art]
The scooter-type vehicle of this type has been known, for example, from Japanese Patent Laid-open No. Hei 3-213482. U30Q3J
[Problem to be Solved by the Invention]
The above-described prior art scooter-type vehicle is configured such that a carburetor is disposed in front of both links and a cross-member is disposed behind the links in such a manner as to extend upwardly. Accordingly, the carburetor must be disposed in a limited space between the links and the engine, with a result that the degree of freedom in arrangement of the carburetor
2

becomes small.
frfl 0 0 a ■}
In view of the foregoing, the present invention has been made, and an object of the present invention is to provide an engine supporting structure for a scooter-type vehicle, which is capable of increasing the degree of freedom in arrangement of a carburetor.
[Means for Solving the Problem]
To achieve the above object, according to an invention described in claim 1, there is provided an engine supporting structure for a scooter-type vehicle in which a carburetor and an engine connected to the carburetor are disposed under a seat, and a pair of right and left links for connecting a body frame to a pair of right and left engine side brackets provided on the engine are connected to each other by means of a cross-member, characterized in that the cross-member is formed into an approximately U-shape opened downwardly; and the cross-member is disposed astride, on the front side from the links, an intake pipe for connecting the engine to the carburetor disposed behind the links.
With this configuration, since the carburetor is disposed behind both the links and the cross-member is
3

disposed astride, on th-e front side from both the links, the intake pipe for connecting the carburetor to the engine, it is possible to make the degree of freedom in arrangement of the carburetor higher than that in the prior art structure in which the carburetor is disposed in front of the links.
According to an invention described in claim 2, in addition to the configuration of the invention described in claim 1, the engine is configured as a four-cycle engine disposed substantially in the horizontal direction, and the cross-member is disposed between the engine and a luggage box mounted to the body frame at a position under the seat. With this configuration, it is possible to eliminate an inconvenience of causing the position of the seat being a relatively higher fqjr allowing the cross-member to be freely moved with no interference with the luggage box.
