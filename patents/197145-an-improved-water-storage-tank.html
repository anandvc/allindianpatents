Title of Invention

"AN IMPROVED WATER STORAGE TANK"
Abstract This invention relates to a water storage tank. The water storage has a water container having a central opening in the top cover of the container. A plurality of air pockets are provided around the walls of the container for the circulation of air around the container. An inclined chamber is provided on the bottom of the container so as to constitute a silt chamber in the bottom end and at one side thereof. A synohromised valve assembly is provided in the inclined chamber for allowing the supply of the water to the container and for draining the silt from the container. A transparent window is provided in the silt chamber to determine the accumulation of the silt into the silt chamber.
Full Text This invention relates to an improved water storage tank.
The water storage tank are well known in the art and comprises a cylindrical or square type container. A central opening is provided in the top cover of the container for facilitating the cleaning of the container as and when required. An inlet is provided near the top end having a float valve secured therewith inside the container to allow filling of the container with water. A water outlet having a water tap provided outside the container for the supply of water from said container.
There are certain disadvantages associated with the conventional water tank. One of the main disadvantage is that the float valve is placed below the top cover of the water tank and therefore a substantial space near the top end inside the water tank is not utilized and thus reduces the capacity of the water tank.
Another disadvantage is that the silt is accumulated at the bottom of the water tank and the water tank is not completely cleaned even by a plumber.
Yet another disadvantage is that the water become very hot during the summer season and which is very uncomfortable for use and particularly when it has to be cooled in a refrigerator.
Still another disadvantage is that at lot of water is wasted when the float value is damaged or not working properly which is very common and thus continuous monitoring is required during the filling process of the water tank.
Therefore the main object of the present invention is to provide a novel water storage tank in which the water remains at the atmospheric temperature in all the seasons.
Another object of the present invention is to provide a water storage tank having a novel float valve near the top cover of the water tank for the maximum utilization of the area of the water tank.
Yet another object of the present invention is to provide a water tank having a synchronized valve assembly for water inlet and water draining which avoids the silt accumulation at the bottom of the water tank.
Still another object of the present invention is to provide a water tank having the air pockets around the walls of the water tank which avoids the heating of the water contained inside the water tank.
According to this invention, there is provided an improved water storage tank comprising: a water container having a central opening in the top of the container, a float valve provided near the top of the container to stop supply of water to the container when water level reaches upto level of the valve, an inlet tube connected to the said float valve, an outlet tube provided to get water supply from the said tank, characterized in that: a plurality of air pockets are provided around the wall of the said container for allowing circulation of air around the said container, an inclined chamber with inclined top surface is provided on the bottom of the said container for facilitating of silt accumulation in a silt chamber provided in the bottom on one side of said inclined chamber, the said float valve comprises a hollow sphere with slots/openings provided diametrically opposite to each other, a ball disposed within the said sphere so as to stop the supply of water to the container by closing the opening of said inlet tube when water level reaches upto level of the said valve, a synchronized valve comprising an inlet valve and a drain valve coupled to each other is provided in the said inclined chamber so as to ensure closure of the drain valve when the inlet valve is open and vice versa, and a floating tube with an opening below the top cover of the container provided for draining excess water.
In accordance with this invention the water storage tank comprises a cylindrical or square type container having the air pockets provided outside the wails of the container. The air pockets are provided for the circulation of the environmental air around the water container which facilitate to keep the water inside the container at the atmospheric temperature. An inclined chamber is provided inside the water tank above the bottom of the container so as to constitute a small silt. accumulation chamber at the bottom of the container. The synchronised valve assembly is provided in the inclined chamber such that when the water is supplied in the water tank the cleaning outlet is closed but the water supply from the water outlet may be obtained. A novel valve is provided at the water inlet just below the top cover of the water tank for supplying the water into the water tank and to stop the water supply when the water tank is filled up with the water upto the level of the valve. A drain tube having an opening near the top cover is provided for the draining of the water when the water level crosses the valve level.
