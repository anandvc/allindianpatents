Title of Invention

FUEL SAVING DISPLAY DEVICE FOR A TWO WHEELER
Abstract The present invention relates to a fuel saving display device for a two wheeler. The shed device will automatically lit up or give audible indication during deceleration making the rider aware that the vehicle is consuming less fuel. The display unit will not give any indication when the rider is accelerating or driving at steady speed there by warning him/her that the vehicle is consuming more fuel.

Field of Invention
This invention in general relates to auto mobile. In particular this invention relates to scooter type motor cycle. The present invention relates to a "Fuel saving display device for a two wheeler" State of Art
Whenever an automobile is decelerating, fuel is wasted because combustion of air fuel mixture normally required for furnishing power to the engine will not be necessary. This has been overcome by providing various known devices, which automatically makes the air fuel mixture lean by supplying more air whenever the engine is decelerating thereby constlming less fuel. These devices, even though do provide lean air fuel mixture and give lower fuel consumption, there is no visible indication or communication to the rider that he is operating in deceleration mode and consuming less fuel while driving. In the absence of this there is always a tendency for the riders to drive in steady state or accelerate the vehicles thereby consuming more fuel without their knowledge. This tendency is more likely to happen in congested traffic, planned or unplanned stoppages or when the rider approaches traffic signal.
This invention therefore proposes a fuel saving display device, which will automatically lit up or give audible indication during deceleration making the rider aware that the vehicle is consuming less fuel. The display unit will not give any indication when the rider is accelerating or driving at steady speed there by warning him/her that the vehicle is consuming more fuel. Detailed Description of an embodiment of invention
Sensor means are provided for sensing the rpm of the engine E, One of the possible forms the sensor means can take is the pulsar coil or the charging coil P of the magneto of the engine e, the output of the pulsar coil or charging coil being directly related to the rpm of the engine. The output signal of the pulsar coil is fed to a micro controller M.Throttle potentiometer T means are provided for the throttle of the engine. The potentiometer T is connected to the micro controller M and feeds a signal thereto from which the micro controller M determines the throttle position. The output of the micro controller M is fed to the Solenoid Valve V and a LED or audible display unit D, which is fixed to the instrument panel of the vehicle in front of the rider.
Now assuming that the vehicle is running and the rider releases the throttle control, the engine speed falls as the engine decelerates. This situation is signaled to the micro controller M, which immediately opens the solenoid valve V to let in more air to the engine E, through the air filter F, bypassing the carburetor C and thus provide a lean mixture, thereby saving fuel during deceleration. Under this condition the signal from the solenoid valve also operates a LED display unit D to glow or give an audible sound communicating the rider that fuel is being saved. When the deceleration ceases or when

the vehicle is running at steady speed, the micro controller M closes the solenoid valve V to bring conditions back to normal thereby switching off LED or audible display unit D.
