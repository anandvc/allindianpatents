TRAWLER WITH COLD STORAGE UNIT
Field of Invention
This invention generally relates to a cold storage unit. The cold storage unit is operated based on the refrigeration system. More particularly this invention relates to a cold storage unit in trawler.
This invention is mainly concentrated on storage of fresh fish for a period of 7 to IS days. The storage of fresh fish may be carried out with the help of cold storage unit.
Though the invention is explained with respect to fishing boat this invention will be useful in various applications such as automobiles, mobile units where in cooling or refrigeration is required.
To fabricate a cold storage unit for preserving marine biological product in trawler the components required are engine, alternator, storage batteries, inverter, and compressor.
Prior Art
In fishing boats, to preserve the fish during fishing without spoilage, at present ice is used. Fishermen take ice bars with them during fishing and ice bars occupy the major space in the cold storage units. The major disadvantages of this method are the fishing period depends on the availability of ice cubes and fishermen cannot extend the period of fishing due to melting of ice bars. The initial investment of icebox, maintenance cost, and running cost is high. Transportation of icebox is difficult. Spoilage of fishes is high due to direct contact with ice pieces. Net profit is loss, and one icebox is meant only for the storage of ice bar.
Accordingly what is needed in the art is an improvement that overcomes the above-mentioned deficiencies of the prior art.

It is to be noted that the prior art descriptions give in detail information of the technology, method, process and system known in the art. In the motorized fishing boats, the engine of the boat continuously runs throughout the period of fishing. The engine runs an alternator and it supplies power to the storage batteries (24 V). The battery power is connected to the inverter and a compressor is run by the power from inverter. The refrigeration system thus maintains the temperature for preserving the fish.

2) The I.C Engine is the source for all the power supply inside the
trawler. Normally this equipment is placed at the bottom part of the
trawler. They are normally held in position with the help of long bolts
screwed to the surface of the trawler. They occupy the major portion in

the bottom part. There should be enough space provided around the engine for the purpose of starting and maintenance.
3) The Alternator is normally attached with the body of the LC.engine. The main purpose of the alternator is to convert the mechanical energy from the engine to electrical energy. It also converts the AC form of electrical energy into DC for the storage in the battery. The alternator should be kept such that there is no contact with that of water. It requires periodical checking for the output produced. It occupies less space.
4) The Battery is the device for storage of electrical energy in the form of DC. It gets the DC power continuously from the alternator as far as the engine is running. There are normally two batteries employed in the trawler . The batteries are placed nearer to the I.C engine. They should be kept away from the contact of water.

5) The Inverter is placed nearer to the batteries. They receive power from the batteries. The purpose of the inverter is to produce a 230-volt output from 24-volt input. The connection of the inverter with that of the batteries is done by means of wires. This enables the inverter to be placed anywhere nearer to the battery. It requires special attention that he inverter should not move anywhere during the running condition.
6) The Cold Storage Unit is the last and the final equipment involved in this method. The inverter provides the required power to run the compressor of the cold storage unit. This method provides flexibility to the fisherman for placing this cold storage unit anywhere in trawler according to their convenience. Normally this unit is placed in the top surface of the trawler. The link between the cold storage unit and the inverter is made possible with he help of wires. This unit occupies more space than all the equipments involved in this method.

Advantages of the Invention
1. The available I.C. Engine generates power for running the cold storage unit.
2. Marine products can be stored at the required temperature.
3. There is no direct contact of ice with the marine products.
4. No need to carry ice cubes and the cost of preserving the fish is reduced.
5. The fishing period can be extended as there is no problem of machine the ice and temperature is maintained at a particular value.
6. Overall cost of maintenance at a particular value.
7. Entire cold storage container can be occupied by marine products, as there is no space occupied by ice.
8. Drainage pipes are used for cleaning the storage unit.
9. Separate storing space for storing different varieties of marine products.

We Claim
1) A trawler comprising an LC engine, alternator for producing
24 V D.C. powers, one set of batteries characterized in that it comprises a refrigeration unit connected to batteries through an inverter.
2) The trawler as claimed in claim 1, wherein the alternator gets power
from the LC. engine, which is also used to run the trawler.
3) The trawler as claimed in claim 1, wherein the alternator is directly
connected to the batteries in such a way the power produced from the
alternator is stored in the batteries.
4) The trawler as claimed in claim 1, wherein the inverter is connected to
the batteries and refrigeration unit in such a way that it draws the
required power from the batteries and converts it into A.C current
and supplies to the refrigeration unit.
5) The trawler as claimed in claim 1, wherein the temperature inside the
refrigeration unit is maintained at -22 Degree Celsius continuously to
preserve the marine biological product.
6) The trawler as claimed in claim 1, wherein the refrigeration unit can
be fixed anywhere in the trawler.
