Title of Invention

WATER-SUSPENDABLE AGRICULTURAL CHEMICAL COMPOSITION
Abstract The object of the invention is to provide a water-suspendable agricultural chemical composition comprising a liquid agricultural chemical ingredient and a solid agricultural chemical ingredient that ensures satisfactory dispersion, suspension, emulsification or combinations thereof of the contained ingredients and prevents decomposition of active ingredients while also inhibiting their precipitation and aggregation even when stored for a long period of time. The invention is carried out by preparing a water-suspendable agricultural chemical composition, which comprises at least one liquid agricultural chemical ingredient, at least one solid agricultural chemical ingredient, at least an oil and at least a surfactant.
Full Text The present invention relates to a stable, water-suspendable agricultural chemical composition comprising at least one liquid agricultural chemical ingredient and at least one solid agricultural chemical ingredient.
Agricultural chemicals are classified on the basis of the drug form of the composition, and in the case of agricultural chemicals used for paddy riqe in particular, since the purpose of their application is the eradication of harmful insects and undesired vegetation like, for example, grasses and weeds in rice paddies, solid granules are primarily in consideration for reliably applying the required amount of agricultural chemical while also saving on labor. However, in the case of granular compositions, the problem is that it is difficult to uniformly spray the entire field. In addition, attempts.have recently been made to improvework efficiency using technology that increases the size of the granules themselves, that increases the amount of the active ingredient (herbicidal active ingredient), and reduces the sprayed amount.
On the other hand, in addition to solid granules, there are also emulsions used for pre-transplantation treatment and water-suspendable agricultural chemical compositions used for post-transplantation treatment. Liquid agricultural chemical compositions, and particularly water-suspendable agricultural chemical compositions, have attracted attention in recent years for reasons such as their ability to reduce the burden on the environment, their ease of handling, and their ease of uniform spraying, dilution and adjustment of concentration. However, water-suspendable agricultural chemical compositions have problems such as instability of the active ingredient in the presence of moisture, difficulty in homogeneously suspending and dispersing all active ingredients in the case of blending two or more types of active ingredients, and particularly in the case of combining a liquid ingredient with a solid ingredient, and susceptibility of the active ingredients to separation, precipitation or aggregation.
In the case of formulating a liquid composition in which is blended two or more types of active ingredients having different physical properties, it is necessary to design the composition so as to inhibit hydrolysis, precipitation and aggregation of all active ingredients while ensuring adequate dispersion, suspension, emulsification or combinations thereof of
the active ingredients' Although auxiliary agents such as emulsifiers. emulsion stabilizers and surfactants are frequently used, there are cases in which hydrolysis and aggregation of the active ingredients are promoted corresponding to the compatibility between the active ingredients and auxiliary agents used. For example, in the case of formulating an aqueous liquid composition comprising pretilachlor, a type of chloroacetoamide-based herbicide that is a liquid at normal temperatures, and bensulfuron-methyl, a type of sulfonylurea-based herbicide that is a solid at normal temperatures, although it is essential to use an auxiliary agent such as an emulsion stabilizer or surfactant in order to suspend and disperse both active ingredients, there are problems such as the occurrence of significant hydrolysis of -the bensulfuron-methyl, precipitation and aggregation depending on the type of auxiliary agent used.
For example, Japanese Unexamined Patent Publication No. H10-287503 describes that an aqueous suspended composition composed of a sulfonylurea-based herbicide as the agricultural chemical active ingredient and/or polyvinyl alcohol having an average degree of polymerization of 2000 or less and a degree of saponification of 69 to 90 moI% as a herbicidal active ingredient having barnyard grass herbicidal activity, and water has satisfactory composition stability, improved diffusivity and is able to prevent chemical damage by preventing adhesion of the chemical to the rice plant bodies. In addition, Japanese Unexamined Patent Publication No. H9-183704 describes that the addition of lignin sulfonate allows the obtaining of an aqueous suspension composition containing a sulfonylurea-based herbicide compound having superior long-term storage stability.
An object of the present invention is to provide a water-suspendable agricultural chemical composition comprising a' liquid agricultural chemical ingredient and a solid agricultural chemical ingredient wherein, dispersion, suspension, emulsification or combinations thereof of the contained ingredients are ensured, hydrolysis is inhibited even when stored for a long period of time, and precipitation and aggregation are also inhibited.
As a result of conducting extensive studies that focused on the aforementioned problems, the inventors of the present invention found that the aforementioned problems are solved by blending an oil into a water-suspendable agricultural chemical composition comprising at least a liquid agricultural chemical ingredient and at least a solid agricultural
chemical ingredient, thereby leading to completion of the present invention. Thus, the present application includes the following inventions.

Examples of this oil Include mineral oils such as spindle oil; machine oil and liquid paraffin, aromatic solvents such as Solvesso and Shellspl, vegetable oils such as rapeseed oil, soybean oil, sunflower seed oil, castor oil, pine oil and cottonseed oil, and derivatives of these oils such as methyl esters and other ester derivatives, epoxidized derivatives and fatty acid esters, and these can be used alone or in combination. These oifs are preferably blended in an amount of 0.1 to 30 parts by weight, and more preferably 1 to 10 parts by weight, based on 100 parts by weight for the total weight of the water-suspendable agricultural chemical composition as claimed in the present invention.
A surfactant is preferably contained in a water-suspendable agricultural chemical composition as claimed in the present invention. Examples of preferable surfactants include polyoxyethylene-polyoxypropylene block polymer types of surfactants such as
polyoxyethylene- polyoxypropylene block polymer and alkyl polyoxyethylene-polyoxypropylene block polymer ether, while polyoxyethylene- polyoxypropylene (POE-POP) block polymers are particularly preferable. The use of the aforementioned surfactants is preferable because it makes it possible to expect satisfactory emulsification of liquid agricultural chemical ingredients and satisfactory dispersion of solid agricultural chemical ingredients, as well as inhibit the decomposition of solid agricultural chemical ingredients by liquid agricultural chemical ingredients. The aforementioned surfactants are preferably blended in an amount of 0.01 to 10 parts by weight, and more preferably 0.1 to 5 parts by weight, based on 100 parts by weight for the total weight of the water-suspendable 'agricultural chemical composition as claimed in the present invention.
A preferable surfactant is a POE-POP block polymer, and those having an average molecular weight of 10,000 or more and an ethylene oxide weight ratio of 50% or more (such as Epan U-108, Daiicht Kogyo Seiyaku) are particularly preferable since they are able to inhibit the occurrence of aggregation under severe testing conditions. However, even those having an average molecular weight of 10,000 or less or ethylene oxide weight ratio of 50% or less are able to achieve satisfactory emulsification of liquid active ingredients and inhibit decomposition of solid active ingredients. In addition, the combined use of a plurality of POE-POP block polymers having different average molecular weights or different ethylene oxide weight ratios makes it possible to obtain compositions having desired physical properties.

In addition to each of the aforementioned ingredients, a water-suspendable agricultural chemical composition as claimed in the present invention can also contain a commonly used, vehicle typically used in water-suspendable agricultural chemical compositions, examples of which include antifoaming agents such as silicone, anti-mold agents such as 1,3-benzisothiazolin-3-one and other benzisothiarolinone compounds, pH adjuster's such as K2PO4, antifreeze agents such as propylene glycol, thickeners such as xanthane gum, silica and bentonite, inert carriers such as clay, diatomaceous earth and calcium carbonate, preservatives, bonding agents, fertilizers and other active ingredients.
A water-suspendable agricultural chemical composition as claimed in the present invention may be produced using ordinary methods, such as by mixing the aforementioned active ingredients directly with the aforementioned blending vehicles and liquid or solid carriers, and/or crushing them together.
Preferably, a water-suspendable agricultural chemical composition as claimed in the present invention can be prepared in the manner described below.
(a) Composition of concentrated crushed slurry of solid agricultural chemical ingredient
A portion of various types of surfactants and other assistants are mixed by dissolving or dispersing In water followed by addition of a solid agricultural chemical ingredient. The mixture is then suitably wet crushed with a bead mill such as a batch-type bead mill to prepare a concentrated slurry.
(b) Composition of emulsion of liquid agricultural chemical ingredient
Various types of surfactants and other assistants are mixed in water. An oily phase in which an oil has been dissolved is added to the liquid agricultural chemical ingredient while forcibly stirring with a homogenizer and so forth to emulsify the oily phase in the aqueous phase! This mixture is then suitably forcibly emulsified to a desired particle diameter.
(c) Production of the composition
The solid agricultural chemical ingredient slurry is added to the liquid agricultural chemical ingredient emulsion (in the ratio emulsion to slurry of, for example, 9:1) followed by suitably mixing with a mixer and so forth to obtain a composition.
An herbicide according to a water-suspendable agricultural chemical composition as claimed in the present invention Is preferable for controlling rice paddy weeds when under water. This Is preferably carried out by applying the required amount of the water-suspendable agricultural chemical composition as claimed in the present invention directly to the rice paddy either directly or after diluting to a suitable concentration with water, or by adding to water that flows into the rice paddy (so-called spout treatment). Moreover, a composition as claimed in the present invention can also be applied simultaneous to planting by a planting machine.
Although a water-suspendable agricultural chemical composition as claimed in the present invention can be applied in the same manner as ordinary spraying, it can also be diluted in a larger amount of water. A water-suspendable agricultural chemical composition as claimed in the present invention can be preferably used in an applied amount of 100 to 50,000 ml/ha, and more preferably 1,000 to 10,000 ml/ha.
