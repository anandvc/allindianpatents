Title of Invention

DISPENSING DEVICE FOR FLUID PRODUCT ACTIVATED BY INHALING
Abstract Dispensing device for a fluid product activated by inhaling, which comprises a product reservoir (20) containing a propellant gas, a pump cr dosing valve (30) to selectively dispense the product through a dispensing orifice (9), and an activating device controlled by inhaling, an activating device comprising: - a control element (3; mobile between an inoperative position in which it blocks the dispensing orifice (9) and a control position, said control element (3) being displaced by the user, - an actuating member (7), mobile between a non-actuating position, in which the pump cr dosing valve (30) is not actuated and an actuating position in which the pump or dosing valve (30) is actuated, said actuating member (7) being urged towards its actuating position when the control element (3) is in its control position, - a retaining element (8), mobile between a retaining position in which it co-operates with the actuating element (7) to retain it in its non-actuating position and a releasing position, in which said actuating member (7) moves into its actuating position, and - a cam element (2), mobile between a storing position in which said retaining element (3) is locked in its retaining position, and an inhaling position, in which said retaining element moves into its releasing position, said cam element (2) being urged towards its storing position and being moved into its inhaling position when the user of the device inhales, English translation of the amended sheets of International Preliminary Examination Report characterized in that the retaining element (8) is elastically deformable between its retaining position and its releasing position, the retaining element (8) being urged towards its releasing position by the actuating member (7) when the control element (3) is in its control position.

This invention relates to a dispensing device for 5 fluid product activated by inhaling and more particularly, a device of the aerosol type activated by inhaling.
Devices activated by inhaling (generally referred to by the term B.A.I.) are well known in the state of
10 the technology. The main advantage of this type of device is that the dispensing of the product is synchronized with the inhalation of the patient, to guarantee good dispensation of the product. Hence, within the field of aerosol devices, that is to say
15 devices in which the products are dispensed using a propellant gas, numerous types of devices which are activated by inhaling have been proposed. However, all of these devices have the disadvantage of being made up of a large number of components, that is to say, they
20 are complicated and expensive to manufacture and to assemble, which is obviously disadvantageous.
The aim of this invention is to provide a dispensing device for a fluid product activated by inhaling, which functions in a reliable way and which
25 is simple and inexpensive to manufacture and to assemble.
A further aim of this invention is to provide a dispensing device for a fluid product activated by inhaling, which operates in a reliable way and does not
30 require a high degree of aspiration to activate the dispensing of the product.

Referring to the drawings, the dispensing device comprises a product reservoir 20, containing the product to be dispensed and a propellant gas, and a
25 pump or dosing valve 30 mounted on said reservoir 20 to selectively dispense the product. The output orifice from the pump or dosing valve 30 is connected via a channel to a dispensing orifice 9, such as an end mouth-piece, through which the user inhales the
30 dispensed product. This dispensing orifice may obviously be created in any manner whatsoever as a function of the desired application for the device. This description will be made with reference to an

6
inhaler comprising a end mouth-piece. However, it is clear that the invention is also applicable to other types of uses, such as, for example, an endonasal treatment. 5 According to the invention, the device comprises an activating device, controlled by inhalation of the user which is intended to activate the pump or dosing valve 30 when the user inhales through the end mouth¬piece 9. Advantageously, the reservoir 20, the pump or
10 dosing valve 30 and the device for actuation by inhaling are contained in a casing 1, which advantageously incorporates the dispensing orifice 9, and on which a control element 3 is mounted which blocks the dispensing orifice 9 in the inoperative
15 position.
According to the invention, this control element 3 is mobile between its inoperative position represented in Figures 1, 2 and 3 and a control position represented in Figures 4 and 5. Movement of the control
20 element 3 from its inoperative position into its control position has the effect of pre-stressing the activating device, which will subsequently be actuated by inhaling, with, as a consequence, the dispensing of a dose of product through the dispensing orifice 9. To
25 this end, the device for actuation by inhaling comprises a cam element 2 which includes a part, such as a flap which is able to co-operate with an opening 11 provided in the casing 1 of the device. This opening 11 is preferably created in the casing 1 opposite the
30 dispensing orifice through which the user inhales, so that during the inhalation a pressure drop is created inside the casing 1 causing movement of the cam element 2 from a storing position, shown in Figures 1, 3 and 4

7
towards an inhaling position, shown in Figure 5. The cam element 2 is, when inoperative, urged towards its storing position, but this urging is sufficiently light to be overcome, even by weak inhaling. The device for 5 actuation by inhaling additionally comprises an actuating member 7 which is mobile between a non-actuating position, in which the pump or dosing valve 30 is not actuated, as shown in Figures 1 to 4, and an actuating position in which the pump or dosing valve 30 10 is actuated to dispense a dose of product, as shown in Figure 5. When the control element 3 is moved towards its control position, the actuating member 7 is urged in the direction of its actuating position. This is preferably done by means of a spring 6 which is 15 suitable for urging the actuating member 7 towards its actuating position. As shown in the Figures, the spring 6 may preferably be arranged between the control element 3 and the actuating member 7. Of course this spring may equally well be provided at another position 20 in the device, for example acting on the reservoir 20 in order to urge it in the direction of the actuating member 7.
The activating device additionally comprises a retaining element 8 which is able to co-operate with 25 the actuating member 7. This retaining element 8 is mobile between a retaining position, in which it retains the actuating member 7 in its position of non-actuation, as shown in Figures 2 and 3, and a releasing position, in which said actuating member 7 can be moved 30 in the direction of its actuating position, as shown in Figure 5. This retaining element 8 is preferably elastically deformable between its retaining position and its releasing position and is urged towards its

8
releasing position by the actuating member 7 which itself is urged towards its actuating position. The retaining element 8 can be created in the form of a split ring 8b, as shown in Figure 3, or in the form of 5 one or more elastic flexible legs 8a as shown in Figure 2. Advantageously, the retaining element 8 is housed in the groove 71 made in the actuating member 7, this groove 71 preferably having a slanting side wall, which has the effect of exerting a radial force en the
10 retaining element 8 when the actuating member 7 is subjected to an axial force while being urged towards its actuating position.
According to the invention, the cam element 2 in its storing position, is able to lock the retaining
15 element 8 in its retaining position. When the cam element 2 is moved into its inhaling position, said retaining element 8 can be moved towards its releasing position so that the actuating member 7 can then be moved towards its actuating position in order to
20 activate the pump or dosing valve 30.
Preferably, the activating device comprises a locking member 5 which co-operates on the one hand, with the retaining element 8 to lock it in its retaining position while the cam element 2 is in its
25 storing position, and which, on the other hand, co¬operates with the cam element 2 so that movement of the cam element 2 towards its inhaling position acts on the locking member 5 to move it from its locking position towards a position for unlocking the retaining element.
30 Advantageously, this locking member is created in the form of a sleeve 5 that is able to slide around the retaining member 8 in order, when in the locking position, to prevent radial deformation of it towards

9
its releasing position. Preferably, said sleeve 5 comprises a first cylindrical part which, in the locking position, co-operates with the retaining element 8, and a second part of greater diameter which 5 in the unlocking position, co-operates with the retaining element 8 to enable it to move by elastic deformation towards its releasing position. Advantageously, this second part of the sleeve 5 is made conical, this particular shape being advantageous,
10 after inhalation of a dose of product, to bring the device for activation by inhaling back into its non-operative position as will be explained below.
Advantageously, the device for activation by inhaling comprises a structural element 4 which is
15 fixed to the casing 1, and on which the control member 3 and the cam element 2 are pivotably mounted as may be seen in Figure 2. According to an embodiment variant which is shown in Figure 2, the retaining element 8 can be produced in the form of one or more flexible elastic
20 feet 8a, these being advantageously produced with said structural element 4, as one component. The structural element 4 may possibly comprise a flexible tongue arranged between the control member 3 and the spring 6 to facilitate co-operation between these two
25 components.
The operation of the device -according to the invention is as follows.
Before the user wishes to use the device to administer a dose of the product to himself, said
30 device is in the storing position, as shown in Figures 1, 2 and 3, in which the control member 3 is in the storing position, blocking the end mouth-piece 9, the actuating member 7 is in the non-operative position and

10
is not being urged in the direction of its actuating position by the spring 6 which is not compressed, the retaining element 8 being in the retaining position and the locking member 5 in the locking position, while the 5 cam element 2 is in the storing position blocking the opening 11 of the casing. Preferably, the retaining element 8 is not towards its releasing position while the actuating member 7 is not being urged towards its actuating position.
10 When the user wishes to use the device, he moves the control element 3 towards its control position, shown in Figure 4. This has the effect, on the one hand, of opening the end mouth-piece 9 and, on the other hand, of compressing the spring 6 which then
15 exerts an axial force on the actuating member 7 to urge it towards its actuating position. The actuating member 7 is however retained in its non-operative position by the retaining element 8 which is co-operating with the groove 71 of the actuating member 7. The slanting side
20 wall of this groove 71 exerts a radial force on the retaining element 8 to urge it towards its releasing position. However, the retaining element 8 is locked in its retaining position by the locking sleeve 5 which is in the locking position and which prevents any
25 deformation of the retaining element 8. The device is therefore in the stand-by position.
When the user inhales through the end mouth-piece 9, it creates a pressure drop inside the casing 1 which has the effect of causing the cam element 2 to pivot
30 from its storing position shown in Figure 4, towards its inhaling position, shown in Figure 5. In particular, the flap part of the cam element 2 is moved away from the opening 11 in the casing 1 to balance the


11
pressure drop created inside the casing. This movement of the cam element 2 from its storing position towards its inhaling position has the effect of causing the locking sleeve 5 to slide axially in relation to the 5 retaining element 8. Advantageously, a cam part 2a of the cam element 2 crosses the sleeve 5 and turns in relation to it when the cam element 2 is moved towards its inhaling position, so that said sleeve 5 moves in synchronization with said cam element 2. The retaining 10 element 8, under the effect of the force exerted on it by the actuating member 7, is then deformed towards its releasing position which then allows the actuating member 7 to be propelled towards its actuating position by the compressed spring 6 which is acting upon it. The 15 product dose is then expelled through the pump or dosing valve 30 in the direction of the end mouth-piece 9, to be inhaled by the user in a way that is synchronized with his inhaling.
At the end of the inhalation, the user returns the
20 control element 3 to its inoperative position in which
it blocks the end mouth-piece 9. This enables the
spring 6 to be decompressed and returned to its
inoperative position so that the actuating member 7
returns to its non-actuating position with the groove
25 71 opposite the retaining element 8. This then returns
to its retaining position, either through its own
elasticity, or by being gently assisted by the conical
part of the locking member 5 which exerts an inward
radial force on said retaining element 8, when axially
30 displaced from its unlocking position towards its
locking position. The retaining element 8 therefore
returns to its retaining position, the locking sleeve 5
returns to its locking position and the cam element 2

12
returns to its storing position, so that the device is ready for later use.
This invention therefore provides a fluid product dispensing device activated by inhaling, in which the 5 device activated by inhaling is particularly simple and therefore inexpensive to manufacture and to produce since it comprises a minimum number of constituent items. Hence in its preferred embodiment which is the one shown in the drawings, the activating device
10 comprises six or seven components, namely the control element 3, the structural element 4, the spring 6, the actuating member 7, the retaining element 8 if it is not produced as one component with the structural element 4, the locking member 5 and the cam element 2.
15 It could even be envisaged that the activating device might be produced with even fewer components, for example by producing the cam element 2 so that it incorporates the locking member 5. This invention therefore allows one to provide a device activated by
20 inhaling that is reliable while at the same time being simple and inexpensive.
