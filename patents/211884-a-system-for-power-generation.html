Title of Invention

A SYSTEM FOR POWER GENERATION
Abstract A system for power generation comprises a fractional HP motor (1/6 "'"'- HP) is connected through a set of gear trains to boost the torque by reducing the speed so as to couple with a compatible alternator, say 5 KVA, the fractional HP motor being initially supplied from a battery, inverter combination with a booster protection arrangement, the compatible alternator, being say 5 KVA output, not directly connected to the load but the output booster connected to the alternator supplying, say initial wattage of about 200 watts to the fractional HP motor to render the entire system self-sustaining.

The present invention relates to a system wherein a fractional HP motor (1/6) of 6000 RPM is connected through a set of gear trains to boost the torque and reduce the speed to 1500 RPM so as to couple with a 5 KVA alternator. The output booster connected to the 5 KVA alternator supplies the load. This booster also supplies about 200 watts to the fractional HP motor so that the entire system becomes a self-sustaining one. The battery and the inventor arrangement gets cutoff once the output booster starts supplying the fractional HP motor. This invention has a unique distinction of a small primeover (fractional HP motor) connected to a large machine (5KVA alternator) and the output booster arrangement enables the system to be a self sustaining one as well as avoids a direct impact of the load onto the alternator.
One kind of booster between the inverter and the fractional HP motor is to ensure adequate power supply to the motor and to protect any short circuit in the inverter.
The output booster after 5 KVA has a three fold operations to effect.
1. Firstly to supply the loads connected to the 5 KVA alternator.
2. Thereafter to protect against the load being transferred on to the 5 KVA
alternator and is prime over viz the fractional HP motor. Since the transfer

of such direct load will immediately burn the fractional HP motor and the gear train systems. 3. Further thereafter to take a part of the output of the alternator to supply back to the fractional HP motor so that the entire system is self-sustaining and the motor so far fed from the battery and the inverter system can be cutoff and become independent. This will also safeguard against the continuous usage of the battery.

The Input Booster is provided with a 10 Primary coil connected with the inverted AC power of 230V supply of 200 W, The secondary coil of the Booster is the single coil which boosts up the power in the ratio of 1: 2,5, thereby generates 500W output power. This output boosted power is fed to the gear wheels to generate high torque.
Understanding the relationship between torque, power and engine speed is vital in automotive engineering, concerned as it is with transmitting power from the engine through the drive train to the wheels. Typically power is a function of torque and engine speed. The gearing of the drive train must be chosen appropriately to make the most of the motor's torque characteristics. Steam engines and electric motors tend to produce maximum torque close to zero rpm, with the torque diminishing as rotational speed rises (due to increasing friction and other constraints).
Thereby with the decrease in spped close to zero rpm here say 1.2 rpm, generates high torque, which in turn been utilised to generate power of 7.5hp.
The output booster also has 10 primary coll and a single secondary coil, the primary coil is of 8 Q , Since the primary coil are been earthed by the diode the impedance loss in the coil is negligible and thereby takes the low input current and increases the output current by 8% thereby generates a output power of 5600 W.
