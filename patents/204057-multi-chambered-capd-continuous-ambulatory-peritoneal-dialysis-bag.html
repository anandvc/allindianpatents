Title of Invention

MULTI CHAMBERED CAPD (CONTINUOUS AMBULATORY PERITONEAL DIALYSIS) BAG.
Abstract The invention relates to a multi chambered bag to be used in Continuous Ambulatory Peritoneal Dialysis system made out of two embossed sheets, radio frequency sealed, having three chambers, wherein the chambers are designed in such a way that no liquid is left over once the easy break-away valve is broken.
Full Text FIELD OF THE INVENTION :
The present invention relates to a multiple chambered bag to be used in Continuous Ambulatory Peritoneal Dialysis system, more specifically to a TRI CHOICE chambered bag having chambers A & B are provided with easily breakaway valves having the significant slope from both sides facilitating drainage of the solution from this respective chambers. Atleast three chambers of different volume capacity are provided to store the solution of different strength.
BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION :
The patients afflicted with the loss functions have been treated by various methods since long time. Earlier, the patient had to be tied to the apparatus during the dialysis. With the advent of Continuous Ambulatory Peritoneal Dialysis the patient has more freedom of movement and is independent to handle the dialysis needs at home. Generally, the system of continuous ambulatory peritoneal "dialysis relies upon the delivery of dialysate from a flexible bag or the dialysate bag which the patient rolls up and wears during the time the dialysate is dwelling in the peritoneal cavity. Duringjhe dwell time the patient has complete freedom of movement even though the bag remains connected to the tubing inserted in the patient. After the dwell time is completed the patient allows the peritoneal cavity to drain with the used dialysate once again being collected into the flexible bag which the patient has unrolled. Once the collection is completed the patient disconnects the used bag and the contents. Then a new filled dialysate bag is connected once again and the cycle is repeated. Normally, in one day 4 to 5 such exchanges are required during dialysis process.

The aim of CAPD system is to provide a simple, easy, non-complicated and convenient system which can be used by the patient at home rather than going to the hospital or a doctor. It is also intended that the system allows the patient to make his daily needs at one filling from the delivery system rather than filling each bag separately to be used during the daily period.
The patient may need different formulation depending upon his condition. The
Tri Choice dialysis bag gives the liberty to the patient to have the desired formulation.

The disadvantages of the dialysate bags which have been used for CAPD that no protective container assembly is provided. Also, the drainage of the dialysate is not complete thereby the solution is wasted. Lack of protecture sheet may result into leakage if the pressure increases inside the bag. The existing bags are made out of lay flat films. Due to this there is a lot of tackiness with the transfer bag and separation has to be achieved either by free or by use of chemicals. Therefore, there always existed a need to provide a multichambered dialysate bag which has better strength enabling the compete drainage of the solution. DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF THE INVENTION :
The present invention relates to a Tri-Choice dialysate bag which conveniently serves a CAPD patients needs. The invention is novel, simple and convenient to operate and can be easily handled by the patient without the help of doctor. Fig. 1 shows the unfilled dialysate bag of the prior art. The bag has plurality of compartments each separated by the other by plurality of internal walls.

:7:
The bag is made up of flat film. The compartments are having the horizontal bottom so that thesolution is not completely drained out when they are emptied out. With the result the correct formulation may not be achieved in the main chamber. The valves provided there to connect the chambers to the main chambers are not easily breakable and if they break sometimes the flow of the solution is not smooth. The dialysate bag is not provided with any protecture covering and there is nothing to support at the bottom of the bag. The valve often blocks the passage of the outlet port
Fig. 2 shows the bag of the present invention. The Tri-choice bag as the name suggests give the patient choice to fill three solutions at one time in this bag. The bag has three compartments A, B and C. Each of these compartments is provided with an inlet opening (1A, 1B and 1C) respectively so that the dialysate or any solution can be delivered into each of compartments during filling operation.
A medication port (3) is also provided for sampling / medication to the main compartment, upper compartment (A and B) are provided with outlet openings where two breakaway valves (4A, 4B) are fixed. These valves (4A, 4B) are novel, easy to handle and for unidirectional flow of the solution from the compartments A and B. Both the compartments (A,B) have a novel shape and bottom well is slightly curved providing significant slope to sides so that no solution remains into the compartments A and B once the valves (4A, 4B) are broken and the solution contained therein is completely drained out to compartment C so that a solution of appropriate formulation can be achieved in this compartment (C).

The solution from chamber C finally goes into peritoneal cavity of the patient throught out opening (5) for peritoneal dialysis and hence it will give appropriate dialysis results.
In this embodiment being described the preferred material for the dialysate bag is soft PVC sheet giving complete RF sealing on the sides. The bag is made out of two embosssed sheets of soft PVC in order to minimise tackiness or leaching of plastisizer into the dialysate. The strength of this bag is better than the existing dialysate bags as the thickness of the bag does not vary like in the case of lay flet films where the thickness variation is quite high resulting into leakage.

During use by a CAPD patient filled bag is customarily mounted so that outlet opening 5 faces downwardly for reliance on gravity feed of the dialysate in the bag. CAPD patient break the breakaway valves 4A, 4B one by one and this will make the solution of these compartments (A,B) flow into compartment C. An outlet tubing section is provided at the outlet opening (5) of the bag.
