Title of Invention

A PROCESS FOR PREPARING THE COMPOSITION FOR PURIFYING AND ADDING MEDICINAL PROPERTIES TO THE AIR, IN THE FORM OF AIR - FRESHENER
Abstract A process for making herbal air-freshener for purifying the surrounding air comprising the steps of: (i) Extracting the herbal/plant oils/extracts from the plant parts of the selected plants as herein described with steaming and distillation process as herein described; (ii) Mixing in a planetary mixer the concentrated oils/extracts obtained from the plant/herb parts of step (i) with aloe-vera gel prepared by conventional process in a ratio of 1:9 v/v at room temperature; (iii) Packing the contents of step (iii) in a plastic container with perforated lid covered by aluminum foil or any other peelable cover.

Aloe-vera a medicinal plant, has been the most important component of traditional Indian Medicine from ancient period. Aloe-vera is also known as "Ghrita Kumari" in Sanskrit, "Guarpatha" in Hindi, "Korphad" in Marathi, "Chothukatrali" in Tamil, "Chinna Kalabanda" in Telugu and "Kumari" in Oriya and Bengali. Reffered as 'plant of immortality' in Egyptian documents, its healing benefits are well recognized in the ancient Indian, Chinese, Greek and Roman Civilization.
Aloe-vera, a plant family of Liliaceae, is reported to be originated in the warm, dry climate of Africa. It is scattered across the warm regions of the globe because of its adaptive nature. It is known that Arabians had brought Aloe-vera plant to India. It is found as a wild plant along the coast of southern India. It is grown as an ornamental plant as well as medicinal plant in almost all states of India. Such as Chattisgarh, Gujarat, UttarPradesh, Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana, Tamilnadu, Kerala, Karnataka, Maharashtra , and Andhra Pradesh. Around 240 species of Aloe-vera are known to the scientific world. Out of which only 3 to 4 species have medicinal properties such as the Aloe-vera Barbadenis which is considered as most potent one.
The first detailed discussion of Aloe-vera's medicinal properties was found in an Egyptian document called 'Papyrus ebers'. The products prepared from Aloe-vera, leaves have multiple properties such as emollient, purgative, vulnerary, anti-bacterial, anti¬inflammatory, anti-oxidant, anesthetic, aphrodisiac and cosmetic. 96% of the plant is water and rest is active ingredients like essential oil, amino acids, minerals, Vitamins, enzymes and glycoproteins. Food and Agricultural organization (FAO) has identified Aloe-vera, as 'a new plant resource with the most promising prospects in the world.' Few developed countries like USA are doing serious medical research on the uses of its products. Food and Drug Administration of USA has already approved the developmental study of Aloe-vera, in the treatment of Cancer and Aids.

The juice and supplement of Aloe-vera, is highly useful to digestive tract irritations such as colitis and peptic ulcers. It also facilitates digestion, blood and lymphatic circulation and functioning of kidneys, liver and gall bladder.
Aloe-vera, is a perennial, tropical crop which can be cultivated in drought areas. It prefers to reside on full sun and does not require much watering. The leaves of the Aloe-vera plant grow from the base in the rosette pattern. Each plant usually has 12 to 16 leaves. The plant can be harvested in every 6 to 8 week by removing 3 to 4 leaves per plant.

A-l Preparation of Aloe-vera gel
a) Aloe-vera plant is easily available in India.
b) We shall purchase Aloe-vera leaves from the farmers.
c) In our unit we shall cut and scrape the gel from the leave with a special wooden knife.
d) Then we shall add oil of Tulsi to it, to preserve it naturally.
e) Since Tulsi is a natural preservative, for it is anti-bacterial, anti-fungal, anti¬biotic , anti -septic.
f) Ratio is 99 gms of Aloe-vera with 1 gm of Tulsi oil.
A-2 Preparation of Herbal Oils /extract from Herbs.
e.g. Tulsi oil / extract preparation.
a) For the preparation of Tulsi oil we shall purchase Tulsi leaves from farmers.
b) Tulsi leaves shall be washed in water thoroughly.
c) Cut the leaves in 1/4 size ( for better result).


d) These shall be put on the perforated mesh in the Steam -Distillation units.
e) This shall be steamed at 90° temperature for 4 to 5 hrs.
f) The steam carried oil then goes into the condenser where it cools.
g) Then it goes into the separator tank where the oil is separated from the water.
h) By this process 70% Oil is obtained.
i) Extract of Tulsi is then mixed with Aloe-vera gel, it is used even to preserve
Aloe-vera gel along with properties of Tulsi also mentioned before.
j) The oil of Tulsi has ether which destroys bacteria.
A-3 Speed of the planetary mixer.
a. The speed of the planetary mixer is maintained below 45 rpm. At room
temperature so as to achieve natural aroma.
b. A speed that is higher may destroy the enzymes and other properties present
in the Herbs and Aloe-vera gel, if it becomes warm.
c. And a lower rpm. may not bring out the maximum result in mixing..
Accordingly there is provided a process of making Herbal air freshener comprising the following steps.
i) Extracting the herbs with steaming / distillation process.
ii) Mixing in a planetary mixer the concentrated extract in step (i) with Aloe-vera gel in 1:9 ratio at room temperature.
iii) Packaging in plastic containers with perforated lid covered by aluminum foil or any other peelable cover.
