BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION [0002] Field of the Invention
The present invention relates to imidazothiazole derivatives useful as an intermediate for the production of carbapenem derivatives having potent antimicrobial activity and a broad antimicrobial spectrum, and a process for producing the same. [0003] Background Art
Carbapenem derivatives have potent antimicrobial activity and a broad antimicrobial spectrum and thus have been energetically studied as a highly useful ÃŸ-lactam agent.
WO 02/42312 reports finding that carbapenem derivatives having a 7-(l-carbamoylmethylpyridinium-3-yl)carbonylimidazo[5,l-b]thiazole group at the 2-positipn on the carbapenem ring, that is, compounds of formula (A), have high antimicrobial activity against Gram-positive bacteria and Gram-negative bacteria including MRSA (methicillin resistant Staphylococcus aureus), PRSP (penicillin resistant Streptococcus pneumoniae), Haemophilus influenzae, and p-lactamase producing bacteria, and, at the same time, have high stability against DHP-l(kidney dehydropeptidase-1). Japanese Patent No. 3527003 reports that imidazothiazoles which have been unsubstituted or
substituted at the 3-position of the cephem ring, are useful for the development of potent antimicrobial activity. In the technique disclosed in this patent, however, there is still room for improvement, for example, in procedure process.
