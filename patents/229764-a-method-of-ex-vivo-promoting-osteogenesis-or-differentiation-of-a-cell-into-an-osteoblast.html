A METHOD OF EX-VIVO PROMOTING OSTEOGENESIS OR DIFFERENTIATION OF A CELL INTO AN OSTEOBLAST
Abstract ABSTRACT 2818/CHENP/2005 "A method of ex-vivo promoting osteogenesis or differentiation of a cell into an osteoblast" The present invention relates to a method of ex-vivo promoting osteogenesis or differentiation of a cell into an osteoblast, the method comprising contacting the cell ex vivo with a nucleic acid agent capable of abrogating or diminishing an expression of TAK1 in the cell, thereby regulating the activity of the SMAD protein in the cell to thereby ex vivo promote osteogenesis or differentiation of the cell into an osteoblast.

BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
[002] TGF-P activated kinase (TAKl) was initially identified as a cytosolic component of mitogen activated protein kinase (MAPK) pathways activated by ligands of the TGF-P and BMP family of secreted factors. TAKl is a MAP kinase kinase kinase (MAPKKK, MAP3K) activated by the C3tokines IL-1 and TNF-a, in addition to TGF-p/BMPs, consisting of roughly 600 amino acids, with an N-terminal kinase-domain of roughly 300 amino acids, which is roughly 30 % homologous to catalytic domains of other MAP3Ks (e.g. Raf-1, MEKK-1). TAKl mediates activation of c-jun N-terminal kinases (JNK), p38-MAPK and NF-KB pathways. However, molecular events in TAKl involved signaling cascades, in particular early events in the cascade are as yet not well defined.
[003] SMAD proteins are a family of eight intracellular proteins (SMADl to SMAD8) whose members transduce signals for TGF-p ligands including the bone morphogenetic proteins (BMPs). SMAD proteins can be classified into three types according to their structure and mechanism of action. The receptor-regulated SMADs (R-SMADs) SMAD 1-3, SMAD5 and SMADS, are directly phosphorylated and activated by a TGF-p /BMP type I receptor. Another SMAD type, the common mediator SMAD (Co-SMAD; SMAD4), associates with activated R-SMADs, although hetero-oligomeric activated R-

SMAD complexes without SMAD4 have also been detected. SMAD complexes accumulate in the nucleus and participate in the regulation of target genes. Regulation is predominantly at the level of complex binding to other transcription factors, and transcriptional regulators, however, direct SMAD binding to DNA via SMAD.MH1 domain, or a combination of both mechanisms, may participate in the regulation of target gene expression. The third type of SMADs (I-SMADs, SMAD6 and SMAD7) are termed anti-SMADs or inhibitory SMADs owing to their interference with the activation of, and subsequent complex formation by R-SMADs.

DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF THE INVENTION
[0029] This invention provides novel nucleic acids, vectors, compositions and methods for regulating SMAD expression or activity via TAKl, and therapeutic applications arising from their utilization.
[0030] SMADs are a family of intracellular signaling proteins, which transduce signals thereby mediating a wide range of biological processes, including regulation of cell proliferation, differentiation, recognition, and death, and thus play a major role in developmental processes, tissue recycling, and repair. Effects that result in enhanced or diminished SMAD protein expression, or otherwise limit or enhance SMAD signal transduction will have substantial impact on biological processes.
[0031]TGF-p activated kinase (TAKl) is a MAP3K activated by ligands of the TGF-|3 and BMP family of secreted factors and by the cytokines IL-1 and TNF-a. Like the SMADs, TAKl is involved in signaling cascades, with a definitive role in negative regulation of SMAD biological activity.
[0032] Direct interaction of TAKl with SMADs is demonstrated in Example 2, hereinbelow, with intact TAKl kinase activity being a prerequisite for SMAD binding. The conserved SMAD MH2 domain is the minimal domain required for the TAKl interaction (Example 3).
[0033] TAKl interaction with SMAD via its MH2 domain results in reduction of SMAD activity, in terms of its transactivation potential and transcription following receptor-mediated activation (Example 4). Thus, in its native form, TAKl negatively-regulates SMAD biological activity.

EXAMPLE 3: SMAD MH2 domains mediate TAKl binding.
[00187] SMAD deletion mutants were prepared in order to determine which domains were necessary and sufficient for TAKl binding.
