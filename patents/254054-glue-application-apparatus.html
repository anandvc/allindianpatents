Title of Invention

GLUE APPLICATION APPARATUS
Abstract The invention relates to Glue application apparatus having at least one glue roll (4) which can be driven so as to rotate about its roll axis, and having at least one glue application and distribution element (3) with a glue dispenser connected to a line (16) for feeding the glue in order to produce an application of glue on a circular-cylindrical circumferential face of the glue roll (4), which circumferential face surrounds the roll axis concentrically and is moved past the glue dispenser, and having a glue buffer chamber (13) connected to the glue dispenser on the glue roll (4) for accommodating a buffer volume of glue, and wherein there is formed between the line (16) for feeding the glue and the glue delivery point (8) an overflow (14) for conveying away into an overflow channel (15), preferably into an overflow (15) leading to the glue reservoir (21) that supplies the gluing apparatus, an excess quantity of glue not applied to the glue roll (4).
Full Text The invention relates to a glue application apparatus having at least one glue roll (4) which can
be driven so as to rotate about its roll axis, and having at least one glue application and
distribution element (3) for producing an application of ink on a circular-cylindrical
circumferential face of the glue roll, which circumferential face surrounds the roll axis
concentrically.
The invention relates to a gluing apparatus according to the generic term of the patent claim 1.
Gluing apparatuses for use in labelling machines, e.g. in labelling units of such machines are
known in most divergent designs and consist essentially of a glue application and distribution
element and a glue roll, which can be driven rotating around the roller shaft and thereby moved
by a glue discharge from a glue application and a distribution element, so that on the glue roll
under the effect of a glue scraper a uniform glue application is created, which is then
transferred to the labels to be glued, and indeed directly or indirectly by gluing the label pallets,
with which the labels are removed from a label magazine.
Object of the invention is to document a gluing apparatus which ensures a particularly uniform
glue application independent of the capacity of the labelling machine and/or the labelling unit.
For solving this task, a gluing apparatus is developed according to the patent claim 1, 2 or 3.
A special feature of the invention consists of the fact that the glue discharge from the glue
application and distribution element is a glue chamber, which is formed at the contact surface of
the glue application and distribution element lying close against the glue roll and is closed at
this contact surface by the glue roll. The uniform glue application as desired is ensured by the
glue volume contained in the glue chamber.
In case of another preferred embodiment of the invention, the glue application and distribution
element indicates a glue buffer chamber which stays connected with the glue discharge, and
serves for receiving a certain glue volume. Thanks to this buffer chamber, a uniform glue
application independent of the capacity of the labelling machine or the labelling unit is ensured,
and indeed during pulsating glue supply by means of a glue pump designed as piston pump.
According to a further preferred embodiment of the invention, an overflow is foreseen between
a connection for feeding the glue and the glue application, so that the glue amount supplied
through this connection can be selected somewhat greater than the actual glue amount yielded
on the glue roll. In doing so, a uniform glue application independent of the capacity of the
labelling machine or the labelling unit can be achieved.

Further developments of the invention are the subject of the subordinate claims. The invention
is described in detail in the following using the figures at the embodiment. It shows:

an otherwise (not shown) labelling unit of a labelling machine;

Fig. 1 shows a gluing apparatus of a labelling unit of a machine (not shown) for labelling bottles
or such containers.
The gluing apparatus consists of an essentially strip-like glue application and distribution
element 3 in a known manner and of the glue roll 4 driven rotating around a vertical roller axis,
at which the labels 2 that are to be provided with label glue at the rear and are contained at the
periphery of a label transport cylinder 5 (vacuum cylinder) or the label pallets to be glued are
moved, as this is known to an expert.
The glue application and distribution element 3 have a strip-like housing 3.1 oriented with its
longitudinal extension parallel to the shaft of the glue roll 1, in which a heating device 6 is
foreseen and which forms a contact surface 7 at a longitudinal side, against which the glue roll
4 fits tight or nearly tight with a part of its cylindrical periphery area in each case. The contact
surface 7 is partly curved in accordance with the cylindrical periphery area of the glue roll 4. At
the contact surface 7, the housing 3.1 is provided with an elongated recess, which is oriented
with its shaft likewise parallel or nearly parallel to the roller shaft of the glue roll 4 and a glue
chamber 8, and indeed with a closed base 9 and a closed surface area 10. At the contact
surface 7 the glue chamber 8 is plugged or covered by the glue roll 4.
Inside the housing 3.1 a further glue channel 11 is designed, which is stretched in the direction
of longitudinal axis of the strip-like housing 3.1, plugged at the bottom of the housing 3.1 and
runs into the glue chamber 8 through a large number of openings or bores 12.
The upper end of the glue channel 11 runs into a glue buffer chamber 13, and indeed, at the
base of this chamber, which is provided with an overflow 14 at their upper range, which likewise
runs into the glue overflow or flume 15 toward the longitudinal axis of the strip-like housing 3.1.
At the top of the housing 3.1, a further connection 16 is foreseen, which serves for supplying
the glue and opens on top into the glue buffer chamber 13. As shown in Fig. 3, the glue
application and distribution element 3 or the housing 3,1 are held on a fixture 17, which is
envisaged at the labelling unit (not shown). The holder 17 is designed in such a way that the
glue application and distribution element 3 can be tilted for cleaning and maintenance purpose,

so that in the tilted condition the glue roll 4 as well as the glue application and distribution
element 3 are freely accessible. Further, holder 17 is designed in such a way that the glue
application and distribution element 3 match the curved contact surface 7 precisely at the glue
roll 4, and indeed, compressed by at least one pressure spring 18.
Laterally from the contact surface 7, a further filler-like stripper 19 is envisaged at the housing
3.1. This is, for example, formed by a spring plate pre-stressed in operation and serves for
removing from glue remainders or label remainders on the glue roll before the new glue
application. The stripper 19 is foreseen for this purpose in the direction of rotation A of the glue
roll 4 before the contact surface 7 and the glue chamber 8.
At the housing 3.1 laterally from the contact surface 7, but in the direction of rotation A of the
glue roll 4, a filler-like glue scraper 20 is foreseen on the glue chamber, which serves for
distribution and wiping off the glue applied on the glue roll and with which, the thickness of the
glue application can be adjusted on the periphery area of the glue roll 4, and indeed, by setting
the column formed between the glue scraper 20 and the glue roll 4 by suitable execution of the
glue application and distribution element 3. The glue scraper 20 is likewise formed by a spring
plate.
During the operation of the gluing apparatus 1, glue is constantly conveyed to connection 16
by means of a (not shown) glue pump from a glue tub 21, from which the glue chamber 8, the
openings 12, the glue channel 11 and also the glue buffer chamber 13 are filled completely with
glue. Surplus glue, no longer withdrawn from the glue buffer chamber 13, reaches the glue
overflow channel 15 via the overflow 14 and from there back to the glue tub 21. In order to
permit a nonporous supply of glue to the glue chamber 8 and the glue buffer chamber 13 as
well as the overflow of the surplus glue into the overflow canal 15, an exhaust 22 is foreseen at
the top of the glue buffer chamber 13, and indeed, in case of the embodiment as shown directly
via overflow 14.
During operation, the glue chamber 8 is constantly filled with glue above its height, so that the
glue roll 4 moving at the open side of the glue chamber 8 above a width, which corresponds to
the longitudinal dimension of the glue chamber 8, is provided with a glue application. The glue
amount supplied to connection 16 is slightly higher than the glue amount transferred to the glue
roll 4, so that a part of the supplied glue flows back via the overflow 14 and the overflow canal
15 into the glue tub 21 and is thus ensured that a sufficient amount of glue is always present in
the glue application and distribution element 3.
Thanks to the volume of the glue chamber 8, especially by the buffer container of the glue
buffer chamber 13, a uniform glue application on the glue roll 4, independent of the capacity
and capacity changes of the labelling machine (container labelled per unit of time), is
guaranteed despite pulsating supply of the glue at the connection 16 by means of a glue pump
designed as piston pump.

Figs. 3-5 show the glue application and distribution element 3 more in the detail. As is evident
from these figures, housing 3.1 is made of a block of a suitable material, e.g. metal, into which
a recess forming the glue chamber 8, openings or bores 12, glue canal 11, glue buffer chamber
13, overflow 14 and overflow canal 15 are inserted by mechanical processing. The glue buffer
chamber 13 formed by a recess at the upper face of the block 23 is plugged by a cover 24, at
which the connection 16 and the vent hole 22 are envisaged. The overflow 14 is thus a recess
forming a lateral expansion the glue buffer chamber 13, into which (expansion) the overflow
canal 15 runs.
The invention was described with an embodiment. It is understood that numerous amendments
as well as modifications are possible, without having to depart from the underlying concept of
the invention.

We Claim:
1. Glue application apparatus having at least one glue roll (4) which can be driven
so as to rotate about its roll axis, and having at least one glue application and
distribution element (3) with a glue dispenser connected to a line (16) for feeding the
glue in order to produce an application of glue on a circular-cylindrical circumferential
face of the glue roll (4), which circumferential face surrounds the roll axis
concentrically and is moved past the glue dispenser, and having a glue buffer chamber
(13) connected to the glue dispenser on the glue roll (4) for accommodating a buffer
volume of glue, and wherein there is formed between the line (16) for feeding the glue
and the glue delivery point (8) an overflow (14) for conveying away into an overflow
channel (15), preferably into an overflow (15) leading to the glue reservoir (21) that
supplies the gluing apparatus, an excess quantity of glue not applied to the glue roll
(4).
2. Glue application apparatus as claimed in claim 1, wherein the glue dispenser is
formed by a glue chamber (8) which the glue application and distribution element (3)
comprises in the region of a bearing face (7) that bears against the circumferential
face of the glue roll (4), and which is closed on its side facing towards the glue roll (4)
by the circumferential face of the glue roll (4).
3. Glue application apparatus as claimed in any one of claims 1 or 2, wherein a
ventilation opening or a ventilation channel (22) is provided on the glue buffer chamber
(13) and/or on the overflow (14).
4. Glue application apparatus as claimed in any one of the preceding claims,
wherein the glue buffer chamber (13) is provided in the connection between the line
(16) for feeding the glue and the glue dispenser (6) on the glue roll (4).

5. Glue application apparatus as claimed in any one of the preceding
claims, wherein the overflow (14) is formed on the buffer chamber (13).
6. Glue application apparatus as claimed in any one of the preceding
claims, wherein the glue buffer chamber (13) is formed by a recess in a
housing (3.1) or in a block (23) forming this housing, and in that the
overflow (14) is formed by a widening of the recess which forms the
glue buffer chamber (13), at the upper edge region of this recess.
7. Glue application apparatus as claimed in any one of the preceding
claims, wherein the line (16) for feeding the glue opens into the glue
buffer chamber (13), preferably at the top of this chamber.
8. Glue application apparatus as claimed in any one of the preceding
claims, wherein the glue chamber (8) is configured as an elongate
chamber and is oriented with its longitudinal dimension parallel or
approximately parallel to the roll axis of the glue roll (4).
9. Glue application apparatus as claimed in any one of the preceding
claims, wherein a glue channel (11) which connects the glue chamber
(8) to the glue buffer chamber (13).
10. Glue application apparatus as claimed in claim 9, wherein the glue
channel (11) opens into the bottom of the glue buffer chamber (13).

11. Glue application apparatus as claimed in any one of the
preceding claims, wherein the glue chamber (8) is connected to the
glue channel (11) via a plurality of openings or bores (12).
12. Glue application apparatus as claimed in any one of the
preceding claims, characterised by a heating means (6) in the glue
application and distribution element (3).


ABSTRACT

TITLE: Glue application apparatus
The invention relates to Glue application apparatus having at least
one glue roll (4) which can be driven so as to rotate about its roll axis,
and having at least one glue application and distribution element (3)
with a glue dispenser connected to a line (16) for feeding the glue in
order to produce an application of glue on a circular-cylindrical
circumferential face of the glue roll (4), which circumferential face
surrounds the roll axis concentrically and is moved past the glue
dispenser, and having a glue buffer chamber (13) connected to the glue
dispenser on the glue roll (4) for accommodating a buffer volume of
glue, and wherein there is formed between the line (16) for feeding
the glue and the glue delivery point (8) an overflow (14) for conveying
away into an overflow channel (15), preferably into an overflow (15)
leading to the glue reservoir (21) that supplies the gluing apparatus,
an excess quantity of glue not applied to the glue roll (4).
