Title of Invention

"STRIP WINDING METHOD"
Abstract NA
Full Text WE CLAIM
1. A process for winding a continuously supplied band (5) onto a bobbin (2), with the bobbin (2) being rotated and the band (5) being reciprocated along the entire length of the bobbin (2) at a winding angle (a) by means of a cross-winding device (4), wherein each time the bobbin diameter has increased by a particular value, the winding ratio, i.e. the ratio between the number of bobbin rotations and the reciprocating motion (to-and-fro stroke) of the cross winding device, is changed in steps, characterized in that the bobbin (2) is driven by a separate motor (Ml) and the cross-winding device (4) is also driven by a separate motor (M2) and the change in the winding ratio is performed electronically by step wisely changing the ratio of the speeds of the two motors and that, with the stepwise change, the winding ratio is changed in essentially integral steps so that, with each change, the post-decimal point part of the winding ratio will change by 0.1 at the most, preferably 0.03 at the most, more preferably 0.01 at the most.
2. A winding process as claimed in claim 1, wherein with each change in the winding ratio, the post-decimal point part of said ratio is changed to such a degree that a constant partial overlap with an underlying band track will result, wherein an axial shift d to the extent of the desired constant partial overlap is selected and the winding ratio is calculated from the formula such as herein described.
3. A winding process as claimed in claims 1 or 2, wherein the post-decimal point part of the winding ratio is at least two-digit and preferably is close to 0 or 0.50 or 0.33 or 0.25.
4. A winding process as claimed in any of claims 1 to 3, wherein the winding ratio is changed such that a forward or backward-moving band winding is created.
5. A winding process as claimed in any of claims 1 to 4, wherein the winding ratio is changed such that the resulting winding angle (a) will stay within a predetermined

band width.
6. A winding process as claimed in claim 1, wherein the motors (Ml, M2) are rotary-current drives with frequency converters or direct-current drives.
7. A winding process as claimed in any of the preceding claims wherein, the instantaneous bobbin diameter is calculated from a variance comparison of the linear band speed and the number of bobbin rotations.
8. A winding process as claimed in claim 2, wherein depending on the winding angle (a), the shift d is selected such that an overlap of bands of appx. V2 a bandlet width b emerges.
9. A winding process, substantially as herein described with reference to the accompanying drawings.
