Air Bleeding Structure for Water-cooled Internal Combustion Engine [Technical Field] [0001]
The present invention relates generally to an air bleeding structure for a water-cooled internal combustion engine. More specifically, the invention relates to a structure for bleeding air in the pump chamber of a water pump connected to a camshaft provided in a cylinder head as well as for bleeding air in a thermostat valve housing. [Background Art] [0002]
A known example of an air bleeding structure for a water-cooled internal combustion engine is a structure in which a draft is provided at the interior upper portion of a case for housing a thermostat so as to incline upward toward an end face of the case and is used as an air bleeding slope and means is provided for bleeding air from this air bleeding slope to an injection hole side.(Refer to, e.g., Patent Document 1.) [Problem to be Solved by the Invention]
[0004]
Meanwhile, in a vehicle having a radiator mounted on the front part of the vehicle body and an engine mounted on the rear part, the engine may be configured such that a housing of a water pump provided outside a cylinder head is provided integrally with a thermostat housing. In this case, respective cooling water passages of the radiator and the engine are connected through a pipe running below an occupant space provided therebetween. When water is poured into the radiator, therefore, it is necessary to bleed air from both the radiator side and the cooling water passage side of the engine. [0005]
If the invention described in patent document 1 is applied to the vehicle mentioned above, however, the air in the housing for the thermostat can be bled, whereas the air in the pump chamber of the water pump cannot be bled. Therefore, a passage, a joint and other components should be additionally provided for bleeding the air in

the housing for the water pump. This makes it possible to complicate the internal structure of the engine and increase manufacturing cost. If the engine is started up with air remaining in the cooling water passage of the engine, cavitation will occur within the pump chamber, or the air in a water jacket will degrade the cooling performance of the engine.
[0006]
In view of the forgoing, the present invention has been made and it is an object of the invention to provide an air bleeding structure for a water-cooled internal combustion engine which can collectively bleed the air in the pump chamber of a water pump and that in a housing for a thermostat valve, with simple structure, and which can reduce manufacturing costs. [Means for Solving the Problem]
[0007]
To achieve the object, the invention recited in claim 1 is characterized in that, in an air bleeding structure for a water-cooled internal combustion engine in which a water pump is attached integrally to one end of a camshaft provided in a cylinder head and a thermostat valve housing with a bypass passage is joined integrally to a housing for the water pump, the air





4
bleeding structure is provided with an air bleeding hole which intersects the bypass passage and allows the bypass passage to communicate with a pump chamber of the water pump.
[0008]
To achieve the object, the invention recited in claim 2 is characterized in that, in addition to the invention recited in claim 1, the air bleeding hole includes a first air bleeding hole communicating with the bypass passage and a second air bleeding hole allowing the first air bleeding hole to communicate with the pump chamber, and the second air bleeding hole has a diameter smaller than that of the first air bleeding hole.
[0009]
The invention recited in claim 3 is characterized in that, in addition to claim 1 or 2, the first air bleeding hole is formed by casting and the second air bleeding hole is formed by machining. [Effect of the Invention]
[0010]
With the air bleeding structure for a water-cooled internal combustion engine recited in claim 1, the air bleeding hole is provided which intersects the bypass passage and causes the bypass passage to communicate with







5
the pump chamber of the water pump. Therefore, air in the pump chamber of the water pump and in the housing for the thermostat valve can be bled in a collective manner. Thus, it is not necessary to additionally provide a passage, a joint and the like used to bleed air, for the housing for the water pump. This allows the internal combustion engine to have a simple configuration, and reduces the number of man-hour for machining and the like and the number of components, thereby providing reduced manufacturing costs.
[0011]
With the air bleeding structure for the water-cooled internal combustion engine recited in claim 2, the air bleeding hole includes the first air bleeding hole communicating with the bypass passage and the second air bleeding hole allowing the first air bleeding hole to communicate with the pump chamber. In addition, the second air bleeding hole has a diameter smaller than that of the first air bleeding hole. It is therefore possible to prevent the cooling water flowing in the bypass passage from flowing in the pump chamber of the water pump through the air bleeding hole. Thus, it is possible to prevent the air bled from the cooling water can be prevented from returning in the pump chamber of the water
