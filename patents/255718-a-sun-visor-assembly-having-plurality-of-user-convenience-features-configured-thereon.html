Title:
Sun visor with notepad
Technical Field:
The present innovation relates to the sun visor assembly for use in automobile vehicle with multiple applications. More particularly, the present invention relates to a sun visor assembly with note pad, penholder and pocket mounted over its backside.
Background and Prior Art:
An important part of conducting business is being equipped with writing material such as pen and notepad. Writing material can be stored in glove box or on the dashboard of a vehicle, but using these storage locations can create problems. Note pad and pen left loosely on the dashboard can move around as the car shifts, making it difficult to reach when the need arises. Loose items lying on dashboard of the vehicle also present a cluttered appearance that most drivers find unattractive. Some drivers tuck pens and other items under the sun visors of their vehicles for easy retrieval. Simply tucking items under the sun visor fixes their locations but allows the items to fall out when the driver needs to lower the visor to protect the driver's eyes from sunlight.
A sun visor is a component of an automobile vehicle located just above the windscreen on the interior and can be lowered to block the sun's rays. It can also be turned to the side to stop lateral sun exposure. Sun visors with LCD TV monitor, rotary video camera, CD holder, pen holder, card holder,


pockets, mirror, office organizer and see through type are well known in prior art.
Visor CD holder has 9 slot CD holder attachments to sun visor with elastic / Velcro straps and silver H logo on it. See through type auto sun visor aims for safer and more comfortable driving by reducing glare (reflected light), brightness of sun light and UV Rays all without restricting a driver's view.
American patent No. US5301856 (April 12, 1994) describes an sun visor with office organizer comprising writing instruments, a calculator, note pad, a calendar, and so on is mounted on the conventional sun visor of a motor vehicle. The organizer includes a hinged panel permitting it to be swiveled to an open position shielding the side window of the vehicle. In an alternatively preferred embodiment, a series of supplemental hinged panels are provided to increase the capacity of the organizer. The hinged panels preferably are transparent and suitably optically coated to provide a safe field of vision when they are unfolded and to afford protection from the harmful effects of sunlight.
American patent application No. US2007/0029360A1 claims sun visor pen holder, which secures writing pens to the visor of a vehicle. The pen holder includes a main strap of an elastic material. The main strap forms a loop that stretches over the sun visor to secure the pen holder to the sun visor. A common lower loop adapted for retaining the point end of a plurality of pens extends transversely across the main strap. A plurality of upper loops extends across the main strap, which is parallel to the lower loop, each pen loop forming a channel for holding the upper end of a single pen. A pen is


inserted into the channel of an upper loop and slid under the lower loop to securing the pen to the holder at both the upper and lower ends of the pen.
European patent No. EP0230778 and American patent No. US 4711483 (Dec. 08, 1987) discloses an improved sun visor assembly particularly adapted for motor vehicle applications. The sun visor assembly includes a mirror which is movable between a stowed position where it is enclosed by the visor, to a withdrawn position where it remains attached to the visor and can be used by the individual seated in the associated seat. The mirror is further completely removable from the visor in accordance with this invention so that it can be used as a hand-held mirror and can be used by other motor vehicle occupants. The visor is provided with a detent mechanism which retains the mirror in the desired stowed or withdrawn position. The entire visor assembly is configured to be aesthetically pleasing and relatively inexpensive to produce in terms of component and production costs.
American patent application No. US2007/0018476A1 and European patent application No. EP04/00382 describes a sun visor for motor vehicles comprising a hollow visor body. The sun visor has a slot like receiving opening and a receiving area arranged in the cavity of the visor body, this receiving area forming a receiving element for holding a flat object such as parking tickets or fuel cards.
Other than these concepts, in the public domain, no patents relating to the present innovation was found.


Deficiencies of Prior Art:
1) None of the prior art sun visors or prior art mentioned above discloses the reusable note pad on the back side of the sun visors.
2) None of the prior art discloses the mounting of note pad, penholder and pocket collectively on the backside of the sun visor.
Summary and Object of the Present Invention:
Present invention "Sun visor with notepad" having a reusable note pad sheet (5) attached on the back side of the sun visor and it faces the driver / co-driver in lowered down position of sun visor surface as shown in figure 1. At least two pen clips (6) are fixed on the surface of sun visor (4) and below the note pad (5) to hold the writing pen (7). Writing pen (sketch pen) can be kept in the clips as shown in the figure. Adjacent to the note pad, a pocket (8) is provided which can be used to put in visiting cards, tour vouchers, fuel cards and likewise.
Object of the present invention is to provide writing facility to the driver without leaving his position for emergency notes / messages while the vehicle is in motion or in stand position.
Further more object of the invention is to utilize backspace of the sun visor effectively and keep the roof / front portion of driver workplace clean.
Further more object of the invention is to facilitate the holding of important travel vouchers.


Advantages of the Present Invention:
1) Easy to use for driver while driving the vehicle
2) Provides aesthetically pleasing look to the sun visor.
3) Protects driver from direct sun light.
4) Facilitate for tracking items on a to-do list.
5) Versatile design of sun visor with multiple uses.
6) Help in plotting driving routes.
7) Presents a neat/orderly appearance to the dashboard of a vehicle.
8) Increase in sale of the vehicles due to the facilities provided on sun visor.

Description of present invention:
In the vehicle normally the pen and writing pad is not available to write message. While driving or in stand position if one (driver) talks on mobile and want to write some mobile number or message immediately, then people use the roof or what ever he get in front and makes the space dirty. The aim of the present invention is to provide the writing facility on sun visor so that any users (driver) want to write mobile number, emergency note, key message, day schedule etc. he can easily write on the note pad mounted on the sun visor while driving the vehicle.


The present invention "Sun visor with notepad" comprising of sun visor assembly (1), hinge (2), hinge rod (3), sun visor surface (4), reusable note pad sheet (5), at least two pen clips (6), writing pen (7), a pocket (8).
According to present invention reusable note pad sheet (5), at least two pen clips (6), writing pen (7), a pocket (8) are collectively mounted on the backside of the sun visor.
The sun visor assembly (1) according to present invention is attached to the vehicle body through hinge (2). The sun visor surface (4) is mounted on the hinge rod (3) and freely rotates around it. The basic function of sun visor surface (4) is to obstruct the path of direct sun light on driver or co-driver seating inside, from the front and side direction. A reusable note pad sheet (5) is attached on the backside of the sun visor and it faces the driver / co-driver in lowered down position of sun visor surface as shown in figure 1. At least two pen clips (6) are fixed on the surface of sun visor (4) and below the note pad (5) to hold the writing pen (7). Writing pen (sketch pen) can be kept in the clips as shown in the figure. Adjacent to the note pad, a pocket (8) is provided which can be used to put in visiting cards, tour vouchers, fuel cards and likewise. Thus, the whole assembly makes a fixed location for writing material and it can be easily retrieved during driving when need arises.
Another embodiment being fabricated in optically transparent material having tinted optical coating applied thereto in a suitable manner to filter out harmful components of sunlight.


This is a very new concept to be brought into practice, which is first of its kind as compared to the existing vehicles until now. The writing pad on the sun visor enables the driver to write immediate messages, notes, phone numbers, travel routes, priority tasks and also can be rubbed it off after its use. Thus, the same note pad can be used again to write the next day's messages/notes.
This shows that apart from its sunlight protection purpose, the sun visor also be used for writing, holding purpose, which proves its versatility. Thus the customer will be attracted towards this new facility been provided and hence will be motivated to buy the vehicle, which in turn will increase the sales of the vehicle.
