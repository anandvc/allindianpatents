Title of Invention

A CLUTCH RELEASE BEARING
Abstract A clutch release bearing comprises a housing enclosing a carbon ring through which the drive shaft of the vehicle is enabled to pass, and is characterized in that the housing is made up of two split parts detachably attachable to each other by fastening means. The carbon ring is also made up of two corresponding split parts respectively fixed to the inner periphery of the split parts of the housing. Thus whenever the split parts of the housing are attached to each other, the split parts of the carbon ring are seated against each other to form a continuous piece. The dimensions of the split parts of the housing are such that the said parts are easily slipped off the drive shaft and slipped on over the drive shaft.
Full Text

This invention relates to a clutch release bearing.
The known clutch release bearing (CRB), also called a clutch carbon assembly, consists of a metal housing enclosing a carbon ring through which the drive shaft of the vehicle, such as, a tractor, passes. Both the housing and the ring are one piece units so much so that while replacing a worn out CRB, the operation involves a time consuming and tedious procedure which finally results in sliding the worn out CRB off the drive shaft and replacing it with a new CRB.
Such operation, apart from being time consuming and tedious, requires the use of sophisticated tools which are not available with all mechanics. Besides, the vehicle, usually a tractor in which the CRB is used, will have to be towed to a garage, often far from the place where it is put to work.
This invention therefore proposes a CRB which eliminates the foregoing drawbacks. Removal and replacement of the proposed CRB now means nothing

more than a job which can be accomplished with a spanner and screwdriver taking about 15 minutes or so, in contrast to the time taken for removal and replacement of the conventional CRB which is anything up to and over 45 minutes in a fully equipped workshop.
The replacement operation involving the proposed CRB can be carried out even by a semi-skilled person at the site where the vehicle is situated.
Various other features of this invention will be apparent from the following description.
The clutch release bearing, according to this invention, comprises a housing enclosing a carbon ring through which the drive shaft of the vehicle is enabled to pass, characterised in that the housing is made up of two split parts detachably attachable to each other by fastening means; and the carbon ring is also made up of two corresponding split parts respectively fixed to the inner periphery of the split parts of the housing, whereby whenever the split parts of the housing are attached to each other, the split

parts of the carbon ring are seated against each other to form a continuous piece, the dimensions of the split parts of the housing, however, being such that the said parts are easily slipped off the drive shaft or slipped on over the drive shaft.
