Title of Invention

A NOVEL ROLLER SUPPORTING REFRACTORY BLOCKS
Abstract A novel roller supporting refractory block as shown in Fig.2 has cylindrical elliptical shape holes wherein the roller supporting refractory block required expansion gap at both ends of supporting block. The vertical length of these cylindrical holes is grater then the horizontal length of these holes. The rollers are rotating into this cylindrical holes (6, 7, 8) due to their up and down oscillation motion the rollers are not touch the internal surface of the blocks, which increase the life of the rotating rollers.

The following specification particularly describes the nature of this invention and the manner in which it is to be performed.

1

The present invention relates to provide better supports to the roller used in ceramic industries and more particularly used to push the raw material into the next stage of operation.
In particular ceramic industries rollers are used to push the raw material in step by step in the next stage. The rollers are not rolling in the exact axial direction. The rollers are rotating with little up and down oscillation motion so, the rollers requires some supports. In the conventional roller supporting blocks the blocks have cylindrical holes. Due to this up and down oscillation the rollers are vulnerable to touch the supporting blocks which cause correction of rollers are vulnerable to touch the supporting blocks which cause corrosion of rolls at a faster rate causing shut down of kilns. Also due to very high temperature inside the kilns which is required for firing the ceramic files, expands blocks esp. longitudinally which causes extra thermo mechanical stress on the supporting blocks ceasing lesser service life of supporting blocks. These limitations are overcome by the present invention of the A Novel Roller Supporting Refractory Blocks.
It has unique design i.e. keeping more space to ensure less probability of touching the rollers to the supporting blocks which improves service life these rollers and also provides required expansion gap at both ends of supporting blocks which takes care of expansion of blocks due to heat so, extra inherent thermo mechanical stress within the supporting blocks are

In Fig. 2 front view of the novel roller supporting refractory blocks the block(5) have cylindrical holes (6,7,8) in the elliptical shape where the vertical length is grater then horizontal length so when the rollers are rotating into these supporting blocks provide more space for rolling with up and down rotating motion.
