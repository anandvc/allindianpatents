Full Text This invention relates to a plucking head assembly for a fibre plucking machine.
In textile processing, fibre plucking machine are used to pluck fibre from the stock of fibre being processed. Plucking head arrangement conventionally mounted on a twin table consists of one or more plucking rollers, having a plurality of plucking teeth disposed on the outer surface thereof. Plucking teeth are essential elements in the machine as when the plucking head travels on and above the stock of fibres to be processed, said plucking rollers pick up the fibers with the help of the plucking teeth provided thereon.
In the conventional machines, plucking teeth form an integral part of the roller and are fixed permanently thereon by welding or such other means. This arrangement is found disadvantageous as failure of a single tooth in the assembly necessitates replacement of the entire roller assembly. In order to avoid this disadvantage, rollers with multiple segments were constructed where each roller is divided into different segments and each segment is provided with fixed teeth thereon. These segments constitute the entire roller assembly. Though this avoids replacement of the entire roller in case of damage or failure of a single tooth as replacement of the damaged segment alone is sufficient. Replacement of segments is also expensive and the down time of the machinery during such replacement is considerably high resulting in production loss. Disassembly of the damaged roller involves removal of contact rollers and grills which are heavy components leading to high labour.

The objective of this invention is to overcome the above discussed problems, and to minimise labour and downtime of the machinery thereby optimising production and minimising cost. This invention contemplates detachable strips provided with plurality of teeth on the periphery of plucking rollers. Strips with damaged teeth may easily be detached from the surface by the toller and can be replaced with a suitable strip in good condition with minimum downtime of the machinery. Removal of the entire roller or a roller segment is thereby avoided. Disassembly of contact rollers and grills involving time and effort is avoided thereby. This invention is particularly but not exclusively suited for textile machinery having a detachable suction hood which provides easy access to the machinery housed within the plucking machine. The strips may be of any desired length such that more than one strip may be fixed to form a single row of teeth. Strips may be fastened to the places with the help of threaded insertion.
This invention relates to a plucking head assembly for a fibre plucking machine which comprises atleast one plucking roller provided with at least one plate circumferential thereto, and atleast one detachable strip mountable on said plate each of said detachable step having a row of teeth disposed thereon.
