Title of Invention

AN IMPROVED GULLY TRAP
Abstract The gully trap comprises a main body having a base element intended to accommodate a cover body; said main body is provided with a central disposal hole which acts as a outlet of the gully trap; said main body is further provided with the inward cavity for maintaining a permanent pool of water when the gully trap is in use, the depth of said cavity in the main body being adjusted to accommodate said cover body; the cover body is provided with a plurality of holes to predetermined size alongwith its periphery for the inlet of water into the gully trap.
Full Text This invention relates to an improved gully trap, namely a gutter, particularly for waste water pipe.
This invention more particularly refers a gully or gully trap for waste water closet, used in homes, hospitals and other public places, such as the toilets, urinals, even the rain water escapes along city roads. The gully trap of the present invention has wide application in the industrial plant, particularly chemical plants for the safe disposal of the waste liquids likely to give off harmful gases, fumes and vapours.
In normal drains polluted waste water are drained out through waste water pipe or drain pipe, which normally is covered with grills. The size of the openings in said grills may vary depending on the nature of the use for the prevention of passage of the waste solid particles.
In the conventional gully trap, the drainage system is covered with grating or mesh thereby preventing the passage of solid particles, larger than size of the mesh holes. This is done so that the entry of the solid and semisolid particles may not cause blockage or choking of the drainage systems. However, such grating or mesh having holes of different sizes, depending upon the nature of its use, will not prevent bad smell from the drain, from spreading all over, and drainage system further would not prevent germs micro-organism insects such as cockroaches, flies,

The said main body is having a central opening for waste water disposal and a circular indented portion around the central opening, which accommodates the circular protrution of the second body, may be called the cover. The cover having perforation or holes along its periphery, which allows the water into the drain. The safety gully trap is described in details with references to the accompanying drawings.
The material for the construction may be metal, fibre, plastics, ceramics or whatever found suitable.

When this assembly is installed over the drain pipe of the drainage system, a small amount of water will fill the cavity (14) of the main body forming a permanent pool of water, the depth of the permanent pool will be determined constant by the wall (12) of the central hole (15) of the body so that any additional water through the water inlet hole (39) will

cause the water level of the permanent pool to rise and finally over flow over the wall (12) of the central disposal hole, which is drained off.
With the cover body over the main body and the permanent pool full, the tip of the circular wall (18) of the cover body will dip below the water surface of the permanent pool. In so doing the permanent pool of water will be divided into an inner circle of water and an outer circle of water. So all contact of the surfaces of the inner circle of water and the outer circle of water will be broken. However, the bottom of both the surfaces (inner and outer) is still common . Any addition of water into the gully trap through the cover body water inlet hole (39) will cause both the water surfaces to rise and finally drain off by over f1owing over the wall (12) of the central hole (15) for disposal, the same amount that has been added. So a free flow of water is maintained into the disposal hole.
In operating condition the inner surface of water and the outer Surface of water are separated from each other by the circular wall (18) of cover body. The inner circle of water, central disposal hole, and the space above it are subject to the same foul environmental condition that prevails within the drainage system.
