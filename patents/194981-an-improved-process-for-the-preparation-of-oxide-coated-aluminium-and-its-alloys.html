Title of Invention

"AN IMPROVED PROCESS FOR THE PREPARATION OF OXIDE COATED ALUMINIUM AND ITS ALLOYS"
Abstract An improved process for the preparation of oxide coated aluminium or its alloys by polishing, degreasing and cleaning the aluminium or aluminium alloy to be coated by conventional method, desmutting in nitric acid and followed by washing, subjecting it to anodic coating using sulphamic acid in the concentration range of 10 to 15% w/v as electrolyte using graphite as cathode at 30° to 40°C using pulse current, recovering the oxide coated aluminium/aluminium ally after washing and drying the substrate.
Full Text aggressive electrolyte and can form oxide coating on
aluminium and its alloys at ambient temperature of 30° to 40°C
with sufficiently thick hard compact film using pulse current of
a programmable high power pulse source.
The main object of the present invention is to provide an improved process for the preparation of oxide coated aluminium or its alloys.
Another object of the present invention is to develop a process for anodic coating of aluminium and its alloys with sulphamic acid as an electrolyte using pulsed current. A PC based power source delivers pulse current with all essential pulse parameters such as on/off time ranging from 1 sec. to 100 sees, on/off current amplitude ranging from 0 to 10 amps, experimental duration from 1 minutes to 60 minutes.
The new result achieved from the present invention is a thick compact hard anodic coating on aluminium and its alloys especially on difficult to anodising alloys containing more than 2% up to 5% copper.
Accordingly, the present invention provides an improved process for the preparation of oxide coated aluminium and its alloys which comprises polishing, degreasing and cleaning the aluminium or aluminium alloy to be coated by conventional method, desmutting in nitric acid and followed by washing, subjecting it to anodic coating using sulphamic acid in the concentration range of 10 to 15% w/v as electrolyte using graphite as cathode at 3 0 to 40 C using pulse current such as herein described for 40 to 60 minutes, recovering the oxide.
