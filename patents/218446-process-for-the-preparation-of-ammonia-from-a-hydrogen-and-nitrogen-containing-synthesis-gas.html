Title of Invention

PROCESS FOR THE PREPARATION OF AMMONIA FROM A HYDROGEN AND NITROGEN CONTAINING SYNTHESIS GAS
Abstract The present invention relates to a process for the preparation of ammonia from a hydroqen and nitrogen-containing synthesis gas comprising steps of ;a) admixing to the synthesis gas a first and a second recycle gas stream from ammonia separation steps (t) and (g) ; (b) pressurizing the admixed gas from step (a) (c) dividing the gas from step (b) into a first make up gas stream and a second make up gas stream; (d) introducing the first make up gas stream into a first ammonia reactor and withdrawing a first process stream being rich in ammonia; (e) introducing the second ammonia make up gas stream into a second ammonia reactor and withdrawing a second process stream being rich in ammonia; (t) separating ammonia from the first process stream and recycling the remainder of the process stream being the first recycle gas stream to step (a); (g) separating ammonia from the second process stream and recycling the remainder of the process stream being the second recycle gas stream to step (a); (h) combining separated ammonia from step (t) and step (g) and forming an ammonia product stream. (i) withdrawing the ammonia product stream.
Full Text Field of the Invention
The present invention relates to certain improvements in the preparation of ammonia from a hydrogen and nitrogen containing ammonia synthesis gas.
More particular, this invention concerns synthesis of ammonia at high production capacity in at least two ammonia synthesis reactors arranged in parallel synthesis loops with one combined compression step of synthesis recycle gas
Background of the Invention
Synthesis of ammonia is conventionally performed by passing a process stream of synthesis gas containing nitrogen and hydrogen in essentially stoichiometric amounts to the formation of ammonia through a catalyst arranged in one or more beds in a reactor.
Thermodynamically, the reaction of hydrogen and nitrogen to ammonia requires the reaction to be performed at elevated temperature and pressure, usually above 10 0 atm and at temperatures between 3 00°C and 600°C. At the above process conditions, the equilibrium concentration of ammonia in a stoichiometric synthesis gas composition is below 20% by volume in the synthesis gas stream from the reactor and the stream has to be recycled to the reactor together with fresh synthesis gas in order to provide reasonably product yield of ammonia. Prior to recycling, the stream is cooled to separate ammonia from unreacted hydrogen, nitrogen and inert diluents present in the synthesis gas and a fraction of the recycle gas is purged to vent inerts. The stripped gas is then passed to a compression stage by which it is recycled to the reaction.
Expenses for compressing and recycling synthesis gas are an important factor in the economy of ammonia

production in general and in particular when production capacity of existing ammonia synthesis loops have to be increased.
It is, therefore, the general object of this invention to provide improvements in the by utilizing one combined compression step the recirculation of synthesis gas from at least two ammonia loops.
Summary of the Invention
This invention is a process for the preparation of ammonia from a hydrogen and nitrogen-containing synthesis gas comprising steps of
(a) admixing to the synthesis gas recycle gas from an ammonia separation step (f) and (g);
(b) pressurizing the admixed gas from step (a);
(c) dividing the gas from step (b) into a first make up gas stream and a second make up gas stream;
(d) introducing the first make up gas stream into a first ammonia reactor and withdrawing a first process stream being rich in ammonia;

(e) introducing the second ammonia make up gas stream into a second ammonia reactor and withdrawing a second process stream being rich in ammonia;
(f) separating ammonia from the first process stream and recycling the remainder of the process stream to step
(a) ;

(g) separating ammonia from the second process stream and recycling the remainder of the process stream to step (a) ;
" (h) combining separated ammonia from step (f) and step (g) and withdrawing an ammonia product stream.
The above process may comprise one or more of the further steps of
prior to step (a) pressurizing the ammonia synthesis gas;
purifying the pressurized ammonia synthesis gas by removing water and carbon dioxide; and
further pressurizing the purified ammonia synthesis gas.
As mentioned hereinbefore, the invention is particularly useful when increased production capacity of an existing ammonia loop is required. When revamping the existing loop an additional ammonia synthesis loop, is connected in parallel to the existing loop. As an advantage of the invention the existing synthesis gas recirculation compressor, optionally after modification, recycles both recycle synthesis gas flows from the existing loop and the connected additional loop. Thereby, only two tie-ins will be required in the synthesis loop, upstream and downstream the recirculation compressor.
