Title of Invention

"AN IMPROVED PROCESS FOR PREPARATION OF CURING AGENT WITH CROSS-LINKING PROPERTIES FOR PROPELLANT FORMULATIONS AND PROCESS OF PREPARATION THEREOF"
Abstract This invention relates to an improved process for preparation of curing agent with cross-linking properties for propellant formulations. The process comprises in reacting a mixture of hydroxy terminated compounds with diisocyanate by heating upto a temperature of 95°C for about 60 minutes.
Full Text The present invention relates to an improved process for preparation of curing agent with cross-linking properties for propellant formulations.
Considerable variety of compounds have already been proposed for the purpose. Cross linking of binding agents preferably polymers to increase their stability and the life in case of propellants or such curing agents for cross linking the hydroxy-terminated polybutadiene, herein after to be named as HTPB, with urethane linkages, isocyanates, terminated compounds like toluene diisocyanate, hexamethylene diisocyanate, dimethyl diisocyanate, isophorone diisocyanate are used as cross linking agents, more particularly curing agents, in the propellant formulations.
However, with long chain polymers, particularly copolymers, more particularly block copolymers, even more particularly hydroxy terminated block copolymers, hereinafter to be named as HTBCP, these known such cross linking agents particularly curing agents as stated herein above produce cross linked products of such polymers having poor mechanical properties.
Yet another drawback of such curing agents is that the solidification of the end product, i.e. of propellants is not achieved to the desired extent for specific applications for which they are made.
Therefore, there is great need for a novel cross linking agent, more particularly a novel curing agent with improved properties to overcome such disadvantages of the known curing agents of the prior art.
Accordingly, an object of the present invention is to propose a novel curing-agent which obviates the disadvantages associated with the curing agents of the prior art.
Another object of the present invention is to propose a curing agent used for cross linking long chain polymers for better mechanical properties.
According to this invention there is provided an improved process for preparation of curing agent with cross-linking properties for propellant formulations wherein the process comprises of heating a mixture of triol and castor oil with a diisocyanate, to a temperature of 85 to 95°C for one hour, adding a polyol, heating further for 45 min to 60 min at 90-100°C obtaining the desired curing agent wherein triol and castor oil are taken in the ratio of 1:1 by weight and diisocyanate is taken in quantity 2 to 3 times the weight of the mixture of castor oil and triol and wherein further triol, polyol and castor oil are in 5:8:5 equivalent weight ratio.
According to another preferred embodiment of the present invention, triol, preferably trimethyiol propane having three hydroxy terminated groups, polyol, preferably sorbitol having essentially six hydroxy groups and castor oil, having 2 to 3 hydroxy groups and these hydroxy terminated reactors are preferably taken in the equivalent weight ratio of 5:8:5.
According to the proposed method of the present invention, a novel curing agent is provided having isocyanate 15 to 25%.

The proposed method of the present invention for the synthesis of proposed novel curing agent is, the said process comprising of reaction of castor oil, & triol like trimethylol propane in 1:1 ratio with toluene diisocyanate which in turn is taken atleast 2 to 3 times equivalent weight proportion of the total hydroxyl value of mixture of the hydroxy terminated compounds like sorbitol and a triol and a polyol which however in turn are taken in ratio of 8:5:5. The said reaction is carried out in three necked round bottomed flask and fitted with the condenser and thermometer and to add the polyol during the ongoing process after one hour of the reaction time. According to the proposed method, the mixture of diisocyanate, castor oil and trimethylol propane is allowed to heat upto 100°C, preferably between 85 to 100°C for one hour to which the polyol like melted sorbitol which in turn is melted at 90°C is added and heating is allowed for another one hour at preferable temperature of 90°C. The whole synthesis is completed preferably in one hr to 2 hrs time, more preferably 1 1/2 to 2 hrs.
