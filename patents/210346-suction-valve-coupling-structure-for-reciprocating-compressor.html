Title of Invention

"SUCTION VALVE COUPLING STRUCTURE FOR RECIPROCATING COMPRESSOR"
Abstract A suction valve coupling structure for a reciprocating compressor is disclosed. The suction valve coupling structure comprises a suction valve (20) firmly coupled at the end portion surface (S) of a piston (10) by welding. By the above suction valve coupling structure, the coupling state of the suction valve (20) is strengthened and the coupling structure is simplified. According to this, a dead volume is excluded and a real volume is increased, thereby enhancing compression efficiency. Also, a stroke control of the piston is facilitated, and a movement of the piston can be precisely controlled. Therefore, a reliability of the coupling structure for the suction valve is increased.

TECHNICAL FIELD
The present invention relates to a reciprocating compressor and particularly to a suction valve coupling structure for a reciprocating compressor, in which the suction valve for opening and closing a gas flow passage is firmly coupled and the coupling structure is simplified, thereby minimizing a dead volume.
BACKGROUND ART
Conventionally, a compressor is a device for compressing a fluid such as air and refrigerant gas. The compressor includes a motor unit installed in the hermetic container for generating driving force, and a compression unit for sucking and compressing gas by receiving the-driving force of the motor unit. In the compressor, if a power source is applied to generate the driving force in the motor unit, the driving force is transmitted to the compression unit, thereby sucking, compressing, and discharging gas in the compression unit.

In the conventional art, since a flange and a cylinder are separately formed from each other, a mechanical tolerance occurs at the time of fabricating components and coupling the components to each other. Since cores of an inner lamination have to be laminated under a state that a core liner has been thermally fixed to the flange, an assembly process is complicated and a mass production is not easily obtained. When the flange is mal-operated, both the core linear and the inner lamination deposited on the core linear and the inner lamination deposited on the core liner are not used, thereby not effectively utilizing the components. In the invention according to KR1998-2840A, a flange and a cylinder are integrally formed, and an inner lamination is constructed to be coupled thereto as an additional component. As the result, a mechanical tolerance due to a process tolerance or an assembly tolerance is minimized, and cores constituting the inner lamination are easily laminated, thereby facilitating a massive production. Furthermore, since a lamination bed to which the inner lamination is coupled and a flange assembly having a flange portion are formed as additional components, the components can be efficiently utilized at the time of a mal-function occurrence.
