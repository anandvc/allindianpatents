Title of Invention

MOTOR-ASSISTED DRIVE UNIT FOR MOTOR-ASSISTED VEHICLE
Abstract A motor-assisted drive unit for a motor-assisted vehicle, comprising a motor (21) as a power assist source housed in a casing (94), wherein said motor-assisted drive unit has a first control board (136) and a second control board (135) on each of which control devices of said motor (21) are mounted; wherein said first (136) and second (135) control boards are disposed in said casing (94) in such a manner as to extend in the direction perpendicular to a motor shaft (83) of said motor (21); wherein said second control board (135) is overlapped, as seen along the axial direction of said motor (21), with part of said first control board (136); wherein the first control board (136) has a hole centered at the motor shaft (103) for insertion of said motor shaft (103); and wherein said second control board (135) has a first region overlapped with the motor (21), characterised in that said second control board (135) has a second region not overlapped with the motor (21), wherein said first region is smaller than said second region.

[Detailed Description of the Invention]
[00-01]
[Technical Field to Which the Invention Pertains]
The present invention relates to a motor-assisted drive unit for a motor-assisted vehicle, and particularly to a motor-assisted drive unit for a motor-assisted vehicle, which assist unit is significantly small-sized and thereby suitable for a motor-assisted vehicle having only a small space for mounting the motor-assisted drive unit.
[0002]
[Prior Art]
A vehicle such as a bicycle, characterized by detecting a manual drive torque, that is, a leg-power, and biasing a power assist source (DC motor) connected to a drive system in accordance with the detected leg-power, thereby assisting the leg-power, has been conventionally proposed. One example of a drive unit including such a power assist source, that is, a motor-assisted drive unit has been disposed, for example, in Japanese Patent Laid-open No. Hei 11-5584. The drive unit is disposed at the back of a crank shaft and its neighborhood, wherein a

controller casing for housing a controller including electronic parts (power transistor, CPU, and the like) for driving the motor, and a the controller casing projects downwardly from a motor casing for housing the motor. [0003]
[Problem to be Solved by the Invention]
The prior art drive unit, which includes the controller casing projecting downwardly from the motor casing, has a problem that it is large-sized. Further, the prior art drive unit has not taken into account the miniaturization thereof. [0004]
An object of the present invention is to solve the above-described problems of the prior art drive unit and to provide a motor-assisted drive unit for a motor-assisted vehicle, which is made as small in size as possible. [0005]
[Means for solving the Problem]
To achieve the above object, according to a first feature of the present invention, there is provided a motor-assisted drive unit for a motor-assisted vehicle, including a motor as a power assist source housed in a

casing, characterized by including a control board on which control devices of the motor are mounted; wherein the control board is provided in the casing in such a manner as to be disposed in the direction nearly-perpendicular to the motor shaft of the motor; and at least part of the control board extends, as seen along the axial direction of the motor, to a position at which it is overlapped to the motor. With this feature, since part of the control board is overlapped to the motor, as seen along the axial direction of the motor, the motor-assisted drive unit can be made as small in size as possible, and since the area of the control board extends to a position at which it is overlapped to the motor, the inner space of the casing can be made effectively used. [0006]
According to a second feature of the present invention, a CPU as one of the control devices of the motor is mounted on a portion, overlapped to the motor, of the control board in such a manner as to project in a gap between the overlapped portion of the control board and the motor. With this feature, it is possible to dispose a CPU having a relatively large mounting area in the conventional dead space. [0007]

According to a third feature of the present invention, there is provided a motor-assisted drive unit for a motor-assisted vehicle, including a motor as a power assist source housed in a casing, characterized by-including a first control board and a second control board on each of which control devices of the motor are mounted; wherein the first and second control boards are disposed in the casing in such a manner as to extend in the direction nearly perpendicular to the motor shaft of the motor; and the second control board is overlapped, as seen along the axial direction of the motor, to part of the first control board. With this feature, since the second control board is overlapped to part of the first control board, as seen from the axial direction of the motor, the motor-assisted drive unit can be made small in size as possible. Further, control devices mounted on the first and second control boards can be efficiently disposed in consideration of the correlation between the control devices, and the first and second control boards can be disposed in the casing without occurrence of a dead space. [0008]
According to a fourth feature of the present invention, control devices, typically, a CPU, a capacitor,

and a relay are mounted on the first control board, and a FET is mounted on the second control board. With this feature, since the control devices being small in heat generation, such as the CPU, capacitor, and relay, and the control devices being large in heat generation, such as the FET are mounted on the first and second control boards separated from each other, respectively, it is possible to prevent the heat generated from the FET from being directly transferred to the control devices such as the CPU, capacitor, and relay. [0009]
According to a fifth feature of the present invention, the first control board is configured as a printed wiring board, and the second control board is configured as a light metal board. Also, according to a sixth feature of the present invention, the second control board is directly attached to the inner wall surface of the casing, and the first control board is disposed over the second control board with a specific gap put therebetween. With these features, it is possible to easily transfer the heat, which is generated from the FET and the like mounted on the second control board, to the casing via the light metal board, and to prevent the first control board from being affected by the above heat

as much as possible. [0010]
According to a seventh feature of the present invention, the control board is elastically supported on the casing; according to an eighth feature of the present invention, the control board is elastically supported by using a vibration-proof rubber disposed around a casing boss portion for rotatably supporting the motor shaft of the motor; and according to a ninth feature of the present invention, the vibration-proof rubber is compressed between the control board and a motor supporting portion of the casing. With these features, vibration of the vehicle generated during running is less transmitted to the control board, to stabilize the operation of the electric parts mounted on the control board, thereby prolonging the service life of the electric parts. [0011]
According to a tenth feature of the present invention, the control devices of the motor are mounted on both surfaces of the control board; a semiconductor device and a board having a good thermal conductivity at least part of which is in contact with the semiconductor device are provided on the surface, on the casing side,

of the control board; and the board having a good thermal conductivity is in contact with the casing. With this configuration, it is possible to effectively transfer heat generated from the semiconductor device by a simple, inexpensive structure.
Accordingly, the present invention relates to a motor-assisted drive unit for a motor-assisted vehicle, comprising a motor as a power assist source housed in a casing, wherein said motor-assisted drive unit has a first control board and a second control board on each of which control devices of said motor are mounted; wherein said first and second control boards are disposed in said casing in such a manner as to extend in the direction perpendicular to a motor shaft of said motor; wherein said second control board is overlapped, as seen along the axial direction of said motor, with part of said first control board; wherein the first control board has a hole centered at the motor shaft for insertion of said motor shaft; and wherein said second control board has a first region overlapped with the motor, characterised in that said second control board has a second region not overlapped with the motor, wherein said first region is smaller than said second region.

In this way, the transmission plate 40 and the drive sprocket 80 are rotated while being offset from each other in the rotational direction depending on the load, to give a drive force to the rear wheel via the chain 82 . The load applied to the drive sprocket 80 is detected as a force generated when the pedals 79 are actuated by a driver, that is, a leg-power. [0055]
Since the projection 48 projecting from the sensor portion 1 of the leg-power detector is rotated together with the transmission plate 40, the positional relationship between the leg-power transmission ring (inner ring) 5 supporting the projection 48 and the outer ring 2 fixed to the drive sprocket 80 is determined depending on the leg-power. The positional relationship is detected by the sensor portion 1, and is supplied to a

control unit (not shown) for detecting the leg-power. [0056]
