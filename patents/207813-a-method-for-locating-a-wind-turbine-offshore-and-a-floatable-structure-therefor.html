A METHOD FOR LOCATING A WIND TURBINE OFFSHORE AND A FLOATABLE STRUCTURE THEREFOR
Abstract A method for locating a wind turbine (3) offshore comprises locating the turbine (3) on a mast (6) extending upwardly from a floatable semi-submersible structure (5), anchoring the structure (5) by a primary ligature (23) extending downwardly from the structure (5), and urging the structure downwardly in the water by the ligature (23) below its normal flotation level, wherein the structure (5) has a main central geometric axis (12), extending vertically when the structure (5) is in its equilibrium flotation state, the centre of flotation and the centre of gravity lying on the axis, and the ligature (23) being connected to the structure (5) at a point of connection (22) on the axis (12), and the structure (5) is urged downwardly by the ligature (23) for rising the centre of flotation above the centre of gravity of the structure (5), and for locating the point of connection (22) between the centre of flotation and the centre of gravity, for increasing the righting moment acting on the structure (5) for returning the structure (5) to its equilibrium flotation state.

The present invention relates to a method for locating a wind turbine offshore and a floatable structure therefor. The invention also relates to a wind farm containing wind turbines mounted offshore.
With the ever increasing cost of fossil fuels and the attendant environmental damage resulting from energy generation from fossil fuels, and the potential danger associated with generation of energy from nuclear power, it is desirable that increasingly higher proportions of energy be generated from renewable energy sources. Wind being one of the primary renewable energy sources, it is desirable that maximum utilisation should be made of wind energy in the generation of electricity and other forms of energy. However, wind powered generators suffer from a number of disadvantages. In general, wind powered generators comprise a mast with a wind turbine mounted on top thereof. The wind energy may be converted directly to electricity by the wind turbine at the top of the mast, or alternatively, a drive transmission arrangement may be provided within the core of the mast for transmitting drive from the wind turbine to an electricity generator located at the bottom or towards the bottom of the mast. Typically, such wind turbines are horizontal axis turbines, and in order to efficiently capture sufficient energy from the wind, in general, are provided with relatively long blades. Because of the relative length of the turbine blades, and furthermore, the need to locate the turbine blades in an uninterrupted air stream, such turbines are generally mounted on top of relatively high masts. Such masts tend to be relatively unsightly, and are particularly unsightly when a plurality of such masts with their corresponding wind turbines are mounted relatively closely together in what is typically referred to as a wind farm. As well as being relatively unsightly, wind turbines tend to create a significant amount of noise. This is undesirable. Accordingly,

such wind turbines are unsuitable for mounting adjacent centres of population, and because of their relatively undesirable appearance, are unsuitable for mounting in remote areas, which, in general, tend to be beauty spots.
Attempts to locate such wind turbines, and indeed, farms of wind turbines offshore have been made. However, due to the relatively hazardous environment in offshore locations, the location of wind farms offshore so far have had limited success. In general, wind turbines which are located offshore are mounted on top of a mast which is secured in the sea bed and extends upwardly therefrom. Thus, because the mast extends upwardly from the sea bed, such constructions of wind turbines and masts are suitable only for relatively shallow waters, which in general are found only relatively close to the shore. In general, shorelines are considered to be high amenity areas, and thus, in general, the siting of wind turbines relatively close to the shore is not desirable, and in general, is not permissible. Furthermore, wind speeds and direction tend to fluctuate more relatively close to the shoreline, than well out from the shoreline, for example, in relatively deep waters. Thus, for the above reasons, known methods for locating wind turbines offshore in general are unsatisfactory.
There is therefore a need for a floatable structure which is suitable for locating a wind turbine offshore. There is also a-need for a method for locating a wind turbine offshore, as is there a need for an offshore wind farm.
The present Invention is directed towards providing such a floatable structure, a method for locating a wind turbine offshore and a wind farm.
