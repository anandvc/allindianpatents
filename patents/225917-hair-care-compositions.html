FIELD OF THE INVENTION
5 This invention relates to hair care compositions, in
particular rinse-off hair care compositions such as shampoos and conditioners, which incorporate water-soluble nonionic polymers.
10 BACKGROUND AND PRIOR ART
Rinse-off hair care compositions typically comprise one or more surfactants for cleansing purposes and one or more conditioning agents, so as to provide a combination of
15 cleansing and conditioning to the hair. Typically, these conditioning agents are water-insoluble oily materials, cationic polymers or cationic surfactants.
A problem associated with such rinse-off hair care
20 compositions is that for many consumers they do not provide a sufficient level of "wet" sensory benefits, such as improved hair feel during washing.
The present inventors have found that a combination of
25 certain water-soluble nonionic polymers improves wet sensory benefits significantly when they are incorporated into rinse-off hair care compositions such as shampoos. Compositions of the invention also show a surprising improvement in ease of rinsing.
