BACKGROUND OF INVENTION:
At present, coconut shell powder is manufactured through Pulverizer or Hammer Mill wherein the quantity and quality of the coconut shell powder is in the order of around 3 tons per day with a wastage of around 30%, for a given set of machinery and inputs.
DRAWBACKS OF EXISTING METTHODS:
The currently available methods of production of coconut shell powder consist of using the broken coconut shell of various sizes and usin^ Pulverizer or Hammer Mill to cany out the grinding to produce coa>nut shell ix>wder. The foUowing are the draw backs in the available method:-
a. The broken coconut shell (raw material) is not of uniform size and
shape.
b. When this raw material is put through Pulverizer or Hammer Mill,
uniformity of the finished product is not ensured since the impact
on the raw mat^ial is not adequate to ensure required quantity
and quality of the end product.
c. Thgre is no reprocess method available while using the Hammer
Mill resulting in loss of production due to excess wastage.
d. The speed of operation and uniformity of the output are not
correlated resulting in low production per day.

e. The coconut shell powder is required to have a fine blending as per the specification of the end user without which the end user/manufecturer will not be in a position to make use of coconut shell powder for manufacture of quality mosquito coils. If a fine blending is not ensured the mosquito coils made there from would not be uniform and the mosquito coil carmot be used for longer hours. This fine blending could not be achieved with this existing method.
