The scrub/mask is prepared in a defined process involving mixing intimately the ingredients sequentually and adding balanced water to make the composition a dough type. FIELD OF INVENTION
The present invention relates to the field of hygienic/cosmetic facial scrub/mask and its preparation for moisturizing the skin and decreasing established acne and in some cases a prevention of acne and removal of makeup and dirt, and protects the skin from bacteria and contaminants comprising substances of natural origin.
BACKGROUND
During their lifetimes, most people experience undesirable facial skin conditions, such as acne, often beginning at pre-adolescenee. Dry skin, loss of firmness and elasticity accompanies the aging process. Women, in particular, turn to cosmetic treatments for enhancing the appearance of the skin, however, men can also benefit from this procedure. This has been a multi billion business now-a-days and growing tremendously. The need of the hour is further research and development in this field and to come out with an invention for skin treatment that would be easy to use, economically benefit having no side effects.
Skin care systems using masks are not a new idea as exemplified by the following patents: U.S. Pat. No. 3,806,593, to Swanbeck, issued Apr. 23, 1974. This invention is limited to treatment of skin problems, especially acne. U.S. Pat. No. 4,634,436, to La Tour, issued Jan. 6,1987 provided temporary relief fromaffliction. U.S. Pat. No. 2,002,449, to Dohm, issued Oct. 18, 1934 is for a dermatoid face mask used with commercial lotions, creams, astringents, etc. U.S. Pat. No. 3,810,996 to Sutliff; issued May 14, 1974 is a cosmetic facial mask composed of an aqueous paste. U.S. Pat. No. 5,026,552 to Gueret, issued Jun. 25,1991 is for a cleaning compound using a mesh in which gel is confined.
The above cited documents almost use synthetic substances along with other natural substances for preparation of the composition which on long use may cause adverse complications and may lead to other side effects. The above compositions may not further be economically viable involving synthetic substances. The present invention makes an innovative approach to skin problem especially facial skin problems given above involves a composition from natural origin and a process of its preparation comprising the ingredients multani matti, sandal wood powder, rose water, aloe vera extracts, caster oil and glycerine. This composition will be used as facial scrub or mask in form of a soap/powder. The soap/powder needn't be limited for facial use only and can be used on all parts of the body for getting the desired benefits.

OBJECTIVE OF THE INVENTION
The objective of the invention is to develop a facial scrub/mask comprising the ingredients multani matti, sandal wood powder, rose water, aloe vera extracts, caster oil and glycerine for moisturizing the skin and decreasing established acne and in some cases a prevention of acne and removal of makeup and dirt, and protects the skin from bacteria and contaminants comprising substances of natural origin.
Further objective of the invention is to devise a process by which the facial composition containing the natural ingredients can be prepared.
Furthermore the objective of the invention is to prepare a composition in the form of a soap that would be used on all parts of the body to obtain the beneficial effect.
Furthermore the objective of the invention is to devise a process for preparation of a facial/skin scrub/mask from the sources of natural origin.
SUMMARY OF THE INVENTION
Facial scrub/mask is prepared taking the ingredients multani matti, sandal wood powder, rose water, aloe vera extracts, caster oil and glycerine in varying weight percentage. The scrub/mask is prepared in a defined process involving mixing intimately the ingredients sequentially and adding balanced water to make the composition a dough type. Finally, shaping the mould and drying to obtain the soap in different shapes. The beneficial effect of using this soap is confirmed by applying to a panel of women for a specific duration in a specific way.
DESCRIPTION OF THE INVENTION
Multani matti is an excellent skin tone and astringent. Reduce oiliness and while nourishing the skin, keeps it soft and sparkling, helps in the removal of blemishes, impurities and grime. Improves complexion by facilitating better blood circulation. This can be used in daily bath as body pack for glowing health. The raw form of the multani matti was ground to fine powder and was subject to sieving. Particle size of -150|xm to +50|im were used for the composition for facial scrub/mask of the present invention.
Sandalwood is a relaxant with sensual properties. With a fragrance described as rich, sweet, fragrant yet delicate, woody and warm, sandalwood promotes a feeling of well-being. The sandal tree grows mainly in south India, and is used not only for medicinal purposes, but also as a perfume because of
3

its aromatic property. Sandalwood is good for all skin types, in particular dry and oily skin and acne. Even as it has moisturizing properties, it is a mild astringent and antiseptic. Its main component santalol (~90%) has antimicrobal property. Sandalwood is also soothing for cracked, chapped and irritated skin. It is also recommended for mature and tired skin as well as stretch marks and scars. Sandalwood paste is found to be effective as an effective remedy for prickly heat. The paste applied prevents excessive sweating and heals inflamed skin. Having these properties it is chosen as a component for the facial scrub/mask of the present invention. The raw wood is ground into fine powder and sieved and particle size of -150 µm to +50 µm were used for the composition for facial scrub/mask.
Rose water is one of the all-time best and most traditional facial cleansers, because rose is astringent, anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and has a lovely scent. Pure Rose Water is considered an excellent facial toner, especially for dry or sensitive skin. The water is prepared as follows: 1 cup rose-infused water, 2 teaspoons glycerin, 5 to 10 drops rose essential oil. Placed a handful of dried rose petals in a mason jar, covered with boiling water, seal, and let set overnight. After the rose petal infusion has set overnight, strain. Combine the ingredients in a pretty jar, and shaked to blend. The antiseptic quality of essential oils also acts as a preservative. The enhanced property of rose water makes a good choice as an ingredient in facial scrub/mask. The final form of the rose water as obtained is taken as an ingredient for the present invention.
Aloe vera, a tropical or subtropical plant of the genus Aloe, has lance-shaped leaves with jagged edges and sharp points. The leaves contain a viscous but essentially clear gel given structural rigidity by hair-like connected fibers that run through it. Freshly excised from the plant and applied in vitro, this gel has been used medicinally for centuries by those living where the plant normally grows to relieve the pain of plant and animal stings, such as jellyfish stings. Aloe Vera uses in all cosmetic industries (Aloe vera is very important ingredients in all cosmetics like powder, facial cream, shampoo, oil etc.), medical industries, food processing industries and etc. The gel of the aloe vera plant has been known to have healing powers for centuries. Practically everybody is aware of aloe vera gel for treating burns, skin inflammation etc. In higher concentrations, (60 to 90 %) aloe vera extracts can eliminate dozens of harmful bacteria.
Aloe vera gel product is produced by mechanically separating the aloe vera gel matrix from the outer cortex of the aloe vera leaf and, after filtering particles of the aloe vera leaf from the gel matrix, the extracted gel is then mixed in a suitable vessel equipped for temperature control and heated to the range of from about 30.degree. C. to about 70.degree. C. When the gel reaches this range, ascorbic acid is added to compensate for the loss due to oxidation and exposure to air; while maintaining temperature and continuing mixing, other ingredients can be added to stabilize the color, stabilize the
oxidant, and to adjust pH, as well as surfactants. The hot gel is admixed for a sufficient period of time
4

so that it is thoroughly admixed and bacteria is killed. The hot gel is immediately passed to a heat exchanger where the temperature is dropped to ambient temperature or below (25.degree. C. or below) within about one hour. The gel once cooled to ambient temperature is then ready for storage at room temperature and will exhibit an increased shelf life. The gel so obtained is ready for use in the facial scrub composition of the present invention.
Castor oil is a vegetable oil obtained from the castor bean (or preferably castor seed as the castor plant, Ricinus communis, is not a member of the bean family). Castor oil has an unusual composition and chemistry, which makes it quite valuable. About 1 % of global castor oil production goes into medical or health store products. It is used to ease constipation and as an emetic to induce vomiting. Castor Oil is regarded as one of the most valuable laxatives in medicine. Caster oil used in the present invention is extracted in the conventional manner.
Glycerine is a clear, colourless liquid with a slightly sweet taste. Glycerine is used as an ingredient in cosmetics, medicines, toothpaste, esters, liquid soaps, tobacco products, and food products. It is also used extensively as an intermediate in the production of polyester, polyurethane, and alkyd resin formulations. In cosmetics, glycerine is a favorite in keeping the skin soft and is used in body and shaving creams. It is the basic material in which toothpaste is formed and preserves the desired smoothness and viscosity of the paste. Glycerine is used in cough medicines and anesthetics, for ear treatments, and in bacteriological media. Glycerine available in the market was used as such for the present invention. The main purpose of using glycerine in the composition is to produce a desired smoothness and making skin soft.

5. A process for preparation of a facial scrub/mask using the sources of natural origin as claimed in claim (1-4) comprising the steps of

a) adding required amount of sandalwood powder to mattanie matte and blending/mixing thoroughly;
b) adding required amount of castor oil and glycerine to the mixture of step (a);
c) adding required/calculated amount of rose water and aloe vera extract to the mixture obtained in step (b);

(d) finally adding water as required to the mixture of step constantly stirring to form a dough;
(e) moulding the dough formed into different shapes as desired;
(f) leaving the moulded shapes for two days for drying to get the final product.
