In our non pre-published patent application: PCT/EP97/03634 it has been described that AFPs can be isolated from natural sources such as cold-acclimatised grass by means of Via new relatively simple process. This process leads for the 15 first time to the identification of AFPs which can

This process for the recovery of AFPs from grass involves 20 the steps of
a) isolating a AFP containing juice from the grass;
b) heat treating the grass or the AFP containing juice to a temperature of at least 60°C;
c) removing the insoluble fraction.
25
Step c of the above process will usually take place after steps a and b. Step a and b can be done in any desired order, for example step a followed by step b (in that case the AFP rich juice will be heated) or step b followed by 30 step a (in that case the natural source will be heated) or step a and b simultaneously.
