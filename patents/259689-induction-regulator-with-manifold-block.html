Title of Invention

INDUCTION REGULATOR WITH MANIFOLD BLOCK
Abstract The invention provides a manifold block (140) for improving the efficiency of an internal combustion engine. The manifold block is arranged to be located between an inlet manifold and a cylinder head of the engine (130), and comprises a block of material having at least one manifold channel (110) there through. The manifold channel has an input end and an output end, wherein the input end is connected to the inlet manifold (102), and the output end is connected to the cylinder. The manifold channel includes at least one injector head channel (132) located towards its input end and adapted to receive a fuel injector. The manifold block may also incorporate one or more induction regulators for improving the efficiency of fuel/air mixing in the manifold channels (104, 106, 108, 110).

INDUCTION REGULATOR BLOCK
The present invention relates to a manifold block for an internal combustion engine and in particular a manifold block that promotes mixing of fuel and air 5 in the engine upstream of the cylinder head, improving the efficiency, emissions profile and/or power of the engine. The block may also incorporate an induction regulator further to facilitate fuel/air mixing.
It is well known to use a fuel injector to control the fuel/air mixture ratio in an 10 engine. Conventional fuel injection systems are generally adapted to monitor the amount of fuel supplied to the engine, or to the or each cylinder thereof, the timing and duration of each injection event being controlled to provide a desirable fuel/air mixture in the cylinder. However, conventional injector systems are generally not designed to optimise the mixing of fuel and air 15 upstream of the cylinder(s), and the present invention seeks to provide a means for optimising this aspect of injector systems.

Abstract:
The invention provides a manifold block (140) for improving the efficiency of an internal combustion engine. The manifold block is arranged to be located between an inlet manifold and a cylinder head of the engine (130), and comprises a block of material having at least one manifold channel (110) there through. The manifold channel has an input end and an output end, wherein the input end is connected to the inlet manifold (102), and the output end is connected to the cylinder. The manifold channel includes at least one injector head channel (132) located towards its input end and adapted to receive a fuel injector. The manifold block may also incorporate one or more induction regulators for improving the efficiency of fuel/air mixing in the manifold channels (104, 106, 108, 110).
