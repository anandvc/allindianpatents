FIELD OF THE INVENTION
This invention relates in general to communication systems, and more
specifically to a method and apparatus for affiliation with associated groups by
a wireless communication device.
10
BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
There currently exist in the market wireless communication devices
capable of "instant communication" between devices without requiring dialing
the phone number of the other wireless communication device. An example of
15 this is the Nextel™ system in the United States and iDEN™ wireless
communication devices from Motorola™. In such devices, it is possible to
arrange a number of users into a unit or group for ease of operation in using the
instant communication feature. When the wireless communication device is a
member of a particular group it is said to be "associated" with that group. A
20 wireless communication device may be associated with one or more groups.
When the wireless communication device is programmed to operate within one

WO 2004/080090 PCT/US2004/004101
2
of its associated groups, that is, to be able to select a user from that group and
commence instant communication, the wireless communication device is said to
be "affiliated" with that group. Currently, with one exception noted below, the
wireless communication device may only be affiliated with one group with
5 which it is associated. In addition, in the example of the Nextel system, the
user of a wireless communication device with group communication capability
has no formal way of knowing what groups the wireless communication device
belongs to, leaving that to word of mouth and other informal means. The
exception to single group affiliations mentioned above is the case in some
10 utility-oriented applications where a 'super group' may be configured by a
system operator where the super group is a joining of one or more existing
groups. Similar to the above case, however, the wireless communication device
may only be affiliated with one of these super groups at a time and only if
previously configured by the system operator.
15 Clearly a need exists for a method and apparatus for improved affiliation
with associated groups.

DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF PREFERRED EMBODIMENT
In overview, the present disclosure concerns wireless communication
devices and a network controller that provide group communication service and
5 more particularly such service to a user thereof. More particularly various
inventive concepts and principles embodied in the methods and apparatus for
the selection of groups and rules associated with the selection are discussed.
The wireless communication devices and network controllers of particular
interest are those constructed to allow users to communicate in a mode
10 commonly known as "instant communication" or sometimes referred to as
"walkie-talkie mode." When so enabled, the wireless communication device
can be used to select another particular wireless communication device, and
thereby its user, and press a "talk" button. Communication with the selected
particular wireless communication device may then commence without the need
15 for dialing a telephone number, but rather by simply pressing the talk button to
talk and releasing it to listen. A concept of "group call" may also be supported
whereby a user of the wireless communication device may simultaneously
communicate with multiple users as a group. The wireless communication
device of the user may belong to more than one group.
20 While the concept of the group used in connection with the instant
communication feature offers a convenience to the user, the need to shift
between groups limits its usefulness. There are situations where the user of the
wireless communication device may need or desire to have instant

WO 2004/080090 PCT/US2004/004101
5
communication with members of separate groups. This might be the ease when
on a job site and the need to be in touch with a co-worker on one group is high
but when a family situation requires that communication be maintained with a
family member whose wireless communication device belongs to a second
5 group. Another purpose for communicating with multiple groups would be to
allow a work group to exist in smaller, more efficient units, but when required
for a large project or emergency situation, the work group could be easily united
with another work group or work groups, allowing for more efficient operation.
As further discussed below, various inventive principals and combinations
10 thereof are advantageously employed to allow the user of the wireless
communication device greater flexibility in both discovering available groups
and selecting from those groups to enable multiple group communication.
