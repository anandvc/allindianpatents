Title of Invention

"STRUCTURE FOR LUBRICATING FORWARD TILTING ENGINE"
Abstract [Object]&nbsp;To provide an engine lubricating structure suitable for a motorcycle. [Solving Means]&nbsp;In a comparative example shown in Fig. 15(a), oil flows in an engine lubricating structure 171 through an oil passing hole 172 and eventually the engine lubricating structure 171 is filled with the oil.&nbsp;In an embodiment shown in Fig. 15(b), oil flows in the engine lubricating structure 171 through the oil passing hole 172; however, since the oil in the engine lubricating structure 171 is discharged through an oil escape groove 178, it is not accumulated in the engine lubricating structure 171 so much. [Effect]&nbsp;Even for an engine having a structure in which a cylinder axis is disposed substantially in the horizontal direction and oil is liable to be accumulated in an engine lubricating structure, the oil in the engine lubricating structure can be effectively escaped through an oil escape groove.&nbsp;As a result, a valve mechanism of the engine can be continuously smoothly operated.-
Full Text The present invention relates to a structure for lubricating a forward tilting engine, particularly to an engine lubricating structure suitable for a hybrid type motorcycle including an electric motor in addition to an engine. -.[00 02-]
[Related Art]
Vehicles of a type using gasoline engine as a drive source are mainly available at present; however, vehicles of a type using an electric motor as a drive source are required at locations where occurrence of exhaust gas must be avoided. A motor-driven vehicle, however, has an inconvenience in the increased body weight and the shortened running distance. To cope with such an inconvenience, it has been increasingly required to develop a hybrid type vehicle including an electric motor in addition to an engine. [0003]
For example, Japanese Patent Laid-open No. Hei 8-1 175477 discloses a hybrid type motorcycle entitled "Device for Switching Engine Power to/from Motor Power in Motorcycle or the Like".
In the above document, an engine 10 is a simple air-cooled two cycle engine.
[0004]
A motorcycle, however, is required to mount a four cycle engine in consideration of fuel consumption and emission. In this case, since a four cycle engine is higher in vehicle height than a two cycle engine, it is preferably designed such that a cylinder axis tilts forward for lowering the center of gravity of the vehicle.

Further, to further improve power consumption, it is required to make friction (frictional resistance) as small as possible. From this viewpoint, a valve chamber for containing a valve drive mechanism is desired to be formed into a closed space isolated from engine oil for keeping the valve chamber in a dry state thereby eliminating friction of the engine oil.
[Problem to be Solved by the Invention]
As the cylinder axis tilts forward, an angle of a lower valve may tilt downward with respect to a horizontal axis. In this case, an engine including a valve lifter may cause a problem that oil is less escaped from the oil passing hole 211 and is accumulated in the engine lubricating structure 212, so that new oil is difficult to flow in the engine lubricating structure 212, thereby
exerting adverse effect on valve operation.
The engine structure in which the valve chamber is formed into a closed space not allowing permeation of engine oil causes a problem that it is difficult for engine oil supplied to a cylinder head to return to an oil pan.
Accordingly, an object of the present invention is to provide an engine lubricating structure suitable for a hybrid type power unit in which the center of gravity is lowered.
To achieve the above object, according to an invention described in claim 1, there is provided a structure for lubricating a forward tilting engine in which a valve is driven by a cam shaft through a valve lifter; a cylinder axis tilts forward and a valve axis tilts downward from a horizontal axis; and an engine lubricating structure for containing said valve lifter is provided in a cylinder head, characterized in that an oil escape groove is provided in a lower wall of said engine lubricating structure,
When the valve axis tilts downward with respect to the horizontal axis, oil is liable to be accumulated in the engine lubricating structure; however, according to the present invention, it is possible to effectively escape the
oil in the engine lubricating structure through the oil escape groove while keeping the lowering of the center of gravity of the hybrid type power unit.
To achieve the above object, according an invention described in claim 2, there is provided a structure for lubricating a forward tilting engine in which a valve drive mechanism is provided in a cylinder head and a valve chamber for containing said valve drive mechanism is provided on a side portion of a cylinder; said valve chamber is formed into a closed space isolated from engine oil; a cylinder axis is disposed substantially in the horizontal direction; and an oil return passage for returning oil in said valve chamber to an oil pan is provided in both said cylinder head and a cylinder block, characterized in that said oil return passage extends downward from a lower portion of said valve chamber, and an air passage extending from said oil pan to an upper portion of said valve chamber is provided in both said cylinder block and said cylinder head.
With this configuration, since oil is returned from the valve chamber to the oil pan through the lower oil return passage and air is supplied from the oil pan to the valve chamber through the upper air passage, the oil can be smoothly returned.
Accordingly there is provided a structure for lubricating a forward tilting engine in which a valve is driven by a cam shaft through a valve lifter; a cylinder axis tilts forward and a valve axis tilts downward from a horizontal axis; and an engine lubricating structure for containing said valve lifter is provided in a cylinder head in the valve lifter containing recess, characterized in that
• an oil escape groove is provided in the axial direction of the valve and
in a surface of a lower wall of said valve lifter containing recess,
