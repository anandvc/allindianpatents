Title of Invention

LIQUID HARDENING
Abstract The present invention relates to a curing agents for air- drying alkyd-based resins, coatings, such as paint, varnish or wood stain, inks and linoleum floor coverings, based on an iron/manganese complex containing tetradentate, pentadentate or hexadentate nitrogen donor ligands.

BACKGROUND OF INVENTION
10
Recent reviews describe different alternatives, especially based on Mn and Fe compounds (Bieleman, J.H. in Additives in Plastics and Paints, Chimia, 56, 184 (2002); Bieleman, J.H., Marcomol. Symp., 187, 811 (2002); van Gorkum R, Bouwman E,
15 Coord. Chem. Rev., 249, 1709 (2005)).
WO 03/093384 describes the use of transition-metal salts or complexes based on pyrazoles, aliphatic and aromatic amines, 2,2'-bipyridine, 1,10'-phenanthroline, 1,4,7-trimethyl-
20 1,4,7-triazacyclononane in combination with a reducing agent as drying agent. Especially Fe and Mn salts and complexes were preferred in combination with ascorbic acid or derivatives thereof. WO03/093384 demonstrates that iron compounds have a rather poor activity and hence high dosages
25 are needed to get satisfactory drying activity. A drawback of using iron compounds at high levels is that an unwanted yellowish/brownish colour is imparted to the mix.

The present invention relates to a siccative for alkyd-based
resins, coatings, inks, and linoleum floor coverings,

10 ligand. Whilst certain paints/inks contain unsaturated oils/acids as cross-linking agent, most of them contain alkyd-based resins that contain unsaturated groups. The alkyd-based air-drying coatings to which the siccative of the present invention can be added, comprise coatings, such
15 as paint, varnish or wood stain, and also includes inks and linoleum floor coverings and the like. The siccative is equally applicable to setting paints/inks/print which do not contain alkyd-based resins, but do contain at least 2% of double or triple unsaturated compound.
20
The coatings, inks, and linoleum floor coverings may also include compositions wherein besides the alkyd based binder also other binders are present, e.g. compositions comprising 1) an alkyd- based binder and 2) a polyacrylate and/or a
25 polyurethane binder. Conventional air~drying alkyds can be obtained by a polycondensation reaction of one or more polyhydric alcohols, one or more polycarboxylic acids or the corresponding anhydrides, and long chain unsaturated fatty acids or oils.
